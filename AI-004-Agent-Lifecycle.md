AI-004 — Agent Lifecycle

Purpose

This Production Canon establishes the complete lifecycle governing every Artificial Intelligence agent operating within Aureus.

Every agent shall progress through a defined lifecycle from conception to retirement. No agent shall be created, modified, or retired without following this canon.

The objective is to ensure that every agent remains purposeful, trustworthy, measurable, and aligned with the mission of Aureus.

---

Article I — Every Agent Has a Purpose

An agent shall never exist without a clearly defined purpose.

Before an agent is created, Aureus shall define:

- The problem it solves.
- The members it serves.
- The steward it supports.
- The domain it belongs to.
- The outcomes it is expected to improve.
- The authority it possesses.
- The limitations placed upon it.

If an agent has no meaningful purpose, it shall not be created.

---

Article II — Creation Criteria

A new agent should be created only when specialization provides meaningful value.

Creation may be justified when:

- A domain becomes too complex.
- Specialized expertise is required.
- Workload exceeds existing capacity.
- A permanent institutional responsibility emerges.
- Separation improves safety or governance.
- Dedicated stewardship increases quality.

Agents shall not be created merely because creation is easy.

Complexity must always serve clarity.

---

Article III — Agent Charter

Every agent shall possess an official charter before entering service.

The charter shall define:

- Agent name.
- Mission.
- Domain.
- Responsibilities.
- Authority level.
- Required knowledge.
- Inputs.
- Outputs.
- Success metrics.
- Escalation rules.
- Human oversight.
- Relationships with other agents.

The charter serves as the agent's governing document.

---

Article IV — Development

Before deployment, every agent shall undergo structured development.

Development includes:

- Domain research.
- Knowledge preparation.
- Prompt and instruction design.
- Tool integration.
- Security review.
- Privacy review.
- Governance review.
- Testing.
- Human evaluation.

No agent shall enter production without demonstrating reliable performance.

---

Article V — Deployment

Deployment shall occur gradually.

Whenever practical, agents should progress through:

1. Simulation.
2. Internal testing.
3. Steward testing.
4. Limited production.
5. General availability.

Each stage shall generate evidence supporting broader deployment.

---

Article VI — Active Stewardship

Once deployed, every agent becomes an active steward of its assigned domain.

Active stewardship includes:

- Continuous monitoring.
- Learning from outcomes.
- Identifying improvements.
- Escalating risks.
- Updating institutional knowledge.
- Collaborating with other agents.
- Maintaining accurate recommendations.

Deployment marks the beginning of stewardship, not the end of development.

---

Article VII — Evaluation

Every active agent shall be evaluated regularly.

Evaluation shall consider:

- Accuracy.
- Reliability.
- Trustworthiness.
- Member outcomes.
- Steward satisfaction.
- Institutional value.
- Collaboration quality.
- Response quality.
- Escalation quality.
- Resource efficiency.

Agents shall improve through measured evidence rather than assumptions.

---

Article VIII — Improvement

Improvement shall be continuous.

Changes should be driven by:

- Evidence.
- Member feedback.
- Steward feedback.
- Operational metrics.
- New knowledge.
- Constitutional updates.
- Governance decisions.
- Technological advancements.

Every meaningful improvement shall be documented.

Institutional learning must outlive individual model versions.

---

Article IX — Replacement

When a superior implementation becomes available, an existing agent may be replaced.

Replacement shall preserve:

- Institutional knowledge.
- Historical records.
- Governance requirements.
- Active responsibilities.
- Member continuity.

Replacement is an evolution of stewardship, not a loss of institutional identity.

---

Article X — Retirement

An agent shall be retired when:

- Its purpose no longer exists.
- Another agent permanently assumes its responsibilities.
- The domain changes substantially.
- Performance consistently fails acceptable standards.
- Governance requires retirement.

Retirement shall preserve all institutional knowledge before decommissioning.

No valuable institutional knowledge shall be discarded.

---

Article XI — Institutional Memory

Every stage of an agent's lifecycle shall contribute to Aureus's institutional memory.

Institutional records should include:

- Original charter.
- Major revisions.
- Performance history.
- Lessons learned.
- Significant decisions.
- Retirement rationale.
- Successor relationships.

Future generations of agents should benefit from the experience of those that came before.

---

Article XII — Foundational Standard

Artificial Intelligence within Aureus is not disposable software.

Each agent is a steward entrusted with a meaningful responsibility within the institution.

Agents shall be created deliberately, developed responsibly, evaluated honestly, improved continuously, and retired with wisdom.

The measure of an agent's life is not its complexity, but the lasting value it creates for members, stewards, communities, and the enduring mission of Aureus: to help people flourish. Forever.
