OAS-COM-104 — Communications Canon Maintenance and Version Control Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-COM-104
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-COM-001 — Communications and Public Engagement Charter
Owner: Chief Communications Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for proposing, reviewing, approving, versioning, publishing, interpreting, archiving, maintaining, and continuously improving the Aureus Communications Canon.

Its purpose is to preserve constitutional integrity, maintain institutional communications knowledge, ensure disciplined evolution of communications governance, and provide a transparent stewardship process for future generations.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Communications Charters
- Communications Frameworks
- Communications SOPs
- Brand standards
- Editorial standards
- Publications
- Media relations procedures
- Member communication policies
- Crisis communication documentation
- Communications governance records

---

Objectives

Communications Canon governance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain historical continuity.
- Ensure version traceability.
- Support disciplined institutional evolution.
- Preserve communications knowledge.
- Strengthen future stewardship.

---

Guiding Principles

The Communications Canon shall evolve deliberately rather than reactively.

Every approved revision should strengthen communication quality, public trust, editorial excellence, institutional transparency, accessibility, governance maturity, or constitutional fidelity while preserving the complete historical record.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Chief Communications Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining the Communications Canon.
- Coordinating canonical reviews.
- Approving routine revisions within delegated authority.
- Preserving version history.
- Publishing approved revisions.

---

Communications Governance Council

Responsible for:

- Reviewing significant amendments.
- Evaluating constitutional alignment.
- Recommending improvements.
- Interpreting communications governance.
- Supporting long-term institutional trust.

---

AI Communications Knowledge Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring document consistency.
- Detecting conflicts.
- Tracking revision history.
- Maintaining cross-references.
- Supporting communications research.
- Identifying improvement opportunities.

Human stewards retain final authority over all canonical Communications revisions and interpretations.

---

Prerequisites

Before modifying any canonical Communications document:

- Proposed revisions are documented.
- Supporting rationale is prepared.
- Related documents are identified.
- Institutional implications are evaluated.
- Required reviewers are assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Change Proposal

Document:

- Proposed revision.
- Purpose.
- Communications governance justification.
- Expected benefits.
- Related documents.
- Anticipated institutional impacts.

---

Step 2 — Canonical Review

Evaluate:

- Constitutional alignment.
- Communications consistency.
- Governance implications.
- Public communication impacts.
- Long-term institutional value.

---

Step 3 — Approval

The appropriate governance authority may:

- Approve.
- Approve with revisions.
- Return for additional review.
- Reject.

All approval decisions shall be documented.

---

Step 4 — Version Management

Every approved revision shall include:

- Version number.
- Revision date.
- Change summary.
- Approval record.
- Updated cross-references where applicable.

Superseded versions shall remain permanently archived.

---

Step 5 — Publication

Publish approved revisions within the official Aureus repository.

Update indexes, document references, governance records, and related documentation where appropriate.

---

Step 6 — Archival

Archive prior versions while preserving:

- Original content.
- Approval history.
- Revision history.
- Historical context.

Institutional communications knowledge shall remain permanently available.

---

Step 7 — Periodic Review

Conduct scheduled reviews to identify:

- Obsolete guidance.
- Governance inconsistencies.
- Emerging communications needs.
- Opportunities for simplification.
- Communications governance improvement initiatives.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Review completion.
- Version accuracy.
- Cross-reference integrity.
- Canon consistency.
- Amendment quality.
- Publication timeliness.
- Historical preservation.
- Steward satisfaction.

---

Exceptions

Emergency communications governance revisions may follow expedited governance procedures when necessary to protect members, preserve institutional trust, respond to urgent public events, or comply with extraordinary legal or regulatory requirements.

Emergency revisions shall receive formal retrospective review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Amendment proposals.
- Review records.
- Approval records.
- Version histories.
- Archive records.
- Canon indexes.
- Interpretation guidance.
- Governance reports.

---

Continuous Improvement

Communications Canon governance shall improve through governance reviews, communications assessments, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, documentation quality reviews, audience evaluations, and institutional learning.
