OAS-COM-003 — Brand, Media, and Publications Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-COM-003
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-COM-002 — Communications Governance and Public Engagement Framework
Owner: Chief Communications Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the constitutional model for brand governance, media relations, publications, educational content, digital communications, social media stewardship, content lifecycle management, and institutional reputation throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure all public-facing communications consistently strengthen trust, accurately represent Aureus' mission, preserve constitutional alignment, and support long-term institutional credibility.

---

Mission

Brand stewardship exists to faithfully communicate Aureus' identity, values, mission, and work through truthful, consistent, educational, and responsible public communications.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Brand governance
- Visual identity
- Media relations
- Publications
- Educational content
- Digital communications
- Social media
- Content lifecycle management
- Public reputation
- Communication archives

---

Guiding Principles

Brand stewardship shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote truthfulness.
- Encourage consistency.
- Strengthen institutional trust.
- Support education.
- Foster long-term credibility.

The Aureus brand is an institutional trust held in stewardship rather than a marketing asset alone.

---

Brand Governance

Brand governance shall establish:

- Approved visual identity standards.
- Messaging standards.
- Voice and tone guidance.
- Publication approval processes.
- Brand consistency reviews.
- Continuous improvement.

Every public representation shall strengthen institutional trust.

---

Media Relations

Media engagement shall:

- Provide accurate information.
- Respect confidentiality.
- Promote transparency where appropriate.
- Support informed public understanding.
- Preserve institutional integrity.
- Maintain professional relationships.

Only authorized stewards may issue official institutional statements.

---

Publications

Official publications shall:

- Be factually accurate.
- Reflect constitutional values.
- Support education.
- Maintain editorial quality.
- Include appropriate review.
- Preserve historical records.

Published materials become part of Aureus' institutional knowledge.

---

Digital Communications

Digital communications shall include:

- Website content.
- Member communications.
- Social media.
- Email communications.
- Digital publications.
- Multimedia content.

Digital communication standards shall remain consistent across all platforms.

---

AI-Assisted Communications

AI may assist through:

- Draft preparation.
- Editorial review.
- Accessibility improvements.
- Translation support.
- Metadata generation.
- Publication scheduling.

Human stewards remain responsible for official publications, media interviews, brand standards, institutional messaging, and governance decisions.

---

Reputation Stewardship

Institutional reputation shall be strengthened through:

- Truthful communication.
- Consistent conduct.
- Educational leadership.
- Responsible engagement.
- Transparent governance.
- Continuous improvement.

Trust shall be earned through faithful stewardship.

---

Performance Measurement

Communications governance shall evaluate:

- Brand consistency.
- Publication quality.
- Audience engagement.
- Educational impact.
- Media effectiveness.
- Public trust.
- Governance maturity.
- Mission support.

Measurements shall strengthen institutional communications.

---

Continuous Improvement

Brand stewardship shall improve through:

- Editorial reviews.
- Audience feedback.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance evaluations.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens Aureus' public voice.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all brand management, media relations, publications, digital communications, and institutional reputation activities throughout Aureus.

All Communications SOPs, publication procedures, media policies, digital communication standards, branding documentation, educational content standards, and communications governance documents shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Communications Charter.
