OAS-SEC-104 — Privacy and Data Protection Operations Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-SEC-104
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-SEC-006 — Privacy and Data Protection Framework
Owner: Chief Security Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for collecting, classifying, storing, accessing, using, sharing, retaining, archiving, and securely disposing of protected information throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to preserve individual privacy, protect sensitive institutional information, maintain constitutional alignment, support lawful information handling, and strengthen institutional trust.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Personal information
- Sensitive information
- Confidential institutional information
- Data collection
- Data classification
- Data storage
- Information sharing
- Data retention
- Secure disposal
- Privacy governance

---

Objectives

Privacy operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Respect individual privacy.
- Protect confidential information.
- Maintain lawful information practices.
- Reduce information risk.
- Strengthen institutional trust.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Privacy Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining privacy procedures.
- Coordinating privacy reviews.
- Managing privacy documentation.
- Supporting privacy compliance.
- Preparing governance reports.

---

Information Owners

Responsible for:

- Classifying information.
- Approving authorized access.
- Reporting privacy concerns.
- Supporting information protection.

---

AI Privacy Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring information classification.
- Tracking retention schedules.
- Detecting unusual access patterns.
- Supporting privacy reporting.
- Preparing compliance summaries.
- Organizing documentation.

Human stewards remain responsible for privacy decisions, disclosure approvals, legal interpretation, and governance actions.

---

Prerequisites

Before collecting or processing protected information:

- A legitimate operational purpose is documented.
- Information classification is determined.
- Required approvals are obtained.
- Privacy safeguards are established.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Information Collection

Collect only information that is:

- Necessary.
- Relevant.
- Accurate where practicable.
- Authorized.
- Consistent with institutional purposes.

Avoid unnecessary collection of sensitive information.

---

Step 2 — Classification

Classify information according to:

- Sensitivity.
- Confidentiality.
- Legal requirements.
- Operational importance.
- Security requirements.

Apply appropriate handling controls.

---

Step 3 — Storage and Protection

Protect information through:

- Secure storage.
- Access controls.
- Encryption where appropriate.
- Backup procedures.
- Monitoring.

---

Step 4 — Access and Use

Permit access only to authorized individuals with a legitimate operational need.

Monitor significant access activities where appropriate.

---

Step 5 — Information Sharing

Before sharing protected information:

- Verify authorization.
- Confirm lawful purpose.
- Apply secure transmission methods.
- Record disclosures where appropriate.

---

Step 6 — Retention and Archival

Maintain information according to approved retention schedules.

Archive records that require long-term preservation while maintaining confidentiality and integrity.

---

Step 7 — Secure Disposal

After retention requirements are satisfied:

- Obtain required approvals.
- Confirm no legal hold exists.
- Securely destroy information.
- Document disposal where appropriate.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Privacy compliance.
- Information classification accuracy.
- Access control effectiveness.
- Retention compliance.
- Secure disposal completion.
- Privacy incident frequency.
- Governance reporting quality.
- Member trust indicators.

---

Exceptions

Legal requirements, investigations, audits, or governance directives may require protected information to be retained beyond standard schedules.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Information inventories.
- Classification registers.
- Retention schedules.
- Privacy assessments.
- Access records.
- Disclosure records.
- Disposal records.
- Governance reports.

---

Continuous Improvement

Privacy operations shall improve through privacy reviews, security assessments, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance evaluations, and lessons learned.
