OAS-SEC-103 — Security Incident Response and Recovery Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-SEC-103
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-SEC-005 — Incident Response and Business Continuity Framework
Owner: Chief Security Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for detecting, reporting, assessing, containing, investigating, eradicating, recovering from, documenting, and reviewing security incidents affecting Aureus.

Its purpose is to minimize operational disruption, protect members and institutional assets, preserve evidence, restore services safely, and strengthen institutional resilience through continuous learning.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Cybersecurity incidents
- Physical security incidents
- Information security incidents
- Privacy incidents
- Operational security incidents
- Business continuity events
- Disaster recovery activation
- Evidence preservation
- Incident communications
- Post-incident reviews

---

Objectives

Incident response shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Minimize operational disruption.
- Preserve evidence.
- Coordinate effective response.
- Restore operations safely.
- Strengthen institutional resilience.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Incident Response Steward

Responsible for:

- Coordinating incident response.
- Maintaining incident records.
- Directing response activities.
- Coordinating communications.
- Preparing incident reports.

---

System Owners

Responsible for:

- Supporting containment.
- Assisting recovery.
- Validating restored services.
- Reporting system impacts.

---

AI Security Operations Steward

Responsible for:

- Detecting anomalies.
- Prioritizing alerts.
- Correlating security events.
- Generating incident timelines.
- Tracking response tasks.
- Preparing incident summaries.

Human stewards remain responsible for incident classification, containment decisions, external communications, recovery approval, and governance actions.

---

Prerequisites

Before responding to an incident:

- Incident reporting channels are available.
- Response roles are assigned.
- Communication procedures are documented.
- Evidence preservation procedures are understood.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Detection and Reporting

Upon identifying a potential incident:

- Record the initial report.
- Notify appropriate stewards.
- Preserve available evidence.
- Begin preliminary assessment.

---

Step 2 — Assessment

Determine:

- Incident type.
- Severity.
- Scope.
- Affected assets.
- Potential impact.
- Immediate response priorities.

---

Step 3 — Containment

Implement appropriate containment measures to:

- Limit further harm.
- Protect unaffected systems.
- Preserve evidence.
- Maintain essential operations where possible.

---

Step 4 — Investigation

Investigate:

- Root cause.
- Attack or failure sequence.
- Affected resources.
- Evidence.
- Contributing factors.

Document findings throughout the investigation.

---

Step 5 — Eradication and Recovery

Remove the cause of the incident and:

- Restore systems.
- Validate integrity.
- Confirm security controls.
- Resume operations safely.

Recovery shall prioritize mission-critical services.

---

Step 6 — Communication

Provide timely communications to authorized stakeholders regarding:

- Incident status.
- Operational impacts.
- Recovery progress.
- Required actions.

Protect confidential and security-sensitive information.

---

Step 7 — Post-Incident Review

Evaluate:

- Response effectiveness.
- Recovery performance.
- Lessons learned.
- Policy improvements.
- Training needs.
- Preventive measures.

Assign corrective actions and track them to completion.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Detection time.
- Response time.
- Containment time.
- Recovery time.
- Evidence preservation.
- Incident recurrence.
- Corrective action completion.
- Operational resilience.

---

Exceptions

Extraordinary incidents may require emergency governance actions while maintaining documentation, preserving evidence, and conducting a formal retrospective review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Incident reports.
- Investigation records.
- Evidence inventories.
- Recovery logs.
- Communication records.
- Corrective action plans.
- Post-incident reviews.
- Governance reports.

---

Continuous Improvement

Incident response shall improve through exercises, incident analyses, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance reviews, threat intelligence, and lessons learned.
