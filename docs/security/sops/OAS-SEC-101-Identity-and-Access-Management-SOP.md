OAS-SEC-101 — Identity and Access Management Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-SEC-101
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-SEC-003 — Identity and Access Management Framework
Owner: Chief Security Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for identity provisioning, authentication, authorization, privileged access management, credential lifecycle management, periodic access reviews, and identity deprovisioning throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure that access to institutional systems and information is granted only to authorized identities, remains appropriately limited, and is continuously governed according to constitutional principles and operational necessity.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Identity provisioning
- Authentication
- Authorization
- Role-based access control
- Privileged accounts
- Service accounts
- Credential management
- Access reviews
- Identity monitoring
- Identity deprovisioning

---

Objectives

Identity management shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Enforce least privilege.
- Strengthen accountability.
- Protect sensitive information.
- Reduce security risk.
- Support operational continuity.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Identity Steward

Responsible for:

- Provisioning identities.
- Managing access requests.
- Maintaining identity records.
- Coordinating access reviews.
- Supporting identity governance.

---

System Owners

Responsible for:

- Approving appropriate access.
- Reviewing assigned permissions.
- Reporting unnecessary access.
- Supporting periodic reviews.

---

AI Identity Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring identity activity.
- Detecting unusual access patterns.
- Preparing access review reports.
- Tracking privileged accounts.
- Supporting provisioning workflows.
- Identifying orphaned accounts.

Human stewards remain responsible for identity verification, access approvals, privileged access decisions, and governance actions.

---

Prerequisites

Before granting access:

- Identity is verified.
- Operational need is documented.
- Appropriate role is identified.
- Required approvals are obtained.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Identity Provisioning

Create identities only after:

- Identity verification.
- Approval by authorized steward.
- Assignment of appropriate role.
- Recording required metadata.

---

Step 2 — Authentication

Configure authentication according to approved security standards, including:

- Secure credential creation.
- Multi-factor authentication where appropriate.
- Password management.
- Authentication logging.

---

Step 3 — Authorization

Grant permissions based on:

- Stewardship responsibilities.
- Operational need.
- Least privilege.
- Separation of duties where appropriate.

---

Step 4 — Privileged Access

Privileged accounts shall:

- Require enhanced approval.
- Be monitored.
- Be periodically reviewed.
- Be promptly revoked when no longer required.

---

Step 5 — Periodic Access Review

Review identities to confirm:

- Continued operational need.
- Appropriate permissions.
- Role alignment.
- Privileged access justification.
- Account activity.

Remove unnecessary access promptly.

---

Step 6 — Identity Monitoring

Monitor:

- Authentication activity.
- Failed login attempts.
- Privileged account use.
- Unusual access patterns.
- Dormant accounts.

Investigate significant anomalies.

---

Step 7 — Deprovisioning

When access is no longer required:

- Disable accounts promptly.
- Remove permissions.
- Archive identity records where appropriate.
- Document deprovisioning activities.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Provisioning timeliness.
- Access review completion.
- Least privilege compliance.
- Privileged account oversight.
- Authentication success.
- Unauthorized access attempts.
- Identity lifecycle accuracy.
- Governance effectiveness.

---

Exceptions

Emergency access may be granted through approved emergency access procedures while maintaining complete documentation and subsequent governance review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Identity records.
- Access requests.
- Approval records.
- Access review reports.
- Privileged account inventories.
- Authentication logs.
- Deprovisioning records.

---

Continuous Improvement

Identity management shall improve through security reviews, access assessments, steward feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance evaluations, and lessons learned.
