OAS-SEC-003 — Identity and Access Management Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-SEC-003
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-SEC-002 — Security Governance and Risk Management Framework
Owner: Chief Security Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the canonical model for identity lifecycle management, authentication, authorization, access governance, credential protection, privileged access management, and access review throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure only authorized individuals and systems receive appropriate access to institutional resources while protecting members, preserving constitutional alignment, and reducing security risk.

---

Mission

Identity and access management exists to provide secure, accountable, and appropriately limited access that enables Aureus' mission while protecting institutional resources and member information.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Digital identities
- Authentication
- Authorization
- Role-based access control
- Privileged access management
- Service accounts
- Credential management
- Identity lifecycle
- Access reviews
- Access monitoring

---

Guiding Principles

Identity and access management shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Apply least privilege.
- Verify identity before granting access.
- Promote accountability.
- Strengthen operational resilience.
- Protect sensitive information.

Access is a stewardship responsibility, not a permanent entitlement.

---

Identity Lifecycle

Every identity shall follow an approved lifecycle including:

- Creation.
- Verification.
- Provisioning.
- Modification.
- Periodic review.
- Suspension where appropriate.
- Deactivation.
- Secure archival of identity records.

---

Authentication

Authentication mechanisms shall:

- Verify identity.
- Protect credentials.
- Support multi-factor authentication where appropriate.
- Detect unauthorized access attempts.
- Balance security with usability.

---

Authorization

Access shall be granted according to:

- Stewardship responsibilities.
- Operational need.
- Delegated authority.
- Least privilege.
- Separation of duties where appropriate.

All access shall be documented and traceable.

---

Privileged Access

Privileged access shall:

- Be limited to authorized stewards.
- Require enhanced protections.
- Be reviewed regularly.
- Be monitored.
- Be revoked promptly when no longer required.

---

Access Reviews

Periodic access reviews shall verify:

- Continued operational need.
- Appropriate permissions.
- Role alignment.
- Separation of duties.
- Privileged access justification.

Unnecessary access shall be removed promptly.

---

AI-Assisted Identity Operations

AI may assist through:

- Access review preparation.
- Identity monitoring.
- Anomaly detection.
- Role analysis.
- Provisioning support.
- Reporting.

Human stewards remain responsible for access approvals, privileged access decisions, identity verification, and governance actions.

---

Performance Measurement

Identity governance shall evaluate:

- Authentication success.
- Access review completion.
- Least privilege compliance.
- Privileged access oversight.
- Unauthorized access attempts.
- Identity lifecycle accuracy.
- Governance effectiveness.

Measurements shall strengthen institutional stewardship.

---

Continuous Improvement

Identity governance shall improve through:

- Security reviews.
- Access assessments.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance evaluations.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens institutional resilience.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all identity and access management activities throughout Aureus.

All Security SOPs, authentication standards, authorization procedures, credential management policies, privileged access standards, and identity governance documents shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Security Charter.
