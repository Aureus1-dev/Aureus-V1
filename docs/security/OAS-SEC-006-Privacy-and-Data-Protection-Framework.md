OAS-SEC-006 — Privacy and Data Protection Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-SEC-006
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-SEC-002 — Security Governance and Risk Management Framework
Owner: Chief Security Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the canonical model for protecting personal information, confidential institutional information, and other sensitive data throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure information is collected, processed, stored, shared, retained, and disposed of responsibly while preserving privacy, maintaining constitutional alignment, protecting members, and supporting lawful institutional operations.

---

Mission

Privacy stewardship exists to respect the dignity of individuals, protect sensitive information, strengthen institutional trust, and enable responsible information use in support of Aureus' mission.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Personal information
- Sensitive information
- Confidential institutional information
- Data classification
- Data collection
- Data processing
- Data sharing
- Data retention
- Data disposal
- Privacy governance

---

Guiding Principles

Privacy and data protection shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Respect individual privacy.
- Collect only appropriate information.
- Protect confidentiality.
- Promote transparency.
- Strengthen institutional trust.

Information stewardship shall always be proportional to legitimate operational need.

---

Data Classification

Information shall be classified according to its:

- Sensitivity.
- Confidentiality.
- Operational importance.
- Legal requirements.
- Security requirements.

Classification shall determine appropriate safeguards.

---

Data Lifecycle

Every category of protected information shall follow an approved lifecycle including:

- Collection.
- Verification.
- Storage.
- Authorized use.
- Controlled sharing.
- Retention.
- Secure archival where appropriate.
- Secure disposal.

---

Privacy Controls

Privacy safeguards shall include:

- Access controls.
- Encryption where appropriate.
- Secure storage.
- Data minimization.
- Purpose limitation.
- Retention controls.
- Secure destruction.

Controls shall remain proportional to identified risk.

---

Information Sharing

Protected information shall be shared only:

- For authorized purposes.
- With appropriate approval.
- Using secure methods.
- According to applicable law.
- Consistent with institutional governance.

Unauthorized disclosure shall be prohibited.

---

AI-Assisted Privacy Operations

AI may assist through:

- Data classification.
- Retention monitoring.
- Privacy impact assessments.
- Access analysis.
- Compliance reporting.
- Documentation support.

Human stewards remain responsible for privacy decisions, disclosure approvals, legal interpretation, and governance actions.

---

Performance Measurement

Privacy governance shall evaluate:

- Privacy compliance.
- Access control effectiveness.
- Data classification accuracy.
- Information protection.
- Retention compliance.
- Incident frequency.
- Member trust indicators.

Measurements shall strengthen institutional stewardship.

---

Continuous Improvement

Privacy governance shall improve through:

- Privacy reviews.
- Security assessments.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance evaluations.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens institutional resilience.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all privacy and data protection activities throughout Aureus.

All Security SOPs, privacy procedures, data governance standards, information handling policies, retention procedures, access management standards, and confidentiality requirements shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Security Charter.
