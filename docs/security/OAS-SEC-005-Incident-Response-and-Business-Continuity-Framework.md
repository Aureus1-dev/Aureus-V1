OAS-SEC-005 — Incident Response and Business Continuity Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-SEC-005
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-SEC-002 — Security Governance and Risk Management Framework
Owner: Chief Security Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the canonical model for preparing for, responding to, recovering from, and learning from security incidents, operational disruptions, and disasters affecting Aureus.

Its purpose is to preserve operational continuity, protect members and institutional assets, minimize disruption, strengthen resilience, and ensure the long-term continuity of Aureus' mission.

---

Mission

Incident response and business continuity exist to enable Aureus to withstand, respond to, recover from, and adapt after disruptive events while maintaining stewardship, constitutional integrity, and member trust.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Security incident response
- Cybersecurity incidents
- Physical security incidents
- Business continuity
- Disaster recovery
- Crisis management
- Evidence preservation
- Operational resilience
- Recovery planning
- Post-incident improvement

---

Guiding Principles

Incident management shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Minimize operational disruption.
- Preserve evidence.
- Promote coordinated response.
- Strengthen resilience.
- Learn continuously.

Preparedness is an ongoing stewardship responsibility.

---

Incident Lifecycle

Every significant incident shall follow an approved lifecycle including:

- Detection.
- Reporting.
- Assessment.
- Containment.
- Investigation.
- Recovery.
- Post-incident review.
- Improvement implementation.

---

Business Continuity

Business continuity planning shall:

- Identify critical functions.
- Establish recovery priorities.
- Define continuity procedures.
- Assign responsibilities.
- Maintain communication plans.
- Be tested periodically.

Continuity plans shall remain practical and current.

---

Disaster Recovery

Recovery planning shall address:

- Technology restoration.
- Data recovery.
- Operational recovery.
- Facility recovery where applicable.
- Service restoration.
- Validation of recovery activities.

Recovery priorities shall support mission-critical operations.

---

Evidence Preservation

During significant incidents:

- Relevant evidence shall be identified.
- Evidence shall be preserved.
- Chain of custody shall be documented where appropriate.
- Unauthorized alteration shall be prevented.

Evidence preservation supports accountability and learning.

---

AI-Assisted Incident Management

AI may assist through:

- Incident detection.
- Alert prioritization.
- Log analysis.
- Timeline generation.
- Recovery tracking.
- Post-incident reporting.

Human stewards remain responsible for incident command, recovery decisions, communications, and governance actions.

---

Performance Measurement

Operational resilience shall evaluate:

- Detection speed.
- Response time.
- Recovery time.
- Service availability.
- Incident recurrence.
- Continuity readiness.
- Recovery effectiveness.
- Governance performance.

Measurements shall strengthen institutional resilience.

---

Continuous Improvement

Incident response and continuity planning shall improve through:

- Incident reviews.
- Recovery exercises.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance assessments.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens institutional preparedness.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all incident response, disaster recovery, business continuity, and operational resilience activities throughout Aureus.

All Security SOPs, incident response procedures, disaster recovery plans, continuity procedures, crisis communication plans, evidence preservation standards, and resilience programs shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Security Charter.
