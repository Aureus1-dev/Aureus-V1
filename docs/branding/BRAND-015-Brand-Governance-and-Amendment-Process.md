# BRAND-015 — Brand Governance & Amendment Process

Status: Draft for Founder Review

---

# Purpose

This document establishes the governance framework for the Aureus Brand Canon.

It defines how branding decisions are created, reviewed, approved, amended, and preserved over time.

The objective is to ensure that the Aureus brand remains consistent, trustworthy, and aligned with the Constitution regardless of changes in leadership, technology, or design trends.

---

# Relationship to the Constitution

The Brand Canon is subordinate to the Aureus Constitution.

No branding document may contradict:

- The Founding Charter
- The Constitutional Principles
- The Mission
- The Vision
- The Purpose
- The Identity
- Any future constitutional amendments

Where a conflict exists, the Constitution prevails.

---

# Brand Governance Principles

The Aureus brand shall be governed according to the principles of:

- Stewardship
- Trust
- Clarity
- Accessibility
- Consistency
- Transparency
- Long-term thinking
- Institutional integrity

---

# Canonical Lifecycle

Every branding document shall progress through the following stages:

1. Draft
2. Founder Review
3. Approved
4. Canonical

Only Canonical documents become the official source of truth.

---

# Brand Amendment Proposals (BAPs)

Changes to Canonical branding documents shall be submitted through a Brand Amendment Proposal (BAP).

Every BAP should include:

- The affected document(s)
- The proposed change
- The reason for the change
- Expected benefits
- Potential risks
- Compatibility with the Constitution
- Compatibility with the existing Brand Canon

---

# Review Standards

Every proposed amendment should be evaluated by asking:

- Does it strengthen trust?
- Does it improve clarity?
- Does it improve accessibility?
- Does it better express the Aureus mission?
- Does it remain timeless?
- Does it improve consistency?
- Does it preserve institutional integrity?

If the answer is "no" to any of these questions, the proposal should be revised or rejected.

---

# Version Control

Every Canonical branding document should include:

- Document number
- Version
- Status
- Approval date
- Amendment history

Historical versions should be preserved for institutional learning.

---

# Conflict Resolution

If two branding documents appear to conflict:

1. The Constitution takes precedence.
2. The higher-numbered governing branding document does not automatically override an earlier one.
3. Resolve the conflict through a documented BAP.
4. Update affected documents together to restore consistency.

---

# Stewardship Responsibility

Every steward responsible for the Aureus brand shall:

- Protect consistency.
- Preserve trust.
- Maintain accessibility.
- Document significant decisions.
- Respect the Brand Canon.

Brand stewardship is an institutional responsibility rather than a personal preference.

---

# Future Expansion

Future versions of this document may define:

- Brand Review Board
- Design approval workflow
- AI-assisted brand governance
- External brand licensing
- Third-party usage guidelines
- Audit procedures
- Brand compliance process

---

# Canonical Principle

The Aureus brand is a long-term institutional asset.

Every change should strengthen the mission, preserve trust, and leave the institution stronger than before.
