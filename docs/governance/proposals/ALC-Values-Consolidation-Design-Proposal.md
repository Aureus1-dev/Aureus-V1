**Authorized by:** Founder Decision — CAR-001 Accepted, Decision 3, 2026-07-21 ("I authorize a design proposal, not an implementation... Do not begin collapsing those documents yet.")
**Status:** Proposal only. No file has been moved, edited, merged, or archived. Awaiting Founder review before any consolidation occurs.
**Scope:** `ALC-003` through `ALC-013` (9 documents, ~380 Articles). Does not touch `ALC-001`, `ALC-002`, or anything in the OAS lineage.

---

## 1. Method

All nine documents were read in full during the Constitutional Architecture Report pass. This proposal builds a theme-by-theme duplication matrix from that reading, distinguishes genuinely unique content from repeated content, and proposes a structural (not yet drafted) consolidated architecture.

## 3. What is genuinely unique per document

Despite the overwhelming duplication, each document does contribute something the others don't fully replicate:

- **ALC-003 ("Nature of Stewardship")** — the only document treating **applied domains** of stewardship directly: Family (Art. IV), Time (Art. VII), Creation/environment (Art. XXXVII), Work (Art. XXXVIII), Prosperity (Art. XXXIX). These are concrete life-domains, not abstract virtues, and have no real counterpart elsewhere in the family.
- **ALC-004 ("Nature of Human Flourishing")** — the only document naming **Beauty** (Art. XXVI) and **Creativity** (Art. XXVII) as their own themes.
- **ALC-005 ("Institutional Mission")** — the only document framed around **practical mission-guardrails**: Growth vs. Mission (Art. V), Profit vs. Mission (Art. VI) — genuinely operational-sounding commitments distinct from the other documents' pure character language.
- **ALC-006 ("Constitutional Identity")** — stylistically distinct: instead of "Article on Theme X," it's a list of **relational self-descriptions** ("Aureus Is a Steward," "...a Builder of Trust," "...a Guide," "...a Teacher," "...a Convener," "...a Faithful Listener," "...a Faithful Neighbor"). This rhetorical device — defining identity through role/metaphor rather than flat virtue-listing — is not duplicated in structure anywhere else, even though the underlying values are the same ones repeated throughout.
- **ALC-009 ("Constitutional Virtues")** — the most genuinely distinct of the nine. It introduces several **personal-character virtues with no dedicated Article anywhere else in the family**: Prudence (Art. XIII), Patience (Art. XIV), Self-Control (Art. XVII), Perseverance (Art. XVIII), Diligence (Art. XXVII), Discernment (Art. XXVIII), Teachability (Art. XXIII), Joy (Art. XXX). This document is oriented toward the *personal* character formation of an individual steward, not the *institutional* values of Aureus — a real, defensible distinction, even though it shares the "Wisdom/Integrity/Humility/Courage/Compassion/Justice/Service..." core with its siblings.
- **ALC-011, ALC-012, ALC-013 ("Commitments," "Responsibilities," "Obligations")** — the least distinct of the nine. Their own Purpose sections attempt to draw a line ("Values define what is important. Virtues define who we become. Culture defines how we live together. Commitments define what we promise. Responsibilities define what we must faithfully do." — ALC-012's own words), but the actual Article content for each theme is close to interchangeable across all three (compare, e.g., ALC-011 Art. VII "Commitment to Justice," ALC-012 Art. VII "Responsibility to Administer Justice," ALC-013 Art. VII "Obligation to Administer Justice" — same content, different modal verb).

## 4. What would be preserved

Nothing described above would be lost under the proposed architecture — every genuinely distinct contribution identified in §3 has an explicit home in the consolidated structure proposed in §5. The ~30 repeated themes would each survive in the consolidated document, stated once, at whatever combination of the nine documents' best phrasing represents the fullest treatment (a drafting judgment for the actual consolidation pass, not this proposal). Nothing is deleted at any point in this proposal — see §6.

## 5. Proposed consolidated architecture (structural outline only — not drafted)

A single new document, tentatively **"Aureus Constitutional Values Canon"**, organized the way `OAS-004` already organizes its 16 principles into thematic Parts (a proven, working pattern already in this repository):

| Proposed Part | Themes consolidated into it (each stated once) | Primary source material |
|---|---|---|
| **Part One — The Person** | Human Dignity, Human Agency/Freedom, Responsibility, Integrity | ALC-004 Art. II, 007 Art. I/IX/XI/XXIII, 008 Art. I/IX/XI/XXIII |
| **Part Two — Truth and Wisdom** | Truth, Wisdom, Transparency, Accountability | ALC-007/008 Arts. III/IV/XXI/XXII (best-phrased pass of each) |
| **Part Three — Relationship and Community** | Trust, Love, Compassion, Hospitality, Community/Belonging, Unity, Peace, Justice, Restoration | ALC-007/008/010's treatments, cross-checked against ALC-003/004 for phrasing |
| **Part Four — Service and Stewardship** | Stewardship (general), Service, Excellence, Long-Term/Generational Stewardship, Responsible Innovation | ALC-003 (as the deepest treatment of Stewardship generally), 005, 007 |
| **Part Five — Applied Domains** | Family, Time, Work, Prosperity, Creation/environment, Beauty, Creativity — the themes unique to ALC-003/004 | ALC-003 Arts. IV/VII/XXXVII–XXXIX, ALC-004 Arts. XXVI–XXVII |
| **Part Six — Personal Character (Virtues)** | Prudence, Patience, Self-Control, Perseverance, Diligence, Discernment, Teachability, Joy, Humility, Courage, Faithfulness | ALC-009 in full — this Part is closest to a straight carry-over, since it's the family's most distinct document |
| **Part Seven — Institutional Identity** | The relational self-description device ("Aureus Is a Steward/Guide/Teacher/Neighbor...") | ALC-006 in full — preserved as its own Part precisely because its rhetorical form isn't duplicative of the flat-list Parts above |
| **Closing — Constitutional Test** | One consolidated self-assessment device, replacing the six near-identical versions currently scattered across ALC-006/007/008/010/011/012 | Best-phrased version of the recurring "Does this strengthen human dignity? / increase trust? / help people flourish?" question set |

This is a **structural proposal**, not a draft — actually writing Part-by-Part prose, and choosing which of several near-identical treatments of each theme to keep, is consolidation work, explicitly not authorized yet.

## 6. How Git history and lineage would be preserved

Consistent with `OAS-010, Article 13` (Archival and Permanent Preservation — *"No Archived text may be deleted... preserved exactly as it read at the time it ceased to be operative"*) and `AMI-001`'s Supersession Rules (*"the replacement shall be identified; the reason shall be recorded; historical references shall be preserved"*):

1. **`git mv`, not delete.** Each of `ALC-003` through `ALC-013` would be moved (not deleted) to an archive path, e.g. `docs/governance/archive/alc-values-family-pre-consolidation/`, preserving 100% of Git blame/history for every line, exactly as was done for the AICP-001 identity fix earlier in this engagement.
2. **A repair/lineage record**, analogous to `docs/governance/repairs/MOVE-001-AICP-001-identity-correction.md`, documenting: what was superseded, by what, why, and a byte-for-byte manifest (md5 hashes) of the archived originals — so the consolidation is independently auditable after the fact.
3. **The new consolidated document explicitly states its lineage** in its own header (the same "Relationship to Canon" convention every ALC/OAS document already uses): *"Supersedes ALC-003 through ALC-013 in full. Original documents archived, with full Git history, at [path]. See [lineage record] for the complete duplication analysis and consolidation rationale."*
4. **No renumbering of ALC-001/ALC-002.** This proposal does not touch either document; only ALC-003–013's numbering range would need a decision — either retire the numbers entirely (leaving a permanent, documented gap) or reassign the consolidated document a new number in that range (e.g., a single "ALC-003" carrying the consolidated content) — a choice for Founder direction at consolidation time, not this proposal.

## 7. What this proposal does not do

It does not move, edit, merge, or archive any file. It does not decide which of several overlapping treatments of a theme is "best" — that is consolidation-time drafting judgment. It does not resolve MDR-001 (Steward definition) or CAP-001's broader architecture questions, which are handled separately. It is submitted for Founder review per Decision 3, and awaits approval before any of §5–6 is executed.
