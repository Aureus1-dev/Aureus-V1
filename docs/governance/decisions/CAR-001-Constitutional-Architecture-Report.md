## 2. Which documents overlap

**Severe, confirmed overlap: ALC-003 through ALC-013 overlap with each other, not primarily with the OAS lineage.** This is the most consequential finding of this pass. Concretely:

- "Human Dignity" is its own Article in ALC-004 (Art. II), ALC-006 (implicit throughout), ALC-007 (Art. I), ALC-008 (Art. I) — four near-identical treatments.
- "Stewardship" is the entire subject of ALC-003, and also its own Article in ALC-004, 005, 006, 007, 008, 009, 010, 011, 012, 013 — the same concept given a dedicated Article in *at least ten* separate documents.
- "Truth/Truthfulness" appears as its own themed Article in ALC-003, 004, 005 (implicit), 006, 007, 008, 009, 010, 012, 013 — again, roughly ten times.
- The same pattern holds for Justice, Compassion, Hope, Trust, Hospitality, Wisdom, Accountability, Transparency, Human Agency, Restoration, Unity, Long-Term Stewardship, Responsible Innovation, and Human Flourishing itself — each recurs, as its own dedicated Article, across most or all of the nine documents.
- Confirmed via direct reading, not inference: ALC-007 Article XXXI, ALC-008 Article XXXI, and ALC-004's closing all state, nearly verbatim, "Human flourishing is the highest constitutional objective/value/expression of [this document's subject]" — the same sentence, restated as the capstone of three different documents.

**Secondary overlap, smaller in scope:** `OAS-002`/`OAS-003` (Preamble, Identity/Mission/Vision/Purpose) cover similar *ground* to `ALC-002` (Preamble) and `ALC-005`/`ALC-006` (Institutional Mission, Constitutional Identity) — but not the same way: the OAS pair is short, tightly disciplined, and explicitly creates no new law; the ALC set is far longer and, like the rest of the ALC family, restates the same virtue-list under a "Mission" or "Identity" framing.

**No overlap found between the OAS operative documents (OAS-005–011) and anything in the ALC family.** Nothing in ALC-001 through ALC-013 defines rights, duties, membership tiers, a three-branch government, an amendment lifecycle, or a justice system with the specificity OAS-006 through OAS-011 do. This confirms and sharpens the prior addendum's finding.

## 3. Which documents complement one another

- **OAS-001 (Charter) ↔ OAS-004–011 (operative series):** genuine complementary layering — each operative document explicitly extends OAS-001 without restating it, and each cites the ones before it correctly.
- **ALC-001 ↔ its own Appendix A and ceremonial content:** internally complementary (modulo the already-known defects — duplicated Appendix A, missing Article XI).
- **OAS-002/003 ↔ ALC-005/006:** partially complementary — the OAS pair could plausibly serve as the *disciplined, legally-clean* Identity/Mission/Vision layer, while the ALC pair (and the rest of the ALC family) could serve as the *expressive, values-forming* layer that gives the same commitments emotional and cultural weight — **if** the ALC family's internal eight-fold redundancy were resolved first.
- **The whole ALC-003–013 family, taken together, complements OAS-004's 16 Foundational Principles** in the sense that it's the same subject matter (principles/values of the institution) given book-length devotional treatment instead of OAS-004's single, disciplined, cross-referenced document. Whether that's a feature (a companion volume of institutional scripture) or a defect (redundant sprawl) is exactly the architectural question this report exists to help resolve — see §6.

## 5. Is Aureus one constitution, or a constitutional family?

**A constitutional family, whether or not that was the original intent.** The evidence: two entirely separate drafting efforts (OAS-numbered, ALC-numbered) exist in the same directory, both claiming supremacy, with no document anywhere reconciling them, and with fundamentally different registers — one a legal instrument, the other a devotional/cultural one. Treating this as "one constitution" today would require pretending one of the two lineages doesn't exist, which the evidence doesn't support.

## 6. What is the cleanest long-term constitutional architecture

Evaluating the four options against the evidence above, not against age or completeness alone:

**Option A (one supreme constitution, subordinate acts) — not supported as a simple pick between ALC-001 and OAS-001.** Neither document alone is a complete constitution: OAS-001 lacks any devotional/cultural dimension; ALC-001 lacks operative rights/duties/institutions/justice machinery at anything like OAS-004–011's depth. Declaring either one supreme "as-is" and subordinating the other would either (a) subordinate a fully-built operative legal system under a document with no equivalent legal depth, or (b) subordinate an extensive values/identity treatise under a document with no equivalent expressive depth. Neither serves repository integrity.

**Option B (a constitutional family with distinct constitutional roles) — the best-supported option, with one hard precondition.** The evidence points toward a natural division of labor that already exists in substance, just not in name:
- A **Founding Charter + Operative Constitution** role: `OAS-001` (ratified) + `OAS-004` through `OAS-011` — this is already a coherent, internally disciplined, non-redundant legal-institutional system requiring no restructuring, only ratification.
- An **Identity/Mission/Vision** role: `OAS-002`/`OAS-003` as the disciplined statement, or `ALC-005`/`ALC-006` as the expressive statement, or (most likely, per the evidence) a reconciliation of the two — not both in full, since they overlap.
- A **Values/Character** role: **one** consolidated document distilling ALC-003 through ALC-013's actual, non-redundant content (the ~30 recurring themes, stated once each, well) — not nine documents restating the same 30 themes eight times over. This is the precondition: Option B only works if this eight-fold redundancy is resolved into something closer to one document (perhaps organized the way `OAS-004` already organizes its 16 principles into four thematic Parts), not preserved as nine.

**Option C (a merged constitution via full reconciliation) — technically cleanest, highest cost.** Would fold OAS-001–011 and a consolidated ALC-values-document into a single ratified instrument. This is architecturally the most elegant end state, but understates how much of the OAS lineage's value is precisely that it is *already* a complete, working, non-redundant system — merging it wholesale risks re-introducing exactly the kind of drift and duplication this audit exists to catch, unless done with real discipline.

**Option D — not indicated by the evidence.** Nothing in the 24 documents read suggests an architecture outside the three named above; no third lineage, no alternative organizing principle, and no repository evidence for a different structure was found.

### Recommendation, stated as evidence supports it

**Option B, conditioned on resolving the ALC-003–013 redundancy first.** The OAS lineage does not need architectural rescue — it needs ratification. The ALC lineage's genuine, distinctive contribution (ceremonial, covenantal, identity-forming language with no OAS equivalent) is currently diluted across nine largely-redundant documents; consolidating it into one disciplined values document would let it serve its complementary role without either overwhelming the operative constitution in volume or duplicating it in the collision sense already documented elsewhere in this repository (the OAS-OPS/OAS-TECH/OAS-LEG/OAS-FIN pattern). This is not a recommendation to discard ALC-001's Stewardship Covenant, the ceremonial content, or any of the 30 genuine themes — it is a recommendation to state each of them once, well, instead of eight times with cosmetic variation.

This does not resolve MDR-001 (Steward definition) or the specific mechanics of ratifying OAS-001 (its 9 open Founder questions) — those remain open, separate decisions this report does not attempt to make for you.

All of that awaits your direction on which option — A, B, C, or a variant — to pursue.
