# FDB-001 Addendum — Content-Coverage Comparison: OAS-001 Lineage vs. ALC-001

**Requested by:** Founder Decision 1 (2026-07-21) — "perform a content-coverage comparison between OAS-001 and ALC-001 to ensure nothing unique would be lost."
**Status:** Analysis only. No repository file modified.
**Date:** 2026-07-21

---

## Bottom line, stated plainly

**ALC-001 does not subsume OAS-001's governing substance.** On nearly every operative dimension this audit checked, the opposite is closer to true: the OAS-001 lineage (OAS-001 plus OAS-002 through OAS-011, ten documents) is substantially more developed, more specific, and more operative than ALC-001's corresponding Articles. This means the precondition in Founder Decision 1 — "if ALC-001 fully subsumes OAS-001, then ALC-001 can become supreme" — does not hold as stated, and I'm presenting this rather than proceeding to archive OAS-001, per your own instruction.

## Method

I read OAS-001 (the draft Founding Charter itself) and OAS-004 through OAS-011 in full this session (OAS-002 and OAS-003 not yet read in full — see "Not yet verified" below). I compared each against the corresponding Article(s) of ALC-001, which I already had comprehensive knowledge of from this session's earlier audit and repair work. Where a direct, same-subject comparison was possible, I read both sides in full rather than relying on titles or summaries.

## The clearest single data point: Justice and Due Process

This is the cleanest apples-to-apples test, because both documents address the identical subject:

- **ALC-001, Article X ("Justice, Review, and Due Process")** — 13 Sections, ~150 lines. Every provision is aspirational/principle-level: *"Every constitutional review shall **strive to be** impartial, respectful, transparent **where reasonably possible**..."* (Section 4); *"Aureus shall **seek** restoration before punishment"* (Section 6); *"Every constitutional process... shall **strive to provide** notice... an opportunity to respond..."* (Section 11). There is no defined adjudicative body, no jurisdiction rule, no independence/recusal rule, no evidentiary standard, no remedy categories, no appeal mechanism, no finality rule.
- **OAS-011 ("Constitutional Justice, Review, Dispute Resolution, and Due Process")** — 18 Articles across 7 Parts. It names the Tribunal (established in OAS-009) and gives it actual jurisdiction (Article 3), extends that jurisdiction to disputes between private persons for the first time (Article 5), establishes recusal rules (Article 8), evidentiary standards (Article 10), a defined remedy taxonomy with a proportionality rule (Articles 12–13), an internal appeal mechanism (Article 14), and finality/reopening rules (Article 15).

ALC-001 states values. OAS-011 builds a working justice system. This is not a close call.

## The pattern repeats across every major operative area

| Subject | ALC-001 | OAS-001 lineage |

| Governance / separation of powers | Article VI, "Governance and Constitutional Stewardship" — First Steward office, Stewardship Council, ~10 Sections | OAS-009 — 18 Articles across 6 Parts: three coequal bodies (Council/Administration/Tribunal), non-delegable authorities, jurisdictional-dispute rules, emergency continuity, and an explicit mechanism for transitioning the Founder's authority to durable institutions |
| Amendment process | Article VII, "Constitutional Decision-Making, Participation, and Amendment" — ~10 Sections | OAS-010 — 18 Articles across 5 Parts: a full Constitutional Amendment Proposal (CAP) lifecycle (Draft → Under Review → Approved → Canonized/Rejected/Withdrawn/Archived), entrenchment-conflict review by the Tribunal, non-retroactivity rules, and permanent version preservation |

In every row, ALC-001's treatment is a single Article of principle-level commitments; the OAS-001 lineage's treatment is a dedicated, multi-Part constitutional document with defined institutions, procedures, and cross-references back to a coherent 26-term Definitions article (OAS-005) and a 16-principle foundational-principles document (OAS-004).

## Where ALC-001 is genuinely stronger, or unique

This comparison is not one-sided, and the Founder's instruction was specifically to check for what would be *lost*, not merely to declare a winner:

- **Ceremonial and covenant framing.** ALC-001 has extensive ceremonial content (the Founding Declaration, Constitutional Ratification, Founder's Declaration, Institutional Blessing, Eternal Mission, Constitutional Seal, Constitutional Motto, Final Words) with no counterpart anywhere in the OAS lineage, which is written in a purely legal-instrument register throughout. This is a real, distinct strength — it is the document that would actually read as inspiring to a Member, not just legally sound.
- **The Stewardship Covenant concept itself** (Article V) — a voluntary, opt-in identity every Member can adopt, with an "Apprentice Steward" progression — has no equivalent in the OAS lineage's membership system, which is a tiered but purely functional/legal status system (Guest/Participant/Contributor/Member) without this relational, aspirational framing.
- **"Steward" as delegated authority** — importantly, **OAS-005, Article I, Section 22 defines "Steward" the same narrow way ALC-001's Definitions §5 does** ("any officer, body, or person who holds authority under this Constitution... not itself a formal office"), and the OAS lineage never uses the word "Steward" in ALC-001 Article V's broader, opt-in-membership sense at all. This is a new, relevant data point for Founder Decision 3 (already approved in principle): if the OAS lineage's "Steward" definition is ever brought into the same document as ALC-001's, Article V's now-canonical broad sense would create a *new* three-way collision with OAS-005's narrow sense — worth flagging even though it doesn't change what you already approved for ALC-001 taken alone.
