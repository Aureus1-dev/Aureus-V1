OAS-RISK-003 — Risk Assessment and Mitigation Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-RISK-003
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-RISK-002 — Enterprise Risk Governance and Resilience Framework
Owner: Chief Risk Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the canonical model for identifying, analyzing, assessing, prioritizing, mitigating, monitoring, communicating, and continuously improving enterprise risk management throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure institutional risks are evaluated consistently, addressed responsibly, and managed in a manner that strengthens mission execution, constitutional fidelity, and long-term organizational resilience.

---

Mission

Risk assessment exists to transform uncertainty into informed stewardship by enabling prudent decision-making, reducing preventable harm, and strengthening Aureus' ability to pursue meaningful opportunities responsibly.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Risk identification
- Risk assessment
- Risk analysis
- Risk prioritization
- Risk mitigation
- Risk monitoring
- Risk communication
- Risk documentation
- Risk reporting
- Continuous improvement

---

Guiding Principles

Risk assessment shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote informed decision-making.
- Strengthen resilience.
- Encourage responsible innovation.
- Foster accountability.
- Support continuous learning.

Risk management shall reduce unnecessary uncertainty without discouraging responsible action.

---

Risk Identification

Risk identification shall include:

- Strategic risks.
- Operational risks.
- Financial risks.
- Technology risks.
- Legal risks.
- Reputational risks.
- Governance risks.
- Emerging risks.

Risk identification shall remain continuous.

---

Risk Assessment

Each identified risk shall be evaluated according to:

- Likelihood.
- Potential impact.
- Urgency.
- Scope.
- Existing controls.
- Organizational exposure.

Assessments shall remain evidence-based and consistently documented.

---

Risk Prioritization

Risks shall be prioritized according to:

- Mission impact.
- Member impact.
- Institutional resilience.
- Legal obligations.
- Financial exposure.
- Recovery difficulty.

Priority shall guide mitigation planning and resource allocation.

---

Risk Mitigation

Risk responses may include:

- Avoidance.
- Reduction.
- Transfer.
- Acceptance.
- Contingency planning.
- Continuous monitoring.

Selected responses shall remain proportional to the assessed risk.

---

AI-Assisted Risk Assessment

AI may assist through:

- Trend analysis.
- Risk detection.
- Scenario modeling.
- Early warning indicators.
- Risk summaries.
- Administrative support.

Human stewards remain responsible for risk acceptance, mitigation approvals, governance decisions, and institutional accountability.

---

Risk Communication

Material risks shall be communicated through:

- Risk reports.
- Governance briefings.
- Operational updates.
- Escalation procedures.
- Periodic reviews.

Communication shall remain timely, accurate, and appropriately documented.

---

Performance Measurement

Risk assessment shall evaluate:

- Risk identification effectiveness.
- Assessment accuracy.
- Mitigation completion.
- Risk reduction.
- Organizational resilience.
- Governance maturity.
- Steward accountability.
- Mission support.

Measurements shall strengthen institutional stewardship.

---

Continuous Improvement

Risk assessment shall improve through:

- Risk reviews.
- Incident analysis.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance evaluations.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens Aureus' long-term resilience.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all enterprise risk assessment and mitigation activities throughout Aureus.

All Risk SOPs, risk registers, mitigation plans, assessment methodologies, resilience documentation, governance reviews, and enterprise risk management practices shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Enterprise Risk Charter.
