OAS-RISK-004 — Crisis Management and Business Continuity Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-RISK-004
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-RISK-002 — Enterprise Risk Governance and Resilience Framework
Owner: Chief Risk Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the constitutional model for crisis governance, incident response, business continuity, emergency coordination, organizational recovery, resilience planning, post-incident learning, and long-term institutional continuity throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure Aureus remains capable of protecting members, preserving critical operations, maintaining constitutional governance, and faithfully advancing its mission during and after significant disruptions.

---

Mission

Crisis governance exists to prepare Aureus to anticipate disruption, coordinate effective responses, recover essential operations, strengthen institutional resilience, and preserve trust through disciplined stewardship.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Crisis governance
- Incident response
- Business continuity
- Emergency coordination
- Operational recovery
- Organizational resilience
- Recovery planning
- Lessons learned
- Continuity governance
- Institutional sustainability

---

Guiding Principles

Crisis governance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote preparedness.
- Strengthen resilience.
- Encourage coordinated action.
- Foster accountability.
- Support continuous learning.

Preparedness shall remain a permanent institutional responsibility.

---

Crisis Governance

Crisis governance shall establish:

- Clearly defined responsibilities.
- Crisis leadership.
- Escalation procedures.
- Decision authority.
- Communication protocols.
- Governance oversight.

Governance shall remain effective under both routine and extraordinary conditions.

---

Business Continuity

Business continuity planning shall include:

- Identification of critical services.
- Recovery priorities.
- Continuity strategies.
- Resource planning.
- Communication procedures.
- Periodic testing.

Essential institutional functions shall remain protected.

---

Incident Response

Incident response shall include:

- Detection.
- Assessment.
- Escalation.
- Coordinated response.
- Service restoration.
- Documentation.

Responses shall remain timely, disciplined, and proportionate.

---

Organizational Recovery

Recovery planning shall:

- Restore critical services.
- Protect members.
- Preserve institutional trust.
- Reduce future vulnerability.
- Document improvements.
- Strengthen resilience.

Recovery shall restore operations while improving future preparedness.

---

AI-Assisted Crisis Stewardship

AI may assist through:

- Incident monitoring.
- Early warning analysis.
- Resource coordination.
- Recovery tracking.
- Situation summaries.
- Administrative support.

Human stewards remain responsible for crisis leadership, emergency decisions, continuity priorities, governance actions, and institutional accountability.

---

Organizational Learning

Following significant incidents Aureus shall document:

- Root causes.
- Response effectiveness.
- Recovery performance.
- Lessons learned.
- Governance implications.
- Recommended improvements.

Learning shall strengthen future institutional resilience.

---

Performance Measurement

Crisis governance shall evaluate:

- Incident response effectiveness.
- Recovery time.
- Service continuity.
- Organizational resilience.
- Preparedness.
- Governance maturity.
- Institutional sustainability.
- Mission support.

Measurements shall strengthen responsible stewardship.

---

Continuous Improvement

Crisis governance shall improve through:

- Incident reviews.
- Continuity exercises.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance evaluations.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens Aureus' long-term resilience.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all crisis management and business continuity activities throughout Aureus.

All Risk SOPs, continuity plans, incident response procedures, emergency coordination plans, recovery documentation, resilience exercises, and crisis governance practices shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Enterprise Risk Charter.
