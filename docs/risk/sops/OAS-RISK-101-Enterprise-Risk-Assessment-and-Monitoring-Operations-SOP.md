OAS-RISK-101 — Enterprise Risk Assessment and Monitoring Operations Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-RISK-101
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-RISK-002 — Enterprise Risk Governance and Resilience Framework
Owner: Chief Risk Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for enterprise risk identification, assessment, monitoring, reporting, escalation, risk register management, and continuous enterprise risk governance throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure institutional risks are consistently identified, evaluated, documented, communicated, and managed in a manner that strengthens mission execution, protects members, preserves constitutional alignment, and supports long-term institutional resilience.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Enterprise risk identification
- Risk assessment
- Risk monitoring
- Risk reporting
- Risk escalation
- Risk registers
- Risk reviews
- Risk governance
- Risk documentation
- Continuous improvement

---

Objectives

Enterprise risk operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote informed decision-making.
- Strengthen institutional resilience.
- Reduce preventable harm.
- Improve governance.
- Enable continuous improvement.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Enterprise Risk Steward

Responsible for:

- Coordinating enterprise risk assessments.
- Maintaining the enterprise risk register.
- Monitoring enterprise risks.
- Preparing governance reports.
- Supporting enterprise risk reviews.

---

Risk Owners

Responsible for:

- Identifying risks.
- Implementing mitigation activities.
- Reporting changes in risk exposure.
- Updating documentation.
- Supporting governance reviews.

---

AI Enterprise Risk Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring risk indicators.
- Detecting emerging trends.
- Preparing risk summaries.
- Tracking mitigation progress.
- Supporting enterprise reporting.
- Producing risk analytics.

Human stewards remain responsible for risk acceptance, mitigation approvals, governance decisions, crisis declarations, and institutional accountability.

---

Prerequisites

Before conducting enterprise risk operations:

- Governance responsibilities are assigned.
- Risk categories are defined.
- Assessment methodology is approved.
- Risk documentation standards are available.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Risk Identification

Document:

- Risk description.
- Risk category.
- Potential causes.
- Potential consequences.
- Affected services.
- Responsible owner.

---

Step 2 — Risk Assessment

Evaluate each identified risk according to:

- Likelihood.
- Impact.
- Urgency.
- Existing controls.
- Residual risk.
- Overall priority.

Document all assessment results.

---

Step 3 — Risk Register Management

Maintain a centralized risk register including:

- Risk status.
- Assigned owner.
- Mitigation strategy.
- Review schedule.
- Escalation status.
- Historical changes.

---

Step 4 — Monitoring

Continuously monitor:

- Risk indicators.
- Mitigation progress.
- Emerging threats.
- Operational changes.
- External developments.
- Organizational impacts.

Investigate material changes promptly.

---

Step 5 — Escalation

When predefined thresholds are exceeded:

- Notify appropriate governance authorities.
- Document escalation.
- Coordinate response activities.
- Monitor mitigation efforts.
- Record outcomes.

---

Step 6 — Reporting

Prepare reports including:

- Enterprise risk summaries.
- High-priority risks.
- Mitigation status.
- Emerging trends.
- Governance recommendations.
- Institutional impacts.

---

Step 7 — Continuous Improvement

Improve enterprise risk management through:

- Risk reviews.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance evaluations.
- Incident analysis.
- Lessons learned.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Risk identification effectiveness.
- Assessment completion.
- Mitigation progress.
- Escalation timeliness.
- Risk register accuracy.
- Governance compliance.
- Organizational resilience.
- Mission support.

---

Exceptions

Emergency circumstances may require expedited enterprise risk decisions while maintaining documentation and retrospective governance review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Enterprise risk register.
- Assessment reports.
- Monitoring records.
- Escalation logs.
- Governance reports.
- Mitigation plans.
- Risk metrics.
- Improvement records.

---

Continuous Improvement

Enterprise risk operations shall improve through risk assessments, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance evaluations, incident reviews, and lessons learned.
