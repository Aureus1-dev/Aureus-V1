OAS-RISK-102 — Risk Mitigation and Control Operations Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-RISK-102
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-RISK-003 — Risk Assessment and Mitigation Framework
Owner: Chief Risk Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for implementing risk controls, mitigation strategies, corrective actions, control verification, residual risk management, and ongoing effectiveness reviews throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure identified risks are addressed consistently, responsibly, and transparently while preserving constitutional alignment, protecting members, strengthening institutional resilience, and enabling responsible mission advancement.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Risk mitigation
- Risk controls
- Preventive controls
- Detective controls
- Corrective actions
- Residual risk management
- Control verification
- Effectiveness reviews
- Risk treatment documentation
- Continuous improvement

---

Objectives

Risk mitigation operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Reduce preventable harm.
- Strengthen organizational resilience.
- Improve governance.
- Promote accountability.
- Enable continuous improvement.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Risk Mitigation Steward

Responsible for:

- Coordinating mitigation activities.
- Monitoring control implementation.
- Maintaining mitigation documentation.
- Preparing governance reports.
- Supporting effectiveness reviews.

---

Control Owners

Responsible for:

- Implementing assigned controls.
- Monitoring control performance.
- Reporting deficiencies.
- Maintaining documentation.
- Supporting periodic reviews.

---

AI Risk Mitigation Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring mitigation progress.
- Tracking control performance.
- Identifying emerging issues.
- Preparing mitigation summaries.
- Producing risk analytics.
- Supporting administrative workflows.

Human stewards remain responsible for approving mitigation strategies, accepting residual risk, authorizing corrective actions, and exercising governance authority.

---

Prerequisites

Before implementing mitigation measures:

- Risks are assessed.
- Risk owners are assigned.
- Mitigation objectives are documented.
- Required approvals are obtained.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Mitigation Planning

Document:

- Risk description.
- Selected treatment strategy.
- Required controls.
- Responsible owners.
- Success measures.
- Implementation timeline.

---

Step 2 — Control Implementation

Implement approved controls including:

- Preventive controls.
- Detective controls.
- Corrective controls.
- Administrative controls.
- Technical controls.
- Monitoring activities.

Record implementation status.

---

Step 3 — Verification

Verify that controls:

- Operate as designed.
- Reduce identified risks.
- Meet governance requirements.
- Remain properly documented.
- Continue functioning effectively.

Document verification results.

---

Step 4 — Residual Risk Assessment

After implementation:

- Reassess remaining risk.
- Evaluate control effectiveness.
- Determine residual exposure.
- Document findings.
- Escalate when necessary.

---

Step 5 — Corrective Actions

When deficiencies are identified:

- Record the issue.
- Determine root cause.
- Assign responsibility.
- Implement corrective action.
- Verify completion.
- Record lessons learned.

---

Step 6 — Governance Review

Conduct periodic reviews of:

- Control effectiveness.
- Residual risks.
- Mitigation progress.
- Governance compliance.
- Improvement opportunities.

---

Step 7 — Continuous Improvement

Improve mitigation practices through:

- Risk reviews.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance evaluations.
- Incident analysis.
- Lessons learned.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Control implementation.
- Mitigation completion.
- Control effectiveness.
- Residual risk reduction.
- Corrective action completion.
- Governance compliance.
- Organizational resilience.
- Mission support.

---

Exceptions

Emergency situations may require expedited mitigation decisions while maintaining documentation and retrospective governance review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Mitigation plans.
- Control documentation.
- Verification reports.
- Residual risk assessments.
- Corrective action records.
- Governance reports.
- Risk metrics.
- Improvement records.

---

Continuous Improvement

Risk mitigation operations shall improve through control reviews, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance evaluations, incident reviews, and lessons learned.
