OAS-RISK-103 — Crisis Management and Business Continuity Operations Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-RISK-103
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-RISK-004 — Crisis Management and Business Continuity Framework
Owner: Chief Risk Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for crisis response, emergency coordination, business continuity activation, organizational recovery, continuity testing, resilience exercises, post-incident reviews, and continuous institutional resilience throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure Aureus responds to crises rapidly, responsibly, and consistently while protecting members, preserving constitutional governance, maintaining critical services, and strengthening long-term institutional resilience.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Crisis response
- Emergency coordination
- Business continuity activation
- Operational recovery
- Service restoration
- Continuity testing
- Resilience exercises
- Post-incident reviews
- Recovery documentation
- Institutional learning

---

Objectives

Crisis management operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain critical operations.
- Strengthen organizational resilience.
- Enable coordinated response.
- Improve preparedness.
- Support continuous improvement.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Crisis Response Steward

Responsible for:

- Coordinating crisis response.
- Activating continuity plans.
- Managing emergency communications.
- Monitoring recovery.
- Preparing governance reports.

---

Service Owners

Responsible for:

- Executing continuity procedures.
- Maintaining service continuity.
- Reporting operational impacts.
- Supporting recovery activities.
- Updating operational documentation.

---

AI Crisis Support Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring operational conditions.
- Supporting incident tracking.
- Preparing situation summaries.
- Tracking recovery progress.
- Organizing lessons learned.
- Producing resilience analytics.

Human stewards remain responsible for emergency declarations, crisis leadership, recovery priorities, governance actions, and institutional accountability.

---

Prerequisites

Before crisis response operations:

- Business continuity plans are approved.
- Crisis roles are assigned.
- Emergency communications are established.
- Critical services are identified.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Crisis Detection

Document:

- Nature of the incident.
- Time detected.
- Initial impact.
- Affected services.
- Responsible stewards.
- Immediate actions taken.

---

Step 2 — Crisis Assessment

Evaluate:

- Severity.
- Scope.
- Member impact.
- Operational impact.
- Resource requirements.
- Escalation needs.

Determine whether continuity procedures should be activated.

---

Step 3 — Business Continuity Activation

When required:

- Activate continuity plans.
- Notify responsible stewards.
- Protect critical operations.
- Coordinate resources.
- Maintain essential services.
- Record all significant decisions.

---

Step 4 — Operational Recovery

Restore operations through:

- Recovery prioritization.
- Resource coordination.
- Service restoration.
- Operational verification.
- Stakeholder communication.
- Documentation updates.

---

Step 5 — Continuity Testing

Conduct periodic:

- Tabletop exercises.
- Recovery simulations.
- Communication testing.
- Operational readiness reviews.
- Continuity assessments.

Document all findings.

---

Step 6 — Post-Incident Review

Following significant events:

- Conduct after-action reviews.
- Identify root causes.
- Evaluate response effectiveness.
- Document lessons learned.
- Recommend improvements.
- Update continuity documentation.

---

Step 7 — Continuous Improvement

Improve crisis preparedness through:

- Incident reviews.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance evaluations.
- Continuity exercises.
- Lessons learned implementation.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Incident response time.
- Recovery time.
- Service restoration success.
- Continuity exercise completion.
- Operational readiness.
- Lessons learned implementation.
- Governance compliance.
- Mission support.

---

Exceptions

Extraordinary emergencies may require temporary deviations from established procedures while maintaining documentation and retrospective governance review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Crisis response logs.
- Business continuity plans.
- Recovery reports.
- Continuity exercise records.
- After-action reviews.
- Lessons learned documentation.
- Governance reports.
- Improvement plans.

---

Continuous Improvement

Crisis management operations shall improve through resilience assessments, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance evaluations, continuity exercises, and institutional learning.
