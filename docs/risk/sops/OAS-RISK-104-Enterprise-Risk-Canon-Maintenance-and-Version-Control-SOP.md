OAS-RISK-104 — Enterprise Risk Canon Maintenance and Version Control Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-RISK-104
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-RISK-001 — Enterprise Risk and Resilience Charter
Owner: Chief Risk Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for proposing, reviewing, approving, versioning, publishing, interpreting, archiving, maintaining, and continuously improving the Aureus Enterprise Risk Canon.

Its purpose is to preserve constitutional integrity, maintain institutional risk knowledge, ensure disciplined evolution of enterprise risk governance, and provide a transparent stewardship process for future generations.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Enterprise Risk Charters
- Enterprise Risk Frameworks
- Enterprise Risk SOPs
- Risk governance standards
- Risk assessment methodologies
- Business continuity documentation
- Crisis management procedures
- Enterprise risk records
- Institutional resilience documentation

---

Objectives

Enterprise Risk Canon governance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain historical continuity.
- Ensure version traceability.
- Support disciplined institutional evolution.
- Preserve enterprise risk knowledge.
- Strengthen future stewardship.

---

Guiding Principles

The Enterprise Risk Canon shall evolve deliberately rather than reactively.

Every approved revision should strengthen enterprise risk governance, organizational resilience, crisis preparedness, institutional sustainability, governance maturity, or constitutional fidelity while preserving the complete historical record.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Chief Risk Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining the Enterprise Risk Canon.
- Coordinating canonical reviews.
- Approving routine revisions within delegated authority.
- Preserving version history.
- Publishing approved revisions.

---

Enterprise Risk Governance Council

Responsible for:

- Reviewing significant amendments.
- Evaluating constitutional alignment.
- Recommending improvements.
- Interpreting enterprise risk governance.
- Supporting long-term institutional resilience.

---

AI Enterprise Risk Knowledge Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring document consistency.
- Detecting conflicts.
- Tracking revision history.
- Maintaining cross-references.
- Supporting enterprise risk research.
- Identifying improvement opportunities.

Human stewards retain final authority over all canonical Enterprise Risk revisions and interpretations.

---

Prerequisites

Before modifying any canonical Enterprise Risk document:

- Proposed revisions are documented.
- Supporting rationale is prepared.
- Related documents are identified.
- Institutional implications are evaluated.
- Required reviewers are assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Change Proposal

Document:

- Proposed revision.
- Purpose.
- Risk governance justification.
- Expected benefits.
- Related documents.
- Anticipated institutional impacts.

---

Step 2 — Canonical Review

Evaluate:

- Constitutional alignment.
- Risk governance consistency.
- Operational implications.
- Governance impacts.
- Long-term institutional value.

---

Step 3 — Approval

The appropriate governance authority may:

- Approve.
- Approve with revisions.
- Return for additional review.
- Reject.

All approval decisions shall be documented.

---

Step 4 — Version Management

Every approved revision shall include:

- Version number.
- Revision date.
- Change summary.
- Approval record.
- Updated cross-references where applicable.

Superseded versions shall remain permanently archived.

---

Step 5 — Publication

Publish approved revisions within the official Aureus repository.

Update indexes, document references, governance records, and related documentation where appropriate.

---

Step 6 — Archival

Archive prior versions while preserving:

- Original content.
- Approval history.
- Revision history.
- Historical context.

Institutional enterprise risk knowledge shall remain permanently available.

---

Step 7 — Periodic Review

Conduct scheduled reviews to identify:

- Obsolete guidance.
- Governance inconsistencies.
- Emerging enterprise risks.
- Opportunities for simplification.
- Enterprise risk governance improvement initiatives.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Review completion.
- Version accuracy.
- Cross-reference integrity.
- Canon consistency.
- Amendment quality.
- Publication timeliness.
- Historical preservation.
- Steward satisfaction.

---

Exceptions

Emergency enterprise risk governance revisions may follow expedited governance procedures when necessary to protect members, preserve institutional continuity, respond to urgent threats, or comply with extraordinary legal or regulatory requirements.

Emergency revisions shall receive formal retrospective review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Amendment proposals.
- Review records.
- Approval records.
- Version histories.
- Archive records.
- Canon indexes.
- Interpretation guidance.
- Governance reports.

---

Continuous Improvement

Enterprise Risk Canon governance shall improve through governance reviews, enterprise risk assessments, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, documentation quality reviews, resilience evaluations, and institutional learning.
