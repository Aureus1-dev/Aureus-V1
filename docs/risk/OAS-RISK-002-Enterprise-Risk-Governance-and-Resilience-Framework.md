OAS-RISK-002 — Enterprise Risk Governance and Resilience Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-RISK-002
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-RISK-001 — Enterprise Risk and Resilience Charter
Owner: Chief Risk Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the constitutional model for enterprise risk governance, institutional resilience, risk oversight, organizational accountability, AI-assisted risk management, crisis preparedness, resilience planning, and continuous institutional improvement throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure Aureus consistently identifies, evaluates, monitors, and responsibly manages uncertainty while preserving constitutional alignment, protecting members, strengthening institutional resilience, and advancing its mission.

---

Mission

Enterprise risk governance exists to transform uncertainty into informed stewardship by enabling responsible decision-making, protecting institutional capability, and strengthening Aureus' resilience across generations.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Enterprise risk governance
- Risk oversight
- Institutional resilience
- Strategic risk management
- Operational risk management
- Crisis preparedness
- Risk reporting
- AI-assisted risk management
- Governance reviews
- Continuous improvement

---

Guiding Principles

Enterprise risk governance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote informed decision-making.
- Encourage responsible innovation.
- Strengthen accountability.
- Foster organizational resilience.
- Support continuous learning.

Risk governance shall enable responsible action rather than unnecessary hesitation.

---

Enterprise Risk Authority

Enterprise risk authority shall be exercised according to:

- The Constitution.
- Approved governance documents.
- Applicable law.
- Delegated authority.
- Stewardship responsibilities.

Authority shall remain transparent, documented, and accountable.

---

Risk Governance

Enterprise risk governance shall establish:

- Clearly defined responsibilities.
- Enterprise-wide risk ownership.
- Regular risk reviews.
- Institutional resilience planning.
- Crisis governance.
- Continuous improvement.

Governance shall strengthen both resilience and responsible innovation.

---

Risk Identification

Enterprise risk governance shall identify:

- Strategic risks.
- Operational risks.
- Financial risks.
- Technology risks.
- Legal risks.
- Reputational risks.
- Emerging risks.
- Mission risks.

Risk identification shall remain continuous.

---

AI-Assisted Risk Stewardship

AI may assist through:

- Risk monitoring.
- Trend analysis.
- Early warning indicators.
- Risk reporting.
- Scenario modeling.
- Administrative coordination.

Human stewards remain responsible for risk acceptance, mitigation decisions, crisis leadership, governance actions, and institutional accountability.

---

Governance Reviews

Enterprise risk governance shall periodically review:

- Risk exposure.
- Mitigation effectiveness.
- Institutional resilience.
- Crisis preparedness.
- Governance maturity.
- Improvement opportunities.

Reviews shall produce measurable institutional improvements.

---

Performance Measurement

Enterprise risk governance shall evaluate:

- Risk identification.
- Mitigation effectiveness.
- Organizational resilience.
- Crisis preparedness.
- Governance maturity.
- Steward accountability.
- Institutional sustainability.
- Mission support.

Measurements shall strengthen institutional stewardship.

---

Continuous Improvement

Enterprise risk governance shall improve through:

- Risk reviews.
- Incident evaluations.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance assessments.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens Aureus' resilience.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all enterprise risk governance and resilience activities throughout Aureus.

All Risk Frameworks, Risk SOPs, risk assessment methodologies, resilience plans, crisis procedures, governance reviews, and enterprise risk documentation shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Enterprise Risk Charter.
