OAS-COM-103 — Member and Crisis Communications Operations Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-COM-103
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-COM-004 — Member and Crisis Communications Framework
Owner: Chief Communications Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for member communications, stakeholder engagement, emergency notifications, crisis communications, communication accessibility, feedback management, transparency reporting, and institutional trust stewardship throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure members receive timely, accurate, respectful, and constitutionally aligned communications that strengthen trust, protect members, preserve institutional integrity, and faithfully advance Aureus' mission.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Member communications
- Stakeholder engagement
- Emergency notifications
- Crisis communications
- Communication accessibility
- Feedback management
- Transparency reporting
- Community updates
- Trust stewardship
- Communication records

---

Objectives

Member and crisis communications shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote truthful communication.
- Strengthen institutional trust.
- Encourage respectful engagement.
- Improve communication accessibility.
- Enable continuous improvement.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Communications Steward

Responsible for:

- Coordinating member communications.
- Managing crisis communications.
- Overseeing emergency notifications.
- Monitoring communication effectiveness.
- Preparing governance reports.

---

Service and Program Stewards

Responsible for:

- Providing accurate operational information.
- Supporting member engagement.
- Escalating communication issues.
- Maintaining communication records.
- Responding to member inquiries.

---

AI Communications Support Steward

Responsible for:

- Preparing communication drafts.
- Supporting accessibility improvements.
- Organizing communication schedules.
- Tracking engagement metrics.
- Producing communication analytics.
- Supporting administrative coordination.

Human stewards remain responsible for official communications, emergency notifications, crisis messaging, public statements, and governance decisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before member or crisis communications:

- Communication purpose is documented.
- Intended audience is identified.
- Required approvals are assigned.
- Appropriate communication channels are available.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Communication Planning

Document:

- Purpose.
- Audience.
- Key messages.
- Delivery method.
- Responsible stewards.
- Required timeline.

---

Step 2 — Message Preparation

Prepare communications according to:

- Constitutional principles.
- Institutional policies.
- Accessibility standards.
- Brand standards.
- Crisis communication guidance where applicable.

Verify factual accuracy before release.

---

Step 3 — Review and Approval

Review communications for:

- Accuracy.
- Clarity.
- Constitutional alignment.
- Accessibility.
- Appropriate approvals.

Document approval prior to distribution.

---

Step 4 — Distribution

Deliver communications through approved channels.

Record distribution details and maintain communication history.

---

Step 5 — Member Engagement

Support members by:

- Responding respectfully.
- Providing accurate information.
- Recording significant feedback.
- Escalating unresolved concerns.
- Encouraging constructive participation.

---

Step 6 — Crisis Communications

During emergencies:

- Activate approved communication procedures.
- Coordinate official messaging.
- Provide timely updates.
- Correct misinformation when identified.
- Continue updates until normal operations resume.

---

Step 7 — Continuous Improvement

Improve communications through:

- Member feedback.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance evaluations.
- Communication reviews.
- Lessons learned.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Communication timeliness.
- Message accuracy.
- Member engagement.
- Crisis response effectiveness.
- Accessibility compliance.
- Stakeholder satisfaction.
- Governance compliance.
- Mission support.

---

Exceptions

Emergency circumstances may require expedited communication approvals while maintaining documentation and retrospective governance review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Communication plans.
- Notification records.
- Approval records.
- Feedback logs.
- Crisis communication logs.
- Communication metrics.
- Governance reports.
- Improvement plans.

---

Continuous Improvement

Member and crisis communications shall improve through communication reviews, audience feedback, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance evaluations, and institutional learning.
