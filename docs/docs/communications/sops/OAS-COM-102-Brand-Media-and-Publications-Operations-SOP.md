OAS-COM-102 — Brand, Media, and Publications Operations Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-COM-102
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-COM-003 — Brand, Media, and Publications Framework
Owner: Chief Communications Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for brand management, media relations, publication approval, editorial workflows, digital communications, social media stewardship, content lifecycle management, and institutional reputation management throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure all public-facing communications are accurate, consistent, accessible, constitutionally aligned, and capable of strengthening institutional trust while faithfully advancing Aureus' mission.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Brand management
- Media relations
- Publications
- Editorial workflows
- Digital communications
- Social media stewardship
- Content lifecycle management
- Institutional reputation
- Communication archives
- Publication governance

---

Objectives

Brand and publication operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote truthful communication.
- Strengthen public trust.
- Maintain brand consistency.
- Support education.
- Enable continuous improvement.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Brand Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining brand standards.
- Coordinating publication workflows.
- Supporting media engagement.
- Monitoring brand consistency.
- Preparing governance reports.

---

Content Owners

Responsible for:

- Developing content.
- Verifying factual accuracy.
- Following editorial standards.
- Maintaining publication documentation.
- Supporting review processes.

---

AI Brand Steward

Responsible for:

- Draft preparation.
- Editorial assistance.
- Accessibility reviews.
- Publication scheduling.
- Metadata management.
- Communications analytics.

Human stewards remain responsible for official publications, media interviews, brand approvals, public messaging, and governance decisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before publication:

- Purpose is documented.
- Audience is identified.
- Content is reviewed.
- Required approvals are assigned.
- Brand standards are available.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Content Planning

Document:

- Purpose.
- Audience.
- Key messages.
- Publication channel.
- Responsible stewards.
- Publication schedule.

---

Step 2 — Content Development

Prepare content according to:

- Constitutional principles.
- Editorial standards.
- Brand guidelines.
- Accessibility requirements.
- Institutional policies.

Verify all factual statements before approval.

---

Step 3 — Editorial Review

Review for:

- Accuracy.
- Clarity.
- Brand consistency.
- Accessibility.
- Constitutional alignment.
- Editorial quality.

Document all required revisions.

---

Step 4 — Approval

Obtain all required approvals before publication.

Record approval history and publication authorization.

---

Step 5 — Publication

Publish through approved communication channels.

Maintain publication archives and distribution records.

---

Step 6 — Reputation Monitoring

Monitor:

- Public engagement.
- Brand consistency.
- Media coverage.
- Audience feedback.
- Publication performance.
- Emerging communication risks.

Escalate significant issues when appropriate.

---

Step 7 — Continuous Improvement

Improve brand and publication operations through:

- Editorial reviews.
- Audience feedback.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance evaluations.
- Lessons learned.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Publication quality.
- Editorial accuracy.
- Brand consistency.
- Audience engagement.
- Media responsiveness.
- Accessibility compliance.
- Governance compliance.
- Mission support.

---

Exceptions

Emergency publications may follow expedited approval procedures while maintaining documentation and retrospective governance review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Publication plans.
- Editorial records.
- Approval documentation.
- Publication archives.
- Brand reviews.
- Media engagement records.
- Governance reports.
- Improvement plans.

---

Continuous Improvement

Brand and publication operations shall improve through editorial assessments, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance evaluations, audience reviews, and lessons learned.
