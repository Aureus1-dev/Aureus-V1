# OAS Constitutional Amendment Proposal (CAP) Register
### Session Closeout — OAS-SESSION-2026-07-13

This register contains every Constitutional Amendment Proposal (CAP) generated by reconciling the constitutional decisions made during this session. No canonical document has been modified in producing this register. Every CAP below carries **Status: Draft** and requires explicit Founder approval before any incorporation into canon, per the Constitutional Integrity Rules.

## CAP-001 — Establish the Founding Charter of the Open Advancement System

**Date:** 2026-07-13
**Originating Session:** OAS-SESSION-2026-07-13
**Related Constitutional Documents:** OAS-001 (Founding Charter)
**Current Constitutional Text:** None. No constitutional canon exists prior to this proposal.
**Proposed Constitutional Text:** The complete text of OAS-001, Draft 0.9 (Preamble; Articles I–IX in full), as originally drafted this session.
**Reason for the Amendment:** To establish the foundational constitutional instrument of the Open Advancement System, from which all subsequent constitutional authority derives.
**Expected Constitutional Impact:** Creates the entire constitutional order: mission, founding principles, human–AI stewardship rules, governance framework, accountability mechanisms, amendment procedure, and continuity provisions. Every other CAP in this register depends on this one.
**Potential Risks or Tradeoffs:** As a first draft, several structural questions were left open (see OAS-001 Notes N1–N8, carried forward as unresolved constitutional questions below), most significantly the undefined sunset mechanism for Founder authority (N4).
**Dependencies on Other CAPs:** None (foundational).
**Recommendation:** Approve as the base instrument, with the understanding that CAP-002 through CAP-011 propose immediate amendments to it before final canonization, so that the Founder may consider ratifying the amended (Draft 0.95) form directly rather than ratifying Draft 0.9 and then separately amending it.
**Status:** Draft

## CAP-002 — Reframe the Mission Around Human Flourishing

**Date:** 2026-07-13
**Related Constitutional Documents:** OAS-001, Preamble and Article II, Section 1
**Current Constitutional Text:** *Preamble:* "We establish the Open Advancement System in the conviction that human capability, judgment, and dignity are worth strengthening across generations; that knowledge and the means of advancement should be open to those they serve..." *Article II, Section 1:* "The mission of the Open Advancement System is to strengthen human capability, knowledge, and agency, and to make the means of advancement openly available to those the System serves, across generations."
**Proposed Constitutional Text:** *Preamble (revised, two paragraphs)* stating flourishing — growth in capability, wisdom, purpose, dignity, relationship, and contribution — as the founding conviction, with trusteeship language added. *Article II, Section 1 (revised):* "The mission of the Open Advancement System is to help every person flourish. The System pursues this mission by strengthening human capability, knowledge, and agency, and by making the means of advancement openly available to those it serves, across generations."
**Reason for the Amendment:** Canonical institutional philosophy establishes human flourishing, not capability or knowledge alone, as the institution's ultimate purpose; capability, knowledge, and agency are properly instrumental to that end.
**Expected Constitutional Impact:** Becomes the interpretive anchor for the entire Charter (Article II, Section 6) and for all subordinate documents (OAS-002, OAS-003). Elevates the mission from an operational statement to a purpose statement.
**Potential Risks or Tradeoffs:** "Every person" reads as universal in scope while "those it serves" reads as bounded; this pre-existing tension is now more visible under the flourishing framing. See unresolved question N2 (OAS-001).
**Dependencies on Other CAPs:** Depends on CAP-001. CAP-011 (entrenchment update) depends on this CAP.
**Recommendation:** Approve. This is the single most consequential change in the register and should be reviewed first.
**Status:** Draft

## CAP-003 — Define Human Development as the Components of Flourishing

**Date:** 2026-07-13
**Related Constitutional Documents:** OAS-001, Article II (new Section 2)
**Current Constitutional Text:** None (new section).
**Proposed Constitutional Text:** "Flourishing, as this Charter uses the term, is not reducible to productivity or information. It consists in the growth of capability, wisdom, purpose, dignity, relationship, opportunity, and meaningful contribution. The System shall pursue all of these together, and shall not mistake advancement in any one of them for the whole."
**Reason for the Amendment:** Without a definition, "flourishing" is vulnerable to future narrowing into a purely productivity- or information-based metric. This section fixes its meaning at the constitutional level.
**Expected Constitutional Impact:** Becomes the canonical reference definition cited throughout OAS-002 and OAS-003. Renumbers subsequent Article II sections.
**Potential Risks or Tradeoffs:** Enumerated lists can date poorly or invite narrow textual parsing; mitigated by "shall pursue all of these together" language preventing cherry-picking.
**Dependencies on Other CAPs:** Depends on CAP-001 and CAP-002.
**Recommendation:** Approve.
**Status:** Draft

## CAP-004 — Establish the Lifelong Journey Principle

**Date:** 2026-07-13
**Related Constitutional Documents:** OAS-001, Article II (new Section 3)
**Current Constitutional Text:** None (new section).
**Proposed Constitutional Text:** "Every person's flourishing is a lifelong journey of growth, not a fixed state to be achieved and left behind. The System shall serve people at every stage of that journey, helping each discover and take the next meaningful step available to them, rather than prescribing a single path all must follow."
**Reason for the Amendment:** Reflects the canonical conviction that flourishing is continuous rather than terminal, without using promotional or inspirational language ("adventure") inappropriate to constitutional prose.
**Expected Constitutional Impact:** Constrains future policy from treating flourishing as a checklist or credential to be completed once.
**Potential Risks or Tradeoffs:** Low; primarily interpretive guidance rather than a rule with enforcement mechanics.
**Dependencies on Other CAPs:** Depends on CAP-001 and CAP-002.
**Recommendation:** Approve.

## CAP-005 — Establish the Collective as a Mission Mechanism

**Date:** 2026-07-13
**Related Constitutional Documents:** OAS-001, Article II (new Section 4)
**Current Constitutional Text:** None (new section).
**Proposed Constitutional Text:** "The System is a collective learning community. Its members strengthen the institution, and the institution strengthens its members; the flourishing of each member increases the System's capacity to help others flourish. The System shall treat the flourishing of those within it as inseparable from, and never as competing with, the flourishing of those it serves."
**Reason for the Amendment:** Establishes, at constitutional level, that internal (member) and external (served-population) flourishing are not a zero-sum tradeoff — foreclosing a foreseeable future rationalization for sacrificing one for the other.
**Expected Constitutional Impact:** Introduces "members" as a constitutional category without defining membership, creating a dependency on a future Charter of Membership.
**Potential Risks or Tradeoffs:** The undefined term "members" is a genuine gap this amendment makes more urgent, not less (see unresolved question N2, OAS-001/OAS-003).
**Dependencies on Other CAPs:** Depends on CAP-001 and CAP-002.
**Recommendation:** Approve, with a companion recommendation that a Charter of Membership be commissioned promptly given the new obligations this section creates.
**Status:** Draft

## CAP-006 — Adopt the Five-Hundred-Year Interpretive Test

**Date:** 2026-07-13
**Related Constitutional Documents:** OAS-001, Article II, Section 6 (Interpretation)
**Current Constitutional Text:** "Every provision of this Charter, and every act taken under it, shall be interpreted in the manner that best serves the mission while respecting the principles entrenched in Article III. Where two lawful interpretations are available, the interpretation that better protects human dignity and agency shall prevail."
**Proposed Constitutional Text:** Adds: "Where the long-term effect of an interpretation is uncertain, it shall be tested by whether it would still strengthen humanity five centuries hence; an interpretation that would not withstand that test shall yield to one that would."
**Reason for the Amendment:** Converts the Founder's five-hundred-year review heuristic from an informal drafting discipline into a standing, binding interpretive obligation for all future officers and bodies.
**Expected Constitutional Impact:** Applies to every future interpretive dispute under the Charter; raises the bar for any interpretation that trades long-term durability for short-term convenience.
**Potential Risks or Tradeoffs:** "Would still strengthen humanity five centuries hence" is inherently unfalsifiable in the moment and relies on the good-faith judgment of interpreters; it is a discipline, not a mechanical test.
**Dependencies on Other CAPs:** Depends on CAP-001.
**Recommendation:** Approve.
**Status:** Draft

## CAP-007 — Broaden the Stewardship Principle to All Authority

**Date:** 2026-07-13
**Related Constitutional Documents:** OAS-001, Article III, Section 6
**Current Constitutional Text:** "Stewardship Across Generations. Those who govern the System hold it in trust for those who will inherit it. They shall not consume, encumber, or endanger the institution's capacity to pursue the mission in the future for advantage in the present."
**Proposed Constitutional Text:** "Stewardship. Every authority within the System is held in trust — for those upon whom it is exercised, for those the System serves, and for those who will inherit the System. No officer, body, or instrument owns the authority entrusted to it; each holds it only in trust, and must exercise it for the benefit of those it serves rather than for its own advantage. Those who govern the System shall not consume, encumber, or endanger its capacity to pursue the mission in the future for advantage in the present."
**Reason for the Amendment:** Canonical principle 2 (Stewardship) applies to every person entrusted with responsibility, not only to future-facing governance. The prior text captured only the future-generations half of the principle.
**Expected Constitutional Impact:** Becomes a general property of all authority in the System — governance, office-holding, and (per CAP-009) technology use alike. Candidate for entrenchment (CAP-011).
**Potential Risks or Tradeoffs:** Broadening a principle this fundamental increases its interpretive reach; officers may need guidance distinguishing ordinary discretion from a stewardship violation.
**Dependencies on Other CAPs:** Depends on CAP-001.
**Recommendation:** Approve.
**Status:** Draft

## CAP-008 — Establish Institutional Learning as a Founding Principle

**Date:** 2026-07-13
**Related Constitutional Documents:** OAS-001, Article III (new Section 8)
**Current Constitutional Text:** None (new section, appended after Section 7 to avoid renumbering existing Article III sections).
**Proposed Constitutional Text:** "Institutional Learning. The System shall continually learn from its service, its experience, its evidence, and its own reflection. No lesson that would strengthen the mission shall be lost through neglect, turnover, or the passage of time. The System shall maintain the means to preserve what it learns and to apply that learning to its future conduct."
**Reason for the Amendment:** Canonical principle 4 requires the institution itself, not merely its officers, to learn continuously and preserve what it learns.
**Expected Constitutional Impact:** Creates a duty with no specified mechanism by design (mechanism is deferred to subordinate/operational documents); anchored operationally by CAP-010.
**Potential Risks or Tradeoffs:** Without a defined mechanism, this duty risks being aspirational only until a subordinate document operationalizes it.
**Dependencies on Other CAPs:** Depends on CAP-001. Directly linked to CAP-010.
**Recommendation:** Approve.
**Status:** Draft

## CAP-009 — Retitle and Strengthen "AI as Stewardship Technology"

**Date:** 2026-07-13
**Related Constitutional Documents:** OAS-001, Article IV, Sections 1 and 2
**Current Constitutional Text:** *Section 1:* "Purpose of Technology. Artificial intelligence and every other technology employed by the System exist to strengthen human capability and to assist human judgment. Technology serves the mission; the mission never serves technology." *Section 2 (Human Accountability),* final sentence ends at: "No officer may disclaim responsibility on the ground that a decision was produced or recommended by a machine."
**Proposed Constitutional Text:** *Section 1 (retitled):* "AI as Stewardship Technology. Artificial intelligence and every other technology employed by the System are stewardship technologies: they exist to strengthen human judgment, human capability, and human flourishing. Technology serves the mission; the mission never serves technology." *Section 2, new final sentence:* "An officer may use artificial intelligence to inform a decision; no officer may use it to escape responsibility for one."
**Reason for the Amendment:** Aligns Article IV's statement of technology's purpose with the flourishing-centered Mission (CAP-002) and makes explicit and unmistakable that AI never substitutes for constitutional human responsibility.
**Expected Constitutional Impact:** Closes an interpretive gap an officer could otherwise exploit to disclaim accountability by attributing a decision to an instrument.
**Potential Risks or Tradeoffs:** None identified; this strengthens an existing principle without altering its scope.
**Dependencies on Other CAPs:** Depends on CAP-001 and CAP-002 (for internal consistency of the flourishing reference).
**Recommendation:** Approve.
**Status:** Draft

## CAP-010 — Establish the Institutional Memory Duty

**Date:** 2026-07-13

## CAP-011 — Update Entrenched Provisions to Include Flourishing and Stewardship

**Date:** 2026-07-13
**Related Constitutional Documents:** OAS-001, Article VII, Section 3
**Current Constitutional Text:** "The following may not be amended in a manner that weakens them: the primacy of the mission (Article II, Section 2); the principles of human dignity and human agency (Article III, Sections 1 and 2); human accountability for institutional decisions (Article IV, Sections 2 and 3); the requirement that authority be matched by accountability (Article III, Section 5); and this Section."
**Proposed Constitutional Text:** "The following may not be amended in a manner that weakens them: the mission of human flourishing (Article II, Section 1); the primacy of the mission (Article II, Section 5); the principles of human dignity and human agency (Article III, Sections 1 and 2); the principle of stewardship (Article III, Section 6); human accountability for institutional decisions (Article IV, Sections 2 and 3); the requirement that authority be matched by accountability (Article III, Section 5); and this Section."
**Reason for the Amendment:** Two changes are bundled here because they are inseparable: (1) the citation to "the primacy of the mission" must be corrected from Article II, Section 2 to Article II, Section 5 as a direct, mechanical consequence of CAP-003–CAP-005 inserting three new sections before it; (2) the newly canonical mission of human flourishing (CAP-002) and the broadened Stewardship principle (CAP-007) are added to entrenched status as foundational commitments.
**Expected Constitutional Impact:** Determines what future amendments may never weaken. This is among the highest-stakes CAPs in the register.
**Potential Risks or Tradeoffs:** Over-entrenchment risks ossifying language future generations may legitimately need to refine. This proposal deliberately does *not* entrench Human Development, the Lifelong Journey, the Collective, or Institutional Learning (CAP-003–CAP-005, CAP-008), on the judgment that they are fully binding but should remain more amendable than the core mission and stewardship principles. See unresolved question N6 (OAS-001).
**Dependencies on Other CAPs:** Depends on CAP-001, CAP-002, CAP-003, CAP-004, CAP-005 (renumbering), and CAP-007.
**Recommendation:** Approve the citation correction as non-discretionary. The Founder should separately confirm or revise the entrenchment-scope judgment.
**Status:** Draft

## CAP-012 — Canonize the Preamble to the Constitution (OAS-002, Base Text)

**Date:** 2026-07-13
**Related Constitutional Documents:** OAS-002 (new document); OAS-001, Article I, Sections 3–4
**Current Constitutional Text:** None (new subordinate document).
**Proposed Constitutional Text:** The complete original text of OAS-002 as first drafted: eight recitals (ordaining, flourishing, the Collective, stewardship/authority, institutional learning, AI as tools, the five-hundred-year horizon, closing declaration) and the operative Article — Status and Effect of this Preamble (Sections 1–3).
**Reason for the Amendment:** Establishes a unified philosophical frontispiece for the entire Constitution, distinct from and subordinate to OAS-001's own internal preamble, which is addressed specifically to the Charter's establishment.
**Expected Constitutional Impact:** Adds a new category of constitutional document (subordinate, interpretive, non-operative) to the canon, and establishes the pattern later followed by OAS-003.
**Potential Risks or Tradeoffs:** Creates two coexisting preambles (OAS-001's and OAS-002's) whose relationship, while designed to be complementary, has not been confirmed by the Founder as intentional (unresolved question N2, OAS-002). Also surfaces a genuine gap: no canonical procedure yet governs amendment of subordinate constitutional documents generally (unresolved question N1, OAS-002).
**Dependencies on Other CAPs:** Depends on CAP-001 and, substantively, on CAP-002–CAP-011, since the original recitals presuppose the flourishing-centered Mission and broadened Stewardship principle already being in force.
**Recommendation:** Approve the base text, understanding that CAP-013 through CAP-016 propose immediate amendments to it, comparable to the CAP-001/CAP-002–011 relationship for OAS-001.

## CAP-014 — Add Explicit Recital on Justice, Freedom, and Responsibility

**Date:** 2026-07-13
**Related Constitutional Documents:** OAS-002, Preamble (new recital, inserted as recital 6)
**Current Constitutional Text:** None (new recital).
**Proposed Constitutional Text:** "We hold that flourishing requires justice: fair and equal regard for every person, regardless of standing or station. We hold that it requires freedom: the standing to think, choose, and act for oneself, secured rather than merely permitted. **Potential Risks or Tradeoffs:** As with CAP-013, creates a citation-dependency risk for OAS-003.

## CAP-018 — Establish Separation of Drafting Notes from Constitutional Text as a Standing Convention

**Date:** 2026-07-13
**Related Constitutional Documents:** Documentation convention applicable to all constitutional documents (OAS-001, OAS-002, OAS-003, and future documents)
**Current Constitutional Text:** None (process convention, not substantive text; OAS-001's original presentation did not clearly wall off its Notes section from its constitutional text).
**Proposed Constitutional Text:** Not itself constitutional text; a documentation rule: every constitutional document shall present its operative text and its Founder-facing drafting notes in clearly separated sections, divided by an explicit marker (e.g., "End of Constitutional Text"), with drafting notes marked as non-canonical and excluded from the constitutional record upon canonization.
**Reason for the Amendment:** Prevents ambiguity about what would actually enter the permanent constitutional record if a document were canonized as-is.
**Expected Constitutional Impact:** Procedural only; affects presentation and record-keeping practice, not rights or authority. Should be applied retroactively to the presentation of OAS-001, OAS-002, and OAS-003 once approved.
**Potential Risks or Tradeoffs:** None identified.
**Dependencies on Other CAPs:** None directly, though it affects the final presentation of CAP-001 and CAP-012's underlying documents.
**Recommendation:** Approve. Consider formalizing as part of a future Constitutional Drafting and Documentation Standards document.
**Status:** Draft

## CAP-019 — Establish the Constitutional Amendment Proposal (CAP) Process as Binding Procedure

**Date:** 2026-07-13
**Related Constitutional Documents:** Fills a gap identified in OAS-002 Note N1 (no canonical procedure yet governs amendment or canonization of subordinate constitutional documents); relevant to OAS-001, Article I, Section 4 and Article VII
**Current Constitutional Text:** None. OAS-001, Article VII establishes how the Charter itself is amended; no canonical text establishes how any constitutional document — including future subordinate documents — is proposed, reviewed, approved, or canonized.
**Proposed Constitutional Text (summary; full drafting reserved for a future dedicated document):** All constitutional change, of any size, must first exist as a CAP containing the fields used throughout this register. No CAP may be merged into canon without explicit Founder approval. No canonical document may be silently modified, superseded, or reinterpreted. The constitutional record must permanently preserve original text, proposed text, approved text, rationale, approval history, and effective date for every amendment.
**Reason for the Amendment:** This session's closeout protocol itself resolves — and should formalize — the exact gap flagged in OAS-002 Note N1. Without canonizing this procedure, the CAP process governing this very register has no constitutional force of its own.
**Expected Constitutional Impact:** Would become the procedural backbone for all future constitutional work, including retroactively governing how CAP-001 through CAP-018 are themselves approved and merged.
**Potential Risks or Tradeoffs:** Procedurally self-referential: this CAP proposes the authority by which CAPs (including itself) are approved. The Founder's approval of this CAP should be treated as an exercise of the Founder's existing constitutional authority under OAS-001, Article V, Section 4, not as something the CAP process retroactively authorizes itself.
**Dependencies on Other CAPs:** None substantively, but it governs the approval mechanics of every other CAP in this register.
**Recommendation:** Approve, and commission a dedicated future document (tentatively OAS-004: Constitutional Amendment and Canonization Procedures) to give this procedure permanent, fully drafted canonical text rather than relying on this CAP's summary.
**Status:** Draft
