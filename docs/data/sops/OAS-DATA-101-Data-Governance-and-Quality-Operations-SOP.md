OAS-DATA-101 — Data Governance and Quality Operations Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-DATA-101
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-DATA-002 — Data Governance and Quality Framework
Owner: Chief Data Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for governing institutional data, assigning stewardship responsibilities, monitoring data quality, managing metadata, resolving data quality issues, and continuously improving the integrity of Aureus' information assets.

Its purpose is to ensure institutional data remains accurate, complete, consistent, timely, trustworthy, and constitutionally aligned throughout its lifecycle.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Data governance
- Data ownership
- Data stewardship
- Data quality monitoring
- Metadata management
- Master data governance
- Data issue management
- Governance reviews
- Quality reporting
- Continuous improvement

---

Objectives

Data governance operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain trustworthy information.
- Strengthen accountability.
- Improve decision quality.
- Reduce information risk.
- Support continuous improvement.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Data Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining governed data.
- Monitoring quality metrics.
- Coordinating stewardship activities.
- Managing metadata.
- Preparing governance reports.

---

Data Owners

Responsible for:

- Approving data standards.
- Maintaining data quality expectations.
- Resolving governance issues.
- Supporting periodic reviews.

---

AI Data Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring data quality.
- Detecting inconsistencies.
- Identifying duplicate records.
- Generating metadata.
- Preparing quality summaries.
- Tracking governance metrics.

Human stewards remain responsible for governance decisions, quality approvals, policy interpretation, and institutional accountability.

---

Prerequisites

Before governing institutional data:

- Data ownership is assigned.
- Stewardship responsibilities are documented.
- Quality standards are established.
- Metadata requirements are defined.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Data Registration

Register significant institutional datasets by documenting:

- Data owner.
- Data steward.
- Business purpose.
- Classification.
- Quality expectations.
- Retention requirements.

---

Step 2 — Quality Monitoring

Monitor data for:

- Accuracy.
- Completeness.
- Consistency.
- Timeliness.
- Validity.
- Uniqueness.

Document quality findings and trends.

---

Step 3 — Metadata Management

Maintain metadata describing:

- Dataset purpose.
- Definitions.
- Ownership.
- Relationships.
- Lifecycle status.
- Applicable governance standards.

---

Step 4 — Issue Resolution

When data quality issues are identified:

- Record the issue.
- Assess severity.
- Assign responsibility.
- Correct the issue.
- Verify resolution.
- Document lessons learned.

---

Step 5 — Governance Review

Conduct periodic reviews to evaluate:

- Data quality performance.
- Stewardship effectiveness.
- Metadata completeness.
- Compliance with governance standards.
- Improvement opportunities.

---

Step 6 — Reporting

Prepare reports summarizing:

- Data quality metrics.
- Governance activities.
- Outstanding issues.
- Stewardship performance.
- Recommended improvements.

---

Step 7 — Continuous Improvement

Implement approved improvements to:

- Data standards.
- Stewardship practices.
- Metadata quality.
- Governance maturity.
- Information reliability.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Data quality scores.
- Issue resolution time.
- Metadata completeness.
- Stewardship participation.
- Governance review completion.
- Duplicate reduction.
- Information reliability.
- Governance effectiveness.

---

Exceptions

Critical operational or legal requirements may require temporary deviations from standard procedures, provided they are documented, approved, and reviewed afterward.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Data inventories.
- Stewardship assignments.
- Metadata catalogs.
- Data quality reports.
- Governance review records.
- Issue logs.
- Improvement plans.
- Governance reports.

---

Continuous Improvement

Data governance operations shall improve through quality assessments, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance reviews, operational evaluations, and lessons learned.
