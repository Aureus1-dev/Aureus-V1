OAS-DATA-102 — Knowledge Management and Information Lifecycle Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-DATA-102
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-DATA-003 — Data Lifecycle and Knowledge Management Framework
Owner: Chief Data Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for creating, classifying, organizing, maintaining, sharing, retaining, archiving, discovering, and retiring institutional knowledge throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to preserve institutional memory, strengthen organizational learning, improve information discoverability, support evidence-based decision-making, and ensure long-term stewardship of Aureus' knowledge assets.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Knowledge repositories
- Information architecture
- Institutional documentation
- Knowledge classification
- Metadata
- Information sharing
- Retention
- Archival
- Knowledge discovery
- Information retirement

---

Objectives

Knowledge management shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Strengthen institutional memory.
- Improve discoverability.
- Encourage responsible knowledge sharing.
- Support informed decisions.
- Enable continuous learning.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Knowledge Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining knowledge repositories.
- Organizing institutional information.
- Coordinating lifecycle activities.
- Managing metadata.
- Preparing governance reports.

---

Information Owners

Responsible for:

- Approving knowledge publication.
- Maintaining content quality.
- Reviewing retained information.
- Supporting governance reviews.

---

AI Knowledge Steward

Responsible for:

- Organizing repositories.
- Generating metadata.
- Detecting duplicate content.
- Supporting semantic search.
- Preparing knowledge summaries.
- Identifying obsolete information.

Human stewards remain responsible for publication decisions, governance approvals, constitutional interpretation, and institutional accountability.

---

Prerequisites

Before adding information to institutional repositories:

- Ownership is assigned.
- Classification is completed.
- Metadata standards are applied.
- Required approvals are obtained.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Knowledge Creation

Document:

- Purpose.
- Author.
- Date created.
- Classification.
- Applicable governance references.
- Related knowledge assets.

---

Step 2 — Organization

Organize knowledge using approved:

- Information architecture.
- Metadata standards.
- Taxonomies.
- Cross-references.
- Repository structures.

---

Step 3 — Knowledge Sharing

Share institutional knowledge according to:

- Authorized access.
- Information classification.
- Operational need.
- Governance requirements.

Protect confidential information appropriately.

---

Step 4 — Lifecycle Management

Maintain information through:

- Periodic review.
- Updates.
- Version control.
- Retention.
- Archival.
- Retirement when appropriate.

---

Step 5 — Knowledge Discovery

Support discovery through:

- Search.
- Metadata.
- Cross-linking.
- Knowledge indexes.
- Semantic organization.

---

Step 6 — Governance Review

Conduct periodic reviews to evaluate:

- Repository quality.
- Metadata completeness.
- Information relevance.
- Knowledge accessibility.
- Governance compliance.

---

Step 7 — Retirement

When information reaches end of life:

- Archive where appropriate.
- Preserve historical records.
- Update indexes.
- Document retirement.
- Maintain governance history.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Repository quality.
- Metadata completeness.
- Information discoverability.
- Knowledge reuse.
- Retention compliance.
- Archive integrity.
- Governance effectiveness.
- Institutional learning.

---

Exceptions

Legal requirements, constitutional directives, audits, or investigations may require information to remain retained beyond standard lifecycle schedules.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Repository inventories.
- Metadata catalogs.
- Knowledge indexes.
- Retention schedules.
- Archive records.
- Retirement logs.
- Governance reports.
- Lifecycle reviews.

---

Continuous Improvement

Knowledge management shall improve through repository reviews, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance evaluations, knowledge audits, and lessons learned.
