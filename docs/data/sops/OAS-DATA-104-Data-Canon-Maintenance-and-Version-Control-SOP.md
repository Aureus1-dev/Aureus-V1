OAS-DATA-104 — Data Canon Maintenance and Version Control Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-DATA-104
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-DATA-001 — Data Governance Charter
Owner: Chief Data Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for proposing, reviewing, approving, versioning, publishing, interpreting, archiving, maintaining, and continuously improving the Aureus Data Canon.

Its purpose is to preserve constitutional integrity, maintain institutional knowledge, ensure disciplined evolution of data governance, and provide a transparent stewardship process for future institutional learning.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Data Charters
- Data Frameworks
- Data SOPs
- Data governance standards
- Metadata standards
- Analytics standards
- Knowledge management documentation
- Data lifecycle procedures
- Governance records

---

Objectives

Data Canon governance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain historical continuity.
- Ensure version traceability.
- Support disciplined institutional evolution.
- Preserve institutional knowledge.
- Strengthen future stewardship.

---

Guiding Principles

The Data Canon shall evolve deliberately rather than reactively.

Every approved revision should strengthen governance, information quality, institutional learning, analytics capability, knowledge stewardship, or constitutional fidelity while preserving the complete historical record.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Chief Data Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining the Data Canon.
- Coordinating canonical reviews.
- Approving routine revisions within delegated authority.
- Preserving version history.
- Publishing approved revisions.

---

Data Governance Council

Responsible for:

- Reviewing significant amendments.
- Evaluating constitutional alignment.
- Recommending improvements.
- Interpreting data governance.
- Supporting long-term institutional stewardship.

---

AI Data Knowledge Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring document consistency.
- Detecting conflicts.
- Tracking revision history.
- Maintaining cross-references.
- Supporting governance research.
- Identifying improvement opportunities.

Human stewards retain final authority over all canonical data revisions and interpretations.

---

Prerequisites

Before modifying any canonical data document:

- Proposed revisions are documented.
- Supporting rationale is prepared.
- Related documents are identified.
- Governance implications are evaluated.
- Required reviewers are assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Change Proposal

Document:

- Proposed revision.
- Purpose.
- Governance justification.
- Expected benefits.
- Related documents.
- Anticipated institutional impacts.

---

Step 2 — Canonical Review

Evaluate:

- Constitutional alignment.
- Data consistency.
- Governance implications.
- Operational impacts.
- Long-term institutional value.

---

Step 3 — Approval

The appropriate governance authority may:

- Approve.
- Approve with revisions.
- Return for additional review.
- Reject.

All approval decisions shall be documented.

---

Step 4 — Version Management

Every approved revision shall include:

- Version number.
- Revision date.
- Change summary.
- Approval record.
- Updated cross-references where applicable.

Superseded versions shall remain permanently archived.

---

Step 5 — Publication

Publish approved revisions within the official Aureus repository.

Update indexes, document references, and governance records where appropriate.

---

Step 6 — Archival

Archive prior versions while preserving:

- Original content.
- Approval history.
- Revision history.
- Historical context.

Institutional knowledge shall remain permanently available.

---

Step 7 — Periodic Review

Conduct scheduled reviews to identify:

- Obsolete guidance.
- Governance inconsistencies.
- Emerging information needs.
- Opportunities for simplification.
- Data governance improvement initiatives.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Review completion.
- Version accuracy.
- Cross-reference integrity.
- Canon consistency.
- Amendment quality.
- Publication timeliness.
- Historical preservation.
- Steward satisfaction.

---

Exceptions

Emergency data governance revisions may follow expedited governance procedures when necessary to protect institutional information, preserve operational continuity, or respond to urgent legal or regulatory requirements.

Emergency revisions shall receive formal retrospective review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Amendment proposals.
- Review records.
- Approval records.
- Version histories.
- Archive records.
- Canon indexes.
- Interpretation guidance.
- Governance reports.

---

Continuous Improvement

Data Canon governance shall improve through governance reviews, data quality assessments, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, documentation quality reviews, knowledge audits, and institutional learning.
