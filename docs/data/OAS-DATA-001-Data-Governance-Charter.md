OAS-DATA-001 — Data Governance Charter

Canonical Designation: OAS-DATA-001
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-001 — Founding Charter
Owner: Chief Data Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Charter establishes the constitutional authority governing all data assets, information resources, analytics, knowledge systems, metadata, and institutional records throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure data is governed as a strategic institutional asset that advances the mission, protects members, preserves constitutional alignment, enables trustworthy decision-making, and strengthens long-term institutional stewardship.

---

Mission

Data stewardship exists to create, protect, organize, govern, analyze, preserve, and responsibly use information that enables Aureus to learn continuously, make better decisions, improve member outcomes, and preserve institutional knowledge across generations.

---

Scope

This Charter governs:

- Data governance
- Data quality
- Master data
- Metadata
- Analytics
- Business intelligence
- Knowledge management
- Data lifecycle management
- Data stewardship
- Institutional information assets

---

Guiding Principles

Data stewardship shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote trustworthy information.
- Respect privacy.
- Strengthen accountability.
- Enable responsible innovation.
- Preserve institutional knowledge.

Data shall be managed as a long-term institutional asset rather than a temporary operational artifact.

---

Data Authority

Data authority shall be exercised according to:

- The Constitution.
- Approved governance documents.
- Applicable law.
- Delegated authority.
- Stewardship responsibilities.

Authority shall remain documented, transparent, and accountable.

---

Data Governance

Data governance shall include:

- Clearly defined ownership.
- Stewardship responsibilities.
- Quality standards.
- Lifecycle management.
- Governance oversight.
- Continuous improvement.

Governance shall balance operational needs with long-term institutional value.

---

Data Stewardship

Data stewardship shall:

- Protect institutional information.
- Preserve accuracy.
- Maintain integrity.
- Enable appropriate access.
- Support informed decision-making.
- Strengthen institutional memory.

Every steward shares responsibility for the quality and responsible use of data.

---

Data Risk Management

Data-related risks shall be:

- Identified.
- Assessed.
- Prioritized.
- Mitigated.
- Monitored.
- Reviewed regularly.

Data governance decisions shall balance accessibility, quality, privacy, and operational resilience.

---

Continuous Improvement

Data governance shall improve through:

- Data quality reviews.
- Steward feedback.
- Governance assessments.
- Analytics.
- Lessons learned.
- Responsible innovation.

Continuous improvement strengthens institutional knowledge and decision-making.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Charter governs the entire Data Canon.

All Data Frameworks, Data SOPs, analytics standards, metadata policies, knowledge management procedures, data quality standards, lifecycle procedures, and governance documents shall conform to this Charter unless superseded by the Constitution.
