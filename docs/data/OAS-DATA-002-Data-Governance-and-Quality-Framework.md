OAS-DATA-002 — Data Governance and Quality Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-DATA-002
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-DATA-001 — Data Governance Charter
Owner: Chief Data Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the constitutional model for governing data, assigning stewardship responsibilities, maintaining data quality, managing metadata, governing master data, and ensuring institutional accountability throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure all institutional data remains accurate, complete, trustworthy, constitutionally aligned, and capable of supporting sound stewardship and informed decision-making.

---

Mission

Data governance exists to transform institutional information into trusted knowledge through disciplined stewardship, measurable quality, responsible governance, and continuous improvement.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Data governance
- Data ownership
- Data stewardship
- Data quality
- Metadata management
- Master data management
- Reference data
- Data governance councils
- AI-assisted data operations
- Governance accountability

---

Guiding Principles

Data governance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote trustworthy information.
- Strengthen accountability.
- Respect privacy.
- Enable responsible innovation.
- Preserve institutional knowledge.

Data governance shall emphasize quality before quantity.

---

Data Authority

Data authority shall be exercised according to:

- The Constitution.
- Approved governance documents.
- Applicable law.
- Delegated authority.
- Stewardship responsibilities.

Authority shall remain transparent, documented, and accountable.

---

Data Ownership

Every significant data domain shall have:

- A designated data owner.
- Assigned data stewards.
- Clearly documented responsibilities.
- Defined quality expectations.
- Governance oversight.

Ownership shall remain traceable throughout the data lifecycle.

---

Data Quality

Data quality shall be evaluated using appropriate measures, including:

- Accuracy.
- Completeness.
- Consistency.
- Timeliness.
- Validity.
- Uniqueness.

Quality expectations shall be documented and periodically reviewed.

---

Metadata and Master Data

Metadata shall describe institutional information consistently.

Master data shall be governed to maintain:

- Consistency.
- Integrity.
- Reusability.
- Interoperability.
- Long-term stability.

---

AI-Assisted Data Operations

AI may assist through:

- Data quality monitoring.
- Metadata generation.
- Data classification.
- Duplicate detection.
- Quality reporting.
- Governance analytics.

Human stewards remain responsible for governance decisions, quality approvals, policy interpretation, and accountability.

---

Governance Reviews

Data governance shall periodically review:

- Data quality performance.
- Stewardship effectiveness.
- Governance maturity.
- Data risks.
- Metadata consistency.
- Improvement opportunities.

Reviews shall produce measurable institutional improvements.

---

Performance Measurement

Data governance shall evaluate:

- Data quality.
- Stewardship effectiveness.
- Governance maturity.
- Metadata completeness.
- Master data consistency.
- Information reliability.
- Mission support.
- Institutional learning.

Measurements shall strengthen institutional stewardship.

---

Continuous Improvement

Data governance shall improve through:

- Data quality assessments.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance reviews.
- Analytics.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens Aureus' institutional knowledge.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all data governance and data quality activities throughout Aureus.

All Data Frameworks, Data SOPs, metadata standards, master data procedures, analytics standards, knowledge management procedures, and governance documents shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Data Governance Charter.
