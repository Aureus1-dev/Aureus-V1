OAS-DATA-003 — Data Lifecycle and Knowledge Management Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-DATA-003
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-DATA-002 — Data Governance and Quality Framework
Owner: Chief Data Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the canonical model for governing the complete lifecycle of institutional data and knowledge throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure information remains accurate, discoverable, useful, secure, appropriately retained, and preserved as enduring institutional knowledge that advances Aureus' mission across generations.

---

Mission

Knowledge stewardship exists to transform institutional information into enduring organizational wisdom through disciplined lifecycle management, structured knowledge systems, responsible stewardship, and continuous learning.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Data lifecycle management
- Knowledge management
- Information architecture
- Data retention
- Data archival
- Institutional memory
- Analytics readiness
- Knowledge repositories
- AI-assisted knowledge systems
- Knowledge preservation

---

Guiding Principles

Knowledge management shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Strengthen institutional memory.
- Promote discoverability.
- Encourage responsible sharing.
- Respect privacy.
- Preserve long-term value.

Knowledge shall be organized for future stewardship rather than only present operations.

---

Data Lifecycle

Every institutional information asset shall follow an approved lifecycle including:

- Creation.
- Classification.
- Storage.
- Authorized use.
- Maintenance.
- Sharing where appropriate.
- Retention.
- Archival.
- Secure retirement.

Lifecycle decisions shall remain documented and governed.

---

Knowledge Architecture

Knowledge systems shall provide:

- Logical organization.
- Consistent metadata.
- Searchability.
- Cross-referencing.
- Version history.
- Long-term accessibility.

Knowledge architecture shall support institutional learning.

---

Institutional Memory

Institutional memory shall preserve:

- Governance history.
- Constitutional development.
- Operational lessons.
- Strategic decisions.
- Technical knowledge.
- Historical records.

Memory preservation strengthens continuity across generations.

---

Analytics Readiness

Institutional information shall remain structured to support:

- Reporting.
- Analytics.
- Decision support.
- Artificial intelligence.
- Institutional research.
- Continuous improvement.

---

AI-Assisted Knowledge Systems

AI may assist through:

- Knowledge organization.
- Metadata generation.
- Semantic search.
- Cross-reference analysis.
- Knowledge summaries.
- Discovery support.

Human stewards remain responsible for knowledge governance, publication decisions, constitutional interpretation, and institutional accountability.

---

Performance Measurement

Knowledge governance shall evaluate:

- Information discoverability.
- Knowledge quality.
- Metadata completeness.
- Repository usage.
- Retention compliance.
- Institutional learning.
- Analytics readiness.
- Mission support.

Measurements shall strengthen institutional stewardship.

---

Continuous Improvement

Knowledge governance shall improve through:

- Repository reviews.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance assessments.
- Knowledge audits.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens Aureus' institutional intelligence.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all data lifecycle management and knowledge management activities throughout Aureus.

All Data SOPs, retention standards, archival procedures, analytics governance documents, metadata standards, knowledge management procedures, and institutional memory systems shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Data Governance Charter.
