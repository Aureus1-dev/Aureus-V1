IC-018 — Incident Response Standard

Canonical Designation: IC-018

Title: Incident Response Standard

Status: Canonical

Authority: This document establishes the mandatory standards for detecting, responding to, resolving, communicating, and learning from operational incidents affecting the Aureus platform. It is subordinate to the OAS Series, the Product Constitution, the Architecture Decision Records (ADRs), the Implementation Decision Records (IDRs), and IC-001 through IC-017.

---

Article I — Purpose

The purpose of this Standard is to ensure that operational incidents are managed consistently, responsibly, and transparently.

The objectives of incident response are:

- Protect members.
- Protect data.
- Restore service safely.
- Preserve evidence where appropriate.
- Learn from every incident.
- Strengthen the platform after resolution.

---

Article II — Scope

This Standard applies to incidents affecting:

- Platform availability
- Security
- Data integrity
- Authentication
- Authorization
- Infrastructure
- APIs
- Background services
- Third-party integrations
- Artificial intelligence services
- Operational tooling

---

Article III — Incident Principles

Every incident response shall:

- Prioritize member safety and trust.
- Protect platform integrity.
- Be proportional to the severity of the incident.
- Be documented.
- Preserve institutional learning.

---

Article IV — Incident Classification

Incidents shall be classified according to operational impact.

Class I — Critical

- Severe platform disruption.
- Active security compromise.
- Significant data integrity risk.
- Widespread member impact.

Class II — High

- Major functionality unavailable.
- Significant degradation.
- Limited security exposure.

Class III — Moderate

- Partial service degradation.
- Isolated failures.
- Limited operational impact.

Class IV — Low

- Minor defects.
- Cosmetic issues.
- Low operational impact.

---

Article V — Response Objectives

The incident response process shall seek to:

1. Detect the incident.
2. Assess severity.
3. Stabilize affected systems.
4. Protect members and data.
5. Restore service.
6. Verify recovery.
7. Document findings.
8. Identify permanent corrective actions.

---

Article VI — Communication

Material incidents shall include appropriate communication to affected stakeholders.

Communications shall:

- Be accurate.
- Be timely.
- Avoid speculation.
- Distinguish confirmed facts from ongoing investigation.

---

Article VII — Investigation

Every significant incident shall include an investigation that identifies:

- What occurred.
- When it occurred.
- Impact.
- Root cause.
- Contributing factors.
- Corrective actions.
- Preventive actions.

The purpose of investigation is institutional learning and system improvement.

---

Article VIII — Corrective Actions

Corrective actions shall be implemented through the established Work Order and Change Control processes.

Permanent fixes shall be preferred over recurring manual intervention whenever reasonably practical.

---

Article IX — Post-Incident Review

Following resolution, a Post-Incident Review shall evaluate:

- Response effectiveness.
- Recovery effectiveness.
- Communication effectiveness.
- Documentation completeness.
- Opportunities for improvement.

Recommendations shall be documented and tracked.

---

Article X — Continuous Operational Resilience

Incident response is a continuing stewardship responsibility.

Every resolved incident should strengthen the reliability, resilience, and trustworthiness of the Aureus platform.

This Standard shall evolve only through approved amendments consistent with the governing implementation documents.
