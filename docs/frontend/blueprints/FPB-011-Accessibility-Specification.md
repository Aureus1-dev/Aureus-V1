FPB-011 — Accessibility Specification

Status: Production Blueprint
Version: 1.0
Authority: Accessibility Engineering Standard

---

1. Purpose

This document defines the accessibility requirements for every Aureus frontend experience.

Accessibility is a foundational design requirement that ensures every member can participate with dignity, independence, and confidence.

---

2. Guiding Principle

Accessibility is not a feature.

Accessibility is hospitality.

Every member deserves equal access to the opportunities, guidance, education, and services provided by Aureus.

---

3. Objectives

The Aureus frontend shall provide:

- Perceivable experiences
- Operable interfaces
- Understandable interactions
- Robust implementations
- Consistent navigation
- Inclusive communication

Accessibility shall be incorporated throughout the design and engineering process.

---

4. Visual Accessibility

The frontend shall support:

- High color contrast
- Scalable text
- Responsive layouts
- Clear typography
- Distinct focus indicators
- Color-independent meaning

No critical information shall rely solely on color.

---

5. Motor Accessibility

Members shall be able to navigate using:

- Touch
- Keyboard
- Switch devices (where supported)
- Assistive technologies

Touch targets shall be appropriately sized and spaced.

---

6. Auditory Accessibility

Every audio experience shall provide equivalent alternatives whenever practical.

Voice interactions should be complemented by visual or textual representations.

Members shall not be excluded from essential functionality because of hearing limitations.

---

7. Cognitive Accessibility

Interfaces shall minimize cognitive burden.

The frontend should:

- use plain language where appropriate,
- avoid unnecessary complexity,
- present information progressively,
- preserve context,
- and clearly explain important decisions.

Members should never feel overwhelmed.

---

8. Motion Accessibility

Members who prefer reduced motion shall receive an equally complete experience.

Animations shall never be required to understand or complete a task.

---

9. Conversation Accessibility

Conversation shall remain accessible across multiple interaction methods.

Members should be able to:

- speak,
- type,
- review previous interactions,
- request clarification,
- and revisit explanations.

Conversation should adapt to member needs rather than requiring members to adapt to Aureus.

---

10. Testing

Accessibility shall be verified through:

- automated testing,
- manual testing,
- assistive technology testing,
- keyboard-only testing,
- and user validation whenever practical.

Accessibility shall be continuously maintained.

---

11. Constitutional Test

Every feature shall answer:

- Can every member reasonably use this?
- Does this preserve dignity?
- Does this reduce barriers?
- Does this communicate clearly?
- Does this strengthen inclusion?

If not, redesign the experience before release.
