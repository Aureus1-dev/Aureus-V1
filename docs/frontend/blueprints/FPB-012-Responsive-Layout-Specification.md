FPB-012 — Responsive Layout Specification

Status: Production Blueprint
Version: 1.0
Authority: Responsive Experience Standard

---

1. Purpose

This document defines how Aureus adapts its interface across different screen sizes, input methods, orientations, and devices.

The objective is to preserve one continuous member experience regardless of platform.

---

2. Guiding Principle

Members should learn Aureus once.

Whether using a phone, tablet, desktop, foldable device, or future platform, the experience should remain familiar, predictable, and conversation-first.

Layouts may adapt.

The institutional experience shall remain consistent.

---

3. Supported Platforms

The frontend shall support:

- Mobile phones
- Tablets
- Desktop browsers
- Laptop computers
- Foldable devices
- Large displays
- Future approved platforms

Responsive behavior shall be planned from the beginning rather than added later.

---

4. Conversation Priority

Conversation remains the primary interface on every platform.

Larger displays may reveal additional supporting information, but conversation remains the central organizing experience.

Visual complexity shall never replace conversational clarity.

---

5. Adaptive Layout

Layouts shall adapt based on:

- Screen size
- Screen orientation
- Input method
- Accessibility preferences
- Available space

Adaptation should improve usability without changing the member's mental model of the application.

---

6. Progressive Enhancement

Larger devices may provide additional capabilities such as:

- Side-by-side comparisons
- Multiple document views
- Expanded timelines
- Richer dashboards
- Simultaneous conversation and workspace panels

These enhancements shall improve productivity without introducing unnecessary complexity.

---

7. Navigation

Navigation shall remain consistent across platforms.

Members should always understand:

- where they are,
- what they are viewing,
- how to continue,
- and how to return to the conversation.

Navigation patterns should scale naturally across devices.

---

8. Performance

Responsive behavior shall prioritize:

- Fast loading
- Efficient rendering
- Minimal layout shifts
- Smooth interaction
- Battery efficiency on mobile devices

Performance is part of the member experience.

---

9. Future Readiness

The responsive architecture shall support future interface technologies without requiring fundamental redesign of Aureus's interaction philosophy.

Institutional consistency should outlast individual device categories.

---

10. Constitutional Test

Every responsive design decision shall answer:

- Does this preserve the member experience?
- Does conversation remain primary?
- Does the layout strengthen understanding?
- Does this reduce friction?
- Does this preserve accessibility?

If not, redesign the layout.
