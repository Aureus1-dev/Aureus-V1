FPB-003 — Member Journey Flows

Status: Production Blueprint
Version: 1.0
Authority: Member Experience Flow Specification

---

1. Purpose

This document defines the complete lifecycle of a member's experience within Aureus.

It specifies how members move through the institution from first discovery to long-term stewardship.

Every journey shall reinforce trust, dignity, hope, capability, and flourishing.

---

2. Journey Philosophy

Members do not move through Aureus by completing screens.

Members move through Aureus by making meaningful progress.

Every interaction should advance the member's journey rather than merely complete a workflow.

---

3. Primary Journey Stages

The canonical member journey consists of:

1. Discovery
2. Welcome
3. Trust Building
4. Understanding
5. First Victory
6. Opportunity Discovery
7. Growth
8. Mastery
9. Contribution
10. Stewardship
11. Legacy

Members may revisit earlier stages as life circumstances change.

The journey is continuous rather than strictly linear.

---

4. Welcome Flow

The welcome experience shall:

- establish emotional safety,
- introduce the steward,
- explain the relationship,
- avoid overwhelming onboarding,
- and invite the member into conversation.

The first interaction focuses on the member's immediate need.

---

5. Trust Progression

Aureus earns trust through demonstrated value.

Requests for additional information, permissions, or connected accounts occur only after members understand their benefit.

Trust precedes access.

---

6. Immediate Problem Flow

Every new member begins by addressing the concern that brought them to Aureus.

The steward shall:

- listen,
- understand,
- reflect,
- recommend,
- prepare,
- and guide the member toward one meaningful next step.

---

7. Opportunity Expansion

As trust develops, Aureus gradually broadens the member's awareness.

Relevant opportunities, programs, education, community, and resources are introduced naturally within conversation.

Members are never overwhelmed with unnecessary information.

---

8. Automation Flow

Administrative work should be prepared whenever practical.

Before any meaningful action:

- Aureus explains,
- the member participates,
- the member reviews,
- the member authorizes.

Automation shall increase understanding rather than replace it.

---

9. Long-Term Growth

The steward continuously adapts as the member grows.

Over time, Aureus transitions from solving immediate problems to supporting long-term flourishing, contribution, and stewardship.

The relationship evolves alongside the member.

---

10. Journey Continuity

Members should never feel like they are starting over.

Aureus preserves context, remembers progress, and resumes meaningful work naturally.

Every return begins where the previous journey paused.

---

11. Success Criteria

Successful journeys produce:

- stronger understanding,
- greater capability,
- increased confidence,
- meaningful progress,
- and deeper trust.

The objective is transformation rather than engagement.

---

12. Constitutional Test

Every journey shall answer:

- Does this strengthen the member?
- Does this build trust?
- Does this reduce unnecessary burden?
- Does this increase capability?
- Does this help the member flourish?

If not, the journey shall be redesigned.
