FPB-007 — Motion & Animation Specification

Status: Production Blueprint
Version: 1.0
Authority: Motion & Interaction Standard

---

1. Purpose

This document defines how motion and animation are used throughout Aureus.

Motion exists to communicate state, orientation, continuity, confidence, and completion.

Animation shall never exist solely for decoration.

---

2. Design Philosophy

Motion should make Aureus feel:

- Calm
- Intentional
- Predictable
- Trustworthy
- Responsive
- Human

Members should always understand why something moved.

---

3. Motion Principles

Every animation must serve one or more of the following purposes:

- Orient the member
- Confirm an action
- Guide attention
- Explain a transition
- Reveal information progressively
- Reduce cognitive effort

If an animation serves none of these purposes, it should not exist.

---

4. Conversation-Led Motion

Conversation remains the primary experience.

When Aureus opens a document, comparison, timeline, or opportunity, visual transitions should feel like a natural extension of the conversation.

The interface follows the dialogue rather than interrupting it.

---

5. State Transitions

Transitions between screens, views, and tasks shall preserve context.

Members should never feel as though they have been abruptly transported to a different application or workflow.

Motion should reinforce continuity.

---

6. Feedback Animations

Animations may acknowledge:

- successful completion,
- progress,
- preparation,
- synchronization,
- approvals,
- uploads,
- and saved work.

Feedback should be subtle, informative, and reassuring.

---

7. Reduced Motion

Members who prefer reduced motion shall receive an equally complete experience.

Alternative transitions shall preserve clarity without relying on animation.

Accessibility preferences always take precedence.

---

8. Performance

Motion shall never degrade responsiveness.

Animations should feel smooth and immediate while preserving battery life and device performance.

When necessary, performance takes priority over visual complexity.

---

9. Institutional Character

The movement of Aureus should reflect the institution itself.

It should never feel hurried, frantic, playful, or manipulative.

It should feel measured, confident, welcoming, and calm.

---

10. Constitutional Test

Before introducing any animation, ask:

- Does this help the member understand?
- Does this reduce uncertainty?
- Does this preserve context?
- Does this reinforce trust?
- Could the experience be clearer without it?

If the answer is no, remove or redesign the animation.
