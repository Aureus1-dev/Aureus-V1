AFX-004 — Visual Design System Canon

Status: Canonical
Version: 1.0
Authority: Governing Visual Design Standard

---

1. Purpose

This canon defines the visual language of Aureus.

Every interface, component, animation, layout, icon, and interaction shall reinforce trust, dignity, clarity, accessibility, and stewardship.

Visual design exists to support the member—not compete for their attention.

---

2. Design Philosophy

Aureus is a trusted institution.

It shall not resemble:

- social media,
- advertising platforms,
- gaming interfaces,
- engagement-driven products,
- or speculative financial applications.

Its appearance should communicate:

- Trust
- Calm
- Competence
- Wisdom
- Hospitality
- Stability
- Hope

---

3. Simplicity

Every screen shall have a clear purpose.

Visual clutter shall be minimized.

Members should immediately understand what matters most and what action, if any, is expected.

---

4. Conversation Leads

Conversation is the primary interface.

Visual elements appear only when they improve understanding or support action.

The interface follows the member's journey through the conversation.

---

5. Motion

Motion shall communicate meaning.

Animations should:

- orient,
- reassure,
- confirm,
- and guide.

Animation shall never distract or manipulate.

---

6. Typography

Typography shall prioritize readability.

Text hierarchy should clearly distinguish:

- guidance,
- explanation,
- actions,
- supporting information,
- and status.

Members should never struggle to understand important information.

---

7. Color

Color communicates purpose.

It shall never be used solely to attract attention.

Colors should reinforce:

- safety,
- progress,
- caution,
- completion,
- opportunity,
- and clarity.

Accessibility standards shall always be maintained.

---

8. Components

Every reusable component shall exhibit consistent behavior.

Buttons, forms, cards, dialogs, timelines, comparisons, and notifications should feel like parts of one coherent institution.

Consistency strengthens trust.

---

9. Accessibility

Accessibility is a foundational requirement.

Interfaces shall be usable by members with diverse visual, auditory, motor, and cognitive needs.

Accessibility is not an enhancement.

It is part of serving every member with dignity.

---

10. Progressive Disclosure

Complexity shall appear gradually.

Members see only the information necessary for their current decision or task.

Additional detail is available when requested.

---

11. Institutional Character

Every visual element should reinforce the feeling that Aureus is:

- dependable,
- welcoming,
- transparent,
- and professionally stewarded.

The interface should feel timeless rather than trendy.

---

12. Constitutional Standard

Every visual decision shall answer:

Does this make it easier for the member to understand, trust, and make progress?

If not, it should be redesigned or removed.
