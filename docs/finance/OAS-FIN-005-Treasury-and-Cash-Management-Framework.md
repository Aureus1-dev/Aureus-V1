OAS-FIN-005 — Treasury and Cash Management Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-005
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-FIN-002 — Financial Governance Framework
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the canonical model for treasury management, cash stewardship, liquidity planning, banking relationships, reserve management, payment processing, and financial resilience throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure institutional financial assets remain secure, available, responsibly managed, and aligned with the Constitution and long-term mission of Aureus.

---

Mission

Treasury management exists to safeguard institutional resources, maintain sufficient liquidity, preserve financial stability, and support sustainable mission execution.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Treasury operations
- Cash management
- Banking relationships
- Liquidity management
- Payment processing
- Reserve management
- Investment of surplus funds
- Treasury risk management
- AI-assisted cash forecasting
- Financial resilience

---

Guiding Principles

Treasury management shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect institutional assets.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain liquidity.
- Promote prudent stewardship.
- Minimize financial risk.
- Preserve transparency.
- Support long-term sustainability.

Liquidity is a strategic institutional asset.

---

Treasury Governance

Treasury activities shall include:

- Documented authority.
- Segregation of duties.
- Internal controls.
- Secure banking relationships.
- Periodic reconciliation.
- Financial oversight.

Treasury governance protects institutional resources.

---

Cash Management

Cash management shall ensure:

- Timely receipt of funds.
- Secure custody.
- Efficient disbursement.
- Accurate reconciliation.
- Appropriate liquidity.
- Ongoing monitoring.

Cash shall remain available for approved institutional purposes.

---

Liquidity Management

Liquidity planning shall evaluate:

- Operating requirements.
- Reserve requirements.
- Forecasted obligations.
- Revenue timing.
- Economic conditions.
- Strategic initiatives.

Liquidity shall support both stability and flexibility.

---

Reserve Management

Institutional reserves shall:

- Protect operational continuity.
- Support emergency response.
- Strengthen long-term sustainability.
- Be managed according to approved financial policies.

Reserve utilization shall require appropriate governance approval.

---

Banking Relationships

Approved financial institutions shall be selected according to:

- Financial stability.
- Regulatory compliance.
- Security.
- Operational capability.
- Service quality.
- Institutional alignment.

Banking relationships shall be reviewed periodically.

---

Treasury Risk Management

Treasury risks shall be:

- Identified.
- Assessed.
- Mitigated.
- Monitored.
- Reviewed.

Risk management protects institutional resilience.

---

AI-Assisted Treasury Operations

AI may assist through:

- Cash flow forecasting.
- Liquidity analysis.
- Risk monitoring.
- Reconciliation support.
- Financial reporting.
- Treasury trend analysis.

Human stewards remain responsible for approvals, banking decisions, investments, and fiduciary oversight.

---

Performance Measurement

Treasury effectiveness shall evaluate:

- Liquidity levels.
- Cash forecast accuracy.
- Reserve adequacy.
- Payment efficiency.
- Banking performance.
- Treasury risk exposure.
- Financial resilience.

Measurements shall support prudent stewardship.

---

Continuous Improvement

Treasury management shall improve through:

- Financial reviews.
- Treasury analyses.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted forecasting.
- Audit findings.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens long-term financial stability.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all treasury and cash management activities throughout Aureus.

All Finance SOPs, treasury procedures, banking standards, reserve policies, payment procedures, investment guidelines, and financial control documents shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Finance Charter.
