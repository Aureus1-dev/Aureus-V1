OAS-FIN-107 — Financial Compliance, Audit, and Fraud Response Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-107
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-FIN-008 — Financial Compliance and Audit Framework
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for monitoring financial compliance, conducting audits, responding to suspected fraud, coordinating regulatory reviews, implementing corrective actions, and continuously strengthening financial governance throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to preserve financial integrity, protect institutional assets, ensure accountability, and maintain public confidence in Aureus' financial stewardship.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Financial compliance monitoring
- Internal financial audits
- External audit coordination
- Fraud reporting
- Fraud response
- Fraud investigation support
- Regulatory compliance
- Corrective actions
- Audit follow-up
- Financial governance improvement

---

Objectives

Financial oversight shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Detect and reduce financial risk.
- Strengthen accountability.
- Promote transparency.
- Support regulatory compliance.
- Improve continuously.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Financial Compliance Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring compliance.
- Coordinating audits.
- Maintaining compliance records.
- Tracking corrective actions.
- Preparing compliance reports.

---

Audit Lead

Responsible for:

- Planning audits.
- Coordinating audit activities.
- Reviewing evidence.
- Preparing audit findings.
- Confirming follow-up actions.

---

AI Financial Oversight Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring financial controls.
- Detecting unusual transactions.
- Supporting audit preparation.
- Tracking corrective actions.
- Identifying compliance trends.
- Preparing governance summaries.

Human stewards remain responsible for audit conclusions, fraud determinations, regulatory communications, and governance decisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before beginning an audit or compliance review:

- Scope is defined.
- Applicable requirements are identified.
- Required documentation is available.
- Responsible stewards are notified.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Compliance Monitoring

Review compliance with:

- The Constitution.
- Finance policies.
- Finance Frameworks.
- Finance SOPs.
- Applicable legal requirements.
- Internal financial controls.

---

Step 2 — Audit Planning

Define:

- Audit objectives.
- Scope.
- Timeline.
- Required evidence.
- Participants.
- Reporting expectations.

---

Step 3 — Audit Execution

Collect and review:

- Financial records.
- Accounting documentation.
- Treasury reports.
- Procurement records.
- Budget reports.
- Internal control evidence.

Document all significant findings.

---

Step 4 — Fraud Reporting

Any suspected fraud shall be:

- Reported immediately.
- Documented.
- Preserved confidentially.
- Escalated to the appropriate governance authority.

Good-faith reporting shall be protected.

---

Step 5 — Investigation Support

Support investigations by:

- Preserving evidence.
- Securing records.
- Limiting unauthorized access.
- Cooperating with authorized investigators.
- Maintaining confidentiality.

---

Step 6 — Corrective Actions

For each confirmed finding:

- Identify the root cause.
- Assign responsibility.
- Establish corrective actions.
- Set completion dates.
- Verify implementation.

---

Step 7 — Audit Closure

Confirm:

- Findings are documented.
- Corrective actions are assigned.
- Follow-up reviews are scheduled.
- Required reports are completed.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Compliance rates.
- Audit completion.
- Corrective action completion.
- Repeat findings.
- Fraud detection effectiveness.
- Reporting timeliness.
- Control effectiveness.
- Governance maturity.

---

Exceptions

Emergency financial circumstances may require expedited review procedures while preserving evidence, documentation, and subsequent governance review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Audit plans.
- Compliance reports.
- Audit findings.
- Fraud reports.
- Investigation records.
- Corrective action logs.
- Follow-up reviews.
- Governance summaries.

---

Continuous Improvement

Financial oversight shall improve through audit findings, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance reviews, regulatory developments, and lessons learned.
