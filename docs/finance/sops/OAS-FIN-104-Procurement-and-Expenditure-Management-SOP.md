OAS-FIN-104 — Procurement and Expenditure Management Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-104
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-FIN-002 — Financial Governance Framework
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for planning, requesting, approving, purchasing, receiving, documenting, paying for, and reviewing all institutional expenditures made by Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure every expenditure supports the mission, protects institutional resources, complies with financial governance, and demonstrates responsible stewardship.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Procurement
- Vendor management
- Purchasing
- Purchase requests
- Purchase approvals
- Contracted services
- Expense reimbursements
- Invoice processing
- Payment authorization
- Expenditure reporting

---

Objectives

Procurement and expenditures shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect institutional assets.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote responsible stewardship.
- Ensure financial accountability.
- Encourage fair vendor practices.
- Maintain accurate documentation.
- Support long-term sustainability.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Procurement Steward

Responsible for:

- Coordinating procurement activities.
- Managing vendor relationships.
- Reviewing purchasing documentation.
- Confirming procurement compliance.
- Maintaining procurement records.

---

Financial Approving Steward

Responsible for:

- Reviewing expenditure requests.
- Confirming budget availability.
- Approving expenditures within delegated authority.
- Monitoring spending limits.

---

AI Procurement Steward

Responsible for:

- Tracking purchase requests.
- Monitoring budgets.
- Comparing vendor information.
- Identifying unusual spending patterns.
- Preparing procurement reports.
- Supporting expenditure forecasting.

Human stewards remain responsible for vendor selection, approvals, contractual commitments, and financial judgment.

---

Prerequisites

Before authorizing an expenditure:

- A legitimate operational need is identified.
- Budget availability is confirmed.
- Required approvals are obtained.
- Procurement policies are satisfied.
- Supporting documentation is complete.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Purchase Request

Document:

- Business purpose.
- Requested goods or services.
- Estimated cost.
- Budget category.
- Required delivery timeline.
- Requesting steward.

---

Step 2 — Budget Verification

Confirm:

- Funding availability.
- Budget alignment.
- Spending authority.
- Financial sustainability.

---

Step 3 — Vendor Selection

Evaluate vendors according to:

- Quality.
- Cost.
- Reliability.
- Service capability.
- Ethical standards.
- Long-term value.

Vendor selection should support responsible stewardship rather than lowest cost alone.

---

Step 4 — Approval

Obtain all required approvals before:

- Placing orders.
- Executing contracts.
- Making commitments.
- Authorizing payments.

No unauthorized financial commitments shall be made on behalf of Aureus.

---

Step 5 — Receipt and Verification

Upon delivery:

- Verify receipt.
- Confirm quality.
- Review invoices.
- Resolve discrepancies.
- Document acceptance.

---

Step 6 — Payment Processing

Process payment after confirming:

- Goods or services were received.
- Documentation is complete.
- Approvals are recorded.
- Payment terms are satisfied.

---

Step 7 — Recordkeeping

Maintain complete records of:

- Purchase requests.
- Approvals.
- Vendor information.
- Contracts.
- Invoices.
- Payments.
- Supporting documentation.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Procurement cycle time.
- Budget compliance.
- Vendor performance.
- Procurement accuracy.
- Invoice processing time.
- Payment timeliness.
- Spending efficiency.
- Procurement compliance.

---

Exceptions

Emergency procurement may follow expedited approval procedures established by financial governance while maintaining complete post-event documentation and review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Purchase requests.
- Vendor records.
- Approval records.
- Contracts.
- Invoices.
- Payment records.
- Procurement reports.
- Audit documentation.

---

Continuous Improvement

Procurement practices shall improve through financial reviews, vendor evaluations, audit findings, steward feedback, AI-assisted analysis, and lessons learned.
