OAS-FIN-102 — Revenue Collection and Revenue Recognition Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-102
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-FIN-004 — Revenue and Income Management Framework
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for collecting, recording, recognizing, reconciling, refunding, and reporting all institutional revenue received by Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure every revenue transaction is accurate, transparent, constitutionally aligned, and properly reflected in the institution's financial records.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Membership subscriptions
- Affiliate income
- Donations
- Grants
- Earned service revenue
- Sponsorship revenue
- Payment processing
- Revenue recognition
- Deferred revenue
- Refunds
- Revenue reconciliation

---

Objectives

Revenue operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Ensure financial accuracy.
- Promote transparency.
- Maintain accountability.
- Support financial sustainability.
- Strengthen institutional trust.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Revenue Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring incoming revenue.
- Reviewing payment activity.
- Recording transactions.
- Coordinating reconciliations.
- Supporting financial reporting.

---

Department Stewards

Responsible for:

- Verifying revenue related to their operational areas.
- Reporting discrepancies.
- Supporting documentation requirements.

---

AI Revenue Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring payment activity.
- Detecting anomalies.
- Supporting reconciliations.
- Preparing revenue summaries.
- Forecasting trends.
- Identifying reporting exceptions.

Human stewards remain responsible for revenue approval, accounting judgments, and financial governance.

---

Prerequisites

Before recognizing revenue:

- Payment has been verified.
- Required documentation is complete.
- Revenue source is identified.
- Recognition criteria are satisfied.
- Applicable approvals are obtained.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Revenue Receipt

Receive and verify:

- Payment source.
- Amount received.
- Transaction date.
- Supporting documentation.
- Member or payer information where appropriate.

---

Step 2 — Payment Verification

Confirm:

- Payment authorization.
- Successful settlement.
- Absence of duplicate transactions.
- Appropriate revenue classification.

---

Step 3 — Revenue Recording

Record:

- Revenue category.
- General ledger account.
- Supporting documentation.
- Applicable restrictions.
- Related contractual obligations.

Every transaction shall remain traceable.

---

Step 4 — Revenue Recognition

Recognize revenue according to approved financial policies, considering:

- Earned revenue.
- Deferred revenue.
- Restricted funds.
- Grant conditions.
- Subscription periods.

Recognition shall reflect the economic substance of the transaction.

---

Step 5 — Reconciliation

Perform periodic reconciliation between:

- Payment processors.
- Banking records.
- Accounting records.
- Revenue reports.

Resolve discrepancies promptly.

---

Step 6 — Refund Management

When refunds are appropriate:

- Verify eligibility.
- Obtain required approvals.
- Process the refund.
- Update financial records.
- Notify affected parties where appropriate.

---

Step 7 — Reporting

Prepare revenue reports including:

- Revenue by source.
- Deferred revenue balances.
- Refund activity.
- Collection trends.
- Revenue variances.
- Forecast updates.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Collection accuracy.
- Reconciliation completion.
- Revenue recognition accuracy.
- Refund processing time.
- Reporting timeliness.
- Forecast accuracy.
- Revenue growth.
- Revenue integrity.

---

Exceptions

Exceptional revenue transactions may require specialized accounting treatment or governance review before recognition.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Payment records.
- Revenue journals.
- Reconciliation reports.
- Refund records.
- Supporting documentation.
- Revenue summaries.
- Audit evidence.

---

Continuous Improvement

Revenue operations shall improve through financial reviews, audit findings, steward feedback, AI-assisted analysis, reconciliation reviews, and lessons learned.
