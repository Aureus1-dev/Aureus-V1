OAS-FIN-103 — Audit and Financial Assurance Operations Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-103
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-FIN-004 — Audit, Risk, and Financial Assurance Framework
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for conducting internal audits, financial reviews, internal control testing, fraud prevention and detection, regulatory reporting, corrective actions, and continuous financial assurance throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to verify the integrity of financial stewardship, strengthen accountability, reduce financial risk, protect entrusted resources, and preserve institutional confidence.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Internal audits
- Financial reviews
- Internal control testing
- Fraud prevention
- Fraud investigations
- Financial risk assessments
- Regulatory reporting
- Corrective action management
- Audit documentation
- Financial assurance reporting

---

Objectives

Financial assurance operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Strengthen accountability.
- Detect and reduce financial risk.
- Improve internal controls.
- Promote transparency.
- Support continuous improvement.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Audit Steward

Responsible for:

- Coordinating audit activities.
- Maintaining audit documentation.
- Preparing audit reports.
- Tracking corrective actions.
- Supporting governance reviews.

---

Financial Control Steward

Responsible for:

- Testing internal controls.
- Monitoring compliance.
- Supporting financial reviews.
- Reporting control deficiencies.
- Recommending improvements.

---

AI Financial Assurance Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring transaction patterns.
- Detecting anomalies.
- Supporting audit preparation.
- Tracking corrective actions.
- Preparing audit summaries.
- Producing risk analytics.

Human stewards remain responsible for audit conclusions, fraud investigations, regulatory reporting, corrective action approvals, and governance decisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before beginning assurance activities:

- Audit scope is defined.
- Objectives are documented.
- Required records are available.
- Responsible stewards are assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Audit Planning

Document:

- Audit objectives.
- Scope.
- Risks.
- Evaluation criteria.
- Required evidence.
- Audit schedule.

---

Step 2 — Evidence Collection

Collect and evaluate:

- Financial records.
- Supporting documentation.
- Internal control evidence.
- Transaction samples.
- Compliance records.
- Governance documentation.

---

Step 3 — Internal Control Testing

Evaluate controls for:

- Authorization.
- Accuracy.
- Segregation of duties.
- Asset protection.
- Documentation.
- Monitoring effectiveness.

Document identified deficiencies.

---

Step 4 — Findings and Reporting

Prepare audit reports including:

- Findings.
- Risk assessments.
- Supporting evidence.
- Recommendations.
- Required corrective actions.

Reports shall remain objective, evidence-based, and clearly documented.

---

Step 5 — Corrective Actions

For each approved corrective action:

- Assign responsibility.
- Establish timelines.
- Monitor implementation.
- Verify completion.
- Document outcomes.

---

Step 6 — Governance Review

Present significant findings to the appropriate governance authority.

Review:

- Control effectiveness.
- Risk exposure.
- Fraud indicators.
- Improvement opportunities.
- Institutional impacts.

---

Step 7 — Continuous Assurance

Continuously improve assurance activities through:

- Audit trend analysis.
- Steward feedback.
- Risk reassessment.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance evaluations.
- Lessons learned.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Audit completion.
- Internal control effectiveness.
- Corrective action completion.
- Financial risk reduction.
- Fraud detection effectiveness.
- Regulatory compliance.
- Reporting quality.
- Governance maturity.

---

Exceptions

Emergency financial investigations may require expedited procedures while maintaining documentation, evidence preservation, and retrospective governance review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Audit plans.
- Working papers.
- Audit reports.
- Internal control assessments.
- Corrective action records.
- Risk assessments.
- Governance reports.
- Assurance summaries.

---

Continuous Improvement

Financial assurance operations shall improve through audit reviews, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance assessments, operational evaluations, and lessons learned.
