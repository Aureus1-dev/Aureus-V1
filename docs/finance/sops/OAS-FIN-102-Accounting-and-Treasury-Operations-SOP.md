OAS-FIN-102 — Accounting and Treasury Operations Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-102
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-FIN-003 — Budgeting, Accounting, and Treasury Framework
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for accounting, treasury management, cash management, banking, reconciliations, payment processing, reserve management, financial reporting, and financial recordkeeping throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure financial transactions are accurately recorded, institutional resources are safeguarded, financial obligations are fulfilled responsibly, and reliable financial information is available for stewardship and governance.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Accounting
- Treasury management
- Cash management
- Banking
- Accounts payable
- Accounts receivable
- Financial reconciliations
- Financial reporting
- Reserve management
- Financial records

---

Objectives

Financial operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain accurate financial records.
- Safeguard institutional resources.
- Promote transparency.
- Support informed decision-making.
- Strengthen fiscal sustainability.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Treasury Steward

Responsible for:

- Managing cash.
- Coordinating banking activities.
- Processing payments.
- Maintaining treasury records.
- Preparing treasury reports.

---

Accounting Steward

Responsible for:

- Recording financial transactions.
- Maintaining the general ledger.
- Reconciling accounts.
- Preparing financial statements.
- Supporting audit readiness.

---

AI Financial Operations Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring transactions.
- Detecting anomalies.
- Assisting reconciliations.
- Supporting cash forecasting.
- Preparing financial summaries.
- Tracking operational metrics.

Human stewards remain responsible for financial approvals, accounting judgments, treasury decisions, and governance actions.

---

Prerequisites

Before conducting financial operations:

- Authorized accounts are established.
- Financial controls are implemented.
- Banking relationships are documented.
- Stewardship responsibilities are assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Transaction Processing

Record financial transactions accurately and promptly.

Verify:

- Authorization.
- Supporting documentation.
- Correct account classification.
- Required approvals.

---

Step 2 — Treasury Management

Manage:

- Cash balances.
- Banking activities.
- Liquidity.
- Payment schedules.
- Reserve allocations.
- Financial obligations.

Maintain appropriate safeguards.

---

Step 3 — Reconciliation

Perform regular reconciliations for:

- Bank accounts.
- Cash balances.
- Ledger accounts.
- Receivables.
- Payables.
- Reserve accounts.

Investigate material discrepancies promptly.

---

Step 4 — Financial Reporting

Prepare financial reports including:

- Balance sheets.
- Income statements.
- Cash flow reports.
- Budget comparisons.
- Treasury summaries.
- Reserve reports.

---

Step 5 — Reserve Management

Monitor reserve funds to ensure:

- Appropriate funding.
- Approved usage.
- Accurate accounting.
- Long-term sustainability.

---

Step 6 — Governance Review

Conduct periodic reviews to evaluate:

- Accounting accuracy.
- Treasury effectiveness.
- Liquidity.
- Financial controls.
- Reporting quality.
- Improvement opportunities.

---

Step 7 — Continuous Operations

Continuously improve:

- Accounting processes.
- Treasury procedures.
- Financial reporting.
- Internal controls.
- Operational efficiency.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Reconciliation accuracy.
- Reporting timeliness.
- Cash management effectiveness.
- Liquidity.
- Accounting accuracy.
- Reserve performance.
- Governance compliance.
- Mission support.

---

Exceptions

Emergency financial transactions may follow expedited procedures while maintaining appropriate documentation and retrospective governance review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- General ledger.
- Bank records.
- Reconciliation reports.
- Treasury reports.
- Financial statements.
- Reserve records.
- Payment records.
- Governance reports.

---

Continuous Improvement

Financial operations shall improve through financial reviews, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance assessments, operational evaluations, and lessons learned.
