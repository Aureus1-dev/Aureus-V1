OAS-FIN-103 — Treasury Operations and Cash Management Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-103
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-FIN-005 — Treasury and Cash Management Framework
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for managing Aureus' treasury operations, including cash receipts, cash disbursements, banking activities, liquidity management, reserve oversight, reconciliations, and treasury reporting.

Its purpose is to safeguard institutional financial assets, maintain operational liquidity, strengthen financial resilience, and support responsible stewardship.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Cash receipts
- Cash disbursements
- Banking operations
- Treasury account management
- Liquidity monitoring
- Reserve management
- Cash forecasting
- Treasury reconciliations
- Payment approvals
- Treasury reporting

---

Objectives

Treasury operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect institutional assets.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain liquidity.
- Promote financial accuracy.
- Strengthen accountability.
- Reduce treasury risk.
- Support long-term sustainability.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Treasury Steward

Responsible for:

- Managing treasury activities.
- Monitoring cash balances.
- Coordinating banking operations.
- Preparing treasury reports.
- Supporting reconciliations.

---

Financial Approving Steward

Responsible for:

- Reviewing significant disbursements.
- Authorizing payments within delegated authority.
- Confirming compliance with financial policies.

---

AI Treasury Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring liquidity.
- Forecasting cash flow.
- Detecting unusual transactions.
- Supporting reconciliations.
- Producing treasury summaries.
- Identifying financial trends.

Human stewards remain responsible for banking decisions, payment approvals, reserve utilization, and fiduciary oversight.

---

Prerequisites

Before processing treasury transactions:

- Required approvals are obtained.
- Supporting documentation is complete.
- Available funds are confirmed.
- Financial policies are satisfied.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Cash Receipts

Verify and record:

- Incoming payments.
- Deposit confirmations.
- Revenue classifications.
- Supporting documentation.

Deposit funds promptly into approved financial accounts.

---

Step 2 — Cash Disbursements

Before disbursement:

- Verify authorization.
- Confirm supporting documentation.
- Validate available funds.
- Review payment details.

Execute payments through approved treasury channels.

---

Step 3 — Banking Operations

Maintain:

- Authorized bank accounts.
- Secure banking credentials.
- Periodic account reviews.
- Banking relationship documentation.

---

Step 4 — Liquidity Monitoring

Monitor:

- Cash balances.
- Expected inflows.
- Planned outflows.
- Reserve levels.
- Forecasted liquidity.

Address potential shortages before they occur.

---

Step 5 — Reserve Management

Review:

- Reserve balances.
- Approved reserve targets.
- Authorized reserve usage.
- Replenishment plans.

Reserve utilization shall follow approved governance procedures.

---

Step 6 — Reconciliation

Reconcile:

- Bank accounts.
- Treasury records.
- Accounting records.
- Payment processors.

Investigate discrepancies promptly.

---

Step 7 — Treasury Reporting

Prepare reports covering:

- Cash position.
- Liquidity forecasts.
- Reserve status.
- Banking activity.
- Treasury performance.
- Significant financial risks.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Cash forecast accuracy.
- Reconciliation completion.
- Liquidity adequacy.
- Payment accuracy.
- Banking performance.
- Treasury reporting timeliness.
- Reserve compliance.
- Treasury risk exposure.

---

Exceptions

Emergency treasury actions may be authorized under approved emergency financial governance procedures and documented immediately afterward.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Bank statements.
- Treasury journals.
- Payment records.
- Cash forecasts.
- Liquidity reports.
- Reserve reports.
- Reconciliation records.
- Treasury approvals.

---

Continuous Improvement

Treasury operations shall improve through financial reviews, audit findings, steward feedback, AI-assisted analysis, treasury assessments, and lessons learned.
