OAS-FIN-110 — Finance Canon Maintenance and Version Control Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-110
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-FIN-001 — Finance Charter
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for governing, maintaining, reviewing, versioning, interpreting, expanding, archiving, and preserving the Aureus Finance Canon.

Its purpose is to ensure every financial framework, SOP, policy, standard, treasury procedure, accounting rule, and governance document evolves through disciplined constitutional stewardship while preserving Aureus' institutional financial knowledge.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Finance Frameworks
- Finance SOPs
- Financial policies
- Treasury procedures
- Accounting standards
- Budget procedures
- Investment policies
- Audit standards
- Financial governance documentation

---

Objectives

Finance Canon governance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain historical continuity.
- Ensure version traceability.
- Support disciplined evolution.
- Preserve institutional knowledge.
- Strengthen future stewardship.

---

Guiding Principles

The Finance Canon shall evolve deliberately rather than reactively.

Every approved revision should improve financial governance, stewardship, transparency, sustainability, operational clarity, or constitutional alignment while preserving a complete historical record.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Chief Financial Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining the Finance Canon.
- Coordinating reviews.
- Approving canonical revisions.
- Preserving version history.
- Overseeing publication.

---

Financial Governance Council

Responsible for:

- Reviewing proposed amendments.
- Evaluating constitutional alignment.
- Approving significant revisions.
- Interpreting financial guidance.
- Recommending future improvements.

---

AI Financial Knowledge Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring document consistency.
- Detecting conflicts.
- Maintaining cross-references.
- Tracking revisions.
- Supporting historical research.
- Identifying opportunities for improvement.

Human stewards retain final authority over all canonical financial revisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before modifying any canonical financial document:

- Proposed revisions are documented.
- Supporting rationale is provided.
- Related documents are identified.
- Financial impacts are evaluated.
- Required reviewers are assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Change Proposal

Document:

- Proposed revision.
- Purpose.
- Financial justification.
- Expected benefits.
- Related documents.
- Anticipated impacts.

---

Step 2 — Canonical Review

Evaluate:

- Constitutional alignment.
- Financial consistency.
- Governance implications.
- Operational impacts.
- Long-term institutional value.

---

Step 3 — Approval

The appropriate governance authority may:

- Approve.
- Approve with revisions.
- Return for additional review.
- Reject.

Approved revisions shall be formally recorded.

---

Step 4 — Version Management

Every approved revision shall include:

- Version number.
- Revision date.
- Change summary.
- Approval record.
- Historical reference.

Superseded versions shall remain permanently archived.

---

Step 5 — Publication

Publish approved revisions within the official Aureus repository.

Update indexes, cross-references, and related documentation where appropriate.

---

Step 6 — Archival

Archive previous versions while preserving:

- Original content.
- Revision history.
- Approval history.
- Historical context.

Institutional financial memory shall remain permanently accessible.

---

Step 7 — Periodic Review

Conduct periodic reviews to identify:

- Obsolete guidance.
- Financial inconsistencies.
- Missing governance.
- Emerging financial needs.
- Opportunities for simplification.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Review completion.
- Version accuracy.
- Cross-reference integrity.
- Canon consistency.
- Amendment quality.
- Publication timeliness.
- Historical preservation.
- Steward satisfaction.

---

Exceptions

Emergency financial revisions may follow expedited governance procedures when necessary to protect members, preserve financial stability, or address critical institutional risks.

Emergency revisions shall receive formal retrospective review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Amendment proposals.
- Approval records.
- Version history.
- Archive records.
- Governance decisions.
- Canon indexes.
- Review schedules.
- Maintenance reports.

---

Continuous Improvement

Finance Canon governance shall improve through governance reviews, financial audits, steward feedback, AI-assisted analysis, documentation quality reviews, and institutional learning.
