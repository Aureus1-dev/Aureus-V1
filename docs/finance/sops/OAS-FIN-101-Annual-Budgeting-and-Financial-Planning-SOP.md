OAS-FIN-101 — Annual Budgeting and Financial Planning Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-101
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-FIN-003 — Financial Planning and Budgeting Framework
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for preparing, reviewing, approving, implementing, monitoring, and evaluating Aureus' annual budget.

Its purpose is to ensure institutional resources are planned strategically, allocated responsibly, monitored continuously, and aligned with the Constitution, mission, and long-term financial sustainability of Aureus.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Annual budgeting
- Strategic financial planning
- Departmental budget requests
- Budget review
- Budget approval
- Budget implementation
- Variance analysis
- Forecast revisions
- AI-assisted forecasting
- Budget reporting

---

Objectives

Budgeting shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote financial sustainability.
- Support strategic priorities.
- Strengthen accountability.
- Encourage prudent stewardship.
- Enable informed decision-making.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Chief Financial Steward

Responsible for:

- Coordinating the annual budget process.
- Reviewing submissions.
- Preparing institutional recommendations.
- Presenting the proposed budget for approval.
- Monitoring implementation.

---

Department Stewards

Responsible for:

- Preparing departmental budget requests.
- Providing supporting documentation.
- Managing approved budgets.
- Monitoring expenditures.
- Reporting significant variances.

---

AI Financial Planning Steward

Responsible for:

- Preparing financial forecasts.
- Modeling scenarios.
- Performing variance analysis.
- Supporting budget preparation.
- Identifying trends.
- Producing planning summaries.

Human stewards remain responsible for budget approvals and strategic financial decisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before preparing the annual budget:

- Strategic priorities are reviewed.
- Financial performance from the prior period is evaluated.
- Revenue forecasts are updated.
- Reserve requirements are confirmed.
- Budget assumptions are documented.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Strategic Planning

Review:

- Institutional priorities.
- Strategic objectives.
- Mission requirements.
- Financial outlook.
- Major initiatives.
- Known financial risks.

---

Step 2 — Budget Preparation

Prepare projected:

- Revenue.
- Operating expenses.
- Capital expenditures.
- Reserve contributions.
- Strategic investments.
- Contingency allocations.

Budget assumptions shall be documented.

---

Step 3 — Departmental Review

Review all departmental submissions for:

- Mission alignment.
- Financial sustainability.
- Operational necessity.
- Resource efficiency.
- Budget completeness.

---

Step 4 — Budget Consolidation

Combine approved departmental budgets into a unified institutional budget and identify:

- Funding gaps.
- Strategic tradeoffs.
- Reserve impacts.
- Resource constraints.

---

Step 5 — Approval

Submit the consolidated budget through the appropriate financial governance process.

Document:

- Approval decisions.
- Conditions.
- Amendments.
- Effective date.

---

Step 6 — Implementation

Communicate approved budgets and:

- Allocate resources.
- Activate budget monitoring.
- Establish reporting schedules.
- Confirm stewardship responsibilities.

---

Step 7 — Monitoring

Monitor:

- Budget performance.
- Revenue.
- Expenditures.
- Variances.
- Cash flow.
- Strategic progress.

Significant variances shall be reviewed promptly.

---

Step 8 — Year-End Review

Evaluate:

- Budget accuracy.
- Financial performance.
- Strategic outcomes.
- Reserve performance.
- Lessons learned.
- Planning improvements.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Budget accuracy.
- Forecast accuracy.
- Variance levels.
- Strategic objective achievement.
- Reserve performance.
- Budget completion timelines.
- Financial sustainability.
- Steward satisfaction.

---

Exceptions

Emergency financial conditions may require budget amendments outside the normal annual planning cycle.

Such amendments shall follow approved financial governance procedures.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Budget assumptions.
- Department submissions.
- Approved budgets.
- Variance reports.
- Forecast revisions.
- Approval records.
- Year-end evaluations.

---

Continuous Improvement

Budget planning shall improve through financial reviews, audit findings, steward feedback, AI-assisted forecasting, governance assessments, and lessons learned.
