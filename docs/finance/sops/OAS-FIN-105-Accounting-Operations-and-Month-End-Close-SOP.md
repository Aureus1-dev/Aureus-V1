OAS-FIN-105 — Accounting Operations and Month-End Close Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-105
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-FIN-006 — Accounting and Financial Reporting Framework
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for recording financial transactions, maintaining accounting records, reconciling accounts, performing month-end and year-end closes, preparing financial statements, and maintaining audit readiness throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure accurate financial reporting, reliable accounting records, responsible stewardship, and continuous financial transparency.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Journal entries
- General ledger maintenance
- Account reconciliations
- Month-end close
- Year-end close
- Adjusting entries
- Financial statement preparation
- Record retention
- Management review
- Audit readiness

---

Objectives

Accounting operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect institutional assets.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain financial accuracy.
- Promote transparency.
- Strengthen accountability.
- Support audit readiness.
- Preserve institutional trust.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Accounting Steward

Responsible for:

- Recording transactions.
- Maintaining accounting records.
- Preparing reconciliations.
- Supporting financial reporting.
- Coordinating accounting close activities.

---

Financial Review Steward

Responsible for:

- Reviewing accounting records.
- Approving adjusting entries where required.
- Confirming financial statement completeness.
- Verifying accounting quality.

---

AI Accounting Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring ledger activity.
- Identifying unusual transactions.
- Supporting reconciliations.
- Preparing close checklists.
- Detecting reporting variances.
- Generating accounting summaries.

Human stewards remain responsible for accounting judgments, financial statements, and approvals.

---

Prerequisites

Before beginning an accounting close:

- All routine transactions are recorded.
- Required supporting documentation is received.
- Banking activity is reconciled.
- Outstanding issues are identified.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Transaction Recording

Record:

- Revenue.
- Expenses.
- Assets.
- Liabilities.
- Equity or fund balances.
- Adjustments.

Every transaction shall be supported by appropriate documentation.

---

Step 2 — Ledger Review

Review the general ledger for:

- Accuracy.
- Completeness.
- Classification.
- Duplicate entries.
- Missing information.

Resolve discrepancies before closing.

---

Step 3 — Account Reconciliation

Reconcile:

- Bank accounts.
- Treasury balances.
- Receivables.
- Payables.
- Deferred revenue.
- Other significant accounts.

Document all reconciliation activities.

---

Step 4 — Adjusting Entries

Prepare adjusting entries where appropriate for:

- Accruals.
- Deferrals.
- Corrections.
- Estimates.
- Reclassifications.

Adjustments shall receive required approval before posting.

---

Step 5 — Financial Statement Preparation

Prepare:

- Statement of Financial Position (Balance Sheet).
- Statement of Activities (or Income Statement).
- Cash Flow Statement.
- Budget Variance Reports.
- Supporting schedules.

Review for completeness and accuracy.

---

Step 6 — Management Review

Financial leadership shall review:

- Material variances.
- Significant transactions.
- Accounting estimates.
- Reporting accuracy.
- Outstanding risks.

Approve the accounting close upon satisfactory review.

---

Step 7 — Record Retention

Archive:

- Journal entries.
- Supporting documentation.
- Reconciliations.
- Financial statements.
- Review records.
- Close checklists.

Maintain records according to approved retention policies.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Close completion time.
- Reconciliation completion.
- Financial accuracy.
- Audit adjustments.
- Reporting timeliness.
- Ledger integrity.
- Documentation completeness.
- Audit readiness.

---

Exceptions

Extraordinary financial events may require special accounting treatment or extended closing procedures while maintaining complete documentation and governance approval.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Journal entries.
- General ledger.
- Reconciliation files.
- Financial statements.
- Close checklists.
- Approval records.
- Supporting schedules.
- Audit documentation.

---

Continuous Improvement

Accounting operations shall improve through financial reviews, audit findings, steward feedback, AI-assisted analysis, accounting quality assessments, and lessons learned.
