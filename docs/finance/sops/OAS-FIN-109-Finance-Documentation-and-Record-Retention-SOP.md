OAS-FIN-109 — Finance Documentation and Record Retention Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-109
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-FIN-006 — Accounting and Financial Reporting Framework
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for creating, maintaining, protecting, retaining, archiving, and disposing of Aureus' financial records and supporting documentation.

Its purpose is to preserve financial integrity, maintain audit readiness, support governance, protect confidential information, and ensure institutional financial history remains available for authorized use.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Accounting records
- Financial statements
- Budget documentation
- Treasury records
- Audit evidence
- Contracts
- Procurement records
- Tax documentation
- Financial correspondence
- Record retention and disposal

---

Objectives

Financial documentation shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect institutional assets.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain financial accuracy.
- Support audits.
- Protect confidential information.
- Preserve institutional history.
- Strengthen accountability.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Finance Records Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining financial records.
- Coordinating record retention.
- Managing archival activities.
- Supporting audits.
- Protecting confidential records.

---

Financial Leaders

Responsible for:

- Confirming documentation quality.
- Approving retention decisions.
- Supporting governance reviews.
- Reporting missing or inaccurate records.

---

AI Records Steward

Responsible for:

- Organizing financial records.
- Monitoring retention schedules.
- Detecting missing documentation.
- Supporting record searches.
- Preparing archival summaries.
- Identifying documentation gaps.

Human stewards remain responsible for approving record disposal, legal holds, and governance decisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before archiving or disposing of records:

- Retention requirements are verified.
- Legal or audit holds are reviewed.
- Required approvals are obtained.
- Records are complete and indexed.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Record Creation

Create and maintain records for:

- Financial transactions.
- Accounting activities.
- Budget approvals.
- Treasury operations.
- Procurement.
- Audits.
- Financial governance.

Every record shall be complete and properly identified.

---

Step 2 — Classification

Classify records according to:

- Record type.
- Confidentiality.
- Operational importance.
- Retention schedule.
- Legal requirements.

---

Step 3 — Storage

Store records using approved systems that provide:

- Security.
- Backup.
- Version control where appropriate.
- Authorized access.
- Disaster recovery capability.

---

Step 4 — Retention

Maintain records according to approved retention schedules.

Retention periods shall consider:

- Legal requirements.
- Regulatory obligations.
- Governance needs.
- Historical value.
- Audit requirements.

---

Step 5 — Access

Provide access only to authorized individuals.

Maintain access logs where appropriate for sensitive financial information.

---

Step 6 — Archival

Archive inactive records while preserving:

- Authenticity.
- Integrity.
- Accessibility.
- Historical context.

Archived records shall remain retrievable.

---

Step 7 — Disposal

Dispose of records only after:

- Retention periods expire.
- Required approvals are obtained.
- Legal holds are cleared.
- Secure destruction procedures are followed.

Record disposal shall be documented.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Documentation completeness.
- Retrieval time.
- Retention compliance.
- Audit readiness.
- Storage integrity.
- Confidentiality compliance.
- Record preservation.
- Documentation quality.

---

Exceptions

Legal holds, regulatory requirements, or governance directives may require records to be retained beyond normal schedules.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Record inventories.
- Retention schedules.
- Disposal logs.
- Archive indexes.
- Access logs.
- Legal hold records.
- Governance approvals.

---

Continuous Improvement

Financial documentation practices shall improve through audits, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance reviews, documentation assessments, and lessons learned.
