OAS-FIN-104 — Finance Canon Maintenance and Version Control Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-104
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-FIN-001 — Financial Governance Charter
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for proposing, reviewing, approving, versioning, publishing, interpreting, archiving, maintaining, and continuously improving the Aureus Finance Canon.

Its purpose is to preserve constitutional integrity, maintain financial governance knowledge, ensure disciplined evolution of financial stewardship, and provide a transparent process for future generations of financial stewards.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Finance Charters
- Finance Frameworks
- Finance SOPs
- Budgeting standards
- Accounting standards
- Treasury procedures
- Audit documentation
- Financial governance records
- Financial stewardship documentation

---

Objectives

Finance Canon governance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain historical continuity.
- Ensure version traceability.
- Support disciplined institutional evolution.
- Preserve financial knowledge.
- Strengthen future stewardship.

---

Guiding Principles

The Finance Canon shall evolve deliberately rather than reactively.

Every approved revision should strengthen fiscal stewardship, governance quality, financial sustainability, audit readiness, institutional resilience, or constitutional fidelity while preserving the complete historical record.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Chief Financial Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining the Finance Canon.
- Coordinating canonical reviews.
- Approving routine revisions within delegated authority.
- Preserving version history.
- Publishing approved revisions.

---

Financial Governance Council

Responsible for:

- Reviewing significant amendments.
- Evaluating constitutional alignment.
- Recommending improvements.
- Interpreting financial governance.
- Supporting long-term fiscal stewardship.

---

AI Financial Knowledge Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring document consistency.
- Detecting conflicts.
- Tracking revision history.
- Maintaining cross-references.
- Supporting financial research.
- Identifying improvement opportunities.

Human stewards retain final authority over all canonical finance revisions and interpretations.

---

Prerequisites

Before modifying any canonical finance document:

- Proposed revisions are documented.
- Supporting rationale is prepared.
- Related documents are identified.
- Financial implications are evaluated.
- Required reviewers are assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Change Proposal

Document:

- Proposed revision.
- Purpose.
- Financial justification.
- Expected benefits.
- Related documents.
- Anticipated institutional impacts.

---

Step 2 — Canonical Review

Evaluate:

- Constitutional alignment.
- Financial consistency.
- Governance implications.
- Operational impacts.
- Long-term institutional value.

---

Step 3 — Approval

The appropriate governance authority may:

- Approve.
- Approve with revisions.
- Return for additional review.
- Reject.

All approval decisions shall be documented.

---

Step 4 — Version Management

Every approved revision shall include:

- Version number.
- Revision date.
- Change summary.
- Approval record.
- Updated cross-references where applicable.

Superseded versions shall remain permanently archived.

---

Step 5 — Publication

Publish approved revisions within the official Aureus repository.

Update indexes, document references, and governance records where appropriate.

---

Step 6 — Archival

Archive prior versions while preserving:

- Original content.
- Approval history.
- Revision history.
- Historical context.

Institutional financial memory shall remain permanently available.

---

Step 7 — Periodic Review

Conduct scheduled reviews to identify:

- Obsolete guidance.
- Governance inconsistencies.
- Emerging financial needs.
- Opportunities for simplification.
- Financial governance improvement initiatives.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Review completion.
- Version accuracy.
- Cross-reference integrity.
- Canon consistency.
- Amendment quality.
- Publication timeliness.
- Historical preservation.
- Steward satisfaction.

---

Exceptions

Emergency financial governance revisions may follow expedited governance procedures when necessary to preserve institutional stability, comply with urgent legal requirements, or protect entrusted financial resources.

Emergency revisions shall receive formal retrospective review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Amendment proposals.
- Review records.
- Approval records.
- Version histories.
- Archive records.
- Canon indexes.
- Interpretation guidance.
- Governance reports.

---

Continuous Improvement

Finance Canon governance shall improve through governance reviews, financial assessments, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, documentation quality reviews, audit findings, and institutional learning.
