OAS-FIN-106 — Reserve and Investment Management Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-106
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-FIN-007 — Financial Risk and Reserve Management Framework
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for funding, maintaining, investing, monitoring, and governing Aureus' institutional reserves and approved investment activities.

Its purpose is to preserve financial stability, strengthen institutional resilience, responsibly grow long-term resources, and ensure every reserve and investment decision remains constitutionally aligned and mission focused.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Reserve funding
- Reserve utilization
- Investment planning
- Investment approvals
- Portfolio oversight
- Liquidity management
- Investment monitoring
- Risk management
- Performance reporting
- Governance reviews

---

Objectives

Reserve and investment management shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect institutional assets.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Strengthen long-term sustainability.
- Maintain appropriate liquidity.
- Promote prudent stewardship.
- Reduce unnecessary financial risk.
- Preserve institutional independence.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Reserve Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring reserve balances.
- Coordinating reserve funding.
- Preparing reserve reports.
- Supporting reserve reviews.
- Maintaining reserve documentation.

---

Investment Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring approved investments.
- Preparing investment analyses.
- Reporting portfolio performance.
- Identifying investment risks.
- Supporting governance reviews.

---

AI Investment Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring portfolio performance.
- Forecasting liquidity.
- Identifying market trends.
- Preparing performance summaries.
- Supporting scenario analysis.
- Detecting portfolio anomalies.

Human stewards remain responsible for investment approvals, portfolio strategy, reserve utilization, and fiduciary decisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before funding reserves or making investments:

- Required governance approvals are obtained.
- Liquidity requirements are satisfied.
- Risk assessments are completed.
- Investment policies are reviewed.
- Supporting documentation is prepared.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Reserve Funding

Determine reserve contributions based on:

- Approved financial policies.
- Budget performance.
- Revenue availability.
- Long-term financial objectives.
- Required reserve targets.

Record all reserve transfers appropriately.

---

Step 2 — Reserve Monitoring

Review:

- Reserve balances.
- Funding progress.
- Liquidity requirements.
- Authorized reserve usage.
- Financial sustainability.

---

Step 3 — Investment Evaluation

Evaluate potential investments according to:

- Risk profile.
- Expected return.
- Liquidity.
- Diversification.
- Constitutional alignment.
- Long-term mission impact.

---

Step 4 — Investment Approval

Obtain required governance approval before:

- Purchasing investments.
- Changing investment allocations.
- Liquidating significant assets.
- Modifying investment strategy.

---

Step 5 — Portfolio Monitoring

Monitor:

- Investment performance.
- Asset allocation.
- Risk exposure.
- Liquidity.
- Market conditions.
- Portfolio diversification.

---

Step 6 — Performance Review

Prepare periodic reports evaluating:

- Investment returns.
- Reserve adequacy.
- Portfolio performance.
- Strategic alignment.
- Financial resilience.

---

Step 7 — Governance Review

Review:

- Reserve policies.
- Investment policies.
- Portfolio strategy.
- Risk tolerance.
- Long-term financial sustainability.

Implement approved improvements where appropriate.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Reserve funding progress.
- Reserve adequacy.
- Investment performance.
- Portfolio diversification.
- Liquidity strength.
- Risk exposure.
- Financial sustainability.
- Governance compliance.

---

Exceptions

Emergency reserve utilization or extraordinary investment actions may follow approved emergency financial governance procedures and shall receive prompt retrospective review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Reserve reports.
- Investment analyses.
- Approval records.
- Portfolio reports.
- Performance summaries.
- Risk assessments.
- Governance reviews.
- Supporting documentation.

---

Continuous Improvement

Reserve and investment management shall improve through financial reviews, audit findings, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, investment evaluations, and lessons learned.
