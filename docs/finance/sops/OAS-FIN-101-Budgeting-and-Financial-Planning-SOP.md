OAS-FIN-101 — Budgeting and Financial Planning Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-101
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-FIN-003 — Budgeting, Accounting, and Treasury Framework
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for strategic financial planning, annual budgeting, financial forecasting, budget approval, variance analysis, budget revisions, and continuous financial planning throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure financial resources are planned, allocated, monitored, and adjusted responsibly while preserving constitutional alignment, fiscal sustainability, and mission effectiveness.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Strategic financial planning
- Annual budgets
- Operating budgets
- Capital budgets
- Forecasting
- Budget approvals
- Budget revisions
- Variance analysis
- Financial planning
- Budget reporting

---

Objectives

Financial planning shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Support prudent resource allocation.
- Strengthen fiscal sustainability.
- Improve financial transparency.
- Enable informed decision-making.
- Promote continuous improvement.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Financial Planning Steward

Responsible for:

- Coordinating budgeting activities.
- Preparing financial forecasts.
- Monitoring budget performance.
- Maintaining planning documentation.
- Preparing governance reports.

---

Budget Owners

Responsible for:

- Developing budget proposals.
- Monitoring expenditures.
- Explaining variances.
- Supporting periodic reviews.
- Maintaining budget accountability.

---

AI Financial Planning Steward

Responsible for:

- Preparing forecasts.
- Performing variance analysis.
- Identifying financial trends.
- Generating budget summaries.
- Supporting planning scenarios.
- Tracking financial performance.

Human stewards remain responsible for budget approvals, resource allocation decisions, policy interpretation, and governance actions.

---

Prerequisites

Before preparing budgets:

- Strategic priorities are identified.
- Financial assumptions are documented.
- Historical financial information is available.
- Planning responsibilities are assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Strategic Planning

Document:

- Institutional priorities.
- Financial objectives.
- Planning assumptions.
- Major initiatives.
- Resource requirements.
- Success measures.

---

Step 2 — Budget Development

Prepare budgets including:

- Revenue projections.
- Expense estimates.
- Capital requirements.
- Reserve contributions.
- Cash flow expectations.
- Supporting documentation.

---

Step 3 — Budget Review

Evaluate:

- Strategic alignment.
- Financial sustainability.
- Resource availability.
- Operational feasibility.
- Risk considerations.

Document review findings.

---

Step 4 — Approval

Submit budgets for appropriate governance approval.

Record:

- Approval decisions.
- Required revisions.
- Final authorized budgets.

---

Step 5 — Monitoring

Monitor:

- Actual performance.
- Budget utilization.
- Variances.
- Forecast updates.
- Financial risks.

Investigate material deviations.

---

Step 6 — Budget Revision

When necessary:

- Document proposed changes.
- Assess operational impact.
- Obtain required approvals.
- Update financial records.

---

Step 7 — Continuous Planning

Conduct periodic reviews to:

- Update forecasts.
- Refine assumptions.
- Improve planning accuracy.
- Support strategic decisions.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Budget accuracy.
- Forecast reliability.
- Variance resolution.
- Planning timeliness.
- Resource utilization.
- Financial sustainability.
- Governance compliance.
- Mission support.

---

Exceptions

Emergency financial needs may require expedited budget adjustments while maintaining documentation and retrospective governance review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Strategic financial plans.
- Budget proposals.
- Approved budgets.
- Forecast reports.
- Variance analyses.
- Revision records.
- Governance reports.
- Planning assumptions.

---

Continuous Improvement

Financial planning shall improve through budget reviews, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance evaluations, operational assessments, and lessons learned.
