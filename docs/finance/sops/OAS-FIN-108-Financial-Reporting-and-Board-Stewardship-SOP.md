OAS-FIN-108 — Financial Reporting and Board Stewardship Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-108
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-FIN-009 — Financial Transparency and Stakeholder Reporting Framework
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for preparing, reviewing, approving, and distributing financial reports to governing bodies, leadership, members, regulators where applicable, auditors, and other authorized stakeholders.

Its purpose is to ensure financial reporting remains accurate, timely, transparent, understandable, secure, and supportive of informed governance.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Financial statements
- Executive financial reports
- Board stewardship reports
- Budget reports
- Treasury reports
- Reserve reports
- Governance disclosures
- Financial dashboards
- AI-assisted financial reporting
- Stakeholder communications

---

Objectives

Financial reporting shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote transparency.
- Strengthen accountability.
- Support informed governance.
- Protect confidential information.
- Improve institutional trust.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Financial Reporting Steward

Responsible for:

- Preparing financial reports.
- Coordinating report reviews.
- Maintaining reporting schedules.
- Managing report distribution.
- Preserving reporting records.

---

Chief Financial Steward

Responsible for:

- Reviewing reports.
- Confirming reporting accuracy.
- Approving official financial reports.
- Presenting reports to governing authorities where appropriate.

---

AI Financial Reporting Steward

Responsible for:

- Preparing dashboards.
- Generating summaries.
- Identifying reporting trends.
- Monitoring reporting deadlines.
- Supporting report validation.
- Producing visualization recommendations.

Human stewards remain responsible for official financial communications and governance reporting.

---

Prerequisites

Before distributing any financial report:

- Financial data is verified.
- Required reconciliations are complete.
- Appropriate reviews are performed.
- Necessary approvals are obtained.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Report Preparation

Prepare reports including, where appropriate:

- Financial statements.
- Budget performance.
- Treasury summaries.
- Reserve reports.
- Revenue analyses.
- Expense analyses.
- Financial forecasts.

---

Step 2 — Review

Review reports for:

- Accuracy.
- Completeness.
- Consistency.
- Constitutional alignment.
- Confidentiality.
- Readability.

Resolve significant discrepancies before approval.

---

Step 3 — Approval

Required financial authorities shall approve reports before official distribution.

Approval records shall be maintained.

---

Step 4 — Distribution

Distribute reports according to approved reporting schedules and stakeholder authorization.

Protect confidential information throughout the distribution process.

---

Step 5 — Governance Presentation

Present significant financial information to governing bodies with:

- Executive summaries.
- Key financial indicators.
- Significant variances.
- Risks.
- Recommendations.
- Stewardship observations.

---

Step 6 — Record Retention

Maintain:

- Published reports.
- Draft versions where appropriate.
- Approval records.
- Distribution logs.
- Supporting schedules.

---

Step 7 — Reporting Evaluation

Evaluate:

- Reporting quality.
- Timeliness.
- Stakeholder understanding.
- Governance usefulness.
- Opportunities for improvement.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Reporting timeliness.
- Reporting accuracy.
- Stakeholder satisfaction.
- Distribution compliance.
- Governance effectiveness.
- Dashboard utilization.
- Confidentiality compliance.
- Financial transparency.

---

Exceptions

Urgent governance situations may require expedited reporting while maintaining appropriate review and subsequent documentation.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Financial reports.
- Approval records.
- Distribution logs.
- Dashboard archives.
- Supporting analyses.
- Governance presentations.
- Reporting schedules.

---

Continuous Improvement

Financial reporting shall improve through governance feedback, audit findings, stewardship reviews, AI-assisted analysis, reporting assessments, and lessons learned.
