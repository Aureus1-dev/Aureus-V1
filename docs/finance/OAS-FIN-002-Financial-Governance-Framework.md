OAS-FIN-002 — Financial Governance Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-002
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-FIN-001 — Finance Charter
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the constitutional governance model for all financial activities throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure financial decisions are transparent, accountable, ethically governed, constitutionally aligned, and supportive of the long-term mission of Aureus.

---

Mission

Financial governance exists to responsibly oversee institutional resources, safeguard financial integrity, ensure accountability, and enable sustainable mission execution.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Financial authority
- Approval structures
- Delegated authority
- Internal controls
- Financial oversight
- Segregation of duties
- Treasury governance
- AI-assisted financial operations
- Financial accountability
- Governance reviews

---

Guiding Principles

Financial governance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote transparency.
- Strengthen accountability.
- Encourage prudent stewardship.
- Maintain financial integrity.
- Preserve institutional trust.

Governance protects both resources and reputation.

---

Financial Authority

Financial authority shall be exercised according to:

- Constitutional authority.
- Approved governance documents.
- Delegated financial authority.
- Documented approval procedures.
- Stewardship responsibilities.

Authority shall remain traceable and accountable.

---

Segregation of Duties

Financial responsibilities shall be appropriately separated to reduce risk and strengthen accountability.

Where practical, no single individual shall control the complete lifecycle of significant financial transactions.

---

Internal Controls

Financial controls shall include:

- Approval requirements.
- Spending limits.
- Documentation standards.
- Reconciliation procedures.
- Record retention.
- Audit readiness.

Controls should remain proportional to operational risk.

---

Financial Oversight

Financial oversight shall include:

- Periodic reviews.
- Budget monitoring.
- Treasury oversight.
- Financial reporting.
- Compliance reviews.
- Independent audits where appropriate.

Oversight strengthens institutional resilience.

---

AI-Assisted Financial Operations

AI may assist with:

- Financial analysis.
- Budget forecasting.
- Reporting.
- Reconciliation support.
- Trend analysis.
- Risk identification.

Human stewards remain responsible for approvals, fiduciary decisions, and constitutional interpretation.

---

Accountability

Every financial responsibility shall have clearly identified:

- Owner.
- Authority.
- Approval limits.
- Reporting responsibilities.
- Performance expectations.

Shared work shall not eliminate individual accountability.

---

Governance Reviews

Financial governance shall be reviewed regularly to evaluate:

- Control effectiveness.
- Policy compliance.
- Financial risks.
- Operational efficiency.
- Governance maturity.

Reviews should result in measurable improvements.

---

Continuous Improvement

Financial governance shall improve through:

- Audit findings.
- Steward feedback.
- Financial reviews.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance assessments.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens long-term stewardship.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all financial governance activities throughout Aureus.

All Finance Frameworks, Finance SOPs, treasury procedures, budgeting standards, accounting policies, financial controls, and governance procedures shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Finance Charter.
