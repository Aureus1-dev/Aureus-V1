OAS-FIN-003 — Budgeting, Accounting, and Treasury Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-003
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-FIN-002 — Financial Governance and Fiscal Accountability Framework
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the canonical model for budgeting, accounting, treasury management, cash management, revenue stewardship, expense management, reserves, investments, and financial reporting throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure institutional financial resources are planned, recorded, safeguarded, allocated, reported, and preserved responsibly in support of Aureus' long-term mission.

---

Mission

Financial operations exist to transform financial resources into sustainable institutional capacity through disciplined planning, accurate accounting, prudent treasury management, and responsible stewardship.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Budgeting
- Accounting
- Treasury management
- Cash management
- Revenue management
- Expense management
- Reserve management
- Investment governance
- Financial reporting
- Financial records

---

Guiding Principles

Financial operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote transparency.
- Exercise prudence.
- Maintain accurate records.
- Strengthen sustainability.
- Preserve institutional resilience.

Financial resources shall be managed according to long-term institutional priorities rather than short-term convenience.

---

Budgeting

Budgeting shall:

- Align with strategic priorities.
- Support approved initiatives.
- Reflect realistic assumptions.
- Include periodic review.
- Document significant variances.

Budgets shall remain adaptable while preserving fiscal discipline.

---

Accounting

Accounting practices shall ensure:

- Accurate recording.
- Complete documentation.
- Timely reconciliation.
- Consistent classification.
- Audit readiness.
- Reliable reporting.

Financial records shall faithfully represent institutional activity.

---

Treasury Management

Treasury activities shall include:

- Cash management.
- Liquidity management.
- Banking relationships.
- Payment administration.
- Reserve oversight.
- Investment coordination.

Treasury decisions shall prioritize institutional stability.

---

Revenue and Expense Stewardship

Revenue and expenses shall be:

- Properly authorized.
- Accurately recorded.
- Regularly reviewed.
- Consistent with approved budgets.
- Evaluated for stewardship effectiveness.

---

Financial Reporting

Financial reporting shall provide:

- Timely information.
- Accurate balances.
- Budget comparisons.
- Trend analysis.
- Stewardship insights.
- Governance transparency.

Reports shall support informed decision-making.

---

AI-Assisted Financial Operations

AI may assist through:

- Budget analysis.
- Cash forecasting.
- Transaction monitoring.
- Variance analysis.
- Financial reporting.
- Operational analytics.

Human stewards remain responsible for financial approvals, treasury decisions, accounting judgments, and governance actions.

---

Performance Measurement

Financial operations shall evaluate:

- Budget performance.
- Accounting accuracy.
- Treasury effectiveness.
- Liquidity.
- Financial sustainability.
- Reporting quality.
- Stewardship outcomes.
- Mission support.

Measurements shall strengthen institutional stewardship.

---

Continuous Improvement

Financial operations shall improve through:

- Financial reviews.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance assessments.
- Operational evaluations.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens Aureus' financial capability.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all budgeting, accounting, treasury, and financial operations throughout Aureus.

All Finance SOPs, accounting standards, treasury procedures, budgeting methodologies, investment governance documents, financial reporting procedures, and operational financial practices shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Financial Governance Charter.
