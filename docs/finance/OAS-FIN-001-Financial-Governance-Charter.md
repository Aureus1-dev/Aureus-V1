OAS-FIN-001 — Financial Governance Charter

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-001
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-001 — Founding Charter
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Charter establishes the constitutional authority governing all financial resources, budgeting, accounting, treasury activities, investments, reserves, financial reporting, audits, and fiscal stewardship throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure financial resources are managed responsibly, transparently, sustainably, and in alignment with Aureus' constitutional mission while protecting members and preserving the institution's long-term financial resilience.

---

Mission

Financial stewardship exists to responsibly acquire, allocate, protect, invest, account for, and preserve financial resources so Aureus can faithfully fulfill its mission across generations.

---

Scope

This Charter governs:

- Financial governance
- Budgeting
- Accounting
- Treasury management
- Revenue management
- Expense management
- Financial reporting
- Auditing
- Financial risk management
- Long-term fiscal sustainability

---

Guiding Principles

Financial stewardship shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote transparency.
- Exercise prudence.
- Strengthen accountability.
- Preserve long-term sustainability.
- Build institutional resilience.

Financial resources shall always be treated as entrusted assets rather than institutional possessions.

---

Financial Authority

Financial authority shall be exercised according to:

- The Constitution.
- Approved governance documents.
- Applicable law.
- Delegated authority.
- Stewardship responsibilities.

Authority shall remain documented, transparent, and accountable.

---

Financial Governance

Financial governance shall include:

- Clearly defined authority.
- Stewardship responsibilities.
- Internal controls.
- Budget oversight.
- Financial reporting.
- Independent review.
- Continuous improvement.

Governance shall balance operational flexibility with disciplined accountability.

---

Financial Stewardship

Financial stewardship shall:

- Protect institutional resources.
- Preserve financial integrity.
- Promote responsible resource allocation.
- Support informed decision-making.
- Maintain accurate records.
- Strengthen long-term fiscal health.

Every steward shares responsibility for protecting Aureus' financial integrity.

---

Financial Risk Management

Financial risks shall be:

- Identified.
- Assessed.
- Prioritized.
- Mitigated.
- Monitored.
- Reviewed regularly.

Financial decisions shall balance opportunity with prudent risk management.

---

Continuous Improvement

Financial governance shall improve through:

- Financial reviews.
- Audit findings.
- Steward feedback.
- Governance assessments.
- Operational learning.
- Responsible innovation.

Continuous improvement strengthens Aureus' long-term financial sustainability.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Charter governs the entire Finance Canon.

All Finance Frameworks, Finance SOPs, accounting standards, treasury procedures, budgeting policies, audit procedures, investment governance documents, and financial stewardship standards shall conform to this Charter unless superseded by the Constitution.
