OAS-FIN-004 — Revenue and Income Management Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-004
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-FIN-002 — Financial Governance Framework
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the canonical model for generating, receiving, recognizing, managing, diversifying, and safeguarding all institutional revenue throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure every revenue source supports the mission, protects institutional independence, strengthens long-term sustainability, and is managed with integrity, transparency, and constitutional alignment.

---

Mission

Revenue management exists to responsibly generate the financial resources necessary for Aureus to fulfill its mission while preserving ethical standards, institutional trust, and long-term financial resilience.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Membership revenue
- Donations
- Grants
- Partnership revenue
- Affiliate revenue
- Earned income
- Investment income
- Deferred revenue
- Revenue recognition
- AI-assisted revenue forecasting

---

Guiding Principles

Revenue management shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain institutional independence.
- Promote transparency.
- Encourage diversification.
- Preserve financial integrity.
- Support long-term sustainability.

Revenue shall never compromise Aureus' constitutional principles.

---

Revenue Sources

Approved revenue sources may include:

- Membership subscriptions.
- Educational programs.
- Service fees.
- Affiliate partnerships.
- Grants.
- Donations.
- Sponsorships approved under institutional policy.
- Investment income consistent with approved financial policies.

Every revenue source shall undergo governance review before adoption.

---

Revenue Recognition

Revenue shall be:

- Properly classified.
- Accurately recorded.
- Timely recognized.
- Fully documented.
- Supported by appropriate records.
- Reported according to approved financial policies.

---

Revenue Diversification

Financial planning shall encourage diversified revenue streams to reduce dependence upon any single funding source and strengthen long-term institutional resilience.

---

Revenue Protection

Revenue management shall include:

- Fraud prevention.
- Internal controls.
- Segregation of duties.
- Secure payment processing.
- Financial reconciliation.
- Monitoring for irregularities.

Institutional resources shall be safeguarded at all times.

---

Revenue Forecasting

Forecasts shall evaluate:

- Historical performance.
- Membership growth.
- Economic conditions.
- Seasonal variations.
- Strategic initiatives.
- Long-term sustainability.

Forecasts should inform responsible planning rather than guarantee outcomes.

---

AI-Assisted Revenue Management

AI may assist through:

- Revenue forecasting.
- Trend analysis.
- Variance detection.
- Financial modeling.
- Opportunity analysis.
- Reporting support.

Human stewards remain responsible for revenue strategy, approvals, and financial governance.

---

Performance Measurement

Revenue management shall evaluate:

- Revenue growth.
- Revenue diversity.
- Collection efficiency.
- Forecast accuracy.
- Financial sustainability.
- Member affordability.
- Mission impact.

Measurements shall support responsible stewardship.

---

Continuous Improvement

Revenue management shall improve through:

- Financial reviews.
- Revenue analysis.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted forecasting.
- Audit findings.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens financial sustainability.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all revenue and income management activities throughout Aureus.

All Finance SOPs, revenue procedures, fundraising standards, partnership revenue policies, accounting procedures, and financial reporting standards shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Finance Charter.
