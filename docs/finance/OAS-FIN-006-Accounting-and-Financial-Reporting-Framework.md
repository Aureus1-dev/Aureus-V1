OAS-FIN-006 — Accounting and Financial Reporting Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-006
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-FIN-002 — Financial Governance Framework
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the canonical model for accounting, financial recordkeeping, reconciliations, financial reporting, internal financial controls, and institutional financial transparency throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure every financial transaction is accurately recorded, properly classified, transparently reported, securely retained, and consistently governed in accordance with the Constitution and approved financial policies.

---

Mission

Accounting and financial reporting exist to provide accurate, timely, reliable financial information that supports responsible stewardship, informed decision-making, institutional accountability, and long-term sustainability.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Financial recordkeeping
- General ledger management
- Financial statements
- Financial reporting
- Account reconciliations
- Internal accounting controls
- Audit readiness
- Financial disclosures
- AI-assisted accounting support
- Financial transparency

---

Guiding Principles

Accounting and reporting shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect institutional assets.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote transparency.
- Ensure accuracy.
- Maintain accountability.
- Support audit readiness.
- Preserve institutional trust.

Reliable financial information is essential to responsible stewardship.

---

Financial Recordkeeping

Financial records shall be:

- Accurate.
- Complete.
- Timely.
- Verifiable.
- Properly classified.
- Securely retained.

Every transaction shall be supported by appropriate documentation.

---

Accounting Standards

Accounting practices shall include:

- Consistent chart of accounts.
- Standardized classifications.
- Documented accounting policies.
- Period-end closing procedures.
- Financial reconciliations.
- Internal quality reviews.

Standards shall remain consistent across the institution.

---

Financial Reporting

Financial reporting shall include:

- Balance sheets.
- Statements of activities or income.
- Cash flow reporting.
- Budget variance reporting.
- Management reports.
- Governance summaries.

Reports shall be understandable, accurate, and timely.

---

Internal Controls

Accounting controls shall include:

- Segregation of duties.
- Approval requirements.
- Reconciliation procedures.
- Documentation standards.
- Access controls.
- Periodic reviews.

Controls protect institutional integrity.

---

Audit Readiness

Financial records shall remain prepared for:

- Internal audits.
- External audits.
- Governance reviews.
- Regulatory examinations where applicable.

Audit readiness shall be maintained continuously rather than created only before an audit.

---

Financial Transparency

Financial information shall be communicated appropriately to authorized stakeholders through documented reporting processes while protecting confidential information.

Transparency strengthens trust.

---

AI-Assisted Accounting

AI may assist through:

- Transaction classification.
- Reconciliation support.
- Variance analysis.
- Financial reporting.
- Trend analysis.
- Audit preparation.

Human stewards remain responsible for approvals, financial statements, accounting judgments, and governance decisions.

---

Performance Measurement

Accounting effectiveness shall evaluate:

- Reporting timeliness.
- Reporting accuracy.
- Reconciliation completion.
- Audit readiness.
- Control effectiveness.
- Financial transparency.
- Data integrity.

Measurements support continual improvement.

---

Continuous Improvement

Accounting practices shall improve through:

- Financial reviews.
- Audit findings.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance assessments.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens institutional stewardship.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all accounting and financial reporting activities throughout Aureus.

All Finance SOPs, accounting procedures, reporting standards, reconciliation policies, audit preparation procedures, and financial control documents shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Finance Charter.
