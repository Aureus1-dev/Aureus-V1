OAS-FIN-003 — Financial Planning and Budgeting Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-003
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-FIN-002 — Financial Governance Framework
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the canonical model for financial planning, budgeting, forecasting, resource allocation, and long-term financial sustainability throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure institutional resources are allocated responsibly, transparently, and strategically in support of the Constitution, the mission, and future generations.

---

Mission

Financial planning exists to align institutional resources with strategic priorities, ensuring Aureus can fulfill its mission while maintaining long-term financial resilience and responsible stewardship.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Strategic financial planning
- Annual budgeting
- Multi-year financial forecasting
- Reserve planning
- Capital allocation
- Resource prioritization
- Scenario planning
- Budget governance
- AI-assisted financial forecasting
- Financial sustainability

---

Guiding Principles

Financial planning shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote transparency.
- Encourage prudent stewardship.
- Support long-term sustainability.
- Preserve financial flexibility.
- Build institutional resilience.

Planning shall prioritize mission impact over short-term convenience.

---

Financial Planning

Financial planning shall include:

- Strategic objectives.
- Revenue projections.
- Expense projections.
- Reserve targets.
- Capital requirements.
- Risk assessments.
- Sustainability analysis.

Planning should consider both current and future institutional needs.

---

Budget Development

Annual budgets shall include:

- Expected revenues.
- Planned expenditures.
- Operating reserves.
- Strategic initiatives.
- Capital investments.
- Contingency allocations.

Budgets shall be balanced according to approved financial policies.

---

Resource Allocation

Institutional resources shall be allocated according to:

- Mission impact.
- Member benefit.
- Strategic priorities.
- Financial sustainability.
- Operational necessity.
- Long-term institutional value.

Resource allocation decisions shall remain documented and reviewable.

---

Financial Forecasting

Forecasting shall evaluate:

- Revenue trends.
- Expense trends.
- Cash flow.
- Reserve growth.
- Economic conditions.
- Strategic scenarios.

Forecasts shall be reviewed and updated regularly.

---

Scenario Planning

Financial planning shall consider:

- Expected conditions.
- Optimistic scenarios.
- Conservative scenarios.
- Adverse conditions.
- Extraordinary events.

Scenario planning strengthens institutional preparedness.

---

Budget Monitoring

Budgets shall be monitored through:

- Periodic variance analysis.
- Financial reporting.
- Operational reviews.
- Executive oversight.
- Corrective action where necessary.

Monitoring supports responsible stewardship.

---

AI-Assisted Planning

AI may assist through:

- Forecast modeling.
- Trend analysis.
- Budget simulations.
- Scenario comparisons.
- Risk identification.
- Performance projections.

Human stewards remain responsible for financial approval and strategic decisions.

---

Performance Measurement

Financial planning effectiveness shall evaluate:

- Budget accuracy.
- Forecast accuracy.
- Reserve growth.
- Resource utilization.
- Strategic objective achievement.
- Financial sustainability.
- Mission impact.

Measurements shall support continual improvement.

---

Continuous Improvement

Financial planning shall improve through:

- Financial reviews.
- Budget evaluations.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Audit findings.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens long-term financial stewardship.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all financial planning and budgeting activities throughout Aureus.

All Finance SOPs, budget procedures, forecasting standards, reserve policies, resource allocation procedures, and financial planning documents shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Finance Charter.
