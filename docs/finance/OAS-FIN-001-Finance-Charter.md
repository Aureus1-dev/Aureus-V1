OAS-FIN-001 — Finance Charter

Canonical Designation: OAS-FIN-001
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-001 — Founding Charter
Owner: Chief Financial Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Charter establishes the constitutional authority governing all financial stewardship throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure every financial resource entrusted to Aureus is managed ethically, transparently, responsibly, sustainably, and in alignment with the Constitution and mission of the institution.

---

Mission

Financial stewardship exists to responsibly acquire, protect, allocate, invest, account for, and preserve institutional resources so Aureus can fulfill its mission across generations.

---

Scope

This Charter governs:

- Revenue management
- Budgeting
- Treasury operations
- Financial planning
- Institutional reserves
- Expenditures
- Financial reporting
- Financial governance
- Financial risk management
- Long-term sustainability

---

Guiding Principles

Financial stewardship shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote transparency.
- Encourage accountability.
- Support long-term sustainability.
- Maintain financial integrity.
- Preserve institutional trust.

Every financial decision is an act of stewardship.

---

Financial Authority

Financial authority shall be exercised according to:

- The Constitution.
- Approved governance documents.
- Delegated authority.
- Documented financial procedures.
- Stewardship responsibilities.

No financial activity shall supersede constitutional authority.

---

Financial Governance

Financial governance shall include:

- Clearly defined responsibilities.
- Documented approval authority.
- Internal controls.
- Transparent reporting.
- Independent oversight.
- Periodic review.

Governance protects institutional resources.

---

Financial Stewardship

Institutional resources shall be managed to:

- Sustain operations.
- Support members.
- Build resilience.
- Preserve reserves.
- Enable responsible growth.
- Protect future generations.

Stewardship prioritizes long-term institutional health over short-term advantage.

---

Financial Planning

Financial planning shall include:

- Strategic objectives.
- Annual budgets.
- Multi-year projections.
- Reserve planning.
- Risk assessments.
- Resource allocation.

Planning should balance sustainability with mission impact.

---

Financial Integrity

Financial records shall be:

- Accurate.
- Complete.
- Timely.
- Verifiable.
- Secure.
- Properly retained.

Integrity strengthens institutional trust.

---

Risk Management

Financial risks shall be:

- Identified.
- Evaluated.
- Mitigated.
- Monitored.
- Reviewed regularly.

Risk management protects both members and the institution.

---

Continuous Improvement

Financial governance shall improve through:

- Financial reviews.
- Independent audits.
- Steward feedback.
- Operational learning.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance refinement.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement is a permanent stewardship responsibility.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Charter governs the entire Finance Canon.

All Finance Frameworks, Finance SOPs, financial policies, treasury standards, budgeting procedures, accounting guidance, and financial governance documents shall conform to this Charter unless superseded by the Constitution.
