OAS-TECH-110 — Data Management, Backup, and Disaster Recovery Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-TECH-110
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-TECH-006 — Data and Knowledge Architecture Framework
Owner: Chief Data and Infrastructure Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for managing data throughout its lifecycle while ensuring reliable backups, disaster recovery, business continuity, and restoration of Aureus technology systems.

Its purpose is to protect institutional knowledge, member information, operational data, and technology services from accidental loss, corruption, cyberattacks, infrastructure failures, and catastrophic events.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Production databases
- Knowledge repositories
- Member information
- Configuration data
- AI operational memory
- Infrastructure configurations
- Application data
- Backups
- Archives
- Disaster recovery systems

---

Objectives

Data management shall:

- Protect members.
- Preserve institutional knowledge.
- Ensure recoverability.
- Support operational continuity.
- Maintain data integrity.
- Minimize downtime.
- Verify recovery capability.
- Strengthen long-term resilience.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Data Steward

Responsible for:

- Defining backup requirements.
- Reviewing retention schedules.
- Coordinating recovery planning.
- Verifying data integrity.
- Maintaining documentation.

---

Infrastructure Steward

Responsible for:

- Operating backup systems.
- Monitoring backup success.
- Maintaining recovery infrastructure.
- Conducting restoration testing.

---

AI Operations Agent

Responsible for:

- Monitoring backup status.
- Detecting failures.
- Tracking retention periods.
- Generating recovery reports.
- Supporting restoration planning.
- Monitoring disaster recovery readiness.

Human stewards remain responsible for recovery authorization.

---

Prerequisites

Before production operation:

- Backup schedules are defined.
- Recovery procedures are documented.
- Recovery objectives are approved.
- Storage locations are secured.
- Restoration testing procedures are established.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Data Classification

Identify:

- Critical data.
- Sensitive data.
- Operational data.
- Archival data.
- Recovery priorities.

---

Step 2 — Backup Operations

Perform backups according to approved schedules.

Backups shall be:

- Automated where practical.
- Encrypted.
- Verified.
- Logged.
- Protected against unauthorized access.

---

Step 3 — Retention Management

Maintain documented retention schedules defining:

- Backup frequency.
- Retention duration.
- Archive requirements.
- Secure disposal procedures.

Retention shall comply with institutional governance requirements.

---

Step 4 — Recovery Testing

Periodically test restoration procedures by verifying:

- Backup integrity.
- Recovery accuracy.
- Recovery time.
- Recovery point.
- Operational readiness.

Testing confirms recoverability rather than assuming it.

---

Step 5 — Disaster Recovery

If a major disruption occurs:

- Assess the event.
- Activate recovery procedures.
- Restore priority systems.
- Verify service integrity.
- Resume operations safely.

Member protection remains the highest priority.

---

Step 6 — Business Continuity

Coordinate disaster recovery with business continuity planning to maintain essential institutional functions throughout recovery.

---

Step 7 — Post-Recovery Review

Document:

- Recovery timeline.
- Recovery effectiveness.
- Issues encountered.
- Lessons learned.
- Improvement recommendations.

---

Recovery Objectives

Technology services shall define:

- Recovery Time Objectives (RTO).
- Recovery Point Objectives (RPO).
- Critical service priorities.
- Recovery dependencies.

These objectives shall be reviewed regularly.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Backup success rate.
- Backup verification rate.
- Recovery testing completion.
- Recovery time.
- Recovery point achievement.
- Data integrity.
- Backup storage health.
- Disaster recovery readiness.

---

Exceptions

Temporary backup interruptions may occur during approved maintenance or emergency operational activities.

Such interruptions shall be documented, monitored, and resolved promptly.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Backup schedules.
- Recovery plans.
- Retention schedules.
- Recovery test reports.
- Disaster recovery plans.
- Recovery logs.
- Improvement records.

---

Continuous Improvement

Data protection, backup, and disaster recovery practices shall be reviewed regularly to improve resilience, automation, recovery speed, AI-assisted monitoring, operational continuity, and institutional learning.
