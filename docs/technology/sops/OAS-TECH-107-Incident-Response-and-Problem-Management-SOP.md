OAS-TECH-107 — Incident Response and Problem Management Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-TECH-107
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-TECH-010 — Technology Operations and Service Management Framework
Owner: Chief Technology Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for detecting, classifying, responding to, resolving, documenting, reviewing, and preventing technology incidents throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to minimize operational disruption, protect members, restore services quickly, preserve institutional trust, and continuously improve technology operations through structured learning.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Production incidents
- Service outages
- Security incidents
- Infrastructure failures
- AI operational incidents
- Database failures
- API disruptions
- Performance degradation
- Critical software defects

---

Objectives

Every incident response shall:

- Protect members.
- Restore service safely.
- Minimize operational disruption.
- Preserve evidence.
- Maintain clear communication.
- Identify root causes.
- Prevent recurrence.
- Strengthen institutional resilience.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Incident Commander

Responsible for:

- Coordinating response activities.
- Assigning responsibilities.
- Approving major operational decisions.
- Coordinating communications.
- Confirming incident closure.

---

Technical Response Team

Responsible for:

- Investigating the incident.
- Restoring affected services.
- Implementing corrective actions.
- Documenting technical findings.

---

AI Operations Agent

Responsible for:

- Detecting anomalies.
- Monitoring system health.
- Collecting operational evidence.
- Tracking response milestones.
- Preparing incident summaries.
- Supporting root cause analysis.

Human leadership remains responsible for operational decisions during incidents.

---

Incident Severity Levels

Incidents shall be classified according to operational impact.

Severity 1 — Critical

- Widespread service outage.
- Significant member impact.
- Critical security event.
- Immediate executive notification required.

Severity 2 — High

- Major service degradation.
- Multiple members affected.
- Urgent response required.

Severity 3 — Medium

- Limited operational disruption.
- Moderate member impact.
- Scheduled corrective action acceptable.

Severity 4 — Low

- Minor operational issue.
- Minimal member impact.
- Routine operational handling.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Detection

Identify incidents through:

- Monitoring systems.
- AI detection.
- Member reports.
- Steward reports.
- Automated alerts.

---

Step 2 — Classification

Determine:

- Severity.
- Scope.
- Impact.
- Affected services.
- Required response level.

---

Step 3 — Response

Initiate response activities including:

- Team coordination.
- Service protection.
- Risk containment.
- Stakeholder notification.

Member protection remains the highest operational priority.

---

Step 4 — Investigation

Collect evidence including:

- System logs.
- Metrics.
- Alerts.
- Configuration changes.
- Deployment history.
- Security events.

Evidence should be preserved throughout the investigation.

---

Step 5 — Resolution

Restore affected services using approved operational procedures.

Verify service stability before closing the incident.

---

Step 6 — Root Cause Analysis

Determine:

- Primary cause.
- Contributing factors.
- Process weaknesses.
- Technology weaknesses.
- Preventive improvements.

Focus on institutional learning rather than individual blame.

---

Step 7 — Post-Incident Review

Document:

- Timeline.
- Actions taken.
- Outcomes.
- Lessons learned.
- Improvement recommendations.
- Assigned follow-up actions.

---

Problem Management

Recurring incidents shall be managed as formal problems requiring:

- Trend analysis.
- Long-term corrective actions.
- Architectural improvements.
- Operational improvements.
- Verification of permanent resolution.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Mean time to detect.
- Mean time to acknowledge.
- Mean time to recover.
- Incident recurrence.
- Service availability.
- Member impact.
- Root cause completion.
- Improvement implementation.

---

Exceptions

Critical emergencies may require immediate operational action before complete investigation.

Emergency actions shall be documented and reviewed after service restoration.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Incident reports.
- Response timelines.
- Root cause analyses.
- Corrective action plans.
- Communication records.
- Operational metrics.
- Lessons learned.

---

Continuous Improvement

Incident management practices shall be reviewed regularly to improve response speed, operational resilience, AI-assisted detection, communication quality, and long-term institutional learning.
