OAS-TECH-102 — Artificial Intelligence and Automation Operations Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-TECH-102
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-TECH-004 — Artificial Intelligence and Automation Framework
Owner: Chief Technology Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for designing, developing, validating, deploying, monitoring, governing, improving, and retiring artificial intelligence systems, automation workflows, and AI agents throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure AI systems operate safely, transparently, ethically, constitutionally, and under meaningful human stewardship while advancing Aureus' mission.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- AI agents
- Large language models
- Machine learning systems
- Automation workflows
- Decision-support systems
- AI-assisted operations
- AI governance
- Model evaluation
- AI safety
- AI retirement

---

Objectives

AI operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain meaningful human oversight.
- Promote transparency.
- Strengthen institutional capability.
- Reduce operational risk.
- Support continuous improvement.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

AI Operations Steward

Responsible for:

- Coordinating AI deployments.
- Maintaining AI inventories.
- Monitoring operational performance.
- Managing AI documentation.
- Preparing governance reports.

---

AI Review Steward

Responsible for:

- Reviewing model performance.
- Evaluating AI safety.
- Confirming constitutional alignment.
- Approving production readiness.
- Supporting AI governance.

---

AI Engineering Steward

Responsible for:

- Model implementation.
- Workflow automation.
- Performance monitoring.
- Evaluation support.
- Documentation generation.
- Operational analytics.

Human stewards remain responsible for governance decisions, deployment approvals, constitutional interpretation, and operational accountability.

---

Prerequisites

Before deploying any AI capability:

- Purpose is documented.
- Requirements are approved.
- Risks are assessed.
- Human oversight is defined.
- Success measures are established.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Planning

Document:

- Intended purpose.
- Operational scope.
- Stakeholders.
- Risks.
- Governance requirements.
- Performance expectations.

---

Step 2 — Design and Development

Develop AI capabilities according to approved:

- Architecture.
- Safety standards.
- Privacy requirements.
- Security requirements.
- Documentation standards.

---

Step 3 — Validation

Evaluate:

- Accuracy.
- Reliability.
- Safety.
- Constitutional alignment.
- Operational readiness.
- Human oversight effectiveness.

Resolve material issues before deployment.

---

Step 4 — Deployment

Deploy AI capabilities through approved governance procedures.

Verify:

- Monitoring.
- Logging.
- Access controls.
- Rollback capability.
- Operational readiness.

---

Step 5 — Operational Monitoring

Monitor:

- Performance.
- Reliability.
- Safety.
- Usage.
- Operational incidents.
- Human feedback.

Investigate significant deviations promptly.

---

Step 6 — Continuous Improvement

Improve AI capabilities through:

- Performance evaluations.
- Steward feedback.
- Operational reviews.
- Safety assessments.
- Governance recommendations.

Document significant changes.

---

Step 7 — Retirement

When AI capabilities are retired:

- Notify stakeholders.
- Archive required documentation.
- Preserve governance records.
- Remove operational access.
- Document lessons learned.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- AI reliability.
- Operational effectiveness.
- Human oversight quality.
- Safety performance.
- Governance compliance.
- Member trust.
- Incident frequency.
- Continuous improvement outcomes.

---

Exceptions

Emergency AI changes may follow expedited governance procedures while maintaining documentation and retrospective review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- AI inventories.
- Design documentation.
- Evaluation reports.
- Deployment approvals.
- Monitoring records.
- Governance reports.
- Retirement documentation.

---

Continuous Improvement

AI operations shall improve through model evaluations, stewardship feedback, governance reviews, AI-assisted analysis, operational assessments, and lessons learned.
