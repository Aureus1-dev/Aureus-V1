OAS-TECH-101 — Technology Governance and Architecture Review Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-TECH-101
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-TECH-002 — Technology Governance Framework
Owner: Chief Technology Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for initiating, evaluating, approving, documenting, implementing, and reviewing technology architecture decisions throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure that every significant technology decision remains constitutionally aligned, technically sound, operationally sustainable, and supportive of Aureus' long-term mission.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Software architecture
- AI architecture
- Infrastructure architecture
- Security architecture
- Data architecture
- API architecture
- Platform engineering
- Major technology integrations
- Significant engineering standards

---

Objectives

Every architecture review shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Strengthen long-term maintainability.
- Improve security.
- Preserve architectural consistency.
- Reduce unnecessary complexity.
- Document institutional knowledge.
- Support future stewardship.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Architecture Requestor

Responsible for:

- Preparing the proposal.
- Providing supporting documentation.
- Explaining objectives.
- Responding to review questions.
- Supporting implementation.

---

Architecture Review Board

Responsible for:

- Evaluating proposals.
- Assessing architectural quality.
- Reviewing risks.
- Ensuring constitutional alignment.
- Approving or rejecting proposals.
- Recording decisions.

---

AI Technology Agent

Responsible for:

- Organizing documentation.
- Identifying architectural conflicts.
- Preparing comparison analyses.
- Checking framework compliance.
- Tracking Architecture Decision Records.
- Supporting review workflows.

Human reviewers retain final approval authority.

---

Prerequisites

Before review:

- Proposal is documented.
- Architecture diagrams are available.
- Alternatives have been evaluated.
- Risks are identified.
- Operational impact is described.
- Related ADRs are referenced where applicable.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Proposal

Document:

- Problem statement.
- Objectives.
- Proposed architecture.
- Alternatives considered.
- Benefits.
- Risks.
- Expected operational impact.

---

Step 2 — Preliminary Review

Confirm:

- Completeness.
- Alignment with Technology Frameworks.
- Documentation quality.
- Readiness for formal review.

---

Step 3 — Technical Evaluation

Evaluate:

- Security.
- Scalability.
- Reliability.
- Maintainability.
- Performance.
- Interoperability.
- Operational complexity.
- AI implications where applicable.

---

Step 4 — Governance Review

Confirm alignment with:

- Constitution.
- Technology Charter.
- Engineering standards.
- Security requirements.
- Data governance.
- Operational objectives.

---

Step 5 — Decision

The Architecture Review Board may:

- Approve.
- Approve with conditions.
- Request revisions.
- Reject with documented rationale.

Every decision shall be recorded through an Architecture Decision Record (ADR) where required.

---

Step 6 — Implementation

Approved architecture shall be implemented according to documented engineering and operational procedures.

Implementation shall remain traceable to the approved decision.

---

Step 7 — Post-Implementation Review

Evaluate:

- Architectural effectiveness.
- Operational outcomes.
- Unexpected impacts.
- Lessons learned.
- Improvement opportunities.

Update documentation where appropriate.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Architecture review completion time.
- ADR completion rate.
- Standards compliance.
- Architectural consistency.
- Security findings.
- Operational stability.
- Technical debt reduction.
- Steward satisfaction.

---

Exceptions

Emergency technology changes may follow expedited review procedures when necessary to protect members or maintain critical operations.

All expedited approvals shall receive a formal retrospective review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Architecture proposals.
- Review notes.
- Architecture Decision Records.
- Approval history.
- Architecture diagrams.
- Risk assessments.
- Post-implementation reviews.

---

Continuous Improvement

Architecture review practices shall be evaluated regularly to improve governance, engineering quality, documentation, AI support, operational resilience, and institutional learning.
