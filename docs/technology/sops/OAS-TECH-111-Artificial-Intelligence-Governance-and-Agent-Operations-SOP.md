OAS-TECH-111 — Artificial Intelligence Governance and Agent Operations Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-TECH-111
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-TECH-004 — Artificial Intelligence Architecture Framework
Owner: Chief AI Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical governance model for every Artificial Intelligence agent operated by Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure AI systems remain constitutionally aligned, human governed, transparent, secure, accountable, and continuously evaluated throughout their operational lifecycle.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- AI operational agents
- AI stewardship agents
- AI copilots
- AI assistants
- AI orchestration systems
- Large Language Models (LLMs)
- Retrieval systems
- AI tool integrations
- AI memory systems
- AI automation workflows

---

Objectives

Every AI system shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Operate within documented authority.
- Preserve institutional trust.
- Support human stewardship.
- Remain observable and auditable.
- Continuously improve.
- Operate safely throughout its lifecycle.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Chief AI Steward

Responsible for:

- AI governance.
- Agent approval.
- Policy oversight.
- Risk management.
- Lifecycle governance.
- Constitutional compliance.

---

AI Agent Owner

Responsible for:

- Defining the agent's mission.
- Maintaining documentation.
- Monitoring performance.
- Coordinating improvements.
- Requesting retirement when appropriate.

---

AI Agent

Every AI agent shall operate only within its documented:

- Mission.
- Authority.
- Responsibilities.
- Approved tools.
- Knowledge boundaries.
- Escalation procedures.

Agents shall never exceed their approved authority.

---

Agent Registration

Before deployment every AI agent shall have documented:

- Agent identifier.
- Name.
- Mission.
- Purpose.
- Human owner.
- Authority level.
- Approved tools.
- Memory configuration.
- Knowledge sources.
- Success metrics.
- Retirement criteria.

No production AI agent may operate without registration.

---

Authority Boundaries

Every AI agent shall have clearly documented:

- Permitted actions.
- Restricted actions.
- Escalation requirements.
- Human approval requirements.
- Operational limitations.

Authority shall follow the principle of least privilege.

---

Memory Governance

AI memory shall distinguish between:

- Session memory.
- Operational memory.
- Institutional knowledge.
- Long-term reference material.

Memory shall follow approved governance, privacy, retention, and access policies.

---

Tool Governance

AI agents may access only approved tools.

Each tool shall have documented:

- Purpose.
- Permissions.
- Operational boundaries.
- Logging requirements.
- Failure handling.

Tool usage shall remain fully auditable.

---

Human Oversight

Human stewards retain responsibility for:

- Constitutional interpretation.
- Ethical decisions.
- Strategic planning.
- Member welfare.
- Approval of significant actions.
- Risk acceptance.

AI recommendations remain advisory unless explicitly authorized.

---

Monitoring and Evaluation

Every AI agent shall be continuously evaluated using:

- Accuracy.
- Reliability.
- Safety.
- Member outcomes.
- Operational performance.
- Constitutional compliance.
- Resource efficiency.

Performance shall be reviewed regularly.

---

Incident Management

If an AI agent behaves unexpectedly:

- Suspend operations when necessary.
- Protect members.
- Preserve operational evidence.
- Investigate root causes.
- Implement corrective actions.
- Review governance controls before returning to service.

---

Lifecycle Management

Every AI agent shall progress through documented stages:

- Proposal.
- Design.
- Registration.
- Development.
- Testing.
- Approval.
- Deployment.
- Monitoring.
- Improvement.
- Retirement.

Each stage requires documented governance.

---

Retirement

AI agents shall be retired through approved governance procedures.

Retirement activities include:

- Archive documentation.
- Preserve operational history.
- Remove unnecessary permissions.
- Update dependent systems.
- Document lessons learned.

Institutional knowledge shall remain preserved.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Agent availability.
- Accuracy.
- Escalation frequency.
- Human intervention rate.
- Member satisfaction.
- Constitutional compliance.
- Operational incidents.
- Continuous improvement progress.

---

Exceptions

Temporary operational exceptions may be approved only through documented AI governance procedures with defined safeguards, review dates, and responsible human oversight.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Agent registry.
- Governance approvals.
- Operational metrics.
- Evaluation reports.
- Audit logs.
- Incident reports.
- Improvement history.
- Retirement records.

---

Continuous Improvement

AI governance practices shall be reviewed regularly to improve safety, transparency, constitutional alignment, operational effectiveness, human-AI collaboration, and institutional learning.
