OAS-TECH-104 — Technology Canon Maintenance and Version Control Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-TECH-104
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-TECH-001 — Technology Charter
Owner: Chief Technology Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for proposing, reviewing, approving, versioning, publishing, interpreting, archiving, maintaining, and continuously improving the Aureus Technology Canon.

Its purpose is to preserve constitutional integrity, maintain institutional technical knowledge, ensure disciplined evolution of technology governance, and provide a transparent stewardship process for future technological development.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Technology Charters
- Technology Frameworks
- Technology SOPs
- Engineering standards
- Software development standards
- Artificial intelligence governance
- Infrastructure governance
- Platform operations
- Technical governance documentation

---

Objectives

Technology Canon governance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain historical continuity.
- Ensure version traceability.
- Support disciplined institutional evolution.
- Preserve technical knowledge.
- Strengthen future stewardship.

---

Guiding Principles

The Technology Canon shall evolve deliberately rather than reactively.

Every approved revision should strengthen engineering quality, architecture, operational excellence, responsible AI governance, technical resilience, or constitutional fidelity while preserving the complete historical record.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Chief Technology Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining the Technology Canon.
- Coordinating canonical reviews.
- Approving routine revisions within delegated authority.
- Preserving version history.
- Publishing approved revisions.

---

Technology Governance Council

Responsible for:

- Reviewing significant amendments.
- Evaluating constitutional alignment.
- Recommending improvements.
- Interpreting technology governance.
- Supporting long-term technical stewardship.

---

AI Technology Knowledge Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring document consistency.
- Detecting conflicts.
- Tracking revision history.
- Maintaining cross-references.
- Supporting technical research.
- Identifying improvement opportunities.

Human stewards retain final authority over all canonical technology revisions and interpretations.

---

Prerequisites

Before modifying any canonical technology document:

- Proposed revisions are documented.
- Supporting rationale is prepared.
- Related documents are identified.
- Technical implications are evaluated.
- Required reviewers are assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Change Proposal

Document:

- Proposed revision.
- Purpose.
- Technical justification.
- Expected benefits.
- Related documents.
- Anticipated institutional impacts.

---

Step 2 — Canonical Review

Evaluate:

- Constitutional alignment.
- Technical consistency.
- Governance implications.
- Operational impacts.
- Long-term institutional value.

---

Step 3 — Approval

The appropriate governance authority may:

- Approve.
- Approve with revisions.
- Return for additional review.
- Reject.

All approval decisions shall be documented.

---

Step 4 — Version Management

Every approved revision shall include:

- Version number.
- Revision date.
- Change summary.
- Approval record.
- Updated cross-references where applicable.

Superseded versions shall remain permanently archived.

---

Step 5 — Publication

Publish approved revisions within the official Aureus repository.

Update indexes, document references, and governance records where appropriate.

---

Step 6 — Archival

Archive prior versions while preserving:

- Original content.
- Approval history.
- Revision history.
- Historical context.

Institutional technical memory shall remain permanently available.

---

Step 7 — Periodic Review

Conduct scheduled reviews to identify:

- Obsolete guidance.
- Governance inconsistencies.
- Emerging technical needs.
- Opportunities for simplification.
- Technology improvement initiatives.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Review completion.
- Version accuracy.
- Cross-reference integrity.
- Canon consistency.
- Amendment quality.
- Publication timeliness.
- Historical preservation.
- Steward satisfaction.

---

Exceptions

Emergency technology revisions may follow expedited governance procedures when necessary to preserve operational continuity, address critical technical risks, or respond to urgent security issues.

Emergency revisions shall receive formal retrospective review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Amendment proposals.
- Review records.
- Approval records.
- Version histories.
- Archive records.
- Canon indexes.
- Interpretation guidance.
- Governance reports.

---

Continuous Improvement

Technology Canon governance shall improve through governance reviews, engineering assessments, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, documentation quality reviews, architecture evaluations, and institutional learning.
