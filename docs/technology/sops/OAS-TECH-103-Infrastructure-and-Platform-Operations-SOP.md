OAS-TECH-103 — Infrastructure and Platform Operations Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-TECH-103
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-TECH-005 — Infrastructure and Platform Operations Framework
Owner: Chief Technology Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for operating, monitoring, maintaining, securing, scaling, backing up, recovering, and continuously improving Aureus' technology infrastructure and platform services.

Its purpose is to ensure reliable, resilient, secure, observable, and maintainable platform operations that consistently support Aureus' mission and institutional stewardship.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Cloud infrastructure
- Platform engineering
- DevOps operations
- Infrastructure as Code
- Configuration management
- Observability
- Capacity planning
- Backup and recovery
- Operational monitoring
- Change management

---

Objectives

Platform operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain platform reliability.
- Strengthen operational resilience.
- Support secure operations.
- Promote automation.
- Enable continuous improvement.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Platform Operations Steward

Responsible for:

- Operating infrastructure.
- Maintaining platform services.
- Coordinating operational activities.
- Monitoring service health.
- Preparing operational reports.

---

Infrastructure Steward

Responsible for:

- Infrastructure provisioning.
- Capacity planning.
- Backup validation.
- Disaster recovery readiness.
- Infrastructure documentation.

---

AI Platform Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring infrastructure performance.
- Detecting operational anomalies.
- Supporting capacity forecasting.
- Prioritizing operational alerts.
- Preparing platform summaries.
- Tracking operational metrics.

Human stewards remain responsible for production changes, infrastructure approvals, disaster recovery activation, and governance decisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before operating production infrastructure:

- Infrastructure standards are implemented.
- Monitoring is operational.
- Backup procedures are validated.
- Operational responsibilities are assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Infrastructure Operations

Operate infrastructure according to approved:

- Configuration standards.
- Security standards.
- Capacity requirements.
- Operational procedures.
- Reliability objectives.

---

Step 2 — Monitoring

Continuously monitor:

- Service availability.
- Infrastructure health.
- Performance.
- Capacity.
- Resource utilization.
- Operational alerts.

Investigate significant deviations.

---

Step 3 — Change Management

Before implementing infrastructure changes:

- Document the change.
- Assess operational impact.
- Obtain required approvals.
- Validate rollback procedures.
- Schedule implementation appropriately.

---

Step 4 — Backup and Recovery

Maintain:

- Scheduled backups.
- Backup verification.
- Recovery testing.
- Disaster recovery readiness.
- Restoration documentation.

Recovery capability shall be tested periodically.

---

Step 5 — Capacity Planning

Review:

- Resource utilization.
- Growth trends.
- Performance metrics.
- Future operational requirements.

Implement capacity improvements proactively.

---

Step 6 — Operational Reporting

Prepare reports summarizing:

- Platform health.
- Availability.
- Capacity.
- Operational incidents.
- Infrastructure improvements.
- Outstanding operational risks.

---

Step 7 — Continuous Operations Review

Conduct periodic reviews of:

- Reliability.
- Performance.
- Automation effectiveness.
- Operational maturity.
- Governance compliance.
- Improvement opportunities.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Platform availability.
- Recovery readiness.
- Infrastructure reliability.
- Capacity utilization.
- Automation effectiveness.
- Operational efficiency.
- Service performance.
- Governance compliance.

---

Exceptions

Emergency infrastructure actions may follow expedited operational procedures while maintaining documentation and retrospective governance review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Infrastructure inventories.
- Configuration records.
- Change records.
- Backup logs.
- Recovery test results.
- Monitoring reports.
- Capacity plans.
- Operational reports.

---

Continuous Improvement

Platform operations shall improve through operational reviews, reliability assessments, steward feedback, AI-assisted analysis, disaster recovery exercises, and lessons learned.
