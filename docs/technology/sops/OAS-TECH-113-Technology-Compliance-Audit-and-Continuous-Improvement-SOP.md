OAS-TECH-113 — Technology Compliance, Audit, and Continuous Improvement Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-TECH-113
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-TECH-002 — Technology Governance Framework
Owner: Chief Technology Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for auditing technology systems, engineering practices, AI governance, security controls, documentation, operational procedures, and constitutional compliance throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure the Technology Canon remains accurate, effective, secure, measurable, continuously improving, and aligned with the long-term mission of Aureus.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Software systems
- AI systems
- Infrastructure
- Engineering practices
- Security controls
- Operational procedures
- Documentation
- Architecture standards
- Technology governance
- Production operations

---

Objectives

Technology audits shall:

- Protect members.
- Verify constitutional compliance.
- Improve engineering quality.
- Strengthen operational resilience.
- Reduce technology risk.
- Preserve institutional trust.
- Support continuous improvement.
- Measure organizational maturity.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Technology Audit Steward

Responsible for:

- Planning audits.
- Coordinating evidence collection.
- Preparing audit reports.
- Tracking corrective actions.
- Reporting audit outcomes.

---

Engineering and Operations Stewards

Responsible for:

- Providing requested evidence.
- Supporting audit activities.
- Implementing corrective actions.
- Verifying completed improvements.

---

AI Audit Agent

Responsible for:

- Collecting operational metrics.
- Reviewing documentation.
- Identifying compliance gaps.
- Detecting trends.
- Preparing audit summaries.
- Tracking remediation progress.

Human stewards remain responsible for audit conclusions and governance decisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before beginning an audit:

- Scope is defined.
- Audit criteria are documented.
- Required participants are identified.
- Relevant documentation is available.
- Previous audit findings are reviewed.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Audit Planning

Define:

- Audit objectives.
- Scope.
- Evaluation criteria.
- Timeline.
- Participants.
- Required evidence.

---

Step 2 — Evidence Collection

Collect:

- Architecture documentation.
- Engineering records.
- Security reports.
- Operational metrics.
- AI evaluation reports.
- Incident history.
- Change records.
- Compliance documentation.

Evidence shall remain traceable and verifiable.

---

Step 3 — Compliance Assessment

Evaluate compliance with:

- Constitution.
- Technology Frameworks.
- Technology SOPs.
- Engineering standards.
- Security requirements.
- AI governance.
- Operational procedures.

---

Step 4 — Findings

Document:

- Strengths.
- Risks.
- Nonconformities.
- Improvement opportunities.
- Maturity observations.

Audit findings should promote institutional learning rather than individual blame.

---

Step 5 — Corrective Actions

Assign:

- Responsible owners.
- Target completion dates.
- Success measures.
- Follow-up verification requirements.

Corrective actions shall be tracked until completion.

---

Step 6 — Follow-Up Verification

Verify that corrective actions:

- Have been completed.
- Address identified issues.
- Improve operational performance.
- Reduce future risk.

---

Step 7 — Continuous Improvement

Review audit trends to identify:

- Recurring issues.
- Systemic weaknesses.
- Technology maturity improvements.
- Governance enhancements.
- Engineering improvements.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Audit completion rate.
- Compliance percentage.
- Corrective action completion.
- Repeat findings.
- Technology maturity improvements.
- Security posture improvements.
- Documentation quality.
- Operational resilience.

---

Exceptions

Emergency operational conditions may temporarily delay scheduled audits.

Any delayed audit shall be rescheduled promptly and documented with justification.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Audit plans.
- Evidence inventories.
- Audit reports.
- Corrective action registers.
- Verification records.
- Compliance metrics.
- Maturity assessments.
- Lessons learned.

---

Continuous Improvement

Technology audit practices shall themselves be reviewed regularly to improve audit quality, governance effectiveness, AI-assisted analysis, engineering maturity, operational resilience, and institutional stewardship.
