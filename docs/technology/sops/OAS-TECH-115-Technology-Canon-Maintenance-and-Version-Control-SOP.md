OAS-TECH-115 — Technology Canon Maintenance and Version Control Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-TECH-115
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-TECH-002 — Technology Governance Framework
Owner: Chief Technology Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for governing, maintaining, versioning, reviewing, expanding, interpreting, archiving, and preserving the Aureus Technology Canon.

Its purpose is to ensure every technology framework, engineering standard, architecture document, and technology SOP evolves through disciplined governance while preserving institutional knowledge and constitutional continuity.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Technology Frameworks
- Technology SOPs
- Engineering standards
- Architecture standards
- Infrastructure standards
- AI governance documents
- Security standards
- Technology templates
- Technical governance policies
- Technology reference documentation

---

Objectives

Technology Canon governance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain historical continuity.
- Ensure version traceability.
- Support disciplined evolution.
- Preserve institutional knowledge.
- Strengthen future stewardship.

---

Guiding Principles

The Technology Canon shall evolve deliberately rather than reactively.

Every revision should improve clarity, governance, operational effectiveness, engineering quality, or constitutional alignment while preserving an accurate historical record.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Chief Technology Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining the Technology Canon.
- Coordinating reviews.
- Approving canonical revisions.
- Preserving version history.
- Overseeing publication.

---

Technology Governance Council

Responsible for:

- Reviewing proposed amendments.
- Evaluating constitutional alignment.
- Approving significant revisions.
- Interpreting ambiguous provisions.
- Recommending future improvements.

---

AI Knowledge Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring document consistency.
- Detecting conflicts.
- Tracking revisions.
- Maintaining cross-references.
- Supporting historical research.
- Identifying opportunities for improvement.

Human stewards retain final authority over every canonical change.

---

Prerequisites

Before modifying any canonical document:

- The proposed change is documented.
- Supporting rationale is provided.
- Related documents are identified.
- Potential impacts are evaluated.
- Review participants are assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Change Proposal

Document:

- Proposed revision.
- Purpose.
- Business justification.
- Technical justification.
- Related documents.
- Expected impacts.

---

Step 2 — Canonical Review

Evaluate:

- Constitutional alignment.
- Technical consistency.
- Operational impact.
- Engineering implications.
- Security considerations.
- AI governance implications.

---

Step 3 — Approval

The appropriate governance authority may:

- Approve.
- Approve with revisions.
- Return for additional review.
- Reject.

All approved revisions shall be recorded.

---

Step 4 — Version Management

Every approved revision shall include:

- Version number.
- Revision date.
- Change summary.
- Approval record.
- Historical reference.

Previous versions shall remain archived.

---

Step 5 — Publication

Publish approved revisions to the official Aureus repository.

Update cross-references and indexes as required.

---

Step 6 — Archival

Archive superseded versions while preserving:

- Original content.
- Revision history.
- Approval history.
- Historical context.

Institutional memory shall never be discarded without formal authorization.

---

Step 7 — Periodic Review

Review the Technology Canon regularly to identify:

- Obsolete guidance.
- Inconsistencies.
- Missing governance.
- Emerging technology needs.
- Opportunities for simplification.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Review completion.
- Version accuracy.
- Cross-reference integrity.
- Canon consistency.
- Amendment quality.
- Publication timeliness.
- Historical preservation.
- Steward satisfaction.

---

Exceptions

Emergency canonical revisions may follow expedited governance procedures when necessary to protect members, preserve operational continuity, or address critical security risks.

Emergency revisions shall receive formal retrospective review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Amendment proposals.
- Approval records.
- Version history.
- Archive records.
- Governance decisions.
- Cross-reference indexes.
- Review schedules.
- Canon maintenance reports.

---

Continuous Improvement

Technology Canon governance shall be reviewed regularly to improve clarity, governance quality, engineering consistency, institutional learning, AI-assisted knowledge management, and long-term stewardship.
