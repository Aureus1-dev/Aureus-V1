OAS-TECH-010 — Technology Operations and Service Management Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-TECH-010
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-TECH-002 — Technology Governance Framework
Owner: Chief Technology Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the canonical operational model for managing, operating, supporting, maintaining, and continuously improving all Aureus technology services.

Its purpose is to ensure technology services remain reliable, secure, resilient, observable, well-governed, and capable of supporting Aureus' mission over the long term.

---

Mission

Technology operations exist to provide dependable, continuously improving services that strengthen members, support stewards, enable AI systems, and preserve institutional trust.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Production services
- Technology operations
- Service ownership
- Operational monitoring
- Incident management
- Problem management
- Change management
- Capacity planning
- Maintenance
- Service continuity
- Operational readiness
- Service improvement

---

Guiding Principles

Technology operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Prioritize reliability.
- Preserve operational continuity.
- Encourage observability.
- Support rapid recovery.
- Promote continuous learning.
- Strengthen institutional resilience.

Operational excellence is an ongoing stewardship responsibility.

---

Service Ownership

Every production service shall have documented:

- Service owner
- Operational steward
- Business purpose
- Dependencies
- Criticality
- Recovery objectives
- Support procedures

Ownership shall remain clearly assigned.

---

Operational Readiness

Before production deployment, services should demonstrate readiness through:

- Architecture review
- Security review
- Testing
- Documentation
- Monitoring configuration
- Recovery procedures
- Operational runbooks
- Approval for production use

Readiness reduces operational risk.

---

Monitoring and Observability

Technology services shall provide:

- Health monitoring
- Metrics
- Logging
- Alerting
- Distributed tracing where appropriate
- Performance dashboards
- Capacity monitoring

Operational visibility supports responsible stewardship.

---

Incident Management

Technology incidents shall follow documented procedures for:

- Detection
- Classification
- Escalation
- Response
- Recovery
- Communication
- Post-incident review

Every significant incident shall contribute to institutional learning.

---

Change Management

Operational changes shall:

- Be documented
- Be reviewed
- Be approved
- Be tested
- Include rollback procedures
- Be monitored after implementation

Controlled change strengthens stability.

---

Capacity Planning

Technology operations shall regularly evaluate:

- Resource utilization
- Growth trends
- Performance limits
- Infrastructure needs
- Future demand

Capacity planning supports sustainable growth.

---

Maintenance

Routine maintenance shall include:

- Software updates
- Security patches
- Infrastructure maintenance
- Database optimization
- Backup verification
- Documentation review

Maintenance windows should minimize member disruption.

---

Service Levels

Technology services should define measurable objectives including:

- Availability
- Reliability
- Performance
- Recovery time
- Recovery point
- Response expectations

Performance should be continuously evaluated.

---

Documentation

Operational documentation shall include:

- Runbooks
- Service catalogs
- Architecture diagrams
- Support procedures
- Escalation paths
- Recovery procedures
- Operational metrics

Documentation supports continuity and stewardship.

---

Continuous Improvement

Technology operations shall improve through:

- Incident reviews
- Performance analysis
- Operational metrics
- Steward feedback
- Member feedback
- Security assessments
- AI-assisted operational analysis
- Lessons learned

Continuous improvement strengthens institutional resilience.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all production technology operations throughout Aureus.

Operational runbooks, service management procedures, production support standards, monitoring standards, incident procedures, maintenance standards, and Technology SOPs shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by higher constitutional authority or an approved Architecture Decision Record.
