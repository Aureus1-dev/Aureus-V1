OAS-TECH-004 — Artificial Intelligence and Automation Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-TECH-004
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-TECH-002 — Technology Governance and Architecture Framework
Owner: Chief Technology Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the canonical model for governing artificial intelligence, automation, agentic systems, machine learning, decision-support systems, and AI-assisted operations throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure AI serves the mission, remains constitutionally aligned, operates transparently, preserves meaningful human oversight, protects members, and strengthens institutional capability without replacing stewardship responsibility.

---

Mission

Artificial intelligence stewardship exists to responsibly augment human judgment, improve institutional effectiveness, automate appropriate work, expand knowledge, and increase Aureus' capacity to serve its members while preserving human dignity, accountability, and constitutional governance.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Artificial intelligence systems
- AI agents
- Automation
- Machine learning
- Large language models
- Decision-support systems
- AI-assisted operations
- Model governance
- AI safety
- Responsible AI

---

Guiding Principles

Artificial intelligence shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Strengthen human stewardship.
- Remain transparent where appropriate.
- Be proportionate to operational risk.
- Respect privacy and security.
- Promote continuous learning.

AI exists to assist stewards, not replace stewardship.

---

Human Oversight

Every significant AI capability shall maintain meaningful human oversight appropriate to its level of impact.

Human stewards remain responsible for:

- Final decisions.
- Constitutional interpretation.
- Governance approvals.
- Ethical judgment.
- Institutional accountability.

Responsibility may be assisted by AI but shall never be delegated entirely to AI.

---

AI Lifecycle

Every AI capability shall follow an approved lifecycle including:

- Discovery.
- Requirements definition.
- Design.
- Development.
- Validation.
- Deployment.
- Monitoring.
- Continuous improvement.
- Retirement.

Lifecycle governance shall remain documented and auditable.

---

Responsible AI

AI systems shall be designed to:

- Produce reliable outputs.
- Support explainability where appropriate.
- Minimize harmful bias.
- Protect confidential information.
- Respect legal obligations.
- Operate within approved governance boundaries.

---

AI Safety

AI safety controls shall include:

- Human approval gates where appropriate.
- Operational monitoring.
- Performance validation.
- Access controls.
- Security protections.
- Incident reporting.
- Periodic review.

Safety controls shall be proportional to system impact.

---

AI-Assisted Operations

AI may support:

- Research.
- Analysis.
- Draft generation.
- Knowledge retrieval.
- Workflow automation.
- Operational recommendations.
- Administrative assistance.

Operational authority remains with authorized human stewards.

---

Performance Measurement

AI governance shall evaluate:

- Mission impact.
- Reliability.
- Human oversight effectiveness.
- Operational efficiency.
- Safety performance.
- Member trust.
- Governance compliance.
- Continuous improvement.

Measurements shall strengthen institutional stewardship.

---

Continuous Improvement

AI governance shall improve through:

- Model evaluations.
- Steward feedback.
- Governance reviews.
- Operational assessments.
- AI safety testing.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens responsible AI stewardship.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all artificial intelligence and automation activities throughout Aureus.

All Technology SOPs, AI governance standards, automation procedures, model management processes, AI safety standards, agent governance policies, and AI operational documents shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Technology Charter.
