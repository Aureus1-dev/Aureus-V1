OAS-TECH-001 — Technology Charter

Canonical Designation: OAS-TECH-001
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-001 — Founding Charter
Owner: Chief Technology Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Charter establishes the constitutional foundation governing all technology systems developed, operated, maintained, and retired by Aureus.

It defines the principles, responsibilities, governance, and long-term objectives that guide every aspect of the Aureus technology ecosystem.

---

Mission

The Technology Canon exists to create secure, reliable, transparent, resilient, maintainable, and mission-aligned technology that enables Aureus to faithfully serve its members and support human stewardship through responsible use of technology and artificial intelligence.

---

Scope

This Charter governs all technology associated with Aureus, including:

- Software systems
- Artificial intelligence systems
- APIs
- Infrastructure
- Cloud services
- Security
- Data platforms
- Mobile applications
- Web applications
- Internal operational tools
- Developer platforms
- Automation systems
- Technology governance

---

Guiding Principles

Technology shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve privacy.
- Strengthen stewardship.
- Be secure by design.
- Be resilient by design.
- Be understandable.
- Be maintainable.
- Be appropriately documented.
- Support long-term institutional continuity.

Technology exists to serve people, not the reverse.

---

Technology Stewardship

Technology is a stewardship responsibility.

Every technological decision should strengthen:

- Members
- Communities
- Institutional trust
- Operational excellence
- Long-term sustainability
- Constitutional governance

---

Governance

Technology governance shall ensure:

- Constitutional alignment
- Responsible AI use
- Security oversight
- Architecture consistency
- Quality assurance
- Operational accountability
- Documentation
- Continuous improvement

---

Human Responsibility

Human stewards remain accountable for:

- Technology strategy
- Architectural decisions
- Security oversight
- Ethical judgment
- AI governance
- Production approvals
- Risk acceptance

Technology assists stewardship but does not replace it.

---

AI Principles

Artificial intelligence shall:

- Operate within approved authority.
- Remain transparent whenever practical.
- Support human decision-making.
- Escalate uncertainty.
- Protect members.
- Preserve institutional trust.
- Remain subject to governance.

---

Security

Security shall be incorporated throughout the technology lifecycle rather than treated as a separate activity.

Technology should minimize unnecessary risk while enabling responsible innovation.

---

Documentation

Technology shall maintain accurate documentation describing:

- Architecture
- Interfaces
- Infrastructure
- Operations
- Security
- AI systems
- Deployment procedures
- Recovery procedures

Institutional knowledge shall never depend solely upon individual memory.

---

Continuous Improvement

Technology governance shall promote continuous learning through:

- Operational reviews
- Architecture reviews
- Security assessments
- Performance monitoring
- Lessons learned
- Innovation
- Responsible experimentation

---

Canonical Relationship

This Charter governs every document contained within the Aureus Technology Canon.

All technology frameworks, engineering standards, architecture documents, security policies, infrastructure standards, AI governance documents, and technical procedures shall conform to this Charter unless superseded by the Aureus Constitution.
