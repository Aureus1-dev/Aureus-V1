OAS-TECH-006 — Data and Knowledge Architecture Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-TECH-006
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-TECH-002 — Technology Governance Framework
Owner: Chief Data and Knowledge Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the canonical architecture governing data, knowledge, institutional memory, metadata, analytics, search, retrieval, lineage, governance, and information lifecycle management throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure that information remains accurate, trustworthy, secure, discoverable, interoperable, and preserved across generations of members, stewards, AI systems, and technology platforms.

---

Mission

The Data and Knowledge Architecture exists to transform information into enduring institutional knowledge that strengthens members, supports stewardship, enables AI, and preserves the long-term memory of Aureus.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Operational data
- Member data
- Organizational data
- Knowledge repositories
- Institutional memory
- Metadata
- Search systems
- Retrieval systems
- Analytics
- Reporting
- Data lineage
- Data governance
- Long-term archives

---

Guiding Principles

Data and knowledge shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve institutional trust.
- Maintain accuracy.
- Support transparency where appropriate.
- Enable responsible AI.
- Remain governed throughout their lifecycle.
- Strengthen institutional learning.

Information is an institutional asset entrusted to Aureus for responsible stewardship.

---

Data Architecture

Data shall be organized through clearly defined domains with documented ownership, standardized models, and governed interfaces.

Architectural decisions should prioritize integrity, interoperability, scalability, and maintainability.

---

Knowledge Architecture

Knowledge systems shall distinguish between:

- Operational knowledge
- Institutional knowledge
- Educational knowledge
- Historical records
- AI reference knowledge
- Public knowledge

Each category shall have documented governance and lifecycle requirements.

---

Metadata

All managed information should include appropriate metadata supporting:

- Classification
- Ownership
- Source
- Version
- Lineage
- Retention
- Access permissions
- Review history

Metadata strengthens discoverability and governance.

---

Search and Retrieval

Search systems shall:

- Retrieve authoritative information.
- Respect access controls.
- Support traceability.
- Prioritize relevance.
- Preserve source integrity.

Retrieval should strengthen decision-making rather than create uncertainty.

---

Institutional Memory

Institutional memory shall preserve:

- Constitutional documents
- Operational decisions
- Architecture decisions
- Lessons learned
- Historical records
- Governance history
- Organizational knowledge

Institutional memory shall survive personnel, technology, and organizational changes.

---

Data Governance

Data governance shall define:

- Ownership
- Stewardship
- Quality standards
- Privacy requirements
- Access management
- Lifecycle management
- Accountability

Human stewards remain responsible for governance decisions.

---

Analytics

Analytics shall support:

- Operational improvement
- Strategic planning
- Member outcomes
- Stewardship effectiveness
- AI evaluation
- Organizational learning

Analytics should inform—not replace—human judgment.

---

Information Lifecycle

Information shall progress through documented lifecycle stages including:

- Creation
- Validation
- Classification
- Active use
- Review
- Archival
- Retirement
- Secure disposal

Lifecycle governance protects institutional integrity.

---

Security and Privacy

Data and knowledge systems shall incorporate appropriate safeguards supporting:

- Confidentiality
- Integrity
- Availability
- Traceability
- Authorized access
- Privacy protection

Security shall remain integrated throughout the information lifecycle.

---

Continuous Improvement

Data and knowledge architecture shall evolve through:

- Operational feedback
- Steward recommendations
- Member feedback
- Technology improvements
- AI-assisted analysis
- Governance reviews
- Lessons learned

Continuous improvement strengthens institutional intelligence.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs every data, knowledge, metadata, analytics, retrieval, reporting, and institutional memory capability operated by Aureus.

All data models, databases, search platforms, analytics systems, AI knowledge systems, reporting platforms, and related technical procedures shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by higher constitutional authority.
