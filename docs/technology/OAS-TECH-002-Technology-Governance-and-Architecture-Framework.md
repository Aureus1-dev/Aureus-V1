OAS-TECH-002 — Technology Governance and Architecture Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-TECH-002
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-TECH-001 — Technology Charter
Owner: Chief Technology Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the constitutional model for technology governance, enterprise architecture, engineering oversight, technical decision-making, architectural review, technical standards, and institutional accountability throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure every technology decision advances the mission, preserves constitutional alignment, strengthens institutional resilience, and supports sustainable long-term engineering excellence.

---

Mission

Technology governance exists to provide disciplined stewardship of Aureus' technology ecosystem through sound architecture, responsible engineering, operational excellence, and continuous improvement.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Technology governance
- Enterprise architecture
- Engineering standards
- Architectural reviews
- Technical decision-making
- Technical debt management
- AI-assisted engineering operations
- Change governance
- Technology strategy
- Governance accountability

---

Guiding Principles

Technology governance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote maintainability.
- Encourage responsible innovation.
- Strengthen operational resilience.
- Reduce unnecessary complexity.
- Support continuous improvement.

Technology decisions shall prioritize long-term institutional value over short-term convenience.

---

Technology Authority

Technology authority shall be exercised according to:

- The Constitution.
- Approved governance documents.
- Applicable law.
- Delegated authority.
- Stewardship responsibilities.

Authority shall remain transparent, documented, and accountable.

---

Enterprise Architecture

Enterprise architecture shall provide:

- Clear system boundaries.
- Modular design.
- Scalability.
- Reliability.
- Interoperability.
- Maintainability.
- Security by design.

Architecture shall evolve deliberately through approved governance.

---

Engineering Standards

Engineering activities shall follow approved standards for:

- Software design.
- Documentation.
- Testing.
- Code quality.
- Change management.
- Operational readiness.

Standards shall be consistently applied across all technology initiatives.

---

Technical Debt Management

Technical debt shall be:

- Identified.
- Documented.
- Prioritized.
- Reviewed.
- Reduced where appropriate.

New technical debt shall require documented justification.

---

AI-Assisted Engineering

AI may assist through:

- Code analysis.
- Architecture reviews.
- Documentation generation.
- Testing support.
- Dependency analysis.
- Engineering reporting.

Human stewards remain responsible for technical approvals, architectural decisions, production releases, and governance actions.

---

Governance Reviews

Technology governance shall periodically review:

- Architectural consistency.
- Engineering quality.
- Technical risks.
- Operational performance.
- Technical debt.
- Improvement opportunities.

Reviews shall produce measurable institutional improvements.

---

Performance Measurement

Technology governance shall evaluate:

- Architecture compliance.
- Engineering quality.
- Technical debt trends.
- Platform reliability.
- Delivery effectiveness.
- Governance maturity.
- Operational resilience.
- Mission support.

Measurements shall strengthen institutional stewardship.

---

Continuous Improvement

Technology governance shall improve through:

- Architecture reviews.
- Engineering assessments.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance evaluations.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens Aureus' long-term technological capability.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all technology governance and enterprise architecture activities throughout Aureus.

All Technology Frameworks, Technology SOPs, engineering standards, AI governance policies, infrastructure procedures, development practices, and technical governance documents shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Technology Charter.
