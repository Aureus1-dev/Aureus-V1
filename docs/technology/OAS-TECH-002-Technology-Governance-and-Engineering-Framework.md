OAS-TECH-002 — Technology Governance and Engineering Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-TECH-002
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-TECH-001 — Technology and Digital Stewardship Charter
Owner: Chief Technology Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the constitutional model for technology governance, software engineering, architecture management, AI-assisted development, platform operations, engineering accountability, technology performance management, and continuous engineering improvement throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure technology systems are designed, developed, operated, and maintained responsibly while preserving constitutional alignment, protecting members, strengthening institutional resilience, and faithfully advancing Aureus' mission.

---

Mission

Technology governance exists to transform constitutional principles into secure, reliable, scalable, and sustainable digital systems that strengthen stewardship and enable long-term institutional flourishing.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Technology governance
- Software engineering
- Architecture management
- Platform operations
- AI-assisted development
- Engineering accountability
- Technology performance
- Technology documentation
- Digital infrastructure
- Continuous improvement

---

Guiding Principles

Technology governance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote secure engineering.
- Encourage responsible innovation.
- Strengthen accountability.
- Foster resilience.
- Support continuous learning.

Technology decisions shall prioritize long-term stewardship over short-term convenience.

---

Technology Authority

Technology authority shall be exercised according to:

- The Constitution.
- Approved governance documents.
- Applicable law.
- Delegated authority.
- Stewardship responsibilities.

Authority shall remain documented, transparent, and accountable.

---

Technology Governance

Technology governance shall establish:

- Clearly defined engineering responsibilities.
- Architecture oversight.
- Platform governance.
- AI governance.
- Engineering standards.
- Continuous improvement.

Governance shall strengthen both innovation and institutional stability.

---

Engineering Stewardship

Engineering stewardship shall:

- Develop secure software.
- Maintain reliable platforms.
- Preserve system quality.
- Document technical knowledge.
- Support responsible automation.
- Strengthen institutional capability.

Every technology steward shares responsibility for engineering excellence.

---

AI-Assisted Engineering

AI may assist through:

- Code generation.
- Documentation support.
- Architecture analysis.
- Quality recommendations.
- Engineering analytics.
- Administrative coordination.

Human stewards remain responsible for architecture decisions, production approvals, security decisions, governance actions, and institutional accountability.

---

Governance Reviews

Technology governance shall periodically review:

- Architecture quality.
- Engineering performance.
- Platform reliability.
- Security posture.
- Technology risks.
- Improvement opportunities.

Reviews shall produce measurable institutional improvements.

---

Performance Measurement

Technology governance shall evaluate:

- Platform reliability.
- Engineering quality.
- Architecture consistency.
- Security performance.
- System availability.
- Governance maturity.
- Institutional resilience.
- Mission support.

Measurements shall strengthen technological stewardship.

---

Continuous Improvement

Technology governance shall improve through:

- Architecture reviews.
- Engineering assessments.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance evaluations.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens Aureus' technology excellence.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all technology governance and engineering activities throughout Aureus.

All Technology Frameworks, Technology SOPs, engineering standards, architecture documentation, AI governance practices, platform procedures, infrastructure standards, and technology governance documents shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Technology Charter.
