OAS-TECH-001 — Technology and Digital Stewardship Charter

Canonical Designation: OAS-TECH-001
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-001 — Founding Charter
Owner: Chief Technology Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Charter establishes the constitutional authority governing technology architecture, software engineering, artificial intelligence systems, digital infrastructure, cybersecurity, automation, platform services, technology operations, and long-term technological stewardship throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure Aureus develops, operates, and evolves technology responsibly while preserving constitutional alignment, protecting members, strengthening institutional resilience, and faithfully advancing its mission.

---

Mission

Technology stewardship exists to create secure, reliable, ethical, resilient, and sustainable technology that empowers people, strengthens stewardship, and enables Aureus to fulfill its constitutional mission for generations.

---

Scope

This Charter governs:

- Technology governance
- Software engineering
- Artificial intelligence
- Digital infrastructure
- Cybersecurity
- Data platforms
- Automation
- Platform architecture
- Technology operations
- Digital stewardship

---

Guiding Principles

Technology stewardship shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote security.
- Encourage responsible innovation.
- Strengthen resilience.
- Foster transparency.
- Support continuous learning.

Technology shall always remain accountable to human stewardship and constitutional governance.

---

Technology Authority

Technology authority shall be exercised according to:

- The Constitution.
- Approved governance documents.
- Applicable law.
- Delegated authority.
- Stewardship responsibilities.

Authority shall remain documented, transparent, and accountable.

---

Technology Governance

Technology governance shall include:

- Clearly defined responsibilities.
- Architecture oversight.
- Engineering standards.
- AI governance.
- Cybersecurity governance.
- Continuous improvement.

Governance shall balance innovation with institutional responsibility.

---

Digital Stewardship

Technology stewardship shall:

- Protect digital assets.
- Maintain secure infrastructure.
- Deliver reliable services.
- Support institutional knowledge.
- Encourage responsible innovation.
- Preserve long-term sustainability.

Every technology steward shares responsibility for maintaining institutional trust.

---

AI Governance

Artificial intelligence shall be governed through:

- Human oversight.
- Transparency.
- Accountability.
- Safety.
- Constitutional alignment.
- Continuous evaluation.

AI shall support, but never replace, accountable human stewardship.

---

Continuous Improvement

Technology governance shall improve through:

- Architecture reviews.
- Engineering assessments.
- Steward feedback.
- Governance evaluations.
- Lessons learned.
- Responsible innovation.

Continuous improvement strengthens Aureus' technological stewardship.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Charter governs the entire Technology Canon.

All Technology Frameworks, Technology SOPs, engineering standards, AI governance documents, cybersecurity policies, infrastructure procedures, and technology governance practices shall conform to this Charter unless superseded by the Constitution.
