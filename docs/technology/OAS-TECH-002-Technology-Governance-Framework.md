OAS-TECH-002 — Technology Governance Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-TECH-002
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-TECH-001 — Technology Charter
Owner: Chief Technology Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the governance system for all technology decisions within Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure technology remains secure, maintainable, constitutionally aligned, ethically governed, and capable of serving the mission of Aureus over generations.

---

Mission

Technology governance exists to provide consistent decision-making, responsible oversight, architectural integrity, operational excellence, and long-term sustainability for every technology system operated by Aureus.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Software architecture
- Engineering standards
- Artificial intelligence
- Infrastructure
- Security
- APIs
- Data platforms
- Cloud services
- Development practices
- Technology investments
- Technical operations

---

Guiding Principles

Technology governance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Encourage simplicity.
- Promote maintainability.
- Support responsible innovation.
- Reduce unnecessary complexity.
- Strengthen institutional resilience.

---

Governance Structure

Technology governance consists of:

- Stewardship Council
- Chief Technology Steward
- Technology Leadership
- Engineering Stewards
- AI Governance Stewards
- Security Stewards
- Infrastructure Stewards
- Architecture Review processes

Each role shall operate within documented authority.

---

Decision Authority

Technology decisions shall be made at the lowest appropriate level while preserving constitutional oversight for significant matters.

Major decisions involving architecture, AI governance, security, privacy, or institutional risk require documented review and approval.

---

Architecture Governance

Technology architecture shall be:

- Documented
- Reviewed
- Version controlled
- Secure
- Scalable
- Maintainable
- Consistent

Significant architectural changes should be evaluated before implementation.

---

Engineering Standards

Engineering activities shall follow documented standards governing:

- Coding practices
- Documentation
- Testing
- Version control
- Deployment
- Security
- Operational readiness

---

AI Governance

AI systems shall operate according to approved governance principles.

AI shall remain:

- Transparent where practical
- Auditable
- Secure
- Human supervised
- Constitutionally aligned

Human stewards retain responsibility for significant decisions.

---

Technology Risk

Technology governance shall identify, evaluate, monitor, and reduce risks involving:

- Security
- Privacy
- Reliability
- Performance
- Compliance
- AI
- Infrastructure
- Operational continuity

---

Architecture Decision Records

Significant technical decisions should be documented through Architecture Decision Records (ADRs).

Each ADR should include:

- Decision
- Context
- Alternatives considered
- Rationale
- Consequences
- Approval

ADRs preserve institutional technical knowledge.

---

Review Process

Technology governance should periodically review:

- Architecture
- Security
- AI systems
- Infrastructure
- Performance
- Operational metrics
- Technical debt
- Documentation

Reviews strengthen long-term technology stewardship.

---

Continuous Improvement

Technology governance shall evolve through:

- Engineering feedback
- Operational experience
- Security assessments
- AI evaluations
- Architecture reviews
- Lessons learned

Continuous improvement strengthens institutional capability.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all technology governance activities throughout Aureus.

All engineering standards, architecture documents, AI governance standards, security frameworks, infrastructure standards, and technical procedures shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Technology Charter.
