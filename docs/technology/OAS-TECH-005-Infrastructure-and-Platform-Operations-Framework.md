OAS-TECH-005 — Infrastructure and Platform Operations Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-TECH-005
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-TECH-002 — Technology Governance and Architecture Framework
Owner: Chief Technology Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the canonical model for governing Aureus' infrastructure, cloud platforms, networking, platform engineering, observability, DevOps, site reliability engineering (SRE), infrastructure automation, disaster recovery, and operational resilience.

Its purpose is to provide a secure, reliable, scalable, observable, maintainable, and resilient technology platform that enables Aureus' mission while preserving constitutional alignment and long-term sustainability.

---

Mission

Infrastructure stewardship exists to design, operate, monitor, and continuously improve the technology foundation that supports every Aureus system, service, steward, and member.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Cloud infrastructure
- Platform engineering
- Networking
- DevOps
- Site Reliability Engineering (SRE)
- Infrastructure as Code
- Observability
- Monitoring
- Capacity management
- Disaster recovery

---

Guiding Principles

Infrastructure stewardship shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Prioritize reliability.
- Build for scalability.
- Automate responsibly.
- Strengthen resilience.
- Promote operational excellence.

Infrastructure should enable innovation without sacrificing stability.

---

Infrastructure Architecture

Infrastructure shall be designed to provide:

- High availability.
- Scalability.
- Fault tolerance.
- Security.
- Performance.
- Maintainability.
- Disaster resilience.

Architecture shall evolve through approved governance.

---

Platform Operations

Platform operations shall include:

- Continuous monitoring.
- Capacity planning.
- Configuration management.
- Change management.
- Release support.
- Performance optimization.
- Service health reporting.

Operational practices shall remain documented and measurable.

---

Observability

Technology platforms shall maintain appropriate observability through:

- Metrics.
- Logging.
- Tracing.
- Alerting.
- Health checks.
- Operational dashboards.

Observability shall support rapid diagnosis and continuous improvement.

---

Infrastructure Automation

Automation may support:

- Provisioning.
- Configuration management.
- Deployment.
- Scaling.
- Backup.
- Recovery.
- Routine maintenance.

Automation shall include safeguards against unintended consequences.

---

Operational Resilience

Infrastructure resilience shall include:

- Redundancy where appropriate.
- Backup strategies.
- Disaster recovery planning.
- Capacity reserves.
- Recovery testing.
- Operational exercises.

Mission-critical services shall receive priority protection.

---

AI-Assisted Platform Operations

AI may assist through:

- Capacity forecasting.
- Performance analysis.
- Infrastructure monitoring.
- Alert prioritization.
- Operational reporting.
- Automation recommendations.

Human stewards remain responsible for production changes, infrastructure approvals, risk acceptance, and governance decisions.

---

Performance Measurement

Infrastructure governance shall evaluate:

- Platform availability.
- Reliability.
- Performance.
- Recovery readiness.
- Automation effectiveness.
- Operational efficiency.
- Capacity utilization.
- Mission support.

Measurements shall strengthen institutional stewardship.

---

Continuous Improvement

Infrastructure operations shall improve through:

- Reliability reviews.
- Operational assessments.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Disaster recovery exercises.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens Aureus' technology foundation.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all infrastructure and platform operations throughout Aureus.

All Technology SOPs, DevOps procedures, infrastructure standards, cloud governance documents, observability standards, disaster recovery procedures, and platform engineering practices shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Technology Charter.
