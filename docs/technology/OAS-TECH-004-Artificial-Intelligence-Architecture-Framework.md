OAS-TECH-004 — Artificial Intelligence Architecture Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-TECH-004
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-TECH-002 — Technology Governance Framework
Owner: Chief AI Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the canonical architecture governing all Artificial Intelligence systems operated by Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure AI capabilities remain secure, transparent, governable, constitutionally aligned, ethically responsible, observable, maintainable, and accountable throughout their lifecycle.

---

Mission

Artificial Intelligence exists to strengthen human stewardship by expanding the ability of Aureus to serve members, improve decision-making, automate appropriate work, preserve institutional knowledge, and responsibly scale the mission.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- AI operational agents
- AI orchestration
- Large Language Models (LLMs)
- Retrieval systems
- Memory systems
- Prompt governance
- AI tool integrations
- AI evaluation
- AI observability
- AI safety systems
- AI lifecycle management

---

Guiding Principles

Every AI system shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Strengthen stewardship.
- Remain human governed.
- Operate transparently where practical.
- Respect documented authority.
- Escalate uncertainty.
- Support continuous learning.

AI exists to augment human stewardship, not replace it.

---

AI Architecture

Every AI capability should be designed using modular components including:

- Orchestration layer
- Reasoning layer
- Memory layer
- Retrieval layer
- Tool layer
- Safety layer
- Observability layer
- Governance layer

Each layer shall have clearly defined responsibilities and interfaces.

---

AI Agents

Every AI operational agent shall have documented:

- Mission
- Authority
- Responsibilities
- Constraints
- Inputs
- Outputs
- Human owner
- Success measures
- Retirement criteria

Agents should perform only those functions for which they have been authorized.

---

Memory Architecture

AI memory should distinguish between:

- Session memory
- Operational memory
- Institutional knowledge
- Long-term reference material

Memory retention and access shall follow approved governance and privacy requirements.

---

Retrieval Architecture

Retrieval systems shall:

- Access approved knowledge sources.
- Preserve source integrity.
- Support traceability.
- Return relevant information.
- Minimize unsupported conclusions.

Institutional knowledge shall remain authoritative.

---

Tool Architecture

AI tools shall:

- Operate through documented interfaces.
- Respect authorization boundaries.
- Record operational activity.
- Fail safely.
- Support auditing.

Tool usage should remain observable and reviewable.

---

Human Oversight

Human stewards remain responsible for:

- Strategic decisions.
- Constitutional interpretation.
- Ethical judgments.
- Member welfare.
- Significant operational approvals.
- AI governance.

AI recommendations shall remain subject to human review whenever required.

---

Safety

AI systems shall include safeguards supporting:

- Operational boundaries.
- Escalation.
- Security.
- Privacy.
- Reliability.
- Responsible automation.
- Risk reduction.

Safety shall be designed into every AI workflow.

---

Observability

AI systems shall support:

- Logging.
- Metrics.
- Evaluation.
- Monitoring.
- Audit trails.
- Operational reporting.
- Incident investigation.

Observability strengthens accountability.

---

Evaluation

AI capabilities should be evaluated using:

- Accuracy
- Reliability
- Member outcomes
- Steward feedback
- Operational performance
- Safety
- Constitutional alignment
- Resource efficiency

Evaluations should guide continuous improvement.

---

Lifecycle Management

AI systems shall progress through documented lifecycle stages including:

- Proposal
- Design
- Development
- Testing
- Approval
- Deployment
- Monitoring
- Improvement
- Retirement

Each stage shall include documented governance.

---

Continuous Improvement

AI architecture shall evolve through:

- Operational experience
- Steward feedback
- Member feedback
- Security reviews
- Technology advancements
- Responsible experimentation
- Lessons learned

Continuous improvement strengthens institutional capability.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs every Artificial Intelligence system operated by Aureus.

All AI engineering standards, AI governance policies, prompt standards, evaluation procedures, operational AI workflows, and AI-related SOPs shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by higher constitutional authority.
