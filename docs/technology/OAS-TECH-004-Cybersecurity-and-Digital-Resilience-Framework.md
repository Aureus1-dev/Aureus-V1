OAS-TECH-004 — Cybersecurity and Digital Resilience Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-TECH-004
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-TECH-002 — Technology Governance and Engineering Framework
Owner: Chief Technology Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the constitutional model for cybersecurity governance, digital resilience, identity and access management, threat detection, incident response, vulnerability management, secure software development, privacy protection, and continuous cybersecurity improvement throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure Aureus protects its members, technology systems, digital assets, and institutional knowledge through disciplined cybersecurity stewardship while preserving constitutional alignment and long-term institutional resilience.

---

Mission

Cybersecurity stewardship exists to preserve the confidentiality, integrity, availability, resilience, and trustworthiness of Aureus' digital ecosystem while enabling the mission through secure and responsible technology.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Cybersecurity governance
- Digital resilience
- Identity and access management
- Threat detection
- Incident response
- Vulnerability management
- Secure software engineering
- Privacy protection
- Security monitoring
- Continuous improvement

---

Guiding Principles

Cybersecurity stewardship shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote security by design.
- Strengthen resilience.
- Encourage responsible innovation.
- Foster accountability.
- Support continuous learning.

Security shall enable trustworthy operations rather than unnecessarily restrict them.

---

Cybersecurity Governance

Cybersecurity governance shall establish:

- Security policies.
- Risk-based security controls.
- Identity governance.
- Security oversight.
- Incident governance.
- Continuous improvement.

Governance shall balance security, usability, and mission effectiveness.

---

Identity and Access Management

Identity governance shall include:

- Identity verification.
- Least-privilege access.
- Role-based authorization.
- Multi-factor authentication where appropriate.
- Access reviews.
- Timely access removal.

Access shall be granted according to stewardship responsibilities.

---

Secure Engineering

Technology systems shall be developed through:

- Secure design.
- Secure coding practices.
- Security testing.
- Code review.
- Dependency management.
- Continuous security improvement.

Security shall be integrated throughout the software lifecycle.

---

Threat Detection and Response

Cybersecurity operations shall include:

- Security monitoring.
- Threat detection.
- Incident identification.
- Investigation.
- Containment.
- Recovery.

Responses shall remain timely, documented, and coordinated.

---

Privacy Stewardship

Privacy protections shall include:

- Data minimization.
- Appropriate access controls.
- Responsible data handling.
- Lawful processing.
- Member transparency.
- Continuous review.

Privacy is a stewardship responsibility.

---

AI-Assisted Cybersecurity

AI may assist through:

- Threat detection.
- Security monitoring.
- Vulnerability analysis.
- Security analytics.
- Incident summaries.
- Administrative support.

Human stewards remain responsible for security approvals, incident leadership, access decisions, governance actions, and institutional accountability.

---

Performance Measurement

Cybersecurity governance shall evaluate:

- Security posture.
- Incident response effectiveness.
- Vulnerability remediation.
- Access governance.
- System resilience.
- Governance maturity.
- Institutional trust.
- Mission support.

Measurements shall strengthen digital stewardship.

---

Continuous Improvement

Cybersecurity governance shall improve through:

- Security assessments.
- Incident reviews.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance evaluations.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens Aureus' digital resilience.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all cybersecurity, digital resilience, identity management, secure engineering, privacy protection, and security governance activities throughout Aureus.

All Technology SOPs, cybersecurity procedures, identity management standards, secure development practices, incident response plans, privacy documentation, and security governance documents shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Technology Charter.
