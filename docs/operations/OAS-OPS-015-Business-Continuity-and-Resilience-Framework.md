OAS-OPS-015 — Business Continuity and Resilience Framework

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-001 — Operations Charter
Related Documents: OAS-OPS-002 through OAS-OPS-014
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Last Updated: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This document establishes the canonical framework governing business continuity, operational resilience, disaster preparedness, incident recovery, and institutional continuity throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure that Aureus can continue fulfilling its mission despite disruptions, emergencies, technological failures, leadership transitions, financial challenges, or other unforeseen events.

---

Mission

Business Continuity and Resilience exists to preserve the ability of Aureus to serve its members, protect institutional knowledge, maintain operational integrity, and recover responsibly from disruptive events.

---

Guiding Principles

Operational resilience shall:

- Protect members.
- Preserve institutional trust.
- Maintain mission continuity.
- Safeguard critical knowledge.
- Encourage preparedness.
- Support timely recovery.
- Learn from disruptions.
- Improve future resilience.

Preparedness is an ongoing responsibility rather than a one-time activity.

---

Scope

This framework applies to:

- Technology disruptions
- Operational failures
- Cybersecurity incidents
- Natural disasters
- Financial disruptions
- Leadership transitions
- Public health emergencies
- Supplier or partner disruptions
- Reputational events
- Other significant operational interruptions

---

Critical Operations

Aureus shall identify and periodically review operations that are essential to continued service.

Critical operations should receive priority during disruption planning and recovery activities.

---

Continuity Planning

Continuity plans should define:

- Critical functions
- Responsible owners
- Required resources
- Backup procedures
- Communication plans
- Recovery priorities
- Dependencies
- Verification methods

Plans should remain current and periodically tested.

---

Incident Response

When significant disruptions occur, Aureus should:

1. Detect the incident.
2. Assess its impact.
3. Protect members and critical assets.
4. Activate appropriate response procedures.
5. Communicate with relevant stakeholders.
6. Restore essential operations.
7. Review the response.
8. Implement improvements.

Timely, coordinated action strengthens institutional resilience.

---

Recovery

Recovery efforts should prioritize:

- Member support
- Operational stability
- Data integrity
- Communication
- Security
- Documentation
- Restoration of critical services

Recovery should balance urgency with responsible stewardship.

---

Knowledge Preservation

Critical institutional knowledge shall be protected through:

- Secure documentation
- Version control
- Redundant storage
- Succession planning
- Periodic verification

Institutional memory should survive personnel and technology changes.

---

Leadership Continuity

Every critical leadership role should maintain:

- Documented responsibilities
- Delegation procedures
- Successor preparation
- Knowledge transfer guidance

Leadership continuity supports long-term institutional stability.

---

Technology Resilience

Technology planning should include:

- Secure backups
- System redundancy where practical
- Recovery procedures
- Monitoring
- Incident logging
- Periodic testing

Technology should support continuity rather than become a single point of failure.

---

AI Support

AI operational agents may assist by:

- Monitoring operational health
- Detecting anomalies
- Coordinating response workflows
- Maintaining documentation
- Tracking recovery progress
- Preparing incident reports
- Identifying improvement opportunities

Human stewards remain responsible for strategic decisions during disruptions.

---

Communication During Disruptions

Communication should be:

- Timely
- Accurate
- Transparent
- Respectful
- Appropriate to the situation

Operational updates should reduce uncertainty whenever practical.

---

Exercises and Testing

Business continuity plans should be reviewed and exercised periodically through simulations, tabletop exercises, or other appropriate methods.

Testing identifies weaknesses before real incidents occur.

---

Post-Incident Review

Following significant disruptions, Aureus should conduct a structured review examining:

- Response effectiveness
- Recovery performance
- Communication
- Decision-making
- Lessons learned
- Recommended improvements

The objective is institutional learning rather than assigning blame.

---

Continuous Improvement

Business Continuity and Resilience shall be reviewed regularly to strengthen preparedness, recovery capability, organizational learning, and long-term sustainability.

Every disruption should leave Aureus stronger than before.

---

Canonical Relationship

This framework governs all continuity planning, resilience initiatives, disaster recovery planning, operational recovery procedures, crisis management activities, and institutional continuity practices throughout Aureus.

All continuity-related procedures shall conform to this framework unless superseded by a higher governing document.
