---

Purpose

This document establishes the operational decision-making framework for Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure that operational decisions are consistent, transparent, well-documented, timely, accountable, and aligned with the mission and governing documents of Aureus.

---

Guiding Principles

Operational decisions shall:

- Strengthen members.
- Strengthen the institution.
- Protect trust.
- Be based on evidence whenever practical.
- Consider both immediate and long-term effects.
- Be documented appropriately.
- Encourage continuous learning.

---

Decision Hierarchy

Operational decisions should be made at the lowest appropriate level of authority.

Routine operational matters should remain close to the work being performed.

Strategic or institution-wide decisions shall be elevated to the appropriate governing authority.

---

Decision Categories

Operational decisions are grouped into the following categories:

- Routine
- Tactical
- Strategic
- Financial
- Technical
- Member Experience
- Stewardship
- Risk Management
- Emergency

Each category may define additional approval requirements.

---

Decision Process

Unless otherwise specified, operational decisions should follow this sequence:

1. Define the issue.
2. Gather relevant information.
3. Identify available options.
4. Evaluate benefits, risks, and trade-offs.
5. Determine whether additional approval is required.
6. Record the decision when appropriate.
7. Execute the decision.
8. Review outcomes.
9. Capture lessons learned.

---

Decision Criteria

Operational decisions should consider:

- Mission alignment
- Member impact
- Steward impact
- Institutional sustainability
- Financial responsibility
- Operational efficiency
- Legal obligations
- Ethical considerations
- Technical feasibility
- Risk exposure

No single factor should automatically outweigh all others.

---

AI Decision Support

AI systems may:

- Organize information
- Analyze data
- Identify patterns
- Estimate operational impacts
- Generate alternatives
- Recommend workflows
- Detect operational risks
- Draft implementation plans

AI recommendations are advisory unless explicitly authorized for automated execution.

---

Human Judgment

Human stewards retain responsibility for decisions involving:

- Ethics
- Constitutional interpretation
- Significant financial commitments
- Member safety
- Personnel matters
- Policy changes
- Dispute resolution
- Strategic direction

Human accountability cannot be delegated to AI.

---

Escalation

Operational decisions should be escalated whenever:

- Authority limits are reached.
- Policies conflict.
- Risks exceed approved thresholds.
- Legal uncertainty exists.
- Multiple operational domains are affected.
- The decision could significantly affect members or institutional trust.

Escalation should occur promptly to avoid unnecessary delay.

---

Emergency Decisions

When immediate action is necessary to protect members, operations, or institutional integrity:

- The smallest effective action should be taken.
- Decisions should be documented as soon as practical.
- Appropriate leaders should be notified promptly.
- A post-incident review should be conducted.

Emergency authority shall not become routine practice.

---

Documentation

Significant operational decisions should include:

- Date
- Decision owner
- Decision summary
- Supporting information
- Alternatives considered
- Risks identified
- Expected outcomes
- Required follow-up
- Review date

Documentation strengthens institutional memory.

---

Review and Learning

Major operational decisions should be reviewed after implementation to evaluate:

- Desired outcomes
- Unexpected consequences
- Lessons learned
- Opportunities for improvement
- Recommended updates to procedures

Learning from experience is a permanent operational responsibility.

---

Decision Integrity

Operational decisions shall never knowingly be based upon:

- False information
- Intentional concealment
- Personal favoritism
- Conflicts of interest
- Manipulated data

Institutional trust depends upon honest decision-making.

---

Continuous Improvement

The operational decision-making framework shall be reviewed regularly to improve:

- Speed
- Quality
- Transparency
- Accountability
- Documentation
- Member outcomes
- Steward effectiveness
- Organizational resilience

---

Relationship to Other Documents

This framework governs decision-making across all operational manuals, standard operating procedures, AI operational workflows, and execution systems unless a higher governing document expressly provides otherwise.
