OAS-OPS-006 — AI-Assisted Operations Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-OPS-006
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-002 — Operations Governance Framework
Owner: Chief AI Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the canonical operating model for AI-assisted operations throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure Artificial Intelligence strengthens operational effectiveness while remaining constitutionally aligned, human governed, transparent, accountable, and focused on improving member outcomes.

---

Mission

AI-assisted operations exist to amplify the effectiveness of human stewards through intelligent automation, operational coordination, knowledge support, and data-driven recommendations while preserving meaningful human oversight.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- AI operational stewards
- Human-AI collaboration
- Operational automation
- Workflow orchestration
- AI task coordination
- AI decision support
- AI monitoring
- Operational supervision
- AI performance evaluation
- Continuous operational improvement

---

Guiding Principles

AI-assisted operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Strengthen human stewardship.
- Promote transparency.
- Encourage accountability.
- Support continuous learning.
- Improve operational resilience.

AI exists to strengthen stewardship, not replace it.

---

Human and AI Roles

Operational activities may be performed by:

- Human stewards.
- AI stewards.
- Human-AI collaborative teams.

Every operational responsibility shall clearly define:

- Human responsibilities.
- AI responsibilities.
- Shared responsibilities.
- Escalation requirements.
- Approval requirements.

Authority shall remain explicit and documented.

---

Operational Authority

AI systems may:

- Coordinate workflows.
- Retrieve knowledge.
- Generate recommendations.
- Automate approved tasks.
- Monitor operational performance.
- Produce operational reports.

AI shall not independently exercise constitutional authority, ethical judgment, or strategic governance unless expressly authorized by higher governing documents.

---

Workflow Automation

Automation shall:

- Be documented.
- Be approved.
- Be monitored.
- Be auditable.
- Include human override capability.
- Preserve operational transparency.

Automation should reduce unnecessary work while maintaining accountability.

---

AI Supervision

Human stewards shall supervise AI through:

- Performance reviews.
- Operational monitoring.
- Exception handling.
- Quality verification.
- Risk assessment.
- Continuous improvement.

Supervision strengthens trust and operational reliability.

---

Operational Measurement

AI-assisted operations shall evaluate:

- Accuracy.
- Reliability.
- Productivity.
- Member outcomes.
- Human workload reduction.
- Escalation frequency.
- Automation quality.
- Constitutional compliance.

Measurements should improve collaboration rather than maximize automation alone.

---

Risk Management

Operational AI risks shall be:

- Identified.
- Assessed.
- Documented.
- Mitigated.
- Continuously monitored.
- Reviewed regularly.

Risk management protects members and institutional trust.

---

Continuous Improvement

AI-assisted operations shall improve through:

- Operational metrics.
- Steward feedback.
- Member feedback.
- AI evaluations.
- Incident reviews.
- Workflow analysis.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens both human and AI stewardship.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs every AI-assisted operational activity throughout Aureus.

All AI operational workflows, automation procedures, AI stewardship SOPs, operational orchestration standards, human-AI collaboration guides, and AI service procedures shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution, the Operations Charter, or approved AI governance documents.
