OAS-OPS-002 — Operations Governance Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-OPS-002
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-001 — Operations Charter
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the constitutional governance model for all operational activities throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure every operational decision, workflow, service, and execution process remains accountable, measurable, constitutionally aligned, and consistently governed.

---

Mission

Operations governance exists to transform institutional strategy into disciplined execution while preserving transparency, accountability, stewardship, and continuous improvement.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Operational authority
- Decision-making
- Workflow ownership
- Accountability
- Operational councils
- Escalation procedures
- Cross-functional coordination
- Performance governance
- AI-assisted operations
- Operational oversight

---

Guiding Principles

Operations governance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote accountability.
- Strengthen stewardship.
- Encourage collaboration.
- Support operational excellence.
- Improve institutional resilience.

Governance enables consistent execution.

---

Operational Authority

Operational authority shall be exercised according to:

- Constitutional authority.
- Delegated responsibilities.
- Approved operational procedures.
- Documented governance structures.
- Stewardship responsibilities.

Authority shall always remain traceable and accountable.

---

Decision Governance

Operational decisions shall:

- Be made at the appropriate level.
- Be documented where significant.
- Consider member outcomes.
- Evaluate operational risks.
- Preserve constitutional consistency.
- Support long-term institutional success.

---

Accountability

Every operational responsibility shall have clearly identified:

- Owner.
- Scope.
- Decision authority.
- Performance expectations.
- Reporting responsibilities.

Shared work shall never eliminate individual accountability.

---

Workflow Governance

Operational workflows shall:

- Be documented.
- Define inputs and outputs.
- Assign responsibilities.
- Include quality controls.
- Support measurement.
- Be reviewed regularly.

Workflows should remain understandable and repeatable.

---

Human and AI Operations

Operational activities may be performed through:

- Human stewards.
- AI stewards.
- Human-AI collaborative workflows.

Human stewards remain responsible for constitutional interpretation, ethical judgment, member welfare, and significant operational decisions.

---

Escalation

Operational governance shall define documented escalation paths for:

- Operational risks.
- Service failures.
- Policy conflicts.
- Member concerns.
- Resource constraints.
- Constitutional questions.

Escalation should resolve issues at the lowest appropriate level while ensuring timely resolution.

---

Performance Governance

Operations shall be evaluated using documented measures including:

- Member outcomes.
- Service quality.
- Operational efficiency.
- Workflow effectiveness.
- Steward performance.
- Continuous improvement progress.

Performance evaluation supports institutional learning.

---

Operational Councils

Where appropriate, operational councils may coordinate:

- Cross-functional planning.
- Resource allocation.
- Operational priorities.
- Improvement initiatives.
- Governance reviews.

Councils support coordinated stewardship rather than replacing established authority.

---

Continuous Improvement

Operations governance shall improve through:

- Performance reviews.
- Operational audits.
- Steward feedback.
- Member feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Lessons learned.
- Governance reviews.

Continuous improvement strengthens institutional effectiveness.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all operational governance activities throughout Aureus.

All Operations Frameworks, Operations SOPs, workflow standards, execution procedures, AI operational governance documents, operational metrics, and service delivery standards shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Operations Charter.
