OAS-OPS-002 — Operations Governance

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-001 — Operations Charter
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Last Updated: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This document establishes the operational governance model of Aureus.

It defines how operational authority is delegated, exercised, reviewed, and improved so that Aureus remains accountable, transparent, resilient, and capable of serving members with excellence.

---

Guiding Principles

Operational governance shall always prioritize:

- The flourishing of members
- Faithful stewardship
- Accountability
- Transparency
- Sound judgment
- Measurable execution
- Continuous improvement
- Long-term institutional health

No operational decision shall knowingly weaken trust in Aureus.

---

Operational Authority

Operational authority exists solely to fulfill the mission of Aureus.

Authority is never granted for personal benefit, status, or privilege.

Every operational role exists to serve the institution and its members.

---

Levels of Operational Responsibility

The operational structure consists of the following levels:

1. Stewardship Council
2. Executive Operations
3. Department Stewards
4. Pod Stewards
5. Certified Stewards
6. AI Operational Agents
7. Members

Each level has clearly documented responsibilities and decision boundaries.

---

Human Stewardship

Human stewards are responsible for:

- Ethical judgment
- Member care
- Conflict resolution
- Strategic decisions
- Policy interpretation
- Exception handling
- Final approval of high-impact decisions
- Continuous improvement

Human judgment remains the final authority wherever ethics, ambiguity, or significant consequences are involved.

---

AI Operational Agents

AI agents exist to strengthen human stewardship.

They may assist with:

- Workflow execution
- Documentation
- Scheduling
- Task tracking
- Research
- Reporting
- Analytics
- Draft preparation
- Quality monitoring
- Risk detection
- Knowledge retrieval
- Operational recommendations

AI agents shall not exercise unrestricted institutional authority.

---

Decision Boundaries

Every operational process shall define:

- Decisions AI may make automatically
- Decisions requiring steward review
- Decisions requiring executive approval
- Decisions reserved for the Stewardship Council

Decision authority must always be explicit.

---

Escalation

Issues shall be escalated whenever:

- Policies conflict
- Ethics are uncertain
- Member safety is affected
- Legal compliance is uncertain
- Financial exposure exceeds approved limits
- Repeated failures occur
- An AI agent reaches its authority boundary

Escalation should occur as early as practical.

---

Operational Accountability

Every operational responsibility shall identify:

- Responsible owner
- Supporting roles
- Required approvals
- Success metrics
- Review frequency

Responsibilities without ownership shall be considered incomplete.

---

Documentation

Major operational decisions shall include:

- Decision summary
- Rationale
- Alternatives considered
- Risks identified
- Expected outcomes
- Responsible steward
- Date adopted
- Review schedule

Institutional knowledge belongs to Aureus.

---

Operational Reviews

Operations shall be reviewed on a recurring basis.

Reviews should evaluate:

- Performance
- Member outcomes
- Workflow efficiency
- Resource utilization
- Automation opportunities
- Risks
- Quality
- Steward feedback
- Member feedback

The purpose of review is improvement, not blame.

---

Continuous Improvement

Every operational process shall be evaluated for opportunities to:

- Reduce complexity
- Improve clarity
- Increase reliability
- Strengthen stewardship
- Improve member outcomes
- Increase automation where appropriate
- Eliminate unnecessary work
- Improve resilience

---

Operational Integrity

No steward or AI agent may knowingly:

- Falsify operational records
- Circumvent required approvals
- Misrepresent institutional data
- Ignore documented procedures without authorization
- Conceal operational failures

Integrity is essential to institutional trust.

---

Governance Amendments

Changes to operational governance shall follow the constitutional amendment process established by Aureus.

Operational governance documents shall remain consistent with the Constitution and the Operations Charter.

---

Canonical Relationship

This document governs all operational manuals, standard operating procedures, workflows, AI operational agents, and execution frameworks developed within Aureus.

Where conflicts arise, the Constitution and the Operations Charter shall take precedence.
