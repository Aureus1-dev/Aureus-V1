OAS-OPS-009 — Operational Communication and Coordination Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-OPS-009
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-002 — Operations Governance Framework
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the canonical model for operational communication, coordination, information flow, and organizational alignment throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure every steward, team, AI system, and operational workflow remains informed, coordinated, accountable, and aligned with the Constitution and mission of Aureus.

---

Mission

Operational communication exists to enable accurate information sharing, coordinated action, timely decision-making, and institutional transparency that strengthens members and supports effective stewardship.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Internal operational communication
- Cross-functional coordination
- Decision communication
- Escalation procedures
- Meeting governance
- Operational reporting
- AI-assisted collaboration
- Knowledge sharing
- Organizational transparency
- Communication standards

---

Guiding Principles

Operational communication shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote clarity.
- Encourage transparency.
- Support accountability.
- Strengthen collaboration.
- Improve institutional resilience.

Clear communication is essential to effective stewardship.

---

Communication Architecture

Operational communication shall include:

- Defined communication channels.
- Responsible communicators.
- Standard reporting structures.
- Escalation pathways.
- Documentation requirements.
- Feedback mechanisms.

Information should move efficiently to those who need it.

---

Information Flow

Operational information shall:

- Be timely.
- Be accurate.
- Be appropriately classified.
- Reach responsible parties.
- Support operational decisions.
- Preserve institutional knowledge.

Information should be shared responsibly and purposefully.

---

Cross-Functional Coordination

Teams shall coordinate through:

- Shared objectives.
- Documented responsibilities.
- Regular operational reviews.
- Defined handoffs.
- Collaborative planning.
- Shared operational metrics.

Coordination strengthens execution.

---

Meeting Governance

Operational meetings shall include:

- Defined purpose.
- Agenda.
- Responsible facilitator.
- Action items.
- Assigned owners.
- Follow-up procedures.

Meetings should produce measurable operational value.

---

Escalation

Operational communication shall define escalation procedures for:

- Service disruptions.
- Operational risks.
- Resource constraints.
- Member concerns.
- Policy questions.
- Constitutional issues.

Escalation should occur promptly and at the appropriate level.

---

AI-Assisted Communication

AI may assist through:

- Meeting summaries.
- Knowledge retrieval.
- Operational reporting.
- Workflow coordination.
- Action tracking.
- Communication recommendations.

Human stewards remain responsible for official institutional communications and significant operational decisions.

---

Performance Measurement

Communication effectiveness shall evaluate:

- Information accuracy.
- Response times.
- Coordination quality.
- Decision timeliness.
- Meeting effectiveness.
- Communication clarity.
- Member impact.
- Operational alignment.

Performance measurement supports continual improvement.

---

Continuous Improvement

Operational communication shall improve through:

- Steward feedback.
- Member feedback.
- Operational reviews.
- Communication audits.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Lessons learned.
- Process refinement.

Continuous improvement strengthens institutional coordination.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all operational communication and coordination activities throughout Aureus.

All communication standards, reporting procedures, meeting governance documents, escalation procedures, collaboration workflows, operational reporting standards, and Operations SOPs shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Operations Charter.
