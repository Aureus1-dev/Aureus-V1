OAS-OPS-008 — Member Operations Framework

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-001 — Operations Charter
Related Documents: OAS-OPS-002 through OAS-OPS-007
Owner: Chief Member Steward
Last Updated: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This document establishes the canonical operational framework governing the complete member lifecycle within Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure every member experiences a consistent, trustworthy, supportive, and flourishing journey from their first interaction with Aureus through lifelong participation.

---

Mission

Member Operations exists to help every member make meaningful progress toward a flourishing life while strengthening the Aureus community through responsible stewardship.

---

Guiding Principles

Every member interaction shall:

- Honor the dignity of the individual.
- Build trust.
- Promote long-term flourishing.
- Encourage personal responsibility.
- Support informed decision-making.
- Strengthen community.
- Preserve privacy.
- Continuously improve the member experience.

---

Member Lifecycle

The standard member lifecycle consists of:

1. Discovery
2. First Contact
3. Application
4. Verification
5. Acceptance
6. Onboarding
7. Orientation
8. Goal Discovery
9. Active Participation
10. Ongoing Stewardship
11. Milestone Recognition
12. Leadership Development
13. Alumni or Membership Conclusion (when applicable)

Each stage shall maintain documented operational procedures.

---

Member Onboarding

Every member should receive:

- A welcome experience.
- An introduction to the mission.
- Orientation to the platform.
- Community expectations.
- Initial goal-setting guidance.
- Assignment of an appropriate steward and supporting AI systems, where applicable.

The objective is clarity, confidence, and belonging.

---

Member Support

Member support includes:

- Questions and answers.
- Resource navigation.
- Opportunity guidance.
- Technical assistance.
- Steward conversations.
- Progress encouragement.
- Escalation when additional support is needed.

Support should be timely, respectful, and solution-oriented.

---

Member Records

Operational records should accurately document member interactions when appropriate.

Records should be:

- Secure
- Accurate
- Relevant
- Accessible to authorized personnel
- Maintained according to institutional policy

---

Member Communication

Communications should be:

- Clear
- Respectful
- Helpful
- Timely
- Accessible
- Consistent

Members should understand what is expected of them and what they may expect from Aureus.

---

Member Success

Success should be evaluated using indicators such as:

- Goal progress
- Engagement
- Learning
- Community participation
- Opportunity utilization
- Member satisfaction
- Retention
- Personal growth

Success measures should support flourishing rather than competition.

---

Steward Responsibilities

Human stewards are responsible for:

- Building relationships.
- Providing encouragement.
- Exercising judgment.
- Resolving complex situations.
- Supporting member growth.
- Escalating significant concerns.

Relationships remain central to stewardship.

---

AI Responsibilities

Operational AI may assist with:

- Scheduling
- Orientation
- Documentation
- Resource recommendations
- Progress reminders
- Routine communications
- Knowledge retrieval
- Workflow coordination

AI supports, but does not replace, human stewardship.

---

Member Feedback

Members should have opportunities to provide constructive feedback regarding:

- Programs
- Services
- Operations
- Technology
- Stewardship
- Community experience

Feedback should contribute to continuous improvement.

---

Recognition

Member achievements should be acknowledged in ways consistent with the mission and values of Aureus.

Recognition should encourage continued growth, contribution, and stewardship rather than unhealthy competition.

---

Continuous Improvement

Member Operations shall regularly review:

- Onboarding effectiveness
- Member retention
- Member satisfaction
- Operational efficiency
- Steward workload
- AI effectiveness
- Member outcomes

Improvements should be documented and incorporated through established governance processes.

---

Canonical Relationship

This framework governs all member-facing operational procedures, onboarding systems, support workflows, stewardship interactions, AI member services, and future member operations developed within Aureus.
