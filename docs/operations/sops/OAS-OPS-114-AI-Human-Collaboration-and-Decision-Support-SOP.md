OAS-OPS-114 — AI-Human Collaboration and Decision Support Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-007 — AI Operational Workforce Framework
Owner: Chief AI Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Quarterly

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process governing collaboration between human stewards and AI operational agents throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure AI strengthens stewardship, improves operational performance, preserves accountability, and remains consistently aligned with Aureus' Constitution, mission, and ethical principles.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to every operational workflow involving both human stewards and AI operational agents, including decision support, automation, planning, analysis, documentation, communication, and member services.

---

Objectives

Every AI-human collaboration should:

- Strengthen members.
- Strengthen stewardship.
- Improve decision quality.
- Increase operational efficiency.
- Preserve transparency.
- Maintain constitutional alignment.
- Protect institutional trust.
- Encourage continuous learning.

---

Guiding Principles

AI exists to assist human stewardship.

Human stewards remain responsible for:

- Constitutional interpretation.
- Ethical judgment.
- Strategic leadership.
- Member relationships.
- Final approval of significant operational decisions.

Responsibility may be supported by AI but shall not be delegated to AI.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Human Steward

Responsible for:

- Defining objectives.
- Exercising judgment.
- Reviewing recommendations.
- Approving significant actions.
- Escalating uncertainty.
- Maintaining accountability.

AI Operational Agent

Responsible for:

- Gathering information.
- Performing analysis.
- Identifying patterns.
- Generating recommendations.
- Drafting documentation.
- Tracking workflows.
- Monitoring operational performance.
- Escalating when authority limits are reached.

---

Decision Authority

AI may independently perform only those actions expressly authorized by governing documents and operational policy.

Actions involving constitutional interpretation, ethics, significant financial commitments, member discipline, organizational governance, or strategic direction require human approval.

---

Decision Support Workflow

Step 1 — Define the Question

Clearly identify the decision or task.

---

Step 2 — Gather Information

AI collects relevant operational information from approved sources.

---

Step 3 — Analyze

AI prepares:

- Findings
- Options
- Risks
- Opportunities
- Supporting evidence

---

Step 4 — Recommendation

AI presents one or more recommended courses of action together with supporting reasoning, assumptions, limitations, and confidence level.

---

Step 5 — Human Review

The responsible steward evaluates:

- Accuracy
- Mission alignment
- Ethical implications
- Operational impact
- Risks
- Constitutional consistency

---

Step 6 — Decision

The steward shall:

- Approve
- Modify
- Reject
- Escalate

The final decision should be documented when appropriate.

---

Step 7 — Implementation

Approved actions shall be executed according to established operational procedures.

---

Step 8 — Review

Following implementation, evaluate:

- Results
- Member impact
- Operational effectiveness
- Lessons learned

Update institutional knowledge where appropriate.

---

Escalation

AI shall immediately escalate when:

- Constitutional questions arise.
- Ethical uncertainty exists.
- Required information is unavailable.
- Conflicting authority exists.
- Operational risk exceeds approved thresholds.
- Human approval is required.

Escalation demonstrates responsible stewardship.

---

Transparency

Whenever practical, AI-assisted decisions should document:

- AI systems involved.
- Human decision owner.
- Supporting evidence.
- Recommendations provided.
- Final decision.
- Approval record.

Transparency strengthens trust.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Decision quality.
- AI recommendation accuracy.
- Human review quality.
- Escalation effectiveness.
- Member outcomes.
- Steward satisfaction.
- Operational efficiency.
- Institutional learning.

---

Exceptions

If AI behavior appears inconsistent with constitutional authority, approved policy, or operational safety:

- Suspend affected workflows if necessary.
- Notify the responsible steward immediately.
- Investigate the issue.
- Document findings.
- Restore operation only after verification.

---

Documentation

Maintain records including:

- AI recommendations.
- Human decisions.
- Supporting evidence.
- Escalations.
- Approval history.
- Lessons learned.
- Performance reviews.

---

Continuous Improvement

Human-AI collaboration shall be reviewed regularly to improve governance, operational quality, transparency, stewardship, member outcomes, and institutional learning.
