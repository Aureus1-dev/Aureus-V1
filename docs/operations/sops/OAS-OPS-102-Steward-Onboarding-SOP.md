OAS-OPS-102 — Steward Onboarding Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-009 — Steward Operations Framework
Owner: Chief Stewardship Officer
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Quarterly

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for onboarding every Aureus steward.

Its purpose is to ensure that every steward begins service with a clear understanding of the mission, constitutional governance, operational responsibilities, ethical expectations, and the tools necessary to faithfully serve members and strengthen the institution.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to every individual appointed or accepted into a stewardship role within Aureus.

---

Objectives

Every steward onboarding should:

- Welcome the steward into service.
- Introduce the constitutional foundation of Aureus.
- Explain operational responsibilities.
- Complete required training.
- Establish mentorship relationships.
- Verify operational readiness.
- Prepare the steward for successful service.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Human Steward

Responsible for:

- Orientation
- Training
- Mentorship
- Readiness assessment
- Final approval for active service

AI Operational Agent

Responsible for:

- Preparing onboarding materials
- Scheduling training sessions
- Tracking progress
- Recording completion
- Delivering routine reminders
- Answering approved operational questions

---

Prerequisites

Before onboarding begins:

- Steward appointment has been approved.
- Required background or eligibility checks (if applicable) are complete.
- Required accounts and permissions are prepared.
- Training materials are available.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Welcome

Welcome the steward and explain the importance of stewardship within Aureus.

---

Step 2 — Constitutional Orientation

Review the constitutional documents, operational principles, and governance structure.

Explain how stewardship differs from authority and emphasizes service.

---

Step 3 — Operational Orientation

Introduce:

- Operational frameworks
- Standard Operating Procedures
- Communication systems
- Documentation expectations
- Escalation procedures

---

Step 4 — Technology Orientation

Provide access to approved systems and demonstrate how to use required operational tools.

---

Step 5 — AI Collaboration

Explain:

- AI operational agents
- Decision boundaries
- Human oversight responsibilities
- Escalation expectations

Stewards remain accountable for the work entrusted to them.

---

Step 6 — Mentorship

Assign a qualified mentor or supervising steward to provide guidance during the initial service period.

---

Step 7 — Readiness Verification

Confirm that the steward can:

- Explain the mission.
- Navigate operational systems.
- Locate required documentation.
- Follow established procedures.
- Identify when escalation is required.

---

Step 8 — Authorization

Following successful completion of onboarding, authorize the steward to begin operational responsibilities appropriate to their role.

---

Success Metrics

The onboarding process should monitor:

- Completion rate
- Training completion
- Certification readiness
- Steward confidence
- Early operational performance
- Feedback from mentors
- Feedback from new stewards

---

Exceptions

If onboarding cannot be completed:

- Document the reason.
- Notify the responsible operational leader.
- Develop a corrective plan.
- Delay operational responsibilities until readiness is confirmed.

---

Documentation

Maintain records including:

- Completion date
- Assigned mentor
- Training completed
- Operational permissions granted
- Outstanding actions
- Follow-up schedule

---

Continuous Improvement

The steward onboarding experience shall be reviewed regularly using operational metrics, mentor feedback, steward feedback, and lessons learned.

Improvements should strengthen both stewardship quality and institutional resilience.
