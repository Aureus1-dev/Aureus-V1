OAS-OPS-113 — Operations Canon Maintenance and Version Control Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-OPS-113
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-001 — Operations Charter
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for governing, maintaining, reviewing, versioning, interpreting, expanding, archiving, and preserving the Aureus Operations Canon.

Its purpose is to ensure every operational framework, SOP, playbook, workflow standard, and governance policy evolves through disciplined constitutional governance while preserving institutional operational knowledge.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Operations Frameworks
- Operations SOPs
- Operational playbooks
- Work instructions
- Workflow standards
- Operational policies
- Governance procedures
- Operational templates
- Canonical operational documentation

---

Objectives

Operations Canon governance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain historical continuity.
- Ensure version traceability.
- Support disciplined evolution.
- Preserve institutional knowledge.
- Strengthen future stewardship.

---

Guiding Principles

The Operations Canon shall evolve deliberately rather than reactively.

Every approved revision should improve clarity, operational effectiveness, governance quality, service delivery, or constitutional alignment while preserving a complete historical record.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Chief Operations Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining the Operations Canon.
- Coordinating reviews.
- Approving canonical revisions.
- Preserving version history.
- Overseeing publication.

---

Operations Governance Council

Responsible for:

- Reviewing proposed amendments.
- Evaluating constitutional alignment.
- Approving significant revisions.
- Interpreting operational guidance.
- Recommending future improvements.

---

AI Knowledge Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring document consistency.
- Detecting conflicts.
- Maintaining cross-references.
- Tracking revisions.
- Supporting historical research.
- Identifying opportunities for improvement.

Human stewards retain final authority over all canonical revisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before modifying any canonical operational document:

- Proposed changes are documented.
- Supporting rationale is provided.
- Related documents are identified.
- Operational impacts are evaluated.
- Required reviewers are assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Change Proposal

Document:

- Proposed revision.
- Purpose.
- Operational justification.
- Expected benefits.
- Related documents.
- Anticipated impacts.

---

Step 2 — Canonical Review

Evaluate:

- Constitutional alignment.
- Operational consistency.
- Governance implications.
- Service impacts.
- AI operational considerations.
- Long-term institutional value.

---

Step 3 — Approval

The appropriate governance authority may:

- Approve.
- Approve with revisions.
- Return for further review.
- Reject.

Approved revisions shall be formally recorded.

---

Step 4 — Version Management

Every approved revision shall include:

- Version number.
- Revision date.
- Change summary.
- Approval record.
- Historical reference.

Superseded versions shall remain archived.

---

Step 5 — Publication

Publish approved revisions within the official Aureus repository.

Update indexes, cross-references, and related documentation where appropriate.

---

Step 6 — Archival

Archive previous versions while preserving:

- Original content.
- Revision history.
- Approval history.
- Historical context.

Institutional operational memory shall remain permanently accessible.

---

Step 7 — Periodic Review

Conduct periodic reviews to identify:

- Obsolete procedures.
- Operational inconsistencies.
- Missing governance.
- Emerging operational needs.
- Opportunities for simplification.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Review completion.
- Version accuracy.
- Cross-reference integrity.
- Canon consistency.
- Amendment quality.
- Publication timeliness.
- Historical preservation.
- Steward satisfaction.

---

Exceptions

Emergency operational revisions may follow expedited governance procedures when necessary to protect members, preserve continuity, or address critical institutional risks.

Emergency revisions shall receive formal retrospective review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Amendment proposals.
- Approval records.
- Version history.
- Archive records.
- Governance decisions.
- Canon indexes.
- Review schedules.
- Maintenance reports.

---

Continuous Improvement

Operations Canon governance shall improve through governance reviews, operational audits, steward feedback, AI-assisted analysis, documentation quality reviews, and institutional learning.
