OAS-OPS-109 — Operational Reporting, Metrics, and Dashboard Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-OPS-109
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-007 — Operational Performance and Continuous Improvement Framework
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for collecting, analyzing, reporting, reviewing, and governing operational performance data throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to provide accurate, timely, actionable operational information that strengthens decision-making, promotes accountability, and supports continuous institutional improvement.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Operational dashboards
- Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
- Operational reports
- Executive summaries
- AI-generated reports
- Performance reviews
- Operational analytics
- Governance reporting
- Institutional metrics

---

Objectives

Operational reporting shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Support evidence-based decisions.
- Promote transparency.
- Improve accountability.
- Strengthen operational excellence.
- Enable continuous improvement.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Operations Reporting Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining reporting systems.
- Reviewing operational metrics.
- Preparing reports.
- Confirming data quality.
- Coordinating governance reviews.

---

Operational Leaders

Responsible for:

- Reviewing reports.
- Acting upon findings.
- Monitoring assigned metrics.
- Supporting continuous improvement.

---

AI Analytics Steward

Responsible for:

- Collecting operational data.
- Preparing dashboards.
- Detecting performance trends.
- Identifying anomalies.
- Generating executive summaries.
- Supporting operational forecasting.

Human stewards remain responsible for interpreting results and making institutional decisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before producing operational reports:

- Data sources are validated.
- Metrics are current.
- Reporting standards are applied.
- Required reviews are completed.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Data Collection

Collect information regarding:

- Member outcomes.
- Service quality.
- Operational performance.
- Workflow execution.
- Steward performance.
- AI operational performance.
- Resource utilization.

---

Step 2 — Data Validation

Verify:

- Accuracy.
- Completeness.
- Timeliness.
- Consistency.
- Source integrity.

Reports shall be based upon trustworthy operational information.

---

Step 3 — Dashboard Preparation

Operational dashboards shall present:

- KPI status.
- Operational trends.
- Service performance.
- Risks.
- Improvement initiatives.
- Organizational health.

Dashboards should remain understandable and actionable.

---

Step 4 — Reporting

Prepare reports appropriate to the audience, including:

- Executive summaries.
- Operational reports.
- Team reports.
- Governance reports.
- Improvement reports.

Reporting should emphasize actionable insights.

---

Step 5 — Review

Operational leaders shall review:

- Performance trends.
- Significant variances.
- Emerging risks.
- Improvement opportunities.
- Strategic alignment.

Reviews should result in documented actions where appropriate.

---

Step 6 — Follow-Up

Assign:

- Responsible owners.
- Improvement initiatives.
- Target dates.
- Review schedules.
- Success measures.

Reporting without action does not fulfill its purpose.

---

Step 7 — Continuous Improvement

Improve reporting through:

- Steward feedback.
- Member feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Dashboard reviews.
- Metric refinement.
- Lessons learned.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Reporting timeliness.
- Data accuracy.
- Dashboard utilization.
- KPI achievement.
- Decision effectiveness.
- Improvement completion.
- Reporting quality.
- Operational transparency.

---

Exceptions

Emergency operational circumstances may require expedited reporting while maintaining appropriate documentation and later verification.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Operational reports.
- Dashboard definitions.
- KPI catalogs.
- Executive summaries.
- Review records.
- Improvement plans.
- Historical metrics.
- Reporting procedures.

---

Continuous Improvement

Operational reporting practices shall be reviewed regularly to improve accuracy, usefulness, transparency, AI-assisted analytics, governance quality, and institutional learning.
