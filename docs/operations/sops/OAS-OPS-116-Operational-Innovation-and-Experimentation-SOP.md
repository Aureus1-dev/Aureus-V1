OAS-OPS-116 — Operational Innovation and Experimentation Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-108 — Continuous Improvement and Operational Learning SOP
Owner: Chief Innovation Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for proposing, evaluating, approving, conducting, reviewing, and institutionalizing operational innovation and experimentation throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to encourage responsible innovation while protecting members, preserving constitutional governance, managing operational risk, and strengthening long-term institutional learning.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to all operational experiments, pilot programs, process innovations, AI-assisted initiatives, technology trials, organizational improvements, and strategic operational prototypes.

---

Objectives

Every innovation effort should:

- Advance the mission.
- Improve member outcomes.
- Strengthen stewardship.
- Encourage responsible creativity.
- Protect participants.
- Generate measurable learning.
- Produce evidence-based decisions.
- Contribute to institutional knowledge.

---

Guiding Principles

Innovation should be:

- Mission aligned.
- Evidence informed.
- Ethically conducted.
- Transparently documented.
- Appropriately governed.
- Measurable.
- Reversible when necessary.
- Focused on long-term flourishing.

Experimentation exists to reduce uncertainty through learning, not through unnecessary risk.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Innovation Steward

Responsible for:

- Reviewing proposals.
- Coordinating experiments.
- Monitoring implementation.
- Evaluating outcomes.
- Recommending institutional adoption or closure.

Operational Stewards

Responsible for:

- Identifying opportunities.
- Participating in approved experiments.
- Providing operational feedback.
- Supporting measurement.
- Documenting lessons learned.

AI Operational Agent

Responsible for:

- Identifying improvement opportunities.
- Preparing analyses.
- Monitoring experiment metrics.
- Organizing documentation.
- Tracking milestones.
- Producing evaluation summaries.

Human leadership remains responsible for approving experiments and adopting permanent changes.

---

Prerequisites

Before an experiment begins:

- Objectives are documented.
- Success criteria are defined.
- Risks are evaluated.
- Participants are identified.
- Required approvals are obtained.
- Measurement methods are established.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Proposal

Document:

- Purpose
- Hypothesis
- Expected benefits
- Potential risks
- Resources required
- Duration
- Success measures

---

Step 2 — Evaluation

Review:

- Constitutional alignment
- Mission alignment
- Ethical considerations
- Member impact
- Operational feasibility
- Risk level
- Resource availability

---

Step 3 — Approval

Obtain approval from the appropriate operational authority before implementation.

---

Step 4 — Pilot Implementation

Conduct the experiment according to the approved plan.

Monitor progress continuously and document significant observations.

---

Step 5 — Measurement

Evaluate results using predetermined success measures, including:

- Member outcomes
- Operational performance
- Steward feedback
- Resource utilization
- Risks encountered
- Lessons learned

---

Step 6 — Review

Determine whether the innovation should be:

- Adopted
- Modified
- Expanded
- Re-tested
- Discontinued

Recommendations should be evidence-based.

---

Step 7 — Institutionalization

If approved for permanent adoption:

- Update operational documentation.
- Revise applicable SOPs.
- Update training materials.
- Communicate changes.
- Archive experimental records.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Experiments completed.
- Innovation adoption rate.
- Operational improvements achieved.
- Member satisfaction.
- Steward satisfaction.
- Time from proposal to evaluation.
- Learning captured.
- Risk management effectiveness.

---

Exceptions

If an experiment creates unacceptable risk or unintended harm:

- Suspend the experiment immediately.
- Notify the responsible steward.
- Protect affected members.
- Conduct an incident review.
- Resume only after appropriate approval.

---

Documentation

Maintain records including:

- Proposal.
- Approval.
- Experiment plan.
- Data collected.
- Evaluation.
- Final recommendation.
- Lessons learned.
- Institutional actions taken.

---

Continuous Improvement

The innovation process shall be reviewed regularly to improve experimentation quality, governance, evidence gathering, AI support, and institutional learning.
