OAS-OPS-120 — Operations Canon Maintenance Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-001 — Operations Charter
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for maintaining, reviewing, amending, versioning, interpreting, expanding, and preserving the Aureus Operations Canon.

Its purpose is to ensure that the Operations Canon remains accurate, coherent, internally consistent, constitutionally aligned, and capable of supporting Aureus across generations of members, stewards, and AI operational agents.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to every document contained within the Aureus Operations Canon, including:

- Operations Frameworks
- Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)
- Operational Policies
- Operational Templates
- Operational Standards
- Supporting operational documentation

---

Objectives

Operations Canon maintenance shall:

- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain internal consistency.
- Protect institutional knowledge.
- Encourage responsible evolution.
- Support operational excellence.
- Preserve historical records.
- Improve clarity.
- Strengthen long-term stewardship.

---

Guiding Principles

The Operations Canon is a living operational governance system.

Changes shall be deliberate, documented, transparent, reviewable, and consistent with the Constitution of Aureus.

No operational document may supersede constitutional authority.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Chief Operations Steward

Responsible for:

- Coordinating canon reviews.
- Maintaining document integrity.
- Supervising revisions.
- Confirming version control.
- Reporting significant changes.

Stewardship Council

Responsible for:

- Reviewing significant operational changes.
- Approving major revisions when required.
- Resolving interpretation questions.
- Preserving constitutional alignment.

AI Operational Agent

Responsible for:

- Monitoring document relationships.
- Detecting inconsistencies.
- Tracking versions.
- Identifying duplicate content.
- Preparing revision summaries.
- Supporting document retrieval.

Human stewards remain responsible for approving all canonical revisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before revising the Operations Canon:

- The proposed revision is documented.
- Related documents are identified.
- Constitutional alignment is reviewed.
- Operational impacts are evaluated.
- Required approvals are identified.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Proposal

Document:

- Proposed change.
- Purpose.
- Expected benefits.
- Related documents.
- Potential impacts.

---

Step 2 — Review

Evaluate:

- Constitutional consistency.
- Operational necessity.
- Internal consistency.
- Member impact.
- Steward impact.
- AI operational impact.
- Long-term sustainability.

---

Step 3 — Approval

Obtain approval according to established governance procedures before publishing significant revisions.

---

Step 4 — Version Control

Record:

- Version number.
- Revision date.
- Summary of changes.
- Approving authority.
- Related revisions.

Historical versions shall remain preserved.

---

Step 5 — Publication

Publish approved revisions within the official Aureus repository.

Communicate significant operational changes to affected stewards.

---

Step 6 — Canon Integrity Review

Periodically review the Operations Canon for:

- Internal consistency.
- Duplicate guidance.
- Outdated references.
- Missing operational guidance.
- Cross-document conflicts.

---

Step 7 — Archival

Retired operational documents shall be archived according to approved institutional records management procedures.

Historical records shall remain available for institutional reference.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Document review completion.
- Version accuracy.
- Cross-reference integrity.
- Duplicate reduction.
- Review timeliness.
- Operational clarity.
- Steward understanding.
- Canon consistency.

---

Exceptions

If significant inconsistencies or constitutional conflicts are discovered:

- Suspend publication when appropriate.
- Escalate immediately.
- Resolve conflicts before final approval.
- Document all corrective actions.

---

Documentation

Maintain records including:

- Revision proposals.
- Review comments.
- Approval records.
- Version history.
- Publication dates.
- Archive records.
- Canon review reports.

---

Continuous Improvement

The Operations Canon maintenance process shall be reviewed regularly to strengthen governance, documentation quality, AI support, institutional learning, and long-term operational resilience.
