OAS-OPS-107 — Operational Meetings and Decision Governance Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-OPS-107
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-002 — Operations Governance Framework
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for planning, conducting, documenting, and following up on operational meetings and decisions throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure operational meetings produce clear outcomes, responsible ownership, documented decisions, and measurable execution while supporting constitutional governance and institutional effectiveness.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Weekly operational meetings
- Team meetings
- Cross-functional coordination meetings
- Decision review sessions
- Planning meetings
- Retrospectives
- AI-assisted meetings
- Operational governance meetings

---

Objectives

Operational meetings shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Produce actionable decisions.
- Strengthen collaboration.
- Promote accountability.
- Minimize unnecessary meetings.
- Improve operational execution.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Meeting Facilitator

Responsible for:

- Preparing the agenda.
- Guiding discussion.
- Managing time.
- Confirming decisions.
- Assigning action items.
- Closing the meeting.

---

Participants

Responsible for:

- Preparing in advance.
- Contributing constructively.
- Respecting meeting protocols.
- Accepting assigned responsibilities.
- Completing follow-up actions.

---

AI Meeting Steward

Responsible for:

- Preparing agendas.
- Recording meeting notes.
- Summarizing discussions.
- Tracking decisions.
- Monitoring action items.
- Generating follow-up reports.

Human stewards remain responsible for decisions and approvals.

---

Prerequisites

Before every meeting:

- A clear purpose is defined.
- Participants are identified.
- The agenda is distributed.
- Required materials are available.
- Previous action items are reviewed.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Meeting Preparation

Confirm:

- Meeting objective.
- Agenda.
- Required participants.
- Supporting documents.
- Desired outcomes.
- Time allocation.

---

Step 2 — Meeting Execution

Conduct the meeting by:

- Reviewing objectives.
- Discussing agenda items.
- Evaluating alternatives.
- Recording decisions.
- Assigning responsibilities.
- Confirming next steps.

Discussion should remain focused on achieving the meeting objective.

---

Step 3 — Decision Documentation

Document:

- Decisions made.
- Responsible owners.
- Supporting rationale.
- Required approvals.
- Implementation deadlines.
- Follow-up requirements.

Significant operational decisions shall remain traceable.

---

Step 4 — Action Tracking

Assign:

- Responsible steward.
- Supporting contributors.
- Due dates.
- Success measures.
- Review schedule.

Every action item shall have an accountable owner.

---

Step 5 — Follow-Up

Verify:

- Assigned work has begun.
- Deadlines remain achievable.
- Obstacles are addressed.
- Decisions are implemented.
- Documentation is complete.

---

Step 6 — Meeting Review

Evaluate:

- Meeting effectiveness.
- Decision quality.
- Time management.
- Participant engagement.
- Completion of action items.
- Opportunities for improvement.

---

Meeting Standards

Operational meetings should:

- Begin on time.
- End on time.
- Follow the agenda.
- Respect participants.
- Encourage constructive discussion.
- Produce measurable outcomes.
- Avoid unnecessary repetition.

Meetings exist to improve execution.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Meeting effectiveness.
- Decision implementation.
- Action item completion.
- Meeting duration.
- Attendance.
- Follow-up completion.
- Operational improvements.
- Participant feedback.

---

Exceptions

Emergency operational situations may require expedited meetings and decision procedures while maintaining appropriate documentation after the event.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Agendas.
- Attendance records.
- Meeting minutes.
- Decision logs.
- Action registers.
- Follow-up reports.
- Meeting evaluations.

---

Continuous Improvement

Meeting governance shall improve through participant feedback, operational reviews, AI-assisted analysis, meeting audits, execution metrics, and lessons learned.
