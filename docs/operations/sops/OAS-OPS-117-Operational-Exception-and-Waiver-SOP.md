OAS-OPS-117 — Operational Exception and Waiver Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-002 — Operations Governance Framework
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for requesting, reviewing, approving, implementing, monitoring, and closing operational exceptions and temporary waivers within Aureus.

Its purpose is to provide controlled flexibility when extraordinary circumstances require temporary deviation from established operational procedures while preserving constitutional governance, accountability, transparency, and institutional trust.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to temporary operational exceptions involving:

- Standard Operating Procedures
- Operational workflows
- Administrative processes
- Technology operations
- AI operational activities
- Project execution
- Documentation requirements
- Internal operational practices

This procedure does not authorize exceptions to the Constitution unless the Constitution itself expressly provides such authority.

---

Objectives

Every operational exception should:

- Protect members.
- Support the mission.
- Minimize operational disruption.
- Remain narrowly tailored.
- Be fully documented.
- Include defined expiration criteria.
- Preserve accountability.
- Strengthen institutional learning.

---

Guiding Principles

Operational exceptions are temporary accommodations, not permanent policy changes.

No exception shall establish institutional precedent unless formally adopted through the appropriate governance process.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Requesting Steward

Responsible for:

- Preparing the request.
- Explaining the operational need.
- Identifying risks.
- Recommending safeguards.
- Reporting implementation results.

Approving Authority

Responsible for:

- Reviewing requests.
- Evaluating risks.
- Approving, modifying, or denying requests.
- Establishing conditions.
- Confirming closure.

AI Operational Agent

Responsible for:

- Recording requests.
- Tracking approvals.
- Monitoring expiration dates.
- Generating reminders.
- Supporting documentation.
- Preparing summary reports.

AI may not approve exceptions independently.

---

Prerequisites

Before requesting an exception:

- The operational issue is clearly identified.
- Existing procedures have been reviewed.
- Alternatives have been considered.
- Risks have been evaluated.
- Proposed safeguards are documented.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Exception Request

Document:

- Operational issue.
- Requested exception.
- Reason.
- Expected duration.
- Risks.
- Proposed safeguards.

---

Step 2 — Review

Evaluate:

- Mission alignment.
- Constitutional consistency.
- Operational necessity.
- Member impact.
- Steward impact.
- Risk level.
- Available alternatives.

---

Step 3 — Approval

The approving authority shall:

- Approve.
- Approve with conditions.
- Request modification.
- Deny the request.

Approval conditions shall be documented.

---

Step 4 — Implementation

Implement the approved exception according to documented conditions.

Monitor implementation throughout the approved period.

---

Step 5 — Monitoring

Review:

- Operational performance.
- Compliance with approval conditions.
- Emerging risks.
- Member impact.
- Steward feedback.

---

Step 6 — Closure

Upon expiration:

- Restore standard procedures unless otherwise approved.
- Document results.
- Identify lessons learned.
- Archive the record.

Permanent operational changes require separate approval through established governance processes.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Number of exceptions.
- Duration of exceptions.
- Compliance with approval conditions.
- Operational outcomes.
- Member impact.
- Repeat requests.
- Lessons incorporated.
- Timely closure.

---

Exceptions

Emergency situations may require immediate temporary action before formal approval when necessary to protect members or institutional operations.

Such actions shall be documented and reviewed as soon as reasonably practicable.

---

Documentation

Maintain records including:

- Request.
- Risk assessment.
- Approval.
- Conditions.
- Monitoring reports.
- Closure report.
- Lessons learned.

---

Continuous Improvement

Operational exception practices shall be reviewed regularly to reduce unnecessary exceptions, improve standard procedures, strengthen governance, and enhance institutional resilience.
