OAS-OPS-101 — Service Delivery and Operational Execution Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-OPS-101
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-002 — Operational Governance and Service Delivery Framework
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for day-to-day service delivery, operational execution, work coordination, operational scheduling, service monitoring, escalation management, operational reporting, and continuous operational excellence throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure institutional operations remain reliable, coordinated, measurable, constitutionally aligned, and capable of consistently advancing Aureus' mission.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Service delivery
- Operational execution
- Work coordination
- Operational scheduling
- Service monitoring
- Escalation management
- Operational reporting
- Daily operational management
- Cross-functional coordination
- Operational oversight

---

Objectives

Operational execution shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Deliver reliable services.
- Strengthen accountability.
- Improve operational efficiency.
- Support collaboration.
- Enable continuous improvement.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Operations Steward

Responsible for:

- Coordinating daily operations.
- Monitoring service delivery.
- Managing operational priorities.
- Preparing operational reports.
- Supporting governance reviews.

---

Service Owners

Responsible for:

- Delivering assigned services.
- Monitoring operational quality.
- Escalating operational issues.
- Supporting continuous improvement.
- Maintaining operational documentation.

---

AI Operations Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring workflows.
- Coordinating schedules.
- Preparing operational summaries.
- Tracking performance metrics.
- Identifying operational bottlenecks.
- Supporting administrative coordination.

Human stewards remain responsible for operational decisions, resource allocation, service prioritization, escalation decisions, and governance actions.

---

Prerequisites

Before daily operations begin:

- Operational priorities are identified.
- Service responsibilities are assigned.
- Required resources are available.
- Communication channels are operational.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Operational Planning

Document:

- Daily priorities.
- Scheduled activities.
- Resource assignments.
- Operational dependencies.
- Success measures.
- Known operational risks.

---

Step 2 — Service Execution

Deliver services according to:

- Approved procedures.
- Quality standards.
- Operational schedules.
- Stewardship expectations.
- Governance requirements.

Maintain appropriate documentation throughout execution.

---

Step 3 — Monitoring

Monitor:

- Service quality.
- Operational progress.
- Workflow status.
- Resource utilization.
- Operational risks.
- Member impacts.

Investigate significant issues promptly.

---

Step 4 — Escalation

When issues arise:

- Document the issue.
- Assess severity.
- Notify appropriate stewards.
- Coordinate corrective actions.
- Monitor resolution.
- Record outcomes.

---

Step 5 — Operational Reporting

Prepare reports including:

- Service performance.
- Operational metrics.
- Outstanding issues.
- Resource utilization.
- Improvement opportunities.
- Governance updates.

---

Step 6 — Coordination

Coordinate cross-functional activities through:

- Regular communication.
- Shared operational priorities.
- Resource planning.
- Issue resolution.
- Governance oversight.

---

Step 7 — Continuous Improvement

Improve operations through:

- Performance reviews.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance evaluations.
- Operational assessments.
- Lessons learned.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Service reliability.
- Operational timeliness.
- Escalation resolution time.
- Operational efficiency.
- Member satisfaction.
- Resource utilization.
- Governance compliance.
- Mission support.

---

Exceptions

Emergency operational circumstances may require expedited procedures while maintaining documentation and retrospective governance review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Operational plans.
- Service logs.
- Monitoring reports.
- Escalation records.
- Performance reports.
- Operational metrics.
- Governance reports.
- Improvement plans.

---

Continuous Improvement

Operational execution shall improve through operational reviews, steward feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance assessments, service evaluations, and lessons learned.
