OAS-OPS-111 — Operational Change Management Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-014 — Project and Execution Management Framework
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Quarterly

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for proposing, evaluating, approving, implementing, verifying, and documenting operational changes throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure that changes strengthen the institution while minimizing unnecessary disruption, protecting members, preserving institutional knowledge, and maintaining constitutional alignment.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to changes affecting:

- Operational processes
- Standard Operating Procedures
- AI operational agents
- Technology platforms
- Organizational structures
- Workflows
- Documentation
- Training materials
- Operational policies

---

Objectives

Every operational change should:

- Strengthen the mission.
- Improve member outcomes.
- Preserve operational stability.
- Reduce unnecessary risk.
- Maintain constitutional alignment.
- Be appropriately documented.
- Include verification after implementation.
- Contribute to institutional learning.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Change Owner

Responsible for:

- Preparing the change proposal.
- Coordinating implementation.
- Communicating with stakeholders.
- Confirming successful implementation.
- Completing required documentation.

Operational Leadership

Responsible for:

- Reviewing change requests.
- Evaluating risks.
- Approving or rejecting changes.
- Monitoring implementation.
- Assigning additional resources when required.

AI Operational Agent

Responsible for:

- Tracking change requests.
- Organizing documentation.
- Monitoring implementation milestones.
- Preparing reports.
- Recording change history.
- Tracking post-implementation metrics.

Human leadership remains responsible for all change approvals.

---

Prerequisites

Before implementation:

- The change is documented.
- Purpose is clearly defined.
- Risks are evaluated.
- Required approvals are obtained.
- A communication plan is prepared.
- A rollback strategy is documented when appropriate.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Change Proposal

Document:

- Purpose
- Scope
- Expected benefits
- Risks
- Operational impact
- Required resources
- Proposed timeline

---

Step 2 — Evaluation

Assess:

- Mission alignment
- Member impact
- Steward impact
- Technical feasibility
- Operational complexity
- Financial implications
- Risk level

---

Step 3 — Approval

Obtain approval from the appropriate operational authority before implementation.

---

Step 4 — Communication

Notify affected members, stewards, and operational teams when appropriate.

Communication should explain:

- What is changing
- Why it is changing
- Expected impact
- Implementation timeline
- Available support

---

Step 5 — Implementation

Implement the approved change according to the documented plan.

Monitor implementation for unexpected issues.

---

Step 6 — Verification

Confirm:

- Objectives achieved
- Operational stability maintained
- Documentation updated
- Training completed
- Required follow-up actions identified

---

Step 7 — Post-Implementation Review

Evaluate:

- Expected outcomes
- Unexpected consequences
- Lessons learned
- Improvement opportunities

Successful changes should become institutional knowledge.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Change success rate
- Implementation timeliness
- Operational disruption
- Member satisfaction
- Steward satisfaction
- Adoption rate
- Number of corrective actions
- Long-term performance improvements

---

Exceptions

Emergency operational changes may proceed using expedited approval procedures when immediate action is required to protect members or institutional operations.

Emergency changes shall receive a formal post-implementation review.

---

Documentation

Maintain records including:

- Change proposal
- Risk assessment
- Approval records
- Communication materials
- Implementation plan
- Verification results
- Lessons learned
- Updated documentation

---

Continuous Improvement

The operational change management process shall be reviewed regularly to improve planning, communication, implementation, governance, and organizational learning.
