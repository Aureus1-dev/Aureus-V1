OAS-OPS-103 — AI Agent Deployment and Governance Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-007 — AI Operational Workforce Framework
Owner: Chief AI Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Quarterly

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for proposing, approving, deploying, monitoring, maintaining, and retiring AI operational agents within Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure every AI agent operates safely, transparently, responsibly, and in alignment with the constitutional governance and operational principles of Aureus.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to every AI operational agent, workflow, automation, assistant, or autonomous service operating on behalf of Aureus.

---

Objectives

Every AI deployment should:

- Support the mission.
- Improve stewardship.
- Protect members.
- Respect constitutional governance.
- Operate within documented authority.
- Remain subject to human oversight.
- Produce measurable value.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Human Steward

Responsible for:

- Defining the AI agent's mission.
- Approving deployment.
- Monitoring performance.
- Reviewing escalations.
- Updating authority boundaries.
- Retiring the agent when appropriate.

AI Operational Agent

Responsible for:

- Performing approved operational tasks.
- Remaining within documented authority.
- Escalating exceptions.
- Recording operational activity.
- Supporting continuous improvement.

---

Prerequisites

Before deployment:

- The AI agent has a documented mission.
- Authority boundaries are defined.
- Human owner is assigned.
- Success metrics are documented.
- Security review is completed.
- Testing has been successfully completed.
- Approval has been granted.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Proposal

Document:

- Agent name
- Purpose
- Operational domain
- Expected benefits
- Risks
- Human owner

---

Step 2 — Design Review

Review:

- Mission alignment
- Authority limits
- Inputs
- Outputs
- Escalation rules
- Operational dependencies

---

Step 3 — Testing

Validate:

- Accuracy
- Reliability
- Security
- Escalation behavior
- Documentation
- Operational readiness

Production deployment shall not occur until testing is successfully completed.

---

Step 4 — Approval

Authorized leadership reviews the deployment request and approves or rejects production use.

---

Step 5 — Deployment

Deploy the AI agent according to documented operational procedures.

Record:

- Deployment date
- Version
- Owner
- Operational status

---

Step 6 — Monitoring

Monitor:

- Performance
- Error rates
- Escalations
- Member impact
- Steward feedback
- Resource utilization

---

Step 7 — Review

Conduct periodic reviews to evaluate:

- Continued mission alignment
- Performance
- Risks
- Required improvements
- Opportunities for expanded or reduced authority

---

Step 8 — Retirement

When an AI agent is retired:

- Document the reason.
- Preserve historical records.
- Transfer institutional knowledge.
- Disable operational authority.
- Archive related documentation.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Operational accuracy
- Escalation quality
- Reliability
- Member satisfaction
- Steward satisfaction
- Operational efficiency
- Error frequency
- Audit results

---

Exceptions

If significant operational concerns arise:

- Suspend the AI agent if necessary.
- Notify the responsible steward.
- Investigate the issue.
- Implement corrective actions.
- Reauthorize deployment only after verification.

---

Documentation

Maintain records including:

- Mission
- Version history
- Operational owner
- Authority boundaries
- Testing results
- Deployment approvals
- Performance reviews
- Retirement records

---

Continuous Improvement

Every deployment should generate lessons that strengthen future AI governance, deployment procedures, operational safety, and stewardship.
