OAS-OPS-112 — Operational Reporting and Dashboard Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-005 — Operational Performance and Continuous Improvement Framework
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Quarterly

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for collecting, validating, analyzing, reporting, and reviewing operational information throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure that members, stewards, operational leaders, executive leadership, and the Stewardship Council have accurate, timely, and meaningful information to support responsible stewardship and informed decision-making.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to all operational reports, dashboards, scorecards, executive summaries, KPI reports, AI-generated operational analytics, and institutional performance reporting.

---

Objectives

Every operational report should:

- Support informed decision-making.
- Reflect accurate information.
- Strengthen accountability.
- Monitor institutional health.
- Encourage continuous improvement.
- Protect sensitive information.
- Promote transparency where appropriate.
- Support constitutional governance.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Report Owner

Responsible for:

- Defining report requirements.
- Verifying report accuracy.
- Reviewing report quality.
- Approving publication.
- Maintaining reporting standards.

Operational Stewards

Responsible for:

- Providing accurate operational data.
- Reviewing department reports.
- Acting upon findings.
- Reporting significant concerns.

AI Operational Agent

Responsible for:

- Collecting approved operational data.
- Preparing dashboards.
- Monitoring KPIs.
- Identifying trends.
- Producing executive summaries.
- Tracking reporting schedules.

Human stewards remain responsible for interpreting reports and making operational decisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before generating reports:

- Required data sources are available.
- Data quality checks are complete.
- Reporting period is defined.
- Report recipients are identified.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Data Collection

Collect approved operational information from authorized sources.

---

Step 2 — Validation

Verify:

- Accuracy
- Completeness
- Consistency
- Timeliness

Questionable information should be investigated before publication.

---

Step 3 — Analysis

Evaluate:

- KPI performance
- Operational trends
- Member outcomes
- Steward performance
- Project progress
- AI operational performance
- Emerging risks

Analysis should focus on meaningful insights rather than raw data alone.

---

Step 4 — Report Preparation

Prepare reports appropriate to the intended audience.

Reports may include:

- Executive summaries
- Department scorecards
- KPI dashboards
- Operational health reports
- Risk summaries
- Improvement recommendations

---

Step 5 — Review

Review reports for:

- Accuracy
- Clarity
- Constitutional alignment
- Appropriate confidentiality
- Actionability

---

Step 6 — Distribution

Provide reports to authorized recipients according to approved reporting schedules.

---

Step 7 — Follow-Up

Identify:

- Required actions
- Responsible owners
- Follow-up deadlines
- Review dates

Reports should lead to meaningful action whenever appropriate.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Reporting accuracy
- Timeliness
- Dashboard availability
- Data quality
- User satisfaction
- Decision support effectiveness
- KPI completeness
- Action item completion

---

Exceptions

If significant reporting errors are identified:

- Correct the report promptly.
- Notify affected recipients.
- Document the correction.
- Investigate the underlying cause.
- Implement preventive improvements.

---

Documentation

Maintain records including:

- Reporting schedules
- Dashboard definitions
- KPI definitions
- Data sources
- Distribution lists
- Revision history
- Follow-up actions

---

Continuous Improvement

Operational reporting practices shall be reviewed regularly to improve clarity, accuracy, efficiency, decision support, AI assistance, and organizational learning.
