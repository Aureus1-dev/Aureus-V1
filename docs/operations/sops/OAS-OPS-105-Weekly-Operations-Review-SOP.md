OAS-OPS-105 — Weekly Operations Review Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-014 — Project and Execution Management Framework
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Quarterly

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for Aureus' Weekly Operations Review.

Its purpose is to provide a consistent operational rhythm for reviewing performance, coordinating execution, identifying risks, supporting members and stewards, and driving continuous improvement across the institution.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to the weekly operational review conducted by Aureus leadership and designated operational stewards.

---

Objectives

Every Weekly Operations Review should:

- Review organizational performance.
- Monitor member outcomes.
- Evaluate steward capacity.
- Track project progress.
- Review operational metrics.
- Identify emerging risks.
- Assign action items.
- Strengthen institutional learning.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Meeting Facilitator

Responsible for:

- Preparing the agenda.
- Leading the meeting.
- Confirming decisions.
- Assigning follow-up actions.
- Maintaining focus.

Operational Stewards

Responsible for:

- Reporting operational updates.
- Identifying blockers.
- Reviewing assigned metrics.
- Proposing improvements.
- Accepting action items.

AI Operational Agent

Responsible for:

- Preparing dashboards.
- Compiling KPIs.
- Tracking action items.
- Summarizing operational changes.
- Recording meeting notes.
- Preparing follow-up reminders.

---

Prerequisites

Before the meeting:

- Operational dashboards are updated.
- KPIs are available.
- Open action items are reviewed.
- Project status reports are prepared.
- Risk register is current.
- Agenda is distributed.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Opening

Confirm attendance, review the meeting purpose, and approve the agenda.

---

Step 2 — Review Previous Actions

Review action items from the previous meeting.

Confirm:

- Completed actions
- Outstanding actions
- Delayed actions
- Required escalations

---

Step 3 — Operational Performance

Review key operational indicators including:

- Member outcomes
- Steward performance
- Operational quality
- Project status
- Community engagement
- AI operational performance

---

Step 4 — Risk Review

Review:

- New risks
- Existing risks
- Incident reports
- Escalations
- Quality concerns

Assign mitigation actions where appropriate.

---

Step 5 — Strategic Priorities

Review progress toward institutional priorities.

Confirm:

- Current focus areas
- Resource allocation
- Emerging opportunities
- Required adjustments

---

Step 6 — Continuous Improvement

Discuss:

- Lessons learned
- Process improvements
- Automation opportunities
- Documentation updates
- Training needs

---

Step 7 — Action Planning

Assign:

- New action items
- Responsible owners
- Due dates
- Success criteria

Confirm accountability before closing.

---

Step 8 — Closing

Summarize:

- Decisions made
- Assigned actions
- Major risks
- Upcoming priorities

Confirm the date of the next review.

---

Success Metrics

Weekly reviews should monitor:

- Action item completion rate
- Meeting attendance
- Decision turnaround time
- KPI trends
- Project progress
- Operational improvements
- Risk reduction
- Member outcomes

---

Exceptions

If urgent operational matters arise outside the normal review cycle:

- Convene a special operational review.
- Document the reason.
- Notify appropriate stewards.
- Follow emergency governance procedures where applicable.

---

Documentation

Meeting records should include:

- Date
- Participants
- KPIs reviewed
- Decisions made
- Risks discussed
- Action items
- Responsible owners
- Follow-up schedule

---

Continuous Improvement

The Weekly Operations Review process shall itself be reviewed periodically to improve effectiveness, efficiency, participation, decision quality, and organizational learning.
