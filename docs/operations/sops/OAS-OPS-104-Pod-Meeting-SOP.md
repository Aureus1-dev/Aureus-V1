OAS-OPS-104 — Pod Meeting Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-010 — Pod Operations Framework
Owner: Chief Community Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Quarterly

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for planning, facilitating, documenting, and following up on Aureus Pod meetings.

Its purpose is to ensure every Pod meeting strengthens relationships, supports member flourishing, advances meaningful goals, and reinforces the mission and values of Aureus.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to all officially recognized Aureus Pods and their recurring meetings unless a specialized meeting procedure has been approved.

---

Objectives

Every Pod meeting should:

- Welcome members.
- Build trust and belonging.
- Review progress.
- Encourage accountability.
- Share knowledge and opportunities.
- Address challenges.
- Establish next actions.
- Strengthen community.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Pod Steward

Responsible for:

- Preparing the meeting.
- Facilitating discussion.
- Encouraging participation.
- Managing time.
- Confirming action items.
- Escalating concerns when necessary.

AI Operational Agent

Responsible for:

- Scheduling reminders.
- Preparing agendas.
- Recording notes.
- Tracking action items.
- Organizing documentation.
- Preparing meeting summaries.

---

Prerequisites

Before the meeting:

- Meeting time is confirmed.
- Agenda is prepared.
- Previous action items are available.
- Required resources are accessible.
- Participants have received reminders.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Welcome

Welcome participants and create a respectful, encouraging environment.

---

Step 2 — Mission Reminder

Briefly reconnect the group with the purpose and mission of Aureus and the Pod.

---

Step 3 — Member Updates

Invite members to share:

- Progress
- Successes
- Challenges
- Questions
- Requests for support

---

Step 4 — Goal Review

Review commitments made during previous meetings.

Celebrate progress and discuss obstacles.

---

Step 5 — Learning and Opportunities

Share:

- Relevant knowledge
- New opportunities
- Resources
- Best practices
- Institutional updates

---

Step 6 — Discussion

Facilitate thoughtful discussion while encouraging respectful participation from all members.

---

Step 7 — Action Planning

Identify:

- Individual commitments
- Shared initiatives
- Responsible owners
- Target completion dates

---

Step 8 — Closing

Summarize:

- Key decisions
- Action items
- Upcoming events
- Encouragement for the week ahead

---

Success Metrics

Pod meetings should monitor:

- Attendance
- Participation
- Member engagement
- Goal completion
- Action item completion
- Member satisfaction
- Steward feedback

---

Exceptions

If significant concerns arise during a meeting:

- Document the concern.
- Notify the appropriate steward when required.
- Protect confidentiality.
- Follow established escalation procedures.

---

Documentation

Meeting records should include:

- Date
- Participants
- Key discussion topics
- Decisions
- Action items
- Responsible owners
- Follow-up schedule

---

Continuous Improvement

Meeting effectiveness should be reviewed regularly using member feedback, steward observations, participation trends, and operational metrics.

Recommended improvements should be incorporated through established governance procedures.
