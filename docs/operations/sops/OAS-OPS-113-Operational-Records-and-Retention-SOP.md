OAS-OPS-113 — Operational Records and Retention Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-012 — Knowledge and Documentation Framework
Owner: Chief Knowledge Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for creating, classifying, storing, protecting, retaining, archiving, and disposing of operational records throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to preserve institutional history, support operational accountability, satisfy legal and governance requirements, and ensure that critical records remain available to future generations of stewards.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to all official operational records created or maintained by Aureus, including:

- Governance records
- Operational reports
- Decision logs
- Standard Operating Procedures
- Project documentation
- Audit records
- Incident records
- AI operational logs
- Training records
- Member and steward operational records
- Other approved institutional records

---

Objectives

Every records management activity should:

- Preserve institutional memory.
- Protect record integrity.
- Maintain confidentiality.
- Ensure appropriate accessibility.
- Support constitutional governance.
- Meet applicable legal requirements.
- Promote operational efficiency.
- Enable responsible archival and disposal.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Records Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining record standards.
- Approving retention schedules.
- Overseeing archival activities.
- Verifying record integrity.
- Authorizing approved disposal.

Operational Stewards

Responsible for:

- Creating accurate records.
- Following retention requirements.
- Protecting confidential information.
- Reporting records issues.

AI Operational Agent

Responsible for:

- Organizing records.
- Applying classification labels.
- Monitoring retention schedules.
- Identifying duplicate records.
- Assisting with retrieval.
- Preparing archival recommendations.

Human approval is required before permanent disposal of controlled records.

---

Prerequisites

Before a record is stored:

- Ownership is identified.
- Classification is assigned.
- Required metadata is complete.
- Storage location is approved.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Record Creation

Create records using approved institutional standards.

Records should be accurate, complete, and appropriately identified.

---

Step 2 — Classification

Classify each record according to:

- Operational category
- Sensitivity
- Retention requirements
- Access permissions

---

Step 3 — Storage

Store records in approved repositories that provide appropriate security, integrity, and availability.

---

Step 4 — Access Management

Provide access only to authorized individuals based upon operational responsibilities and institutional policy.

Access should be periodically reviewed.

---

Step 5 — Retention

Maintain records according to approved retention schedules.

Retention periods should reflect:

- Operational value
- Historical significance
- Governance requirements
- Applicable legal obligations

---

Step 6 — Archiving

Archive records that are no longer actively used but remain valuable for institutional history, legal obligations, or future reference.

Archived records should remain searchable and protected.

---

Step 7 — Disposal

When authorized for disposal:

- Verify eligibility.
- Obtain required approval.
- Document disposal.
- Ensure secure destruction where appropriate.

Permanent disposal should never compromise constitutional or legal obligations.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Record accuracy
- Retrieval time
- Retention compliance
- Archive completeness
- Unauthorized access incidents
- Duplicate record reduction
- Record integrity
- Disposal compliance

---

Exceptions

If records become subject to investigation, litigation, constitutional review, or other preservation requirements:

- Suspend disposal immediately.
- Notify the appropriate authority.
- Preserve affected records until authorized for release.

---

Documentation

Maintain records including:

- Classification
- Ownership
- Storage location
- Retention schedule
- Archive status
- Disposal approvals
- Access history

---

Continuous Improvement

Records management practices shall be reviewed regularly to improve organization, security, accessibility, preservation, AI assistance, and long-term institutional stewardship.
