OAS-OPS-102 — Process Management and Quality Assurance Operations Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-OPS-102
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-003 — Process Management and Operational Excellence Framework
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for process execution, workflow management, quality assurance, process audits, SOP compliance, workflow optimization, corrective actions, and continuous operational excellence throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure every operational process is executed consistently, measured objectively, continuously improved, and aligned with Aureus' constitutional mission.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Process execution
- Workflow management
- Quality assurance
- SOP compliance
- Process audits
- Workflow optimization
- Corrective actions
- Preventive actions
- Operational documentation
- Continuous improvement

---

Objectives

Process management operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Improve operational consistency.
- Strengthen quality assurance.
- Reduce operational errors.
- Promote accountability.
- Enable continuous improvement.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Process Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining operational processes.
- Monitoring workflow execution.
- Coordinating process reviews.
- Managing quality records.
- Preparing governance reports.

---

Process Owners

Responsible for:

- Executing approved procedures.
- Monitoring process performance.
- Reporting operational issues.
- Supporting process improvements.
- Maintaining operational documentation.

---

AI Process Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring workflow execution.
- Detecting process bottlenecks.
- Identifying quality trends.
- Preparing workflow summaries.
- Tracking process metrics.
- Supporting optimization analysis.

Human stewards remain responsible for approving process changes, quality decisions, governance actions, and operational accountability.

---

Prerequisites

Before executing institutional processes:

- Approved procedures are available.
- Process ownership is assigned.
- Required resources are available.
- Performance measures are established.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Process Preparation

Document:

- Process purpose.
- Scope.
- Responsible stewards.
- Required resources.
- Performance measures.
- Success criteria.

---

Step 2 — Process Execution

Execute workflows according to:

- Approved SOPs.
- Governance requirements.
- Quality standards.
- Operational schedules.
- Safety requirements where applicable.

Record significant process activities.

---

Step 3 — Quality Assurance

Verify:

- SOP compliance.
- Process consistency.
- Service quality.
- Documentation accuracy.
- Operational effectiveness.

Document quality findings.

---

Step 4 — Process Audit

Conduct periodic audits to evaluate:

- Workflow compliance.
- Quality performance.
- Documentation completeness.
- Operational efficiency.
- Improvement opportunities.

---

Step 5 — Corrective Actions

When deficiencies are identified:

- Document the issue.
- Determine root cause.
- Assign responsibility.
- Implement corrective action.
- Verify effectiveness.
- Record lessons learned.

---

Step 6 — Process Optimization

Review opportunities to:

- Simplify workflows.
- Reduce delays.
- Improve quality.
- Eliminate waste.
- Increase operational resilience.

Approve changes through appropriate governance.

---

Step 7 — Continuous Improvement

Improve operational processes through:

- Steward feedback.
- Performance reviews.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance evaluations.
- Operational assessments.
- Lessons learned.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Process compliance.
- Workflow completion.
- Quality performance.
- Audit completion.
- Corrective action effectiveness.
- Operational efficiency.
- Governance compliance.
- Mission support.

---

Exceptions

Emergency operational conditions may require temporary deviations while maintaining documentation and retrospective governance review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Process documentation.
- Workflow records.
- Audit reports.
- Quality reviews.
- Corrective action logs.
- Process metrics.
- Governance reports.
- Improvement records.

---

Continuous Improvement

Process management operations shall improve through process audits, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance evaluations, operational reviews, and lessons learned.
