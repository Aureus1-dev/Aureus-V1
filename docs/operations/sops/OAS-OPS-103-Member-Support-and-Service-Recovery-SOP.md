OAS-OPS-103 — Member Support and Service Recovery Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-OPS-103
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-004 — Member Service Delivery Framework
Owner: Chief Member Experience Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for providing member support, resolving service issues, coordinating assistance, recovering from service failures, and maintaining long-term member trust.

Its purpose is to ensure every member receives timely, respectful, effective, and mission-aligned support regardless of the communication channel, service requested, or steward involved.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Member support requests
- Questions and guidance
- Technical assistance
- Service coordination
- Complaint resolution
- Service recovery
- Escalations
- AI-assisted support
- Human steward support

---

Objectives

Member support shall:

- Protect members.
- Resolve concerns promptly.
- Preserve member dignity.
- Strengthen trust.
- Coordinate appropriate services.
- Learn from service failures.
- Improve continuously.
- Advance the mission.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Member Support Steward

Responsible for:

- Responding to member requests.
- Investigating concerns.
- Coordinating service resolution.
- Escalating complex matters.
- Confirming successful resolution.

---

AI Support Steward

Responsible for:

- Answering routine questions.
- Providing knowledge resources.
- Guiding platform navigation.
- Tracking case progress.
- Recommending solutions.
- Escalating issues requiring human judgment.

Human stewards remain responsible for member welfare, exceptions, and significant decisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before handling a support request:

- Member identity is confirmed where appropriate.
- The request is documented.
- Required information is collected.
- Service history is reviewed when relevant.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Receive the Request

Document:

- Member information.
- Nature of the request.
- Urgency.
- Affected services.
- Desired outcome.

Acknowledge receipt promptly.

---

Step 2 — Assess the Request

Determine:

- Request category.
- Operational impact.
- Member impact.
- Resolution priority.
- Need for escalation.

---

Step 3 — Resolve the Issue

Provide:

- Accurate information.
- Appropriate guidance.
- Service coordination.
- Technical assistance where applicable.
- Follow-up communication.

Keep the member informed throughout the process.

---

Step 4 — Escalate When Necessary

Escalate requests involving:

- Constitutional interpretation.
- Ethical concerns.
- Significant operational issues.
- Security concerns.
- Member safety.
- Requests beyond assigned authority.

---

Step 5 — Service Recovery

When Aureus has not met expectations:

- Acknowledge the issue.
- Explain what occurred when appropriate.
- Correct the problem.
- Restore service.
- Document lessons learned.
- Identify preventive improvements.

Service recovery should strengthen trust whenever possible.

---

Step 6 — Confirm Resolution

Verify:

- Member concerns have been addressed.
- Services function correctly.
- Required follow-up is complete.
- Documentation is updated.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Response time.
- Resolution time.
- First-contact resolution.
- Member satisfaction.
- Escalation frequency.
- Service recovery success.
- Repeat requests.
- Support quality.

---

Exceptions

Urgent matters involving member safety, security, or critical operational risks may follow expedited handling procedures.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Support records.
- Case history.
- Escalation records.
- Resolution summaries.
- Member feedback.
- Service recovery reports.
- Improvement recommendations.

---

Continuous Improvement

Member support practices shall improve through member feedback, steward feedback, operational reviews, AI-assisted analysis, quality monitoring, and lessons learned.
