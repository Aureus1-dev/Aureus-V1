OAS-OPS-110 — Operations Documentation and Knowledge Management Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-OPS-110
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-007 — Operational Performance and Continuous Improvement Framework
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for creating, maintaining, reviewing, publishing, versioning, archiving, and improving all operational documentation and institutional knowledge throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to preserve operational wisdom, ensure consistent execution, support AI-assisted operations, and strengthen long-term institutional continuity.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)
- Operational playbooks
- Work instructions
- Process documentation
- Meeting records
- Lessons learned
- Operational templates
- AI operational knowledge
- Training materials
- Institutional operational records

---

Objectives

Operational knowledge management shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain operational consistency.
- Support continuous learning.
- Improve discoverability.
- Preserve institutional memory.
- Enable future stewardship.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Documentation Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining operational documentation.
- Coordinating reviews.
- Managing version control.
- Publishing approved updates.
- Preserving historical records.

---

Operations Stewards

Responsible for:

- Creating accurate documentation.
- Updating procedures.
- Recording lessons learned.
- Reporting obsolete information.
- Following documentation standards.

---

AI Knowledge Steward

Responsible for:

- Organizing operational knowledge.
- Detecting outdated information.
- Identifying missing documentation.
- Maintaining cross-references.
- Supporting knowledge retrieval.
- Generating documentation summaries.

Human stewards remain responsible for approving canonical documentation.

---

Prerequisites

Before publication:

- Content is technically accurate.
- Required reviews are complete.
- Related documents are referenced.
- Ownership is documented.
- Version information is assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Documentation Planning

Identify:

- Purpose.
- Audience.
- Scope.
- Required content.
- Responsible owner.
- Related documents.

---

Step 2 — Documentation Creation

Prepare documentation using approved Aureus standards.

Documentation should emphasize:

- Clarity.
- Accuracy.
- Practical usefulness.
- Consistency.
- Maintainability.

---

Step 3 — Review

Verify:

- Operational accuracy.
- Constitutional alignment.
- Cross-reference integrity.
- Completeness.
- Current applicability.

---

Step 4 — Publication

Publish approved documentation to the official Aureus repository.

Ensure documents remain searchable, version controlled, and properly classified.

---

Step 5 — Maintenance

Review documentation regularly to identify:

- Outdated information.
- Operational changes.
- Missing guidance.
- Process improvements.
- New institutional knowledge.

Documentation shall evolve with operations.

---

Step 6 — Archival

Retired operational documents shall:

- Remain accessible.
- Preserve revision history.
- Maintain historical context.
- Support future institutional learning.

---

Step 7 — Knowledge Improvement

Strengthen institutional knowledge through:

- Operational reviews.
- Steward feedback.
- Member feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Lessons learned.
- Continuous refinement.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Documentation coverage.
- Documentation accuracy.
- Review completion.
- Search effectiveness.
- Knowledge reuse.
- Update timeliness.
- Cross-reference quality.
- Steward satisfaction.

---

Exceptions

Temporary documentation gaps may be permitted during emergency operational activities but shall be completed promptly after operational stability is restored.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Published procedures.
- Version history.
- Review records.
- Archive records.
- Lessons learned.
- Knowledge indexes.
- Documentation metrics.

---

Continuous Improvement

Operational knowledge management shall improve through operational reviews, steward feedback, AI-assisted analysis, documentation audits, and institutional learning.
