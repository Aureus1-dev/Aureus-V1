OAS-OPS-106 — Incident Response and Escalation Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-013 — Quality Assurance and Risk Management Framework
Owner: Chief Quality and Risk Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Quarterly

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for identifying, classifying, escalating, responding to, resolving, documenting, and reviewing operational incidents within Aureus.

Its purpose is to protect members, preserve institutional trust, restore operations efficiently, and strengthen Aureus through continuous learning.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to all operational incidents affecting Aureus, including member issues, steward concerns, technology failures, AI operational issues, security events, and other disruptions requiring coordinated response.

---

Objectives

Every incident response should:

- Protect people.
- Protect institutional integrity.
- Restore operations safely.
- Communicate appropriately.
- Document events accurately.
- Learn from every incident.
- Prevent recurrence whenever practical.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Incident Coordinator

Responsible for:

- Leading the response.
- Coordinating communication.
- Assigning responsibilities.
- Confirming resolution.
- Approving incident closure.

Operational Stewards

Responsible for:

- Reporting incidents.
- Executing assigned response activities.
- Escalating concerns.
- Maintaining documentation.

AI Operational Agent

Responsible for:

- Logging incidents.
- Sending notifications.
- Tracking response timelines.
- Preparing summaries.
- Monitoring follow-up actions.
- Supporting documentation.

---

Incident Classification

Incidents should be classified according to:

- Severity
- Operational impact
- Member impact
- Financial impact
- Security impact
- Reputational impact
- Urgency

Classification guides response priorities.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Detection

Identify and report the incident as soon as practical.

---

Step 2 — Initial Assessment

Determine:

- Nature of the incident
- Scope
- Severity
- Immediate risks
- Required responders

---

Step 3 — Containment

Take appropriate actions to limit further harm while preserving evidence and protecting members.

---

Step 4 — Escalation

Notify the appropriate stewards according to the severity and operational impact of the incident.

Escalate immediately whenever authority limits are reached.

---

Step 5 — Response

Coordinate activities to:

- Resolve the incident.
- Restore affected services.
- Support impacted members.
- Protect institutional assets.

---

Step 6 — Communication

Provide timely updates to appropriate stakeholders.

Communications should be:

- Accurate
- Respectful
- Transparent
- Appropriate to the audience

---

Step 7 — Resolution

Confirm that:

- The incident has been resolved.
- Critical operations have been restored.
- Outstanding risks have been addressed.
- Required follow-up actions are documented.

---

Step 8 — Post-Incident Review

Conduct a structured review examining:

- Root causes
- Response effectiveness
- Communication
- Decision-making
- Lessons learned
- Recommended improvements

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Response time
- Resolution time
- Escalation effectiveness
- Communication quality
- Repeat incidents
- Member impact
- Corrective action completion

---

Exceptions

If an incident exceeds documented operational authority:

- Escalate immediately.
- Document the reason.
- Notify executive leadership or the Stewardship Council when required.
- Follow emergency governance procedures where applicable.

---

Documentation

Incident records should include:

- Incident identifier
- Date and time
- Description
- Classification
- Participants
- Response actions
- Resolution
- Lessons learned
- Corrective actions

---

Continuous Improvement

Incident trends, response effectiveness, and lessons learned shall be reviewed regularly to strengthen operational resilience, quality assurance, training, and institutional knowledge.
