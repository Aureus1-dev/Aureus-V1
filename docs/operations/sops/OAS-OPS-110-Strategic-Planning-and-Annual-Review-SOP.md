OAS-OPS-110 — Strategic Planning and Annual Review Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-014 — Project and Execution Management Framework
Owner: Chief Strategy Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for Aureus' annual strategic planning and institutional review.

Its purpose is to ensure that Aureus regularly evaluates its mission, long-term objectives, organizational performance, and future priorities so the institution remains faithful to its constitutional purpose while adapting responsibly to changing circumstances.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to the annual strategic planning cycle conducted by the Stewardship Council, executive leadership, and designated operational stewards.

---

Objectives

Every annual strategic review should:

- Reaffirm the mission.
- Evaluate institutional health.
- Review long-term objectives.
- Assess organizational performance.
- Identify emerging opportunities.
- Prioritize future initiatives.
- Allocate resources responsibly.
- Strengthen long-term resilience.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Chief Strategy Steward

Responsible for:

- Coordinating the planning process.
- Preparing strategic materials.
- Facilitating planning sessions.
- Producing the strategic plan.
- Monitoring annual objectives.

Stewardship Council

Responsible for:

- Reviewing institutional direction.
- Approving strategic priorities.
- Confirming mission alignment.
- Approving the annual strategic plan.

AI Operational Agent

Responsible for:

- Preparing analytics.
- Summarizing operational performance.
- Identifying trends.
- Organizing planning documents.
- Tracking strategic objectives.
- Producing dashboards.

Human leadership remains responsible for strategic decisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before planning begins:

- Annual operational reports are complete.
- KPIs are available.
- Financial information is prepared.
- Member and steward feedback is summarized.
- Major projects are reviewed.
- Risk assessments are updated.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Mission Review

Review the constitutional mission, vision, values, and long-term purpose of Aureus.

Confirm continued alignment.

---

Step 2 — Institutional Assessment

Evaluate:

- Member outcomes
- Stewardship effectiveness
- Community health
- Financial sustainability
- Operational performance
- AI operational effectiveness
- Organizational resilience

---

Step 3 — Environmental Review

Consider significant external developments including:

- Technology
- Law and regulation
- Economic conditions
- Community needs
- Emerging opportunities
- Significant risks

---

Step 4 — Strategic Priorities

Identify and prioritize institutional objectives for the coming planning period.

Each objective should include:

- Expected outcome
- Responsible owner
- Timeline
- Success measures

---

Step 5 — Resource Planning

Review anticipated resource requirements, including:

- People
- Technology
- Financial resources
- Training
- Infrastructure
- Operational capacity

Resources should be aligned with strategic priorities.

---

Step 6 — Approval

Present the proposed strategic plan to the Stewardship Council for review and approval.

---

Step 7 — Communication

Communicate approved strategic priorities to the appropriate members, stewards, operational teams, and AI systems.

---

Step 8 — Monitoring

Track strategic objectives throughout the year using approved operational dashboards and periodic reviews.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Strategic objective completion
- Organizational health
- Member outcomes
- Steward engagement
- Resource utilization
- Mission alignment
- Long-term progress
- Institutional resilience

---

Exceptions

If extraordinary circumstances require significant strategic changes outside the annual planning cycle:

- Document the reason.
- Conduct an extraordinary strategic review.
- Obtain appropriate approvals.
- Update the strategic plan accordingly.

---

Documentation

Maintain records including:

- Strategic planning agenda
- Supporting analyses
- Strategic objectives
- Approved annual plan
- Resource allocations
- Performance reviews
- Annual lessons learned

---

Continuous Improvement

The strategic planning process itself shall be reviewed after each annual cycle to improve planning quality, participation, decision-making, and long-term organizational effectiveness.
