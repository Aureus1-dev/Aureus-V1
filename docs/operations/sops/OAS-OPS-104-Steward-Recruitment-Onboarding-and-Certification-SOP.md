OAS-OPS-104 — Steward Recruitment, Onboarding, and Certification Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-OPS-104
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-005 — Steward Operations and Workforce Framework
Owner: Chief Stewardship Officer
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for recruiting, evaluating, selecting, onboarding, educating, certifying, authorizing, and continuously developing Aureus stewards.

Its purpose is to ensure every steward is prepared to faithfully uphold the Constitution, serve members with excellence, collaborate effectively with AI systems, and strengthen the long-term mission of Aureus.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Steward recruitment
- Candidate evaluation
- Selection
- Background verification where required
- Steward onboarding
- Constitutional education
- Operational training
- Role certification
- Probation
- Continuing education

---

Objectives

Steward certification shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Develop competent leaders.
- Promote servant leadership.
- Encourage lifelong learning.
- Strengthen institutional trust.
- Ensure operational readiness.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Steward Development Steward

Responsible for:

- Coordinating recruitment.
- Managing onboarding.
- Scheduling training.
- Confirming certification requirements.
- Maintaining stewardship records.

---

Supervising Steward

Responsible for:

- Mentoring new stewards.
- Conducting evaluations.
- Providing feedback.
- Recommending certification.
- Supporting probation.

---

AI Learning Steward

Responsible for:

- Delivering learning materials.
- Tracking educational progress.
- Administering practice exercises.
- Monitoring certification completion.
- Recommending additional learning resources.

Human stewards remain responsible for certification decisions and operational authorization.

---

Prerequisites

Before onboarding:

- Candidate evaluation is complete.
- Eligibility requirements are satisfied.
- Required documentation is collected.
- Appropriate approvals are obtained.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Recruitment

Evaluate candidates based upon:

- Mission alignment.
- Character.
- Integrity.
- Competence.
- Learning mindset.
- Commitment to stewardship.

---

Step 2 — Selection

Complete:

- Interviews where appropriate.
- Qualification review.
- Reference verification where applicable.
- Selection approval.

---

Step 3 — Onboarding

Provide instruction covering:

- Constitution.
- Mission.
- Governance.
- Stewardship principles.
- Operations.
- Technology.
- AI collaboration.
- Member service expectations.

---

Step 4 — Education

Training shall include:

- Constitutional knowledge.
- Operational procedures.
- Ethics.
- Communication.
- Member care.
- Technology systems.
- Security awareness.
- Role-specific instruction.

---

Step 5 — Certification

Certification shall verify:

- Knowledge.
- Practical competence.
- Constitutional understanding.
- Operational readiness.
- Stewardship conduct.

Certification requirements shall be documented.

---

Step 6 — Probation

New stewards may complete a supervised probationary period during which performance, judgment, collaboration, and readiness are evaluated.

---

Step 7 — Operational Authorization

Upon successful completion of certification:

- Operational authority is assigned.
- Required system access is granted.
- Steward responsibilities are confirmed.
- Continuing education requirements are communicated.

---

Step 8 — Continuing Development

Every steward shall participate in continuing education through:

- Refresher training.
- Leadership development.
- Operational exercises.
- AI-assisted learning.
- Constitutional updates.
- Lessons learned.

Learning remains a permanent stewardship responsibility.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Recruitment effectiveness.
- Certification completion.
- Probation success.
- Steward retention.
- Continuing education participation.
- Operational readiness.
- Member satisfaction.
- Leadership development.

---

Exceptions

Temporary certification accommodations may be approved for exceptional circumstances while maintaining constitutional integrity and member protection.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Recruitment records.
- Training records.
- Certification records.
- Evaluation reports.
- Continuing education history.
- Authorization records.
- Steward development plans.

---

Continuous Improvement

Steward recruitment and certification shall improve through operational reviews, steward feedback, member feedback, AI-assisted analysis, training evaluations, and lessons learned.
