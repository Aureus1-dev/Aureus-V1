OAS-OPS-108 — Operational Risk Escalation and Incident Management Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-OPS-108
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-008 — Operational Risk and Business Continuity Framework
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for identifying, reporting, assessing, escalating, resolving, documenting, and reviewing operational incidents throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to protect members, preserve operational continuity, minimize disruption, promote coordinated response, and strengthen institutional learning.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Operational incidents
- Workflow failures
- Member-impacting events
- Policy exceptions
- Service disruptions
- Operational risks
- Cross-functional incidents
- Emergency operational events
- AI-assisted operations

---

Objectives

Operational incident management shall:

- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Restore normal operations quickly.
- Reduce operational risk.
- Maintain clear communication.
- Identify root causes.
- Prevent recurrence.
- Strengthen institutional resilience.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Incident Coordinator

Responsible for:

- Coordinating the operational response.
- Assessing severity.
- Assigning responsibilities.
- Managing communications.
- Confirming incident closure.

---

Operations Stewards

Responsible for:

- Investigating incidents.
- Restoring operations.
- Implementing corrective actions.
- Documenting findings.
- Supporting follow-up reviews.

---

AI Operations Steward

Responsible for:

- Detecting operational anomalies.
- Monitoring workflows.
- Recording timelines.
- Tracking corrective actions.
- Producing incident summaries.
- Supporting trend analysis.

Human stewards remain responsible for operational decisions, escalation, and incident closure.

---

Incident Severity

Operational incidents shall be classified according to:

- Member impact.
- Operational disruption.
- Institutional risk.
- Urgency.
- Scope.
- Recovery complexity.

Severity levels shall guide response priorities.

---

Prerequisites

Before beginning incident response:

- The incident is documented.
- Initial assessment is completed.
- Responsible personnel are identified.
- Immediate member protection measures are taken where necessary.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Incident Detection

Identify incidents through:

- Steward reports.
- Member reports.
- AI monitoring.
- Operational reviews.
- Automated alerts.
- Routine observations.

---

Step 2 — Initial Assessment

Determine:

- Severity.
- Scope.
- Immediate risks.
- Affected members.
- Operational impact.
- Escalation requirements.

---

Step 3 — Escalation

Escalate incidents according to:

- Severity.
- Constitutional implications.
- Member welfare.
- Operational authority.
- Cross-functional impact.

Escalation should occur without unnecessary delay.

---

Step 4 — Incident Response

Coordinate actions to:

- Protect members.
- Stabilize operations.
- Restore affected services.
- Communicate appropriately.
- Preserve relevant evidence.

---

Step 5 — Resolution

Confirm:

- Operations are restored.
- Member concerns are addressed.
- Corrective actions are implemented.
- Risks are reduced.

---

Step 6 — Root Cause Analysis

Evaluate:

- Primary causes.
- Contributing factors.
- Process failures.
- Communication gaps.
- Improvement opportunities.

Root cause analysis shall focus on institutional improvement rather than individual blame.

---

Step 7 — Post-Incident Review

Document:

- Timeline.
- Actions taken.
- Outcomes.
- Lessons learned.
- Improvement recommendations.
- Assigned follow-up actions.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Incident frequency.
- Response time.
- Resolution time.
- Escalation effectiveness.
- Repeat incidents.
- Member impact.
- Corrective action completion.
- Operational resilience.

---

Exceptions

Emergency operational conditions may require immediate action before full documentation is completed.

Documentation shall be completed as soon as operational stability has been restored.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Incident reports.
- Escalation records.
- Investigation findings.
- Root cause analyses.
- Corrective action plans.
- Follow-up reviews.
- Lessons learned.

---

Continuous Improvement

Operational incident management shall improve through incident reviews, steward feedback, member feedback, AI-assisted analysis, trend monitoring, and lessons learned.
