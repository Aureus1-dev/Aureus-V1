OAS-OPS-105 — Steward Performance, Development, and Accountability Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-OPS-105
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-005 — Steward Operations and Workforce Framework
Owner: Chief Stewardship Officer
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for evaluating, supporting, coaching, mentoring, recognizing, developing, and holding Aureus stewards accountable throughout their service.

Its purpose is to cultivate exceptional stewardship, strengthen institutional effectiveness, encourage lifelong learning, and ensure accountability remains restorative, transparent, and aligned with the mission and Constitution of Aureus.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Performance reviews
- Professional development
- Coaching
- Mentoring
- Recognition
- Accountability
- Corrective action
- Leadership development
- Succession planning
- Continuing stewardship

---

Objectives

Steward development shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote servant leadership.
- Encourage professional growth.
- Strengthen accountability.
- Build future leaders.
- Improve institutional performance.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Supervising Steward

Responsible for:

- Conducting performance reviews.
- Providing coaching.
- Supporting development plans.
- Recognizing achievement.
- Addressing performance concerns.
- Confirming readiness for expanded responsibility.

---

Steward

Responsible for:

- Performing assigned responsibilities.
- Pursuing continuous learning.
- Seeking feedback.
- Demonstrating accountability.
- Supporting fellow stewards.
- Upholding constitutional standards.

---

AI Development Steward

Responsible for:

- Tracking performance metrics.
- Monitoring development goals.
- Recommending learning resources.
- Supporting review preparation.
- Identifying growth opportunities.
- Generating development reports.

Human stewards remain responsible for evaluations, promotions, and accountability decisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before conducting a performance review:

- Performance expectations are documented.
- Relevant operational metrics are available.
- Member feedback is reviewed where appropriate.
- Development history is considered.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Performance Review

Evaluate:

- Member outcomes.
- Service quality.
- Operational effectiveness.
- Constitutional compliance.
- Collaboration.
- Professional conduct.
- Learning progress.

Performance discussions should strengthen stewardship rather than merely score performance.

---

Step 2 — Coaching

Provide constructive coaching focused on:

- Strengths.
- Growth opportunities.
- Skill development.
- Leadership capacity.
- Operational excellence.

---

Step 3 — Development Planning

Create or update an individual development plan including:

- Learning objectives.
- Training activities.
- Mentoring opportunities.
- Leadership goals.
- Review milestones.

---

Step 4 — Recognition

Recognize meaningful contributions through appropriate institutional recognition that reinforces stewardship, service, collaboration, innovation, and mission impact.

---

Step 5 — Accountability

When expectations are not met:

- Review the situation.
- Understand contributing factors.
- Develop corrective actions.
- Provide support where appropriate.
- Monitor improvement.

Accountability should seek restoration whenever reasonably possible.

---

Step 6 — Leadership Development

Identify and prepare future leaders through:

- Mentoring.
- Progressive responsibility.
- Advanced education.
- Cross-functional experience.
- Institutional service.

Leadership development preserves long-term continuity.

---

Step 7 — Succession Planning

Maintain succession plans for critical stewardship roles to ensure operational continuity and institutional resilience.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Steward performance.
- Member satisfaction.
- Professional development completion.
- Leadership readiness.
- Steward retention.
- Corrective action completion.
- Internal promotions.
- Organizational capability.

---

Exceptions

Exceptional operational circumstances may require modified review schedules while maintaining accountability and development responsibilities.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Performance reviews.
- Development plans.
- Coaching records.
- Recognition records.
- Accountability actions.
- Leadership assessments.
- Succession plans.

---

Continuous Improvement

Steward development practices shall improve through operational reviews, steward feedback, member feedback, AI-assisted analysis, leadership evaluations, and lessons learned.
