OAS-OPS-118 — Operational Sunsetting and Decommissioning Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-111 — Operational Change Management SOP
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for retiring operational processes, AI operational agents, software systems, organizational structures, committees, services, and other institutional capabilities.

Its purpose is to ensure that operational retirement occurs deliberately, transparently, and responsibly while preserving institutional knowledge, protecting members, and maintaining continuity.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to the retirement of:

- Operational workflows
- AI operational agents
- Software platforms
- Internal tools
- Programs and services
- Committees
- Organizational structures
- Standard Operating Procedures
- Other approved institutional capabilities

---

Objectives

Every decommissioning effort should:

- Protect members.
- Preserve institutional knowledge.
- Minimize operational disruption.
- Maintain constitutional alignment.
- Secure operational records.
- Complete orderly transitions.
- Archive historical information.
- Support continuous improvement.

---

Guiding Principles

Operational retirement is an intentional governance activity.

Capabilities should be retired only after evaluating operational impact, available alternatives, historical value, and long-term institutional interests.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Decommissioning Steward

Responsible for:

- Preparing retirement plans.
- Coordinating transition activities.
- Preserving documentation.
- Confirming completion.
- Reporting results.

Operational Leadership

Responsible for:

- Reviewing retirement proposals.
- Approving decommissioning.
- Monitoring transition risks.
- Protecting member interests.

AI Operational Agent

Responsible for:

- Tracking retirement activities.
- Organizing archival records.
- Monitoring transition milestones.
- Producing status reports.
- Recording lessons learned.

Human leadership remains responsible for retirement decisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before retirement begins:

- The capability has been evaluated.
- Operational impacts are documented.
- Successor systems or processes are identified when appropriate.
- Required approvals are obtained.
- Communication plans are prepared.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Retirement Proposal

Document:

- Capability being retired.
- Reason for retirement.
- Operational impact.
- Alternatives.
- Risks.
- Transition timeline.

---

Step 2 — Evaluation

Review:

- Mission alignment.
- Member impact.
- Steward impact.
- Operational dependencies.
- Technology implications.
- Knowledge preservation requirements.

---

Step 3 — Approval

Obtain approval from the appropriate governing authority before retirement.

---

Step 4 — Transition

Implement the approved transition plan.

Activities may include:

- Migrating information.
- Redirecting workflows.
- Updating documentation.
- Training affected stewards.
- Notifying stakeholders.

---

Step 5 — Archiving

Preserve:

- Historical documentation.
- Operational records.
- Lessons learned.
- Decision history.
- Technical information where appropriate.

Archives should remain accessible according to institutional policy.

---

Step 6 — Decommissioning

Disable or retire the capability according to the approved plan.

Verify that all transition activities have been completed.

---

Step 7 — Post-Retirement Review

Evaluate:

- Transition effectiveness.
- Operational impact.
- Member outcomes.
- Steward feedback.
- Lessons learned.
- Improvement opportunities.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Transition completion.
- Operational continuity.
- Knowledge preservation.
- Member satisfaction.
- Steward satisfaction.
- Documentation completeness.
- Post-retirement issues.
- Lessons incorporated.

---

Exceptions

If retirement introduces unexpected operational risk:

- Pause the transition when practical.
- Escalate immediately.
- Protect affected members.
- Reassess the transition plan.
- Resume only after appropriate approval.

---

Documentation

Maintain records including:

- Retirement proposal.
- Impact assessment.
- Approval records.
- Transition plan.
- Archive inventory.
- Closure report.
- Lessons learned.

---

Continuous Improvement

Operational retirement practices shall be reviewed regularly to strengthen governance, knowledge preservation, organizational resilience, AI lifecycle management, and institutional stewardship.
