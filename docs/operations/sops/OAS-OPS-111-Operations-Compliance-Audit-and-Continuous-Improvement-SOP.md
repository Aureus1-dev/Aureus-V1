OAS-OPS-111 — Operations Compliance, Audit, and Continuous Improvement Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-OPS-111
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-007 — Operational Performance and Continuous Improvement Framework
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for auditing operational activities, verifying compliance, evaluating service quality, monitoring operational maturity, implementing corrective actions, and strengthening continuous improvement throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure operations remain constitutionally aligned, effective, measurable, accountable, and continuously improving.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Operational audits
- SOP compliance
- Workflow verification
- Service quality reviews
- Operational maturity assessments
- Governance reviews
- Corrective actions
- Continuous improvement
- Operational reporting

---

Objectives

Operational audits shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Improve operational quality.
- Strengthen accountability.
- Reduce operational risk.
- Promote institutional learning.
- Encourage continuous improvement.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Operations Audit Steward

Responsible for:

- Planning audits.
- Coordinating evidence collection.
- Preparing audit reports.
- Tracking corrective actions.
- Confirming audit completion.

---

Operational Leaders

Responsible for:

- Supporting audit activities.
- Providing requested evidence.
- Implementing corrective actions.
- Verifying improvements.

---

AI Audit Steward

Responsible for:

- Collecting operational metrics.
- Reviewing workflow data.
- Identifying compliance gaps.
- Detecting operational trends.
- Preparing audit summaries.
- Tracking corrective actions.

Human stewards remain responsible for audit conclusions and governance decisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before beginning an audit:

- Audit scope is defined.
- Evaluation criteria are documented.
- Previous audit findings are reviewed.
- Required records are available.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Audit Planning

Define:

- Audit objectives.
- Scope.
- Evaluation criteria.
- Timeline.
- Participants.
- Required evidence.

---

Step 2 — Evidence Collection

Collect:

- Operational procedures.
- Workflow records.
- Service metrics.
- Performance reports.
- Meeting records.
- Incident reports.
- Training records.
- Operational documentation.

Evidence shall remain complete and verifiable.

---

Step 3 — Compliance Assessment

Evaluate compliance with:

- Constitution.
- Operations Frameworks.
- Operations SOPs.
- Service standards.
- Governance requirements.
- Stewardship principles.

---

Step 4 — Findings

Document:

- Strengths.
- Risks.
- Nonconformities.
- Improvement opportunities.
- Operational maturity observations.

Audit findings should encourage institutional learning rather than individual blame.

---

Step 5 — Corrective Actions

Assign:

- Responsible owners.
- Target completion dates.
- Success measures.
- Follow-up requirements.

Corrective actions shall remain tracked until verified complete.

---

Step 6 — Follow-Up Verification

Confirm that corrective actions:

- Have been implemented.
- Address identified issues.
- Improve operational performance.
- Reduce future risk.

---

Step 7 — Continuous Improvement

Review audit trends to identify:

- Recurring issues.
- Process weaknesses.
- Operational innovations.
- Governance improvements.
- Institutional learning opportunities.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Audit completion rate.
- Compliance percentage.
- Corrective action completion.
- Repeat findings.
- Service quality improvement.
- Operational maturity.
- Steward participation.
- Institutional resilience.

---

Exceptions

Emergency operational conditions may temporarily delay scheduled audits.

Delayed audits shall be documented and completed as soon as practical.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Audit plans.
- Evidence inventories.
- Audit reports.
- Corrective action registers.
- Verification records.
- Operational maturity assessments.
- Lessons learned.

---

Continuous Improvement

Operational audit practices shall themselves be reviewed regularly to improve governance effectiveness, operational quality, AI-assisted auditing, institutional learning, and long-term stewardship.
