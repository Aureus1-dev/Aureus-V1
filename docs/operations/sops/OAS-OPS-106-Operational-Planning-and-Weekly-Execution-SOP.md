OAS-OPS-106 — Operational Planning and Weekly Execution Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-OPS-106
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-003 — Operational Execution and Workflow Framework
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical weekly operational planning and execution process for Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure work is prioritized effectively, responsibilities are clearly assigned, progress is continuously monitored, obstacles are resolved promptly, and operational excellence is achieved through disciplined execution.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Weekly planning
- Operational priorities
- Team execution
- AI-assisted operations
- Work assignment
- Progress tracking
- Operational meetings
- Blocker management
- Weekly reviews
- Continuous improvement

---

Objectives

Weekly operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Prioritize meaningful work.
- Improve execution quality.
- Promote accountability.
- Encourage collaboration.
- Support continuous learning.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Operations Steward

Responsible for:

- Coordinating weekly planning.
- Confirming priorities.
- Monitoring execution.
- Removing operational obstacles.
- Reviewing weekly outcomes.

---

Team Stewards

Responsible for:

- Executing assigned work.
- Reporting progress.
- Escalating blockers.
- Supporting collaboration.
- Completing operational commitments.

---

AI Operations Steward

Responsible for:

- Preparing planning summaries.
- Prioritizing operational queues.
- Monitoring workflow progress.
- Identifying risks.
- Tracking commitments.
- Producing weekly reports.

Human stewards remain responsible for operational priorities and significant decisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before weekly planning:

- Previous week's work is reviewed.
- Outstanding work is identified.
- Operational metrics are available.
- Strategic priorities are updated.
- Resource availability is confirmed.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Weekly Review

Review:

- Completed work.
- Outstanding work.
- Member outcomes.
- Operational metrics.
- Lessons learned.
- Current priorities.

---

Step 2 — Prioritization

Prioritize work according to:

- Mission impact.
- Member impact.
- Strategic importance.
- Operational urgency.
- Risk reduction.
- Resource availability.

---

Step 3 — Work Assignment

Assign:

- Responsible steward.
- Supporting contributors.
- AI assistance where appropriate.
- Expected outcomes.
- Completion targets.

Every operational task shall have a clearly identified owner.

---

Step 4 — Execution

Execute planned work while:

- Monitoring progress.
- Maintaining quality.
- Coordinating dependencies.
- Communicating updates.
- Escalating issues promptly.

---

Step 5 — Blocker Management

Identify and resolve:

- Resource constraints.
- Technical issues.
- Operational conflicts.
- External dependencies.
- Workflow delays.

Blockers should be addressed as early as practical.

---

Step 6 — Weekly Review Meeting

Evaluate:

- Progress achieved.
- Goals completed.
- Operational risks.
- Member outcomes.
- Improvement opportunities.
- Upcoming priorities.

Document action items and ownership.

---

Step 7 — Continuous Improvement

Capture:

- Lessons learned.
- Process improvements.
- Workflow enhancements.
- AI recommendations.
- Steward feedback.

Improvements should be incorporated into future planning cycles.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Weekly completion rate.
- Priority achievement.
- Workflow cycle time.
- Blocker resolution time.
- Member outcomes.
- Team collaboration.
- Operational quality.
- Continuous improvement actions.

---

Exceptions

Emergency operational situations may require temporary changes to planning cycles while maintaining constitutional accountability and appropriate documentation.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Weekly plans.
- Priority lists.
- Work assignments.
- Meeting notes.
- Action items.
- Operational metrics.
- Improvement logs.
- Weekly summaries.

---

Continuous Improvement

Weekly operational planning shall improve through operational reviews, steward feedback, member feedback, AI-assisted analysis, workflow evaluations, and lessons learned.
