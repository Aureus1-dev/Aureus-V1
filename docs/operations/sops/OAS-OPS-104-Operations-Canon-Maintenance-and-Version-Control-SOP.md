OAS-OPS-104 — Operations Canon Maintenance and Version Control Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-OPS-104
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-001 — Operational Governance Charter
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for proposing, reviewing, approving, versioning, publishing, interpreting, archiving, maintaining, and continuously improving the Aureus Operations Canon.

Its purpose is to preserve constitutional integrity, maintain operational knowledge, ensure disciplined evolution of operational governance, and provide a transparent stewardship process for future generations.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Operations Charters
- Operations Frameworks
- Operations SOPs
- Service delivery standards
- Process management documentation
- Operational resilience plans
- Operational governance records
- Workflow standards
- Operational knowledge repositories

---

Objectives

Operations Canon governance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain historical continuity.
- Ensure version traceability.
- Support disciplined institutional evolution.
- Preserve operational knowledge.
- Strengthen future stewardship.

---

Guiding Principles

The Operations Canon shall evolve deliberately rather than reactively.

Every approved revision should strengthen operational excellence, service quality, organizational resilience, workflow discipline, institutional learning, or constitutional fidelity while preserving the complete historical record.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Chief Operations Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining the Operations Canon.
- Coordinating canonical reviews.
- Approving routine revisions within delegated authority.
- Preserving version history.
- Publishing approved revisions.

---

Operations Governance Council

Responsible for:

- Reviewing significant amendments.
- Evaluating constitutional alignment.
- Recommending improvements.
- Interpreting operational governance.
- Supporting long-term operational stewardship.

---

AI Operations Knowledge Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring document consistency.
- Detecting conflicts.
- Tracking revision history.
- Maintaining cross-references.
- Supporting operational research.
- Identifying improvement opportunities.

Human stewards retain final authority over all canonical operations revisions and interpretations.

---

Prerequisites

Before modifying any canonical operations document:

- Proposed revisions are documented.
- Supporting rationale is prepared.
- Related documents are identified.
- Operational implications are evaluated.
- Required reviewers are assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Change Proposal

Document:

- Proposed revision.
- Purpose.
- Operational justification.
- Expected benefits.
- Related documents.
- Anticipated institutional impacts.

---

Step 2 — Canonical Review

Evaluate:

- Constitutional alignment.
- Operational consistency.
- Governance implications.
- Service impacts.
- Long-term institutional value.

---

Step 3 — Approval

The appropriate governance authority may:

- Approve.
- Approve with revisions.
- Return for additional review.
- Reject.

All approval decisions shall be documented.

---

Step 4 — Version Management

Every approved revision shall include:

- Version number.
- Revision date.
- Change summary.
- Approval record.
- Updated cross-references where applicable.

Superseded versions shall remain permanently archived.

---

Step 5 — Publication

Publish approved revisions within the official Aureus repository.

Update indexes, document references, and governance records where appropriate.

---

Step 6 — Archival

Archive prior versions while preserving:

- Original content.
- Approval history.
- Revision history.
- Historical context.

Institutional operational knowledge shall remain permanently available.

---

Step 7 — Periodic Review

Conduct scheduled reviews to identify:

- Obsolete guidance.
- Governance inconsistencies.
- Emerging operational needs.
- Opportunities for simplification.
- Operational governance improvement initiatives.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Review completion.
- Version accuracy.
- Cross-reference integrity.
- Canon consistency.
- Amendment quality.
- Publication timeliness.
- Historical preservation.
- Steward satisfaction.

---

Exceptions

Emergency operational governance revisions may follow expedited governance procedures when necessary to preserve critical operations, protect members, or respond to extraordinary institutional circumstances.

Emergency revisions shall receive formal retrospective review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Amendment proposals.
- Review records.
- Approval records.
- Version histories.
- Archive records.
- Canon indexes.
- Interpretation guidance.
- Governance reports.

---

Continuous Improvement

Operations Canon governance shall improve through governance reviews, operational assessments, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, documentation quality reviews, resilience evaluations, and institutional learning.
