OAS-OPS-107 — Knowledge Management and Documentation Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-012 — Knowledge and Documentation Framework
Owner: Chief Knowledge Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Quarterly

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for creating, reviewing, approving, publishing, maintaining, versioning, archiving, and retrieving institutional knowledge throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure that operational knowledge remains accurate, accessible, trustworthy, and preserved for future generations of members, stewards, and AI operational agents.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to all constitutional documents, operational frameworks, standard operating procedures, policies, decision logs, training materials, technical documentation, and other controlled institutional records.

---

Objectives

Every knowledge management activity should:

- Preserve institutional memory.
- Improve operational consistency.
- Strengthen decision-making.
- Support human stewards.
- Support AI operational agents.
- Maintain documentation quality.
- Protect historical records.
- Encourage continuous learning.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Human Steward

Responsible for:

- Authoring documentation.
- Reviewing content.
- Approving revisions.
- Maintaining document quality.
- Ensuring constitutional alignment.

AI Operational Agent

Responsible for:

- Organizing documentation.
- Detecting duplicates.
- Tracking revisions.
- Preparing document summaries.
- Monitoring review schedules.
- Supporting knowledge retrieval.

Human approval is required before controlled documents become effective.

---

Prerequisites

Before creating or revising documentation:

- The purpose is clearly defined.
- Document ownership is assigned.
- Related governing documents are identified.
- Required references are available.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Creation

Prepare the document using the approved Aureus documentation standards.

---

Step 2 — Review

Review for:

- Accuracy
- Clarity
- Completeness
- Constitutional alignment
- Operational consistency

---

Step 3 — Approval

Obtain approval from the designated document owner or governing authority.

---

Step 4 — Publication

Publish the approved document in the appropriate institutional repository.

Record:

- Version
- Publication date
- Owner
- Review schedule

---

Step 5 — Maintenance

Review documents periodically to ensure continued accuracy and relevance.

Update documents whenever significant operational changes occur.

---

Step 6 — Version Control

Record:

- Version number
- Revision date
- Summary of changes
- Approving authority

Historical versions should remain preserved.

---

Step 7 — Archiving

When documents are retired:

- Archive the final version.
- Preserve historical references.
- Record the retirement reason.
- Link replacement documents when applicable.

---

Step 8 — Retrieval

Knowledge repositories should enable authorized users to locate institutional documentation efficiently.

Knowledge should remain searchable, organized, and appropriately secured.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Documentation completeness
- Review completion rate
- Version accuracy
- Retrieval efficiency
- Duplicate reduction
- Knowledge reuse
- Steward satisfaction
- AI retrieval effectiveness

---

Exceptions

If documentation conflicts with constitutional authority or contains significant errors:

- Flag the document immediately.
- Notify the document owner.
- Restrict use if necessary.
- Complete corrective revisions before republication.

---

Documentation

Maintain records including:

- Document owner
- Version history
- Review dates
- Approval history
- Archive status
- Related documents

---

Continuous Improvement

Knowledge management practices shall be reviewed regularly to improve organization, accessibility, quality, searchability, governance, and institutional learning.
