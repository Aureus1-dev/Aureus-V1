OAS-OPS-103 — Operational Resilience and Business Continuity Operations Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-OPS-103
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-004 — Operational Resilience and Continuous Improvement Framework
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for business continuity planning, incident response, operational recovery, continuity testing, service restoration, resilience exercises, lessons learned, and continuous operational resilience improvement throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure Aureus remains capable of protecting members, maintaining critical operations, recovering from disruptions, and continuously strengthening institutional resilience.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Business continuity
- Incident response
- Operational recovery
- Service restoration
- Continuity planning
- Continuity testing
- Operational resilience exercises
- Lessons learned
- Operational risk response
- Recovery documentation

---

Objectives

Operational resilience operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain operational continuity.
- Strengthen preparedness.
- Improve recovery capability.
- Reduce operational disruption.
- Support continuous improvement.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Operational Resilience Steward

Responsible for:

- Coordinating continuity planning.
- Managing incident response activities.
- Maintaining recovery documentation.
- Organizing resilience exercises.
- Preparing governance reports.

---

Service Owners

Responsible for:

- Maintaining continuity plans.
- Supporting recovery activities.
- Reporting operational incidents.
- Participating in resilience exercises.
- Updating operational documentation.

---

AI Operational Resilience Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring operational alerts.
- Supporting incident tracking.
- Preparing continuity summaries.
- Tracking recovery metrics.
- Organizing lessons learned.
- Producing resilience analytics.

Human stewards remain responsible for incident command, recovery priorities, continuity decisions, governance actions, and institutional accountability.

---

Prerequisites

Before continuity operations:

- Critical services are identified.
- Continuity plans are documented.
- Recovery responsibilities are assigned.
- Communication procedures are established.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Continuity Planning

Document:

- Critical services.
- Recovery priorities.
- Resource requirements.
- Communication plans.
- Recovery objectives.
- Responsible stewards.

---

Step 2 — Incident Response

When incidents occur:

- Detect the incident.
- Document the event.
- Assess severity.
- Notify appropriate stewards.
- Activate response procedures.
- Coordinate response activities.

---

Step 3 — Operational Recovery

Restore operations by:

- Prioritizing essential services.
- Allocating recovery resources.
- Monitoring recovery progress.
- Verifying service quality.
- Recording recovery activities.

---

Step 4 — Continuity Testing

Conduct periodic:

- Tabletop exercises.
- Operational simulations.
- Recovery testing.
- Communication testing.
- Readiness assessments.

Document all results.

---

Step 5 — Service Restoration

Before returning to normal operations:

- Verify service stability.
- Confirm operational readiness.
- Communicate restoration status.
- Update operational records.
- Close incident documentation.

---

Step 6 — Lessons Learned

Following significant incidents:

- Conduct after-action reviews.
- Identify root causes.
- Document lessons learned.
- Recommend improvements.
- Update continuity plans.

---

Step 7 — Continuous Improvement

Improve resilience through:

- Incident reviews.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance evaluations.
- Continuity exercises.
- Lessons learned implementation.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Incident response time.
- Recovery time.
- Service restoration success.
- Continuity exercise completion.
- Operational readiness.
- Lessons learned implementation.
- Governance compliance.
- Mission support.

---

Exceptions

Extraordinary emergencies may require temporary deviations from established procedures while maintaining documentation and retrospective governance review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Business continuity plans.
- Incident logs.
- Recovery reports.
- Continuity exercise records.
- Lessons learned documentation.
- Operational resilience metrics.
- Governance reports.
- Improvement plans.

---

Continuous Improvement

Operational resilience operations shall improve through resilience assessments, steward feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance evaluations, continuity exercises, and lessons learned.
