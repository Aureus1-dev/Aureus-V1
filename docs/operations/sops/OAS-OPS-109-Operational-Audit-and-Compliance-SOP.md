OAS-OPS-109 — Operational Audit and Compliance Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-013 — Quality Assurance and Risk Management Framework
Owner: Chief Quality and Risk Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Quarterly

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for planning, conducting, documenting, and following up on operational audits and compliance reviews throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to verify that operations remain aligned with Aureus' Constitution, operational frameworks, policies, and standards while identifying opportunities to strengthen stewardship and institutional effectiveness.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to all operational departments, projects, AI operational agents, stewardship functions, policies, Standard Operating Procedures, and institutional processes.

---

Objectives

Every audit should:

- Verify constitutional alignment.
- Confirm operational compliance.
- Protect members.
- Strengthen stewardship.
- Identify operational risks.
- Improve documentation.
- Encourage continuous improvement.
- Increase institutional trust.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Lead Auditor

Responsible for:

- Planning audits.
- Coordinating audit activities.
- Reviewing evidence.
- Documenting findings.
- Preparing audit reports.
- Recommending corrective actions.

Operational Stewards

Responsible for:

- Providing requested information.
- Cooperating during audits.
- Implementing corrective actions.
- Reporting completion.

AI Operational Agent

Responsible for:

- Gathering documentation.
- Preparing audit checklists.
- Monitoring corrective action deadlines.
- Organizing evidence.
- Producing summary reports.
- Tracking compliance metrics.

Human auditors remain responsible for audit conclusions.

---

Prerequisites

Before beginning an audit:

- Audit scope is approved.
- Audit objectives are documented.
- Relevant operational documents are available.
- Responsible operational owners are identified.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Audit Planning

Define:

- Scope
- Objectives
- Criteria
- Timeline
- Participants
- Required evidence

---

Step 2 — Evidence Collection

Collect appropriate evidence, including:

- Documentation
- Operational records
- Performance metrics
- Interviews
- System reports
- Observation of operational activities

Evidence should be reliable and appropriately documented.

---

Step 3 — Evaluation

Evaluate operations for:

- Constitutional compliance
- Policy compliance
- SOP compliance
- Operational effectiveness
- Documentation quality
- Risk management
- Quality assurance

---

Step 4 — Findings

Classify findings as appropriate, such as:

- Conforming practices
- Opportunities for improvement
- Minor nonconformities
- Major nonconformities
- Significant risks

Findings should be supported by documented evidence.

---

Step 5 — Audit Report

Prepare an audit report summarizing:

- Scope
- Methodology
- Findings
- Risks
- Recommendations
- Required corrective actions
- Responsible owners
- Target completion dates

---

Step 6 — Corrective Actions

Assign corrective actions for identified deficiencies.

Each corrective action should identify:

- Responsible owner
- Required action
- Due date
- Verification method

---

Step 7 — Verification

Verify completion of corrective actions before formally closing the audit.

Outstanding issues should remain open until appropriately resolved.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Audit completion rate
- Corrective action completion rate
- Repeat findings
- Compliance trends
- Documentation quality
- Operational improvements
- Risk reduction

---

Exceptions

If audits identify significant constitutional, ethical, legal, or operational concerns:

- Escalate immediately.
- Notify the appropriate governing authority.
- Implement interim protective measures when necessary.
- Document all actions taken.

---

Documentation

Maintain records including:

- Audit plan
- Evidence collected
- Findings
- Audit report
- Corrective actions
- Verification records
- Final closure approval

---

Continuous Improvement

Audit results should be analyzed collectively to identify recurring patterns, systemic weaknesses, and opportunities to strengthen Aureus' governance, stewardship, documentation, AI operations, and institutional resilience.
