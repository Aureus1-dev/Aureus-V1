OAS-OPS-108 — Continuous Improvement and Operational Learning Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-005 — Operational Performance and Continuous Improvement
Owner: Chief Continuous Improvement Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Quarterly

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for identifying, evaluating, approving, implementing, measuring, and institutionalizing operational improvements throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure Aureus becomes stronger through experience, learns systematically, and continuously improves its ability to fulfill its mission.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to all operational improvements arising from:

- Member feedback
- Steward feedback
- Projects
- Incidents
- Audits
- Performance reviews
- AI recommendations
- Operational metrics
- Strategic reviews

---

Objectives

Every improvement effort should:

- Strengthen members.
- Strengthen stewardship.
- Improve operational quality.
- Increase efficiency.
- Reduce unnecessary complexity.
- Preserve institutional learning.
- Improve resilience.
- Advance the mission.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Continuous Improvement Steward

Responsible for:

- Coordinating improvement efforts.
- Prioritizing opportunities.
- Monitoring implementation.
- Verifying outcomes.
- Reporting institutional learning.

Operational Stewards

Responsible for:

- Identifying improvement opportunities.
- Participating in evaluations.
- Implementing approved changes.
- Providing operational feedback.

AI Operational Agent

Responsible for:

- Monitoring trends.
- Identifying recurring issues.
- Detecting improvement opportunities.
- Preparing analytics.
- Tracking implementation progress.
- Recording lessons learned.

---

Prerequisites

Before initiating an improvement effort:

- The opportunity is documented.
- Relevant operational data is available.
- Responsible owners are identified.
- Expected benefits are described.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Identify Opportunity

Capture improvement opportunities from operational experience, feedback, metrics, audits, or observations.

---

Step 2 — Evaluate

Assess:

- Mission alignment
- Expected benefits
- Risks
- Required resources
- Operational impact
- Priority

---

Step 3 — Approval

Obtain approval from the appropriate operational authority before implementing significant changes.

---

Step 4 — Implementation

Implement approved improvements using documented project management procedures.

Communicate changes to affected stewards and members when appropriate.

---

Step 5 — Measurement

Evaluate whether the improvement achieved the intended outcomes.

Measure:

- Operational performance
- Member impact
- Steward impact
- Quality improvements
- Efficiency gains

---

Step 6 — Institutional Learning

Document:

- Lessons learned
- Best practices
- Remaining challenges
- Recommended future improvements

Update institutional knowledge where appropriate.

---

Step 7 — Standardization

When improvements prove successful:

- Update documentation.
- Revise SOPs.
- Update training.
- Inform affected operational teams.
- Archive superseded procedures.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Improvements implemented
- Time to implementation
- Operational performance gains
- Member satisfaction
- Steward satisfaction
- Repeat issue reduction
- Knowledge updates completed
- Improvement adoption rate

---

Exceptions

If proposed improvements introduce unacceptable risk or conflict with constitutional authority:

- Pause implementation.
- Escalate for review.
- Document concerns.
- Resume only after approval.

---

Documentation

Maintain records including:

- Opportunity description
- Evaluation
- Approval
- Implementation plan
- Results
- Lessons learned
- Updated documentation
- Follow-up review

---

Continuous Improvement

The Continuous Improvement process itself shall be reviewed regularly to strengthen organizational learning, responsiveness, stewardship, and institutional effectiveness.
