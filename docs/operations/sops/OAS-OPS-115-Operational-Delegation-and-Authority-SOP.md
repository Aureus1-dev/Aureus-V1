OAS-OPS-115 — Operational Delegation and Authority Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-002 — Operations Governance Framework
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for delegating, exercising, reviewing, modifying, and revoking operational authority throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure authority is distributed in a manner that enables effective execution while preserving constitutional governance, accountability, transparency, and institutional trust.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to all operational authority delegated to:

- Executive Stewards
- Department Stewards
- Project Stewards
- Pod Stewards
- Operational Committees
- Human operational staff
- AI operational agents

---

Objectives

Delegation of authority should:

- Improve execution.
- Clarify responsibility.
- Strengthen accountability.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Support timely decision-making.
- Protect members.
- Encourage responsible stewardship.
- Maintain institutional trust.

---

Guiding Principles

Authority exists to serve the mission of Aureus.

Delegating authority transfers responsibility for execution, but ultimate accountability remains with the appropriate governing authority as defined by the Constitution and operational governance documents.

No delegation may exceed constitutional authority.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Delegating Authority

Responsible for:

- Defining delegated responsibilities.
- Establishing authority limits.
- Identifying approval thresholds.
- Monitoring delegated performance.
- Reviewing delegated authority periodically.

Delegate

Responsible for:

- Exercising delegated authority responsibly.
- Remaining within approved limits.
- Escalating matters beyond delegated authority.
- Maintaining required documentation.
- Reporting significant issues.

AI Operational Agent

Responsible for:

- Recording delegations.
- Tracking approval limits.
- Monitoring delegation reviews.
- Alerting stewards to expired or incomplete delegations.
- Supporting audit activities.

AI may not independently redefine or expand delegated authority.

---

Prerequisites

Before authority is delegated:

- Responsibilities are documented.
- Authority limits are defined.
- Reporting relationships are identified.
- Required training is completed.
- Oversight responsibilities are assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Define the Delegation

Document:

- Position or individual receiving authority.
- Scope of authority.
- Operational responsibilities.
- Effective date.
- Review schedule.

---

Step 2 — Establish Boundaries

Specify:

- Actions permitted.
- Actions requiring approval.
- Financial limits.
- Personnel authority.
- AI authority (if applicable).
- Escalation requirements.

---

Step 3 — Acceptance

The delegate shall acknowledge understanding of:

- Responsibilities.
- Authority limits.
- Reporting requirements.
- Accountability expectations.

---

Step 4 — Operational Execution

Exercise delegated authority within documented limits while maintaining constitutional alignment and operational transparency.

---

Step 5 — Monitoring

Review delegated authority periodically by evaluating:

- Performance.
- Compliance.
- Decision quality.
- Documentation.
- Member outcomes.
- Steward feedback.

---

Step 6 — Modification or Revocation

Delegated authority may be modified or revoked when:

- Operational responsibilities change.
- Performance concerns arise.
- Organizational restructuring occurs.
- Constitutional requirements change.
- Institutional interests require adjustment.

Changes shall be documented and communicated promptly.

---

Emergency Authority

Temporary emergency authority may be granted when immediate action is necessary to protect members, institutional operations, or organizational integrity.

Emergency delegations shall:

- Be narrowly tailored.
- Be time-limited.
- Be documented.
- Receive post-incident review.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Delegation clarity.
- Decision quality.
- Escalation effectiveness.
- Compliance.
- Operational efficiency.
- Steward confidence.
- Member outcomes.
- Audit findings.

---

Exceptions

If uncertainty exists regarding delegated authority:

- Pause non-urgent action when practical.
- Escalate to the appropriate supervising steward.
- Document the issue.
- Await clarification before proceeding.

Protection of members and institutional integrity takes precedence over speed.

---

Documentation

Maintain records including:

- Delegation documents.
- Authority limits.
- Review history.
- Modifications.
- Revocations.
- Performance evaluations.
- Audit results.

---

Continuous Improvement

Delegation practices shall be reviewed regularly to strengthen accountability, clarity, operational effectiveness, AI governance, and stewardship throughout Aureus.
