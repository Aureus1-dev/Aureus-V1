OAS-OPS-119 — Operational Maturity and Capability Assessment Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-005 — Operational Performance and Continuous Improvement Framework
Owner: Chief Continuous Improvement Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for assessing, measuring, benchmarking, and improving the operational maturity and institutional capabilities of Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure Aureus continually strengthens its governance, stewardship, member services, technology, AI operations, organizational resilience, and execution through structured capability assessments.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to all operational domains, including:

- Governance
- Stewardship
- Member services
- AI operational systems
- Technology
- Operations
- Knowledge management
- Risk management
- Projects
- Community development
- Organizational leadership

---

Objectives

Every maturity assessment should:

- Measure institutional capability.
- Identify strengths.
- Identify improvement opportunities.
- Prioritize capability development.
- Benchmark organizational progress.
- Support strategic planning.
- Encourage evidence-based improvement.
- Strengthen long-term resilience.

---

Guiding Principles

Capability assessments shall be:

- Objective.
- Evidence-based.
- Repeatable.
- Transparent.
- Constructive.
- Mission aligned.
- Focused on institutional learning rather than blame.

---

Maturity Levels

Operational capability may be assessed using the following maturity model:

Level 1 — Initial

Processes are informal, inconsistent, or largely dependent on individual effort.

Level 2 — Developing

Basic procedures exist but remain inconsistently applied.

Level 3 — Defined

Processes are documented, standardized, and consistently followed.

Level 4 — Managed

Performance is actively measured, monitored, and continuously improved.

Level 5 — Optimizing

Operations demonstrate institutional learning, innovation, resilience, and sustained excellence.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Assessment Lead

Responsible for:

- Planning assessments.
- Coordinating evidence collection.
- Facilitating evaluations.
- Preparing assessment reports.
- Recommending improvement priorities.

Operational Stewards

Responsible for:

- Providing evidence.
- Participating in assessments.
- Reviewing findings.
- Implementing approved improvements.

AI Operational Agent

Responsible for:

- Collecting operational metrics.
- Organizing supporting documentation.
- Producing maturity dashboards.
- Identifying capability trends.
- Tracking improvement initiatives.

Human leadership remains responsible for assessment conclusions.

---

Prerequisites

Before beginning an assessment:

- Scope is defined.
- Assessment criteria are documented.
- Required evidence is available.
- Assessment participants are identified.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Planning

Define:

- Assessment scope.
- Domains.
- Criteria.
- Timeline.
- Participants.

---

Step 2 — Evidence Collection

Gather evidence including:

- Documentation.
- KPIs.
- Audit findings.
- Performance reports.
- Member feedback.
- Steward feedback.
- AI operational metrics.

---

Step 3 — Evaluation

Assess each operational domain using the established maturity model.

Document strengths, weaknesses, risks, and improvement opportunities.

---

Step 4 — Benchmarking

Compare current capability against:

- Previous assessments.
- Institutional objectives.
- Approved capability targets.
- Internal benchmarks where appropriate.

---

Step 5 — Improvement Planning

Develop prioritized capability improvement plans identifying:

- Objectives.
- Responsible owners.
- Timelines.
- Success measures.

---

Step 6 — Reporting

Prepare an assessment report summarizing:

- Current maturity levels.
- Major findings.
- Recommended improvements.
- Strategic implications.

---

Step 7 — Follow-Up

Monitor implementation of approved capability improvements and reassess progress according to the established review cycle.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Maturity level improvements.
- Capability development progress.
- Improvement plan completion.
- Operational performance.
- Member outcomes.
- Steward effectiveness.
- AI operational effectiveness.
- Organizational resilience.

---

Exceptions

If assessments identify significant operational weaknesses or constitutional concerns:

- Escalate immediately.
- Prioritize corrective action.
- Increase monitoring where appropriate.
- Conduct follow-up assessments.

---

Documentation

Maintain records including:

- Assessment plans.
- Evidence.
- Evaluation results.
- Maturity ratings.
- Improvement plans.
- Follow-up reports.
- Historical comparisons.

---

Continuous Improvement

The capability assessment process shall itself be reviewed regularly to improve methodology, evidence quality, benchmarking, AI support, and institutional learning.
