OAS-OPS-112 — Operations Exception and Waiver Management Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-OPS-112
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-002 — Operations Governance Framework
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for requesting, evaluating, approving, monitoring, renewing, and retiring temporary operational exceptions and waivers throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to provide controlled operational flexibility while preserving constitutional alignment, protecting members, maintaining accountability, and ensuring all deviations remain documented, justified, monitored, and time-limited.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Operational procedure exceptions
- Service standard waivers
- Workflow deviations
- Staffing exceptions
- AI operational policy exceptions
- Governance exceptions
- Temporary operational risk acceptances
- Approved operational policy deviations

---

Objectives

Operational exceptions shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Minimize operational risk.
- Remain temporary.
- Be fully documented.
- Include review and expiration dates.
- Strengthen institutional governance.

---

Guiding Principles

Operational exceptions are temporary governance tools rather than permanent policy changes.

No approved exception shall modify the Operations Canon unless adopted through the formal governance process.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Requesting Steward

Responsible for:

- Preparing the exception request.
- Documenting operational justification.
- Identifying risks.
- Recommending compensating controls.
- Reporting implementation results.

---

Operations Review Authority

Responsible for:

- Reviewing requests.
- Evaluating operational impact.
- Assessing risks.
- Approving, modifying, renewing, or denying requests.
- Confirming closure.

---

AI Governance Steward

Responsible for:

- Registering requests.
- Monitoring expiration dates.
- Tracking approvals.
- Identifying policy conflicts.
- Producing governance reports.
- Supporting compliance reviews.

Human stewards remain responsible for approving operational exceptions.

---

Prerequisites

Before submitting a request:

- The exception is clearly defined.
- Existing procedures have been reviewed.
- Alternatives have been evaluated.
- Risks are documented.
- Compensating controls are identified.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Exception Request

Document:

- Requested exception.
- Operational justification.
- Affected services.
- Risks.
- Compensating controls.
- Requested duration.
- Success criteria.

---

Step 2 — Operational Review

Evaluate:

- Member impact.
- Operational impact.
- Service quality.
- Governance implications.
- AI operational implications.
- Long-term sustainability.

---

Step 3 — Risk Assessment

Assess:

- Operational likelihood.
- Member impact.
- Service disruption.
- Institutional risk.
- Recovery capability.

Document the overall operational risk rating.

---

Step 4 — Approval

The Operations Review Authority may:

- Approve.
- Approve with conditions.
- Request revisions.
- Deny the request.

Approved exceptions shall include review and expiration dates.

---

Step 5 — Monitoring

Monitor:

- Compliance with approval conditions.
- Operational performance.
- Emerging risks.
- Continued operational need.

Exceptions shall be reviewed before expiration.

---

Step 6 — Renewal or Closure

At the review date:

- Close the exception.
- Renew with updated approval.
- Replace with a permanent policy change through formal governance.

Expired exceptions shall not remain active without documented renewal.

---

Step 7 — Lessons Learned

Document:

- Operational outcomes.
- Member impacts.
- Governance improvements.
- Recommendations for future standards.

Lessons learned shall strengthen future operational governance.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Active exceptions.
- Average exception duration.
- Renewal frequency.
- Operational compliance.
- Service impacts.
- Timely closures.
- Governance improvements.
- Standards strengthened through exception reviews.

---

Exceptions

Emergency operational conditions may require immediate temporary deviations before formal review.

Such deviations shall be documented and formally reviewed as soon as operational stability has been restored.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Exception requests.
- Risk assessments.
- Approval records.
- Monitoring reports.
- Renewal records.
- Closure reports.
- Lessons learned.
- Governance metrics.

---

Continuous Improvement

Operations exception management shall improve through governance reviews, operational audits, steward feedback, AI-assisted analysis, lessons learned, and continuous refinement of operational standards.
