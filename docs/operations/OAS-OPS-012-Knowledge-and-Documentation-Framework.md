OAS-OPS-012 — Knowledge and Documentation Framework

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-001 — Operations Charter
Related Documents: OAS-OPS-002 through OAS-OPS-011
Owner: Chief Knowledge Steward
Last Updated: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This document establishes the canonical framework governing the creation, organization, verification, maintenance, preservation, and use of institutional knowledge throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure that knowledge remains accurate, accessible, trustworthy, and available to future generations of members, stewards, and AI systems.

---

Mission

Knowledge Management exists to transform individual learning into enduring institutional wisdom that strengthens the mission, improves decision-making, and enables continuous organizational learning.

---

Guiding Principles

Institutional knowledge shall be:

- Accurate
- Organized
- Searchable
- Secure
- Accessible to authorized users
- Version controlled
- Continuously improved
- Preserved for the future

Knowledge should strengthen stewardship rather than depend upon individual memory.

---

Categories of Knowledge

Institutional knowledge includes:

- Constitutional documents
- Operational documents
- Standard Operating Procedures
- Decision logs
- Policies
- Training materials
- Research
- Lessons learned
- Technical documentation
- Member resources
- Historical records

Each category shall maintain appropriate governance and review procedures.

---

Documentation Standards

Documentation should be:

- Written in clear language
- Consistently formatted
- Properly titled
- Version controlled
- Assigned an owner
- Reviewed periodically
- Linked to related documents where appropriate

---

Knowledge Lifecycle

Institutional knowledge follows this lifecycle:

1. Creation
2. Review
3. Approval
4. Publication
5. Use
6. Maintenance
7. Revision
8. Archiving
9. Historical Preservation

Each stage contributes to the long-term integrity of Aureus.

---

Ownership

Every significant document shall identify:

- Document owner
- Responsible operational domain
- Approval authority
- Review schedule
- Current version

No institutional document should exist without responsible stewardship.

---

Version Control

Every controlled document shall maintain:

- Version number
- Revision date
- Summary of changes
- Approval record
- Previous versions

Historical versions should remain preserved unless superseded by legal requirements.

---

Verification

Important institutional knowledge should be reviewed periodically for:

- Accuracy
- Completeness
- Consistency
- Relevance
- Constitutional alignment

Verified knowledge strengthens institutional trust.

---

Decision Logs

Significant institutional decisions should be recorded with:

- Date
- Decision owner
- Summary
- Supporting rationale
- Alternatives considered
- Expected outcomes
- Review schedule

Decision logs strengthen institutional memory.

---

AI Knowledge Support

AI operational agents may assist by:

- Organizing documentation
- Retrieving information
- Drafting updates
- Identifying inconsistencies
- Detecting duplicate content
- Supporting document searches
- Recommending improvements

Human stewards remain responsible for approving institutional knowledge.

---

Access Management

Knowledge should be accessible according to operational responsibilities.

Access should balance:

- Transparency
- Privacy
- Security
- Operational effectiveness

Access privileges should be reviewed periodically.

---

Preservation

Institutional knowledge should be protected against:

- Accidental loss
- Unauthorized modification
- Corruption
- Obsolescence

Long-term preservation supports institutional continuity.

---

Continuous Improvement

Knowledge systems should be reviewed regularly to improve:

- Organization
- Accessibility
- Accuracy
- Searchability
- AI usability
- Steward usability
- Documentation quality

Every improvement strengthens the institution.

---

Canonical Relationship

This framework governs the creation, maintenance, storage, verification, retrieval, version control, and preservation of institutional knowledge throughout Aureus.

All documentation systems, knowledge repositories, decision logs, training materials, and AI knowledge services shall conform to this framework unless superseded by a higher governing document.
