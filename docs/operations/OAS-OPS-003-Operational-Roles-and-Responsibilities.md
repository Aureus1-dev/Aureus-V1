OAS-OPS-003 — Operational Roles and Responsibilities

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-001 — Operations Charter
Related Documents: OAS-OPS-002 — Operations Governance
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Last Updated: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This document establishes the operational roles within Aureus, defines their responsibilities and authority, and ensures that every recurring operational responsibility has a clearly identified owner.

Operational roles exist to advance the mission of Aureus through faithful stewardship, reliable execution, and continuous improvement.

---

Guiding Principles

Every operational role shall:

- Serve the mission before personal interests.
- Exercise authority responsibly.
- Act with integrity and transparency.
- Respect constitutional governance.
- Support collaboration across the institution.
- Seek continuous improvement.
- Leave documented knowledge for future stewards.

---

Categories of Operational Roles

Operational roles fall into the following categories:

1. Executive Stewardship
2. Operational Leadership
3. Member Stewardship
4. Community Stewardship
5. Knowledge Stewardship
6. Technical Stewardship
7. AI Operational Agents
8. Operational Support

Additional roles may be created as Aureus evolves.

---

Executive Stewardship

Executive stewards provide strategic operational leadership.

Responsibilities include:

- Long-term planning
- Organizational coordination
- Resource stewardship
- Policy execution
- Cross-functional alignment
- Operational oversight

Executive stewards are accountable for institutional performance rather than individual tasks.

---

Operational Leaders

Operational leaders manage specific operational domains.

Examples include:

- Member Operations
- Steward Operations
- Community Operations
- Technology Operations
- Finance Operations
- Communications Operations
- Knowledge Operations
- Quality Operations

Each operational domain shall maintain documented procedures and measurable performance indicators.

---

Member Stewards

Member stewards directly support members.

Responsibilities include:

- Onboarding
- Goal planning
- Progress reviews
- Accountability
- Guidance
- Resource coordination
- Escalating member concerns
- Encouraging participation

Member relationships should prioritize trust, dignity, and long-term flourishing.

---

Community Stewards

Community stewards cultivate healthy relationships throughout Aureus.

Responsibilities include:

- Moderation
- Community engagement
- Conflict resolution
- Event coordination
- Pod support
- Recognition programs
- Member participation

Healthy communities strengthen the institution.

---

Knowledge Stewards

Knowledge stewards maintain Aureus' institutional memory.

Responsibilities include:

- Documentation
- Decision logs
- Knowledge verification
- Canonical record maintenance
- Version management
- Knowledge accessibility

Institutional knowledge should remain organized, accurate, and searchable.

---

Technical Stewards

Technical stewards support the operational technology used throughout Aureus.

Responsibilities include:

- Platform reliability
- Automation support
- Security coordination
- Infrastructure oversight
- Systems monitoring
- Technical documentation

Technology should strengthen stewardship rather than complicate it.

---

AI Operational Agents

AI operational agents assist human stewards through specialized responsibilities.

Examples include:

- Onboarding Agent
- Scheduling Agent
- Documentation Agent
- Research Agent
- Analytics Agent
- Quality Assurance Agent
- Knowledge Agent
- Communications Agent
- Workflow Agent
- Risk Monitoring Agent

Each AI agent shall have:

- Defined mission
- Approved scope
- Decision boundaries
- Escalation rules
- Performance metrics
- Audit capability

AI agents operate under human oversight.

---

Operational Support

Operational support roles may assist with:

- Administrative tasks
- Data entry
- Communications
- Event logistics
- Record management
- Internal coordination

Support roles increase organizational capacity.

---

Responsibility Assignment

Every operational responsibility shall identify:

- Primary owner
- Supporting roles
- Required approvals
- Success metrics
- Review schedule

Shared responsibility does not eliminate individual accountability.

---

Authority Limits

Authority shall be proportional to responsibility.

No operational role may exercise authority beyond its documented scope without proper authorization.

Authority may be delegated but accountability remains with the assigned steward.

---

Performance Expectations

Operational performance should be evaluated using measurable criteria including:

- Reliability
- Quality
- Timeliness
- Stewardship
- Collaboration
- Documentation quality
- Member outcomes
- Continuous improvement efforts

Performance evaluation exists to strengthen the institution rather than assign blame.

---

Continuous Development

Every operational role should receive ongoing education, coaching, and opportunities for growth.

Aureus should continually develop future leaders and strengthen institutional capability.

---

Succession Planning

Each critical operational role should maintain:

- Current documentation
- Standard operating procedures
- Transition guidance
- Knowledge transfer materials

No operational role should become dependent upon a single individual.

---

Amendments

Operational roles and responsibilities may evolve through the established constitutional governance process, provided changes strengthen stewardship, accountability, and organizational effectiveness.
