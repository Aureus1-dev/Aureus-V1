OAS-OPS-007 — AI Operational Workforce Framework

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-001 — Operations Charter
Related Documents: OAS-OPS-002 through OAS-OPS-006
Owner: Chief Operations Steward
Last Updated: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This document establishes the canonical framework governing all Artificial Intelligence operational agents used by Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure that AI strengthens stewardship, improves operational excellence, preserves accountability, and remains aligned with the mission, values, and governing documents of Aureus.

---

Guiding Principles

Every AI operational agent shall:

- Advance the mission of Aureus.
- Strengthen human stewardship.
- Improve operational quality.
- Increase consistency.
- Reduce unnecessary administrative work.
- Preserve institutional knowledge.
- Operate transparently.
- Remain accountable through human oversight.

AI exists to augment human capability, not replace responsible stewardship.

---

Definition

An AI Operational Agent is a specialized software system assigned responsibility for one or more defined operational functions under documented authority and supervision.

Each AI agent shall have:

- A defined mission.
- Clearly documented responsibilities.
- Approved decision boundaries.
- Performance measures.
- Escalation procedures.
- Audit requirements.

---

Categories of AI Agents

Operational AI agents may support areas including:

- Member Operations
- Steward Operations
- Scheduling
- Communications
- Documentation
- Knowledge Management
- Research
- Analytics
- Quality Assurance
- Risk Monitoring
- Compliance Support
- Project Coordination
- Continuous Improvement
- Automation Management

Additional categories may be established as Aureus evolves.

---

Required Agent Profile

Every AI agent shall maintain a documented profile including:

- Agent name
- Mission
- Purpose
- Operational owner
- Supported departments
- Inputs
- Outputs
- Tools and systems used
- Decision authority
- Escalation rules
- Success metrics
- Review schedule
- Version history

---

Operational Authority

AI agents may perform only those actions expressly authorized by documented operational policy.

Authority shall always be proportional to responsibility.

No AI agent possesses inherent institutional authority.

---

Decision Boundaries

Each AI agent shall clearly identify:

- Actions it may perform automatically.
- Actions requiring steward review.
- Actions requiring executive approval.
- Actions reserved to constitutional governance.

When uncertainty exists, the agent shall escalate rather than guess.

---

Human Oversight

Every AI agent shall have an assigned human steward responsible for:

- Configuration
- Performance review
- Operational monitoring
- Exception handling
- Policy compliance
- Continuous improvement

Accountability always remains with human leadership.

---

Transparency

Whenever practical, AI-supported operational actions should be traceable.

Operational records should identify:

- Responsible AI agent
- Date and time
- Relevant inputs
- Outputs generated
- Required approvals
- Human reviewer, where applicable

---

Quality Assurance

AI operational performance should be monitored through:

- Accuracy
- Reliability
- Timeliness
- Member satisfaction
- Steward satisfaction
- Escalation quality
- Error frequency
- Recovery effectiveness

Quality measurement exists to improve institutional performance.

---

Risk Management

Potential AI risks include:

- Incorrect outputs
- Incomplete information
- Operational bias
- Unauthorized automation
- Security concerns
- Documentation errors
- Workflow failures

Each identified risk should include documented mitigation strategies.

---

Escalation

AI agents shall immediately escalate when:

- Authority limits are reached.
- Policies conflict.
- Required information is unavailable.
- Ethical uncertainty exists.
- System failures occur.
- Human approval is required.
- Member well-being may be affected.

Escalation is a sign of responsible operation.

---

Continuous Learning

Operational improvements identified through AI execution should be documented and submitted for human review.

No AI agent may independently modify institutional policy or governing documents.

Approved improvements should be incorporated through established governance processes.

---

Retirement and Replacement

AI agents may be:

- Updated
- Replaced
- Consolidated
- Retired

Changes should preserve operational continuity and institutional knowledge.

Retired agents should remain documented for historical reference.

---

Performance Reviews

Each AI agent should undergo periodic review to evaluate:

- Mission alignment
- Operational effectiveness
- Member impact
- Steward feedback
- Quality metrics
- Automation opportunities
- Required improvements

Reviews should strengthen both the AI workforce and the institution.

---

Canonical Relationship

This framework governs every operational AI agent used throughout Aureus.

Department-specific AI agents, workflows, prompts, automation systems, and future AI capabilities shall conform to this framework unless a higher governing document expressly provides otherwise.
