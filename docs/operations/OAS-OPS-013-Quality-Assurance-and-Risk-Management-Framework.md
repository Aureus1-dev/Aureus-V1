OAS-OPS-013 — Quality Assurance and Risk Management Framework

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-001 — Operations Charter
Related Documents: OAS-OPS-002 through OAS-OPS-012
Owner: Chief Quality and Risk Steward
Last Updated: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This document establishes the canonical framework governing quality assurance, operational risk management, audits, incident prevention, corrective actions, preventive actions, and organizational resilience throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to protect members, strengthen institutional trust, improve operational excellence, and ensure Aureus continues to fulfill its mission responsibly.

---

Mission

Quality Assurance and Risk Management exists to promote reliable execution, identify potential risks early, prevent avoidable failures, and foster a culture of continuous improvement and responsible stewardship.

---

Guiding Principles

Quality and risk management shall:

- Protect members.
- Protect institutional integrity.
- Promote excellence.
- Encourage transparency.
- Support informed decision-making.
- Strengthen resilience.
- Learn from failures.
- Continuously improve.

Risk management exists to enable responsible progress rather than discourage innovation.

---

Quality Assurance

Quality Assurance seeks to ensure that operations consistently meet established standards.

Quality activities may include:

- Operational reviews
- Procedure verification
- Documentation reviews
- Process audits
- Member feedback
- Steward feedback
- AI performance reviews
- Continuous monitoring

---

Risk Management

Operational risks should be identified, assessed, documented, monitored, and addressed before they significantly affect members or institutional performance.

Risks may include:

- Operational risks
- Financial risks
- Technology risks
- Information security risks
- Legal and regulatory risks
- Reputational risks
- Strategic risks
- Human resource risks

---

Risk Assessment

Every significant risk should identify:

- Description
- Likelihood
- Potential impact
- Responsible owner
- Existing controls
- Recommended mitigation
- Review schedule

Risk assessments should be reviewed periodically.

---

Risk Register

Aureus shall maintain an institutional Risk Register documenting significant operational risks and the actions taken to manage them.

The Risk Register should remain current and accessible to authorized stewards.

---

Audits

Operational audits should periodically evaluate:

- Policy compliance
- Procedural compliance
- Documentation quality
- Operational effectiveness
- Internal controls
- AI workflow performance
- Member protection
- Stewardship practices

Audits exist to improve operations rather than assign blame.

---

Incident Management

Operational incidents should be:

1. Identified
2. Documented
3. Contained
4. Investigated
5. Resolved
6. Reviewed
7. Used for institutional learning

Timely reporting strengthens institutional resilience.

---

Root Cause Analysis

Significant incidents should include structured analysis of contributing causes.

Reviews should examine:

- Process weaknesses
- Training gaps
- Technology issues
- Communication failures
- Documentation deficiencies
- Resource limitations

The objective is prevention of recurrence.

---

Corrective Actions

Corrective actions shall address identified deficiencies through documented improvement plans.

Each corrective action should include:

- Responsible owner
- Required actions
- Completion timeline
- Verification method
- Review date

---

Preventive Actions

Preventive actions seek to reduce the likelihood of future operational failures.

Whenever practical, Aureus should improve systems instead of relying solely upon individual effort.

---

AI Support

AI operational agents may assist with:

- Risk monitoring
- Trend analysis
- Audit preparation
- Incident documentation
- Compliance tracking
- Dashboard reporting
- Quality analytics

Human stewards remain responsible for final decisions and corrective actions.

---

Organizational Resilience

Operational resilience should include:

- Business continuity planning
- Knowledge preservation
- Succession planning
- Backup procedures
- Disaster recovery
- Operational redundancy
- Continuous improvement

Resilience enables Aureus to continue serving members during disruption.

---

Continuous Improvement

Quality Assurance and Risk Management shall regularly review:

- Operational performance
- Emerging risks
- Incident trends
- Audit findings
- Member feedback
- Steward feedback
- AI performance
- Organizational resilience

Lessons learned should strengthen future operations.

---

Canonical Relationship

This framework governs all quality assurance activities, operational audits, risk management practices, incident response procedures, corrective actions, preventive actions, and resilience planning throughout Aureus.

All related operational procedures shall conform to this framework unless superseded by a higher governing document.
