OAS-OPS-009 — Steward Operations Framework

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-001 — Operations Charter
Related Documents: OAS-OPS-002 through OAS-OPS-008
Owner: Chief Stewardship Officer
Last Updated: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This document establishes the canonical operational framework governing the complete lifecycle of every Aureus steward.

Its purpose is to ensure that every steward is selected, trained, supported, evaluated, and developed according to consistent standards of excellence, integrity, accountability, and service.

---

Mission

Steward Operations exists to develop trustworthy leaders who faithfully advance the mission of Aureus through wisdom, humility, competence, compassion, and disciplined execution.

---

Guiding Principles

Every steward shall strive to:

- Serve before leading.
- Protect institutional trust.
- Act with integrity.
- Demonstrate humility.
- Pursue excellence.
- Continue learning.
- Strengthen members.
- Strengthen fellow stewards.
- Leave the institution stronger than they found it.

---

Steward Lifecycle

The standard steward lifecycle consists of:

1. Discovery
2. Recruitment
3. Application
4. Evaluation
5. Selection
6. Training
7. Certification
8. Active Stewardship
9. Continuing Education
10. Performance Review
11. Leadership Development
12. Succession Planning
13. Transition or Retirement

Each stage shall maintain documented operational procedures.

---

Recruitment

Recruitment should seek individuals who demonstrate:

- Character
- Reliability
- Compassion
- Sound judgment
- Willingness to learn
- Commitment to the mission
- Respect for constitutional governance

Recruitment should emphasize service rather than status.

---

Training

Every steward shall complete foundational training before assuming operational responsibilities.

Training should include:

- Mission and values
- Constitutional governance
- Operational procedures
- Member care
- Ethical decision-making
- AI collaboration
- Documentation standards
- Communication
- Privacy and security
- Conflict resolution

---

Certification

Certification confirms that a steward has demonstrated the competencies required for their operational responsibilities.

Certification standards shall be:

- Documented
- Fair
- Consistent
- Periodically reviewed

---

Continuing Education

Stewards should participate in ongoing education to strengthen:

- Leadership
- Operational excellence
- Member support
- Technology skills
- Institutional knowledge
- Communication
- Problem solving

Learning is a continuing responsibility.

---

Steward Responsibilities

Every steward is responsible for:

- Serving members faithfully.
- Following constitutional governance.
- Maintaining accurate documentation.
- Protecting confidential information.
- Escalating significant concerns.
- Supporting fellow stewards.
- Seeking continuous improvement.

---

Steward Well-Being

Aureus recognizes that healthy stewards serve others more effectively.

Operational practices should encourage:

- Sustainable workloads
- Appropriate rest
- Mutual encouragement
- Professional growth
- Access to support when needed

Steward well-being strengthens institutional resilience.

---

Performance Reviews

Performance reviews should evaluate:

- Mission alignment
- Member outcomes
- Operational quality
- Collaboration
- Documentation
- Leadership
- Continuous improvement
- Professional development

Reviews should encourage growth rather than merely evaluate performance.

---

Recognition

Outstanding stewardship should be recognized in ways that reinforce humility, service, excellence, and commitment to the mission.

Recognition should celebrate faithful stewardship rather than personal prestige.

---

Leadership Development

Aureus should intentionally prepare future leaders through:

- Mentorship
- Practical experience
- Education
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Increasing responsibility
- Constructive feedback

Leadership development is an institutional responsibility.

---

Succession Planning

Every critical stewardship role should maintain:

- Documented responsibilities
- Operational procedures
- Knowledge transfer plans
- Qualified successors where practical

Institutional continuity should never depend upon a single individual.

---

AI Collaboration

Stewards should learn to collaborate effectively with AI operational agents.

Human stewards remain responsible for:

- Ethical judgment
- Relationship building
- Constitutional interpretation
- Final approval of significant operational decisions

AI strengthens stewardship but does not replace it.

---

Continuous Improvement

Steward Operations shall regularly review:

- Recruitment effectiveness
- Training quality
- Certification standards
- Steward satisfaction
- Member outcomes
- Leadership development
- Operational performance

Improvements should be documented and incorporated through established governance processes.

---

Canonical Relationship

This framework governs all steward recruitment, onboarding, education, certification, mentoring, evaluation, advancement, and succession activities conducted within Aureus.

All steward-specific procedures shall conform to this framework unless superseded by a higher governing document.
