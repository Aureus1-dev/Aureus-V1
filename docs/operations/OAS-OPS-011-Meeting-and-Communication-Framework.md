OAS-OPS-011 — Meeting and Communication Framework

Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-OPS-001 — Operations Charter
Related Documents: OAS-OPS-002 through OAS-OPS-010
Owner: Chief Communications Steward
Last Updated: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This document establishes the canonical framework governing meetings and operational communication throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure communication remains purposeful, transparent, respectful, well-documented, and aligned with the mission of Aureus.

---

Mission

Communication exists to improve understanding, coordination, trust, execution, and stewardship.

Meetings exist to make decisions, solve problems, strengthen relationships, and coordinate meaningful action.

---

Guiding Principles

Every communication should be:

- Honest
- Respectful
- Clear
- Timely
- Accurate
- Purposeful
- Action-oriented
- Appropriate to its audience

Communication should reduce confusion rather than create it.

---

Meeting Principles

Every meeting should have:

- A clearly defined purpose
- A designated facilitator
- An agenda shared beforehand when practical
- The appropriate participants
- Documented decisions
- Action items with owners
- Defined follow-up

Meetings without purpose should not be scheduled.

---

Types of Meetings

Aureus may conduct meetings including:

- Daily operational check-ins
- Weekly team meetings
- Pod meetings
- Project meetings
- Steward coaching sessions
- Leadership meetings
- Executive operations reviews
- Stewardship Council meetings
- Strategic planning sessions
- Emergency response meetings
- Retrospectives
- Annual planning meetings

Each meeting type should maintain its own documented procedure.

---

Standard Meeting Agenda

Unless otherwise required, meetings should include:

1. Opening
2. Review of previous action items
3. Current priorities
4. Discussion and decisions
5. Risks or blockers
6. New action items
7. Summary
8. Closing

---

Decision Documentation

Significant meeting decisions should record:

- Date
- Participants
- Decision summary
- Supporting rationale
- Assigned owners
- Due dates
- Follow-up requirements

Institutional memory depends upon reliable documentation.

---

Action Items

Every action item should identify:

- Description
- Responsible owner
- Due date
- Dependencies
- Completion status

Responsibilities should never remain ambiguous.

---

Communication Channels

Operational communication should occur through approved institutional channels.

Communication systems should support:

- Accessibility
- Searchability
- Security
- Appropriate record retention
- Reliable delivery

---

AI Support

AI operational agents may assist meetings by:

- Scheduling
- Agenda preparation
- Note taking
- Action item tracking
- Follow-up reminders
- Knowledge retrieval
- Meeting summaries
- Operational analytics

Human participants remain responsible for final decisions and approvals.

---

Confidentiality

Sensitive operational information shall be shared only with authorized participants.

Confidentiality protects members, stewards, and institutional trust.

---

Conflict During Meetings

Disagreements should be handled respectfully through:

- Listening
- Clarification
- Evidence
- Constructive discussion
- Steward facilitation
- Escalation when necessary

The objective is better decisions, not winning arguments.

---

Communication Quality

Operational communication should be reviewed for:

- Accuracy
- Completeness
- Clarity
- Timeliness
- Respectfulness
- Mission alignment

Improving communication improves execution.

---

Continuous Improvement

Meeting effectiveness should be evaluated regularly.

Reviews should examine:

- Time efficiency
- Decision quality
- Participation
- Follow-through
- Documentation quality
- Operational outcomes

Processes should be refined whenever improvements are identified.

---

Canonical Relationship

This framework governs all operational meetings, communication standards, reporting practices, agendas, meeting records, and communication workflows throughout Aureus unless superseded by a higher governing document.
