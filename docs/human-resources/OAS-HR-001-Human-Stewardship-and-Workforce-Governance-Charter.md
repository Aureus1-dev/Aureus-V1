OAS-HR-001 — Human Stewardship and Workforce Governance Charter

Canonical Designation: OAS-HR-001
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-001 — Founding Charter
Owner: Chief Human Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Charter establishes the constitutional authority governing all human stewardship activities throughout Aureus, including recruitment, onboarding, steward development, workforce planning, leadership development, performance stewardship, compensation, volunteer stewardship, succession planning, culture, and long-term human flourishing.

Its purpose is to ensure every person who serves Aureus is supported, developed, respected, and empowered to advance the mission while preserving constitutional alignment, institutional integrity, and human dignity.

---

Mission

Human stewardship exists to identify, recruit, develop, support, retain, and empower people whose gifts, character, and service strengthen Aureus and its mission across generations.

---

Scope

This Charter governs:

- Workforce governance
- Recruitment
- Onboarding
- Steward development
- Leadership development
- Performance stewardship
- Compensation
- Volunteer stewardship
- Workforce planning
- Succession planning

---

Guiding Principles

Human stewardship shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Respect human dignity.
- Promote fairness.
- Encourage lifelong learning.
- Strengthen accountability.
- Foster long-term flourishing.

People shall always be stewarded as valued partners in the mission rather than merely organizational resources.

---

Human Stewardship Authority

Human stewardship authority shall be exercised according to:

- The Constitution.
- Approved governance documents.
- Applicable law.
- Delegated authority.
- Stewardship responsibilities.

Authority shall remain documented, transparent, and accountable.

---

Workforce Governance

Workforce governance shall include:

- Clearly defined responsibilities.
- Fair recruitment practices.
- Steward development.
- Leadership succession.
- Performance stewardship.
- Continuous improvement.

Governance shall balance institutional effectiveness with individual flourishing.

---

Steward Development

Aureus shall invest in the continuous development of every steward through education, mentorship, meaningful feedback, leadership opportunities, and lifelong learning.

Development is a permanent institutional responsibility.

---

Workforce Risk Management

Human stewardship risks shall be:

- Identified.
- Assessed.
- Prioritized.
- Mitigated.
- Monitored.
- Reviewed regularly.

Workforce decisions shall balance institutional needs with the well-being and dignity of every steward.

---

Continuous Improvement

Human stewardship shall improve through:

- Steward feedback.
- Workforce assessments.
- Leadership reviews.
- Learning evaluations.
- Governance assessments.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens Aureus' people and mission.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Charter governs the entire Human Stewardship Canon.

All Human Stewardship Frameworks, Human Stewardship SOPs, workforce policies, leadership standards, compensation procedures, volunteer stewardship documents, and workforce governance practices shall conform to this Charter unless superseded by the Constitution.
