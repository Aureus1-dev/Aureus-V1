OAS-HR-104 — Human Stewardship Canon Maintenance and Version Control Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-HR-104
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-HR-001 — Human Stewardship and Workforce Governance Charter
Owner: Chief Human Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for proposing, reviewing, approving, versioning, publishing, interpreting, archiving, maintaining, and continuously improving the Aureus Human Stewardship Canon.

Its purpose is to preserve constitutional integrity, maintain institutional knowledge, ensure disciplined evolution of workforce governance, and provide a transparent stewardship process for future generations.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Human Stewardship Charters
- Human Stewardship Frameworks
- Human Stewardship SOPs
- Workforce governance standards
- Recruitment procedures
- Leadership development documentation
- Compensation standards
- Volunteer stewardship policies
- Workforce governance records

---

Objectives

Human Stewardship Canon governance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain historical continuity.
- Ensure version traceability.
- Support disciplined institutional evolution.
- Preserve workforce knowledge.
- Strengthen future stewardship.

---

Guiding Principles

The Human Stewardship Canon shall evolve deliberately rather than reactively.

Every approved revision should strengthen workforce governance, steward development, leadership continuity, institutional culture, organizational resilience, or constitutional fidelity while preserving the complete historical record.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Chief Human Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining the Human Stewardship Canon.
- Coordinating canonical reviews.
- Approving routine revisions within delegated authority.
- Preserving version history.
- Publishing approved revisions.

---

Human Stewardship Governance Council

Responsible for:

- Reviewing significant amendments.
- Evaluating constitutional alignment.
- Recommending improvements.
- Interpreting workforce governance.
- Supporting long-term stewardship.

---

AI Human Stewardship Knowledge Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring document consistency.
- Detecting conflicts.
- Tracking revision history.
- Maintaining cross-references.
- Supporting workforce research.
- Identifying improvement opportunities.

Human stewards retain final authority over all canonical Human Stewardship revisions and interpretations.

---

Prerequisites

Before modifying any canonical Human Stewardship document:

- Proposed revisions are documented.
- Supporting rationale is prepared.
- Related documents are identified.
- Workforce implications are evaluated.
- Required reviewers are assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Change Proposal

Document:

- Proposed revision.
- Purpose.
- Stewardship justification.
- Expected benefits.
- Related documents.
- Anticipated institutional impacts.

---

Step 2 — Canonical Review

Evaluate:

- Constitutional alignment.
- Workforce consistency.
- Governance implications.
- Operational impacts.
- Long-term institutional value.

---

Step 3 — Approval

The appropriate governance authority may:

- Approve.
- Approve with revisions.
- Return for additional review.
- Reject.

All approval decisions shall be documented.

---

Step 4 — Version Management

Every approved revision shall include:

- Version number.
- Revision date.
- Change summary.
- Approval record.
- Updated cross-references where applicable.

Superseded versions shall remain permanently archived.

---

Step 5 — Publication

Publish approved revisions within the official Aureus repository.

Update indexes, document references, and governance records where appropriate.

---

Step 6 — Archival

Archive prior versions while preserving:

- Original content.
- Approval history.
- Revision history.
- Historical context.

Institutional workforce knowledge shall remain permanently available.

---

Step 7 — Periodic Review

Conduct scheduled reviews to identify:

- Obsolete guidance.
- Governance inconsistencies.
- Emerging workforce needs.
- Opportunities for simplification.
- Human Stewardship improvement initiatives.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Review completion.
- Version accuracy.
- Cross-reference integrity.
- Canon consistency.
- Amendment quality.
- Publication timeliness.
- Historical preservation.
- Steward satisfaction.

---

Exceptions

Emergency workforce governance revisions may follow expedited governance procedures when necessary to protect stewards, preserve operational continuity, or comply with urgent legal obligations.

Emergency revisions shall receive formal retrospective review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Amendment proposals.
- Review records.
- Approval records.
- Version histories.
- Archive records.
- Canon indexes.
- Interpretation guidance.
- Governance reports.

---

Continuous Improvement

Human Stewardship Canon governance shall improve through governance reviews, workforce assessments, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, documentation quality reviews, leadership evaluations, and institutional learning.
