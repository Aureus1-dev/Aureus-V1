OAS-HR-102 — Performance and Steward Development Operations Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-HR-102
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-HR-004 — Performance, Leadership, and Succession Framework
Owner: Chief Human Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for steward performance management, coaching, mentoring, performance reviews, learning plans, leadership development, recognition, performance improvement, and continuous steward development throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure every steward receives fair evaluation, meaningful feedback, ongoing learning opportunities, and structured support that strengthens both individual flourishing and institutional effectiveness.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Performance stewardship
- Coaching
- Mentoring
- Performance reviews
- Learning plans
- Leadership development
- Recognition
- Performance improvement
- Career development
- Succession preparation

---

Objectives

Performance stewardship operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote fairness.
- Strengthen stewardship.
- Encourage lifelong learning.
- Develop future leaders.
- Foster long-term flourishing.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Performance Steward

Responsible for:

- Coordinating performance reviews.
- Maintaining development records.
- Supporting learning plans.
- Monitoring development progress.
- Preparing governance reports.

---

Supervising Steward

Responsible for:

- Providing coaching.
- Conducting evaluations.
- Supporting development.
- Identifying leadership potential.
- Recommending recognition and improvement plans.

---

AI Performance Steward

Responsible for:

- Preparing performance summaries.
- Tracking learning progress.
- Monitoring development goals.
- Organizing coaching documentation.
- Producing workforce analytics.
- Supporting administrative workflows.

Human stewards remain responsible for evaluations, promotions, disciplinary decisions, succession recommendations, and governance actions.

---

Prerequisites

Before conducting performance stewardship:

- Role expectations are documented.
- Performance objectives are established.
- Development resources are available.
- Responsible supervisors are assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Performance Planning

Document:

- Steward responsibilities.
- Performance expectations.
- Development objectives.
- Mission contributions.
- Learning goals.
- Review schedule.

---

Step 2 — Coaching and Mentoring

Provide:

- Regular coaching.
- Mentorship opportunities.
- Constructive feedback.
- Skills development.
- Leadership preparation.
- Ongoing support.

---

Step 3 — Performance Review

Evaluate:

- Mission alignment.
- Stewardship behaviors.
- Role performance.
- Learning progress.
- Leadership potential.
- Collaborative contributions.

Reviews shall remain fair, respectful, and evidence-based.

---

Step 4 — Recognition

Recognize meaningful contributions through:

- Stewardship awards.
- Public appreciation where appropriate.
- Development opportunities.
- Leadership responsibilities.
- Mission acknowledgments.

Recognition shall reinforce Aureus' constitutional values.

---

Step 5 — Performance Improvement

When improvement is needed:

- Clarify expectations.
- Develop an improvement plan.
- Provide coaching.
- Monitor progress.
- Review outcomes.
- Document lessons learned.

Improvement efforts shall seek restoration before separation whenever practical.

---

Step 6 — Leadership Development

Identify and prepare future leaders through:

- Progressive responsibilities.
- Mentorship.
- Governance education.
- Leadership training.
- Succession planning.

---

Step 7 — Continuous Improvement

Improve performance stewardship through:

- Steward feedback.
- Leadership reviews.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Governance evaluations.
- Workforce assessments.
- Lessons learned.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Performance review completion.
- Learning plan completion.
- Leadership readiness.
- Steward engagement.
- Recognition participation.
- Improvement plan success.
- Workforce retention.
- Mission support.

---

Exceptions

Urgent operational circumstances may require modified review schedules while maintaining appropriate documentation and retrospective governance review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Performance plans.
- Review records.
- Learning plans.
- Coaching documentation.
- Recognition records.
- Improvement plans.
- Leadership development records.
- Governance reports.

---

Continuous Improvement

Performance stewardship operations shall improve through workforce assessments, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance evaluations, leadership reviews, and lessons learned.
