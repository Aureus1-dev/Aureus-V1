OAS-HR-101 — Recruitment and Onboarding Operations Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-HR-101
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-HR-003 — Recruitment, Onboarding, and Steward Development Framework
Owner: Chief Human Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for recruiting, evaluating, selecting, onboarding, orienting, and integrating new stewards throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure every steward enters Aureus through a fair, transparent, mission-aligned process that prepares them for successful service while preserving constitutional alignment, institutional culture, and long-term stewardship.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Recruitment
- Candidate sourcing
- Candidate evaluation
- Selection
- Hiring
- Volunteer recruitment
- Onboarding
- Orientation
- Probationary periods
- Initial steward integration

---

Objectives

Recruitment and onboarding operations shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote fairness.
- Strengthen stewardship.
- Support successful integration.
- Encourage continuous learning.
- Foster long-term flourishing.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Recruitment Steward

Responsible for:

- Coordinating recruitment.
- Managing candidate communications.
- Scheduling interviews.
- Maintaining recruitment records.
- Supporting onboarding.

---

Hiring Steward

Responsible for:

- Evaluating candidates.
- Conducting interviews.
- Making selection recommendations.
- Supporting steward integration.
- Providing initial coaching.

---

AI Recruitment Steward

Responsible for:

- Organizing applications.
- Scheduling interviews.
- Preparing candidate summaries.
- Tracking onboarding progress.
- Supporting administrative workflows.
- Preparing recruitment reports.

Human stewards remain responsible for candidate evaluation, hiring decisions, volunteer acceptance, and governance actions.

---

Prerequisites

Before recruitment begins:

- Position requirements are documented.
- Selection criteria are established.
- Required approvals are obtained.
- Recruitment responsibilities are assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Recruitment Planning

Document:

- Position purpose.
- Required qualifications.
- Stewardship expectations.
- Reporting relationships.
- Evaluation criteria.
- Recruitment timeline.

---

Step 2 — Candidate Evaluation

Evaluate candidates based on:

- Mission alignment.
- Character.
- Qualifications.
- Relevant experience.
- Stewardship potential.
- Organizational fit.

Maintain fair and consistent evaluation practices.

---

Step 3 — Selection

Select candidates through documented decision-making.

Record:

- Selection rationale.
- Required approvals.
- Offer details.
- Acceptance status.

---

Step 4 — Onboarding

Provide structured onboarding including:

- Constitutional orientation.
- Mission education.
- Governance overview.
- Role expectations.
- Required training.
- Technology access.

---

Step 5 — Initial Integration

Support new stewards through:

- Mentorship.
- Regular check-ins.
- Learning opportunities.
- Team introductions.
- Initial performance guidance.

---

Step 6 — Probationary Review

Evaluate:

- Mission alignment.
- Role understanding.
- Learning progress.
- Performance.
- Stewardship behaviors.

Provide developmental feedback.

---

Step 7 — Continuous Improvement

Improve recruitment and onboarding through:

- Steward feedback.
- Recruitment metrics.
- Governance reviews.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Lessons learned.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Recruitment effectiveness.
- Time to onboarding.
- Onboarding completion.
- Early steward retention.
- Candidate experience.
- Volunteer integration.
- Governance compliance.
- Mission support.

---

Exceptions

Urgent workforce needs may require expedited recruitment while maintaining appropriate documentation and retrospective governance review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Recruitment plans.
- Candidate records.
- Interview documentation.
- Selection decisions.
- Onboarding records.
- Orientation completion.
- Probationary reviews.
- Governance reports.

---

Continuous Improvement

Recruitment and onboarding operations shall improve through workforce assessments, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance evaluations, operational reviews, and lessons learned.
