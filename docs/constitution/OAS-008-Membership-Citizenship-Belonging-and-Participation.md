# OAS-008 — Membership, Citizenship, Belonging, and Participation in the Open Advancement System

## 1. Document Title

**Membership, Citizenship, Belonging, and Participation in the Open Advancement System**
Canonical Designation: OAS-008
Status: Draft for Founder Review
Relationship to Canon: A constitutional document canonized under, subordinate to, and conformant with the Founding Charter (OAS-001), and consistent with OAS-002 through OAS-007. Governed by the default rules of OAS-005, Article VII, except where this document expressly provides otherwise.

## 2. Purpose

This document supplies the definition of membership that OAS-006, Article 15 and OAS-007, Article 12 each anticipated but deliberately left undefined, and that OAS-001, OAS-002, and OAS-003 flagged as an open question in their own Drafting Notes. It establishes what membership means, how it is voluntarily entered and ended, what the System and its Members owe one another, and how membership relates to the other ways a person may engage with the System — as a Guest, Participant, Contributor, or, for organizations, a participant of a different kind. It does not amend OAS-006 or OAS-007; it completes them, and states plainly where it does so.

## 3. Draft Constitutional Text

---

### PART ONE — THE MEANING OF MEMBERSHIP, CITIZENSHIP, BELONGING, AND PARTICIPATION

#### Article 1. Membership Defined

**Section 1.** Membership is the formal, voluntary, and mutually recognized relationship between a natural person and the System, entered under Part Three of this document, that carries the rights of OAS-006, Article 15 and the duties of OAS-007, Article 12 in their full operative form.

**Section 2.** Membership is the fullest expression, available to an individual person, of the Collective established in OAS-001, Article II, Section 4. It is not the only relationship a person may have with the System; Part Two of this document describes the others.

#### Article 2. Citizenship, Belonging, and Participation as Dimensions of Membership

**Section 1.** "Citizenship," as used in the title of this document, names the civic character of membership: the fact that a Member stands in a relationship of mutual rights and duties with a self-governing constitutional order, not merely as a recipient of its services. "Belonging" names the relational dimension of that relationship, closely tied to the Human Development component of the same name (OAS-001, Article II, Section 2). "Participation" names its active dimension: what a Member does in the life of the Collective.

**Section 2.** None of these three terms establishes a status distinct from Membership as defined in Article 1. They describe qualities of the single relationship this document establishes and are used throughout this document accordingly.

#### Article 3. Membership and Stewardship Distinguished

**Section 1.** A Steward, as defined in OAS-005, Article I, Section 22, is any person who holds authority under this Constitution — a role defined by the exercise of authority, not by membership. A person may be a Steward without being a Member, and a Member without being a Steward.

**Section 2.** Where a person holds both statuses, they hold the rights and owe the duties of each concurrently, as OAS-006 and OAS-007 separately establish. Neither status subsumes the other.

#### Article 4. The Two Populations of the Mission

**Section 1.** The Mission's declaration that the System exists to help every person flourish (OAS-001, Article II, Section 1) addresses a wider population than membership does. Every person the System serves, employs, governs, or otherwise affects holds, at minimum, the status and universal protections of a Guest (Article 5) by virtue of that fact alone, without seeking or holding membership.

**Section 2.** Membership is a narrower, voluntary relationship held by a subset of that wider population — those the Collective principle (OAS-001, Article II, Section 4) describes as being "within" the System. This Article clarifies, without amending, the relationship between the Mission's universal language and the Collective's narrower reference to Members. It is offered as an interpretive clarification consistent with existing text, not as new constitutional law (see Drafting Notes, N1).

---

### PART TWO — TIERS OF RELATIONSHIP TO THE SYSTEM

#### Article 5. Guests

**Section 1.** A Guest is any person the System serves, employs, governs, or otherwise affects who has not entered into any more particular relationship described in this Part. Guest status requires no action, application, or acceptance; it exists by virtue of being touched by the System's acts.

**Section 2.** A Guest holds the full universal rights of OAS-006, Parts One through Three, and owes the full universal duties of OAS-007, Parts One and Two, and no more. Guest status carries no expectation of continued relationship and confers none of the additional rights or duties reserved to Members.

#### Article 6. Participants

**Section 1.** A Participant is a person who engages with the System's activities or community on an ongoing basis without having sought or received membership. Participation may be extensive, repeated, and long-standing without ever becoming membership.

**Section 2.** A Participant holds the same rights and owes the same duties as a Guest. No degree or duration of participation, by itself, confers membership or any right to demand it; membership is entered only as Part Three of this document provides.

#### Article 7. Contributors

**Section 1.** A Contributor is a person or organization who contributes work, knowledge, resources, or effort toward the Mission, whether or not they hold membership. Contribution is valuable in itself and is not diminished by a contributor's choice not to seek, or the System's choice not to extend, membership.

**Section 2.** A Member necessarily contributes, in fulfillment of the duty of Article 14, but a Contributor is not thereby a Member. As with Participants, no degree of contribution, by itself, confers membership.

#### Article 8. Organizations

**Section 1.** An organization relates to the System as a Guest, Participant, or Contributor to the same extent a natural person would, holding the rights and owing the duties OAS-006, Article 17 and OAS-007, Article 18 already establish for organizations in those capacities.

**Section 2.** Membership, as defined in Article 1, is reserved to natural persons, for the same reason OAS-006, Article 17 withholds Part One's person-specific rights from organizations. This Article does not foreclose a future constitutional document establishing a distinct, analogous status for organizations; it states only that "Membership" as this document defines it is not that status.

#### Article 9. No Relationship Ripens into Membership Without Consent

**Section 1.** No degree, duration, or intensity of engagement as a Guest, Participant, or Contributor, however extensive, converts into membership without the voluntary, mutual process Part Three of this document requires.

**Section 2.** This Article protects both the person, who cannot be bound to the duties of membership without having agreed to them, and the System, which cannot be understood to have extended membership by implication from ordinary service or engagement.

---

### PART THREE — ENTRY INTO MEMBERSHIP

#### Article 10. Voluntariness

**Section 1.** Membership may never be imposed, presumed, or automatically conferred. It is entered only by the voluntary choice of the person concerned, consistent with Human Agency (OAS-001, Article III, Section 2; OAS-006, Article 2).

**Section 2.** This Article states a constitutional floor that no subordinate governance document may lower. A governance document may add further conditions to entry consistent with Article 11, but none may dispense with the requirement of voluntary, informed consent.

#### Article 11. Eligibility

**Section 1.** Any person capable of undertaking the duties of membership, and willing to do so, is eligible to seek it, subject only to such further criteria as a subordinate governance document may establish.

**Section 2.** Any eligibility criteria a subordinate governance document establishes must themselves satisfy Justice (OAS-004, Article 3) and Human Dignity (OAS-001, Article III, Section 1): they must be applied consistently, must not be arbitrary, and must never reduce a person to a means of satisfying an institutional quota or convenience.

#### Article 12. Process of Entry

**Section 1.** Membership is entered through a process, established by subordinate governance documents, that requires the person's voluntary request or acceptance, mutual acknowledgment of the rights and duties this relationship carries, and formal recognition by the System.

**Section 2.** This Article establishes the constitutional minimum such a process must contain. It does not itself establish the process's form, timing, or administration, which remain reserved to subordinate documents as operational matters.

---

### PART FOUR — THE MEMBERSHIP RELATIONSHIP

#### Article 13. Rights of Members

**Section 1.** A Member holds, in full operative effect, the rights OAS-006, Article 15 states conditionally: the benefit of the Collective principle — that a Member's flourishing and the System's capacity to serve others are never treated as competing — and a Member's share, as both contributor and beneficiary, in the System's Institutional Learning.

**Section 2.** This Article activates OAS-006, Article 15 by supplying the definition of membership that Article previously lacked. It does not amend OAS-006, Article 15; it completes it (see Article 22).

#### Article 14. Duties of Members

**Section 1.** A Member owes, in full operative effect, the duty OAS-007, Article 12 states conditionally: to participate honestly and constructively in the System's collective learning, sharing what a Member learns rather than withholding it from the community that strengthens them.

**Section 2.** This Article activates OAS-007, Article 12 in the manner Article 13 activates OAS-006, Article 15. As OAS-007, Article 12, Section 2 already provides, this duty requires no particular quantity or quality of contribution, only that a Member not deliberately work against the Collective's promise of mutual strengthening.

#### Article 15. What the System Owes Members Beyond the Universal Floor

**Section 1.** Beyond the universal obligations OAS-006 and OAS-007 already establish, the System owes every Member an honest account of the state of the Collective they belong to, and a meaningful opportunity to participate in the System's Institutional Learning, to the extent subordinate governance documents provide the means.

**Section 2.** This Article is established here for the first time. It exists because membership, as a heightened relationship carrying heightened duties (Article 14), should carry a correspondingly heightened obligation from the System in return; a Member asked to contribute honestly to the Collective is owed honest information about it.

---

### PART FIVE — ENDING, SUSPENDING, AND RESTORING MEMBERSHIP

#### Article 16. Voluntary Resignation

**Section 1.** Every Member holds the right to resign their membership at any time, for any reason or none, consistent with Human Agency. This right may not be denied, conditioned on approval, or made contingent on the System's convenience.

**Section 2.** A resigning Member may be asked to participate in an orderly transition of any responsibilities they hold, but such participation is not a precondition of the resignation's effect; resignation is effective upon the Member's clear notice of intent to resign.

#### Article 17. Suspension

**Section 1.** The System may suspend a Member's status temporarily, for cause related to a genuine breach of the duties of Article 14 or the broader duties of OAS-007, subject to the full procedural protections of OAS-006, Part Two.

**Section 2.** Suspension affects only the additional rights and duties of membership; it does not diminish the universal rights a suspended person retains as a Guest under Article 5, which remain unaffected regardless of membership status.

#### Article 18. Removal

**Section 1.** The System may remove a Member's status permanently only for cause, only after the full procedural protections of OAS-006, Part Two, and only in a manner proportionate to the breach found.

**Section 2.** As with suspension, removal affects only the additional rights and duties of membership. A removed person retains every universal right of OAS-006 as a Guest, and this Article does not diminish them.

#### Article 19. Procedural Protection Against Arbitrary Action

**Section 1.** A decision to suspend or remove a Member's status is a decision materially affecting that person within the meaning of OAS-006, and triggers, without qualification, the Right to Notice, the Right to Explanation, the Right to Human Review, and the Right of Petition and Redress (OAS-006, Articles 6 through 9).

**Section 2.** This Article states expressly what would otherwise require inference from OAS-006's general terms, in recognition of how consequential membership status is to the person who holds it.

#### Article 20. Restoration and Reconciliation

**Section 1.** A Member who has resigned may seek to re-enter membership at any later time through the process Article 12 describes; resignation carries no presumption against future re-entry.

**Section 2.** A Member who has been removed may seek restoration through a process established by subordinate governance documents, which may reasonably condition restoration on the circumstances of the removal, but may not categorically foreclose the possibility of reconciliation as a matter of constitutional design. The System's commitment to Institutional Learning and to the Lifelong Journey of flourishing (OAS-001, Article II, Section 3) counsels against treating any person's relationship to the Collective as permanently and irrevocably closed by this Constitution itself.

---

### PART SIX — BELONGING AND THE COLLECTIVE

#### Article 21. Mutual Strengthening in Practice

**Section 1.** The health of the Collective (OAS-001, Article II, Section 4) is measured not only by how it treats its current Members, but by how it treats those entering, departing, suspended, or seeking to return. A community that strengthens its Members while discarding those who leave has not fulfilled the Collective's promise; it has merely relocated where the promise fails.

**Section 2.** This Article creates no new enforceable right beyond those already established in this document. It states the standard by which the System's practice under this document as a whole should be judged.

---

### ARTICLE 22 — ACTIVATION OF CONDITIONAL PROVISIONS

**Section 1.** OAS-006, Article 15 and OAS-007, Article 12 were each drafted conditionally, pending a definition of membership. This document supplies that definition. From the canonization of this document forward, OAS-006, Article 15 and OAS-007, Article 12 apply in their full operative form to every person holding membership as Article 1 of this document defines it.

**Section 2.** This Article does not amend the text of OAS-006 or OAS-007; it completes provisions those documents already anticipated needing completion. The matter those documents left open is resolved here expressly, not silently.

---

### ARTICLE 23 — STATUS AND EFFECT

**Section 1. Operative Effect.** Pursuant to OAS-005, Article VII, Section 3, this document expressly overrides the default presumption of non-operative effect: it establishes the binding definitions, entry and exit procedures, and reciprocal obligations of membership stated in Parts One through Six.

**Section 2. Entrenchment Status.** No provision of this document is entrenched. Only the Founding Charter can entrench its own text or the text of another document (OAS-001, Article VII, Section 3).

**Section 3. Relationship to OAS-006 and OAS-007.** This document completes, rather than amends, OAS-006, Article 15 and OAS-007, Article 12, as Article 22 provides. In every other case of apparent tension with OAS-006 or OAS-007, those documents govern to the extent Article 21 of OAS-007 and Article 19 of OAS-006 already establish rights as prevailing over duties.

---

## 4. Drafting Notes — Outside the Constitutional Text

*The following is provided for Founder review only. It is not part of the Articles in Section 3, and must not be entered into the constitutional record if and when this document is canonized.*

**N1. Article 4 is a clarification, not new law.** The distinction between the Mission's universal "every person" and the Collective's narrower "those within it" was implicit across OAS-001, OAS-002, and OAS-003, each of which flagged the tension without resolving it (OAS-001 N2, OAS-002 N2/N3, OAS-003 N2). Article 4 makes that distinction explicit by defining Guest status as the universal floor and Membership as the voluntary subset. This is offered as an interpretive clarification consistent with existing text, not as an amendment to any prior document. The Founder should confirm whether this resolves the previously flagged notes or whether they should remain open.

**N2. Recommended CAP: cross-reference the Mission text to this document.** OAS-001, Article II, Section 1 states the Mission without reference to the Guest/Member distinction Article 4 of this document establishes. A future Constitutional Amendment Proposal could amend OAS-001, Article II to add a forward cross-reference to this document for readers encountering the Mission first. This is not constitutionally necessary, since OAS-005's interpretive rules already make cross-document reading authoritative, but would improve accessibility. This document does not make that amendment; it is offered here only as a recommendation for a future CAP.

**N3. "Organization" remains undefined as a full foundational category.** This is the third document to note the gap (OAS-006, N2; OAS-007, N2; here, Article 8). Given how many provisions now depend on it, a dedicated future document defining organizational participation is recommended as a priority.

**N4. Person-to-person and Member-to-Member dispute resolution remains absent.** OAS-007's Note N5 flagged the absence of a mechanism for disputes between persons generally. This document does not supply one for disputes between Members specifically. Article 19 of this document governs only System-versus-Member decisions — suspension and removal — not disputes between Members themselves. This gap should be addressed by the same future governance document that would resolve OAS-007's Note N5.

**N5. Recommended CAP: entrenchment of the voluntariness of membership.** Article 10 is tightly bound to Human Agency, already entrenched under OAS-001, Article III, Section 2. The Founder may wish to consider a future CAP amending OAS-001, Article VII, Section 3 to extend entrenched protection to the voluntariness of membership specifically, given how central it is to preventing coercive institutional growth. This document does not take that action; it can only recommend it, since only the Founding Charter can entrench its own text.

**N6. Eligibility criteria are deliberately left open.** Article 11 establishes only the constitutional floor — non-arbitrary, Justice-consistent — that any eligibility criteria must satisfy. It does not establish who is or is not eligible beyond that floor. Any future eligibility policy adopted by a subordinate governance document should be understood as reviewable against Article 11, Section 2.
