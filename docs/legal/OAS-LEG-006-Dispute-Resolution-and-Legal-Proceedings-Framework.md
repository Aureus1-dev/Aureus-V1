OAS-LEG-006 — Dispute Resolution and Legal Proceedings Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-LEG-006
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-LEG-002 — Legal Governance and Compliance Framework
Owner: Chief Legal Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the canonical model for preventing, managing, resolving, documenting, and learning from disputes and legal proceedings involving Aureus.

Its purpose is to promote fair resolution, protect members, preserve institutional integrity, reduce unnecessary legal conflict, and ensure disputes are handled consistently and responsibly.

---

Mission

Dispute resolution exists to resolve disagreements in a manner that is just, respectful, proportionate, constitutionally aligned, and supportive of the long-term mission of Aureus.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Internal disputes
- Member disputes
- Employment-related disputes
- Vendor disputes
- Partnership disputes
- Mediation
- Arbitration
- Litigation management
- Settlement authority
- Evidence preservation

---

Guiding Principles

Dispute resolution shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote fairness.
- Encourage good-faith resolution.
- Minimize unnecessary conflict.
- Protect institutional interests.
- Preserve long-term relationships where appropriate.

Resolution should seek restoration whenever reasonably possible.

---

Early Resolution

Potential disputes should be identified and addressed as early as practical through communication, clarification, negotiation, or other appropriate methods before escalating to formal proceedings.

---

Resolution Methods

Depending upon the circumstances, Aureus may utilize:

- Informal resolution.
- Facilitated discussion.
- Mediation.
- Arbitration.
- Administrative proceedings.
- Litigation.
- Other lawful dispute resolution methods.

The chosen method should be proportionate to the nature of the dispute.

---

Legal Proceedings

When legal proceedings become necessary:

- Appropriate authority shall approve participation.
- Relevant records shall be preserved.
- Legal representation shall be coordinated.
- Communications shall be managed appropriately.
- Significant developments shall be documented.

---

Evidence Preservation

Relevant evidence shall be:

- Identified.
- Protected.
- Preserved.
- Documented.
- Retained according to legal requirements.

Evidence integrity shall be maintained throughout the proceeding.

---

Settlement Authority

Settlement authority shall be exercised only by appropriately authorized stewards acting within delegated authority and applicable governance requirements.

---

AI-Assisted Case Management

AI may assist through:

- Case organization.
- Timeline preparation.
- Document indexing.
- Evidence management support.
- Legal research support.
- Reporting assistance.

Human stewards remain responsible for legal strategy, negotiations, settlements, litigation decisions, and legal representation.

---

Performance Measurement

Dispute management shall evaluate:

- Resolution timeliness.
- Resolution effectiveness.
- Relationship preservation.
- Legal cost management.
- Governance compliance.
- Documentation quality.
- Lessons implemented.

Measurements shall strengthen institutional stewardship.

---

Continuous Improvement

Dispute resolution practices shall improve through:

- Case reviews.
- Steward feedback.
- Governance assessments.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Legal developments.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens institutional resilience.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all dispute resolution and legal proceedings throughout Aureus.

All Legal SOPs, mediation procedures, arbitration guidance, litigation management standards, settlement procedures, and legal case management documents shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Legal Charter.
