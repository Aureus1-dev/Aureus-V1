OAS-LEG-105 — Dispute Resolution and Legal Case Management Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-LEG-105
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-LEG-006 — Dispute Resolution and Legal Proceedings Framework
Owner: Chief Legal Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for preventing, managing, documenting, resolving, and learning from disputes and legal cases involving Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure disputes are managed fairly, consistently, lawfully, constitutionally, and in a manner that protects members, preserves institutional integrity, and supports long-term mission success.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Internal disputes
- Member disputes
- Vendor disputes
- Partnership disputes
- Mediation
- Arbitration
- Litigation support
- Settlement administration
- Evidence preservation
- Legal case closure

---

Objectives

Dispute management shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Promote fairness.
- Encourage early resolution.
- Reduce unnecessary legal conflict.
- Preserve institutional relationships where appropriate.
- Strengthen institutional learning.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Legal Case Steward

Responsible for:

- Coordinating case administration.
- Maintaining case records.
- Monitoring deadlines.
- Coordinating communications.
- Preparing governance updates.

---

Legal Review Steward

Responsible for:

- Evaluating legal strategy.
- Supporting negotiations.
- Reviewing settlement proposals.
- Coordinating legal representation where appropriate.

---

AI Legal Case Steward

Responsible for:

- Organizing case documentation.
- Tracking deadlines.
- Preparing timelines.
- Monitoring procedural milestones.
- Summarizing case activity.
- Supporting document retrieval.

Human stewards remain responsible for legal strategy, negotiations, settlements, litigation decisions, and representation.

---

Prerequisites

Before opening a legal case:

- The dispute is documented.
- Relevant stakeholders are identified.
- Supporting evidence is preserved.
- Applicable agreements and policies are reviewed.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Case Intake

Document:

- Nature of the dispute.
- Parties involved.
- Relevant dates.
- Applicable agreements.
- Initial legal issues.
- Assigned case steward.

---

Step 2 — Preliminary Assessment

Evaluate:

- Legal issues.
- Operational impact.
- Financial implications.
- Constitutional considerations.
- Relationship impacts.
- Resolution options.

---

Step 3 — Early Resolution

Where appropriate, attempt:

- Direct discussion.
- Negotiation.
- Facilitated resolution.
- Mediation.

Document all meaningful resolution efforts.

---

Step 4 — Formal Proceedings

If informal resolution is unsuccessful:

- Coordinate arbitration or litigation where appropriate.
- Preserve evidence.
- Maintain confidentiality.
- Track procedural deadlines.
- Document significant developments.

---

Step 5 — Settlement Review

Settlement proposals shall:

- Be evaluated for legal and operational impact.
- Receive required approvals.
- Be documented completely.
- Preserve institutional interests.

---

Step 6 — Case Closure

Upon resolution:

- Confirm obligations are completed.
- Archive case documentation.
- Record final disposition.
- Notify appropriate stakeholders.

---

Step 7 — Post-Case Review

Evaluate:

- Root causes.
- Lessons learned.
- Policy implications.
- Governance improvements.
- Preventive actions.

Institutional learning shall follow every significant dispute.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Resolution timeliness.
- Settlement effectiveness.
- Litigation costs.
- Documentation quality.
- Relationship preservation.
- Governance compliance.
- Lessons implemented.
- Repeat dispute reduction.

---

Exceptions

Emergency legal matters may require expedited procedures while preserving complete documentation and subsequent governance review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Case files.
- Evidence inventories.
- Correspondence.
- Settlement agreements.
- Court or arbitration records.
- Governance reports.
- Post-case reviews.
- Archive records.

---

Continuous Improvement

Dispute management shall improve through governance reviews, legal developments, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, case evaluations, and lessons learned.
