OAS-LEG-107 — Legal Canon Maintenance and Version Control Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-LEG-107
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-LEG-001 — Legal Charter
Owner: Chief Legal Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for proposing, reviewing, approving, versioning, publishing, interpreting, archiving, maintaining, and continuously improving the Aureus Legal Canon.

Its purpose is to preserve constitutional integrity, maintain institutional legal memory, ensure disciplined legal evolution, and provide a transparent governance process for future legal stewardship.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Legal Charters
- Legal Frameworks
- Legal SOPs
- Legal policies
- Contract standards
- Intellectual property governance
- Compliance procedures
- Legal governance documentation
- Canonical legal interpretations

---

Objectives

Legal Canon governance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain historical continuity.
- Ensure version traceability.
- Support disciplined institutional evolution.
- Preserve legal knowledge.
- Strengthen future stewardship.

---

Guiding Principles

The Legal Canon shall evolve deliberately rather than reactively.

Every approved revision should strengthen constitutional governance, legal clarity, institutional resilience, stewardship quality, or operational effectiveness while preserving the complete historical record.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Chief Legal Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining the Legal Canon.
- Coordinating canonical reviews.
- Approving routine revisions within delegated authority.
- Preserving version history.
- Publishing approved updates.

---

Legal Governance Council

Responsible for:

- Reviewing significant amendments.
- Evaluating constitutional alignment.
- Recommending revisions.
- Interpreting legal governance.
- Supporting long-term legal stewardship.

---

AI Legal Knowledge Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring cross-reference consistency.
- Detecting conflicts.
- Tracking revision history.
- Supporting legal research.
- Maintaining document relationships.
- Identifying improvement opportunities.

Human stewards retain final authority over all canonical legal revisions and interpretations.

---

Prerequisites

Before modifying any canonical legal document:

- Proposed revisions are documented.
- Supporting rationale is prepared.
- Related documents are identified.
- Constitutional implications are evaluated.
- Required reviewers are assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Change Proposal

Document:

- Proposed revision.
- Purpose.
- Legal and constitutional justification.
- Expected benefits.
- Related documents.
- Anticipated institutional impacts.

---

Step 2 — Canonical Review

Evaluate:

- Constitutional alignment.
- Legal consistency.
- Governance implications.
- Operational impact.
- Long-term institutional value.

---

Step 3 — Approval

The appropriate governance authority may:

- Approve.
- Approve with revisions.
- Return for additional review.
- Reject.

All approval decisions shall be documented.

---

Step 4 — Version Management

Every approved revision shall include:

- Version number.
- Revision date.
- Change summary.
- Approval record.
- Cross-reference updates where applicable.

Superseded versions shall remain permanently archived.

---

Step 5 — Publication

Publish approved revisions within the official Aureus repository.

Update indexes, document references, and governance records as required.

---

Step 6 — Archival

Archive prior versions while preserving:

- Original content.
- Approval history.
- Revision history.
- Historical context.

Institutional legal memory shall remain permanently available.

---

Step 7 — Periodic Review

Conduct scheduled reviews to identify:

- Obsolete guidance.
- Governance inconsistencies.
- Missing legal controls.
- Emerging legal needs.
- Opportunities for simplification.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Review completion.
- Version accuracy.
- Cross-reference integrity.
- Canon consistency.
- Amendment quality.
- Publication timeliness.
- Historical preservation.
- Steward satisfaction.

---

Exceptions

Emergency legal revisions may follow expedited governance procedures when necessary to protect members, preserve constitutional integrity, or respond to urgent legal requirements.

Emergency revisions shall receive formal retrospective review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Amendment proposals.
- Review records.
- Approval records.
- Version histories.
- Archive records.
- Canon indexes.
- Interpretation guidance.
- Governance reports.

---

Continuous Improvement

Legal Canon governance shall improve through governance reviews, legal developments, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, documentation quality assessments, and institutional learning.
