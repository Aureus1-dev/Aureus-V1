OAS-LEG-104 — Legal Risk Assessment and Regulatory Change Management Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-LEG-104
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-LEG-005 — Regulatory and Legal Risk Management Framework
Owner: Chief Legal Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for identifying, evaluating, monitoring, mitigating, and reporting legal risks while managing changes to applicable laws and regulations affecting Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure Aureus proactively responds to legal developments, maintains constitutional alignment, protects members, reduces legal exposure, and preserves institutional resilience.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Legal risk assessments
- Regulatory change monitoring
- Compliance impact analysis
- Policy updates
- Corrective actions
- Legal reporting
- Governance notifications
- AI-assisted legal monitoring
- Legal risk registers
- Compliance implementation

---

Objectives

Legal risk management shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain regulatory compliance.
- Reduce legal exposure.
- Promote accountability.
- Strengthen institutional resilience.
- Support continuous improvement.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Legal Risk Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining the legal risk register.
- Coordinating legal assessments.
- Monitoring regulatory developments.
- Tracking corrective actions.
- Preparing governance reports.

---

Department Stewards

Responsible for:

- Implementing required legal changes.
- Reporting emerging legal concerns.
- Maintaining supporting documentation.
- Cooperating during assessments.

---

AI Legal Risk Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring legal developments.
- Identifying emerging risks.
- Tracking implementation deadlines.
- Preparing impact summaries.
- Organizing supporting documentation.
- Supporting governance reporting.

Human stewards remain responsible for legal interpretation, policy approval, risk acceptance, and governance decisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before conducting a legal risk assessment:

- Applicable legal requirements are identified.
- Assessment scope is defined.
- Responsible stakeholders are identified.
- Supporting documentation is available.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Risk Identification

Identify legal risks arising from:

- Regulatory changes.
- Operational activities.
- Contracts.
- Governance decisions.
- Strategic initiatives.
- External legal developments.

---

Step 2 — Risk Assessment

Evaluate each identified risk according to:

- Likelihood.
- Potential impact.
- Legal consequences.
- Operational implications.
- Financial exposure.
- Reputational considerations.

Document assessment results.

---

Step 3 — Regulatory Change Review

Monitor and evaluate:

- New legislation.
- Regulatory amendments.
- Administrative guidance.
- Judicial decisions.
- Industry standards.

Determine whether institutional changes are required.

---

Step 4 — Implementation Planning

When action is required:

- Update policies.
- Revise procedures.
- Assign responsibilities.
- Establish implementation timelines.
- Communicate required changes.

---

Step 5 — Corrective Actions

Track:

- Assigned responsibilities.
- Completion status.
- Verification activities.
- Remaining risks.
- Governance approvals.

---

Step 6 — Governance Reporting

Prepare reports summarizing:

- Current legal risks.
- Regulatory developments.
- Compliance status.
- Outstanding corrective actions.
- Recommendations.

---

Step 7 — Periodic Review

Review the legal risk program regularly to:

- Confirm effectiveness.
- Identify emerging risks.
- Evaluate mitigation strategies.
- Improve governance.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Risk assessment completion.
- Regulatory response time.
- Corrective action completion.
- Policy update timeliness.
- Governance reporting quality.
- Compliance improvements.
- Legal incident reduction.
- Institutional preparedness.

---

Exceptions

Extraordinary legal developments may require expedited implementation while maintaining appropriate documentation and retrospective governance review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Legal risk register.
- Assessment reports.
- Regulatory monitoring records.
- Corrective action logs.
- Governance reports.
- Policy revision history.
- Supporting legal documentation.

---

Continuous Improvement

Legal risk management shall improve through governance reviews, legal developments, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, risk assessments, and lessons learned.
