OAS-LEG-101 — Regulatory Compliance Management Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-LEG-101
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-LEG-002 — Legal Governance and Compliance Framework
Owner: Chief Legal Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for identifying, monitoring, documenting, implementing, reviewing, and reporting legal and regulatory compliance obligations throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to ensure Aureus consistently fulfills applicable legal requirements while preserving constitutional integrity, protecting members, reducing legal risk, and supporting responsible institutional governance.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Regulatory compliance
- Legal obligations
- Compliance registers
- Regulatory monitoring
- Compliance reporting
- Compliance reviews
- Compliance documentation
- Corrective actions
- AI-assisted compliance monitoring
- Governance reporting

---

Objectives

Regulatory compliance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Respect applicable law.
- Reduce legal risk.
- Promote accountability.
- Strengthen institutional trust.
- Support continuous improvement.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Legal Compliance Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining the compliance register.
- Monitoring regulatory developments.
- Coordinating compliance reviews.
- Preparing compliance reports.
- Tracking corrective actions.

---

Department Stewards

Responsible for:

- Implementing applicable compliance requirements.
- Maintaining required documentation.
- Reporting compliance concerns.
- Supporting compliance reviews.

---

AI Compliance Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring regulatory updates.
- Tracking compliance deadlines.
- Identifying potential compliance gaps.
- Preparing compliance summaries.
- Supporting reporting.
- Organizing compliance documentation.

Human stewards remain responsible for legal interpretation, compliance decisions, and governance actions.

---

Prerequisites

Before beginning compliance activities:

- Applicable legal requirements are identified.
- Responsible owners are assigned.
- Compliance documentation standards are established.
- Reporting expectations are documented.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Identify Legal Requirements

Identify all applicable:

- Laws.
- Regulations.
- Licensing requirements.
- Reporting obligations.
- Contractual compliance obligations.
- Internal governance requirements.

---

Step 2 — Maintain Compliance Register

Document:

- Requirement.
- Responsible steward.
- Compliance status.
- Evidence location.
- Review frequency.
- Reporting obligations.

The compliance register shall remain current.

---

Step 3 — Monitor Regulatory Changes

Monitor for:

- New legislation.
- Regulatory amendments.
- Court decisions affecting operations.
- Administrative guidance.
- Industry developments.

Material changes shall be evaluated promptly.

---

Step 4 — Implement Compliance Measures

Implement:

- Required policies.
- Operational procedures.
- Staff guidance.
- Training where appropriate.
- Documentation controls.

---

Step 5 — Review Compliance

Conduct periodic reviews to verify:

- Compliance status.
- Documentation quality.
- Operational implementation.
- Outstanding deficiencies.
- Improvement opportunities.

---

Step 6 — Corrective Actions

For identified deficiencies:

- Document findings.
- Assign responsibility.
- Establish deadlines.
- Verify completion.
- Update compliance records.

---

Step 7 — Governance Reporting

Prepare reports summarizing:

- Compliance status.
- Significant legal developments.
- Outstanding issues.
- Corrective actions.
- Emerging legal risks.
- Recommendations.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Compliance completion.
- Regulatory response time.
- Corrective action completion.
- Documentation quality.
- Review completion.
- Governance reporting timeliness.
- Legal risk reduction.
- Institutional readiness.

---

Exceptions

Emergency legal developments may require expedited compliance actions while preserving appropriate documentation and subsequent governance review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Compliance registers.
- Regulatory updates.
- Review reports.
- Corrective action logs.
- Compliance evidence.
- Governance reports.
- Supporting legal documentation.

---

Continuous Improvement

Regulatory compliance practices shall improve through governance reviews, legal developments, steward feedback, AI-assisted analysis, compliance assessments, and lessons learned.
