OAS-LEG-104 — Legal Canon Maintenance and Version Control Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-LEG-104
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-LEG-001 — Legal and Regulatory Governance Charter
Owner: Chief Legal Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for proposing, reviewing, approving, versioning, publishing, interpreting, archiving, maintaining, and continuously improving the Aureus Legal Canon.

Its purpose is to preserve constitutional integrity, maintain institutional legal knowledge, ensure disciplined evolution of legal governance, and provide a transparent stewardship process for future generations.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Legal Charters
- Legal Frameworks
- Legal SOPs
- Regulatory compliance standards
- Contract governance documents
- Intellectual property documentation
- Legal governance records
- Legal archives
- Institutional legal guidance

---

Objectives

Legal Canon governance shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain historical continuity.
- Ensure version traceability.
- Support disciplined institutional evolution.
- Preserve legal knowledge.
- Strengthen future stewardship.

---

Guiding Principles

The Legal Canon shall evolve deliberately rather than reactively.

Every approved revision should strengthen legal governance, regulatory compliance, contractual integrity, intellectual property stewardship, institutional resilience, or constitutional fidelity while preserving the complete historical record.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Chief Legal Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining the Legal Canon.
- Coordinating canonical reviews.
- Approving routine revisions within delegated authority.
- Preserving version history.
- Publishing approved revisions.

---

Legal Governance Council

Responsible for:

- Reviewing significant amendments.
- Evaluating constitutional alignment.
- Recommending improvements.
- Interpreting legal governance.
- Supporting long-term legal stewardship.

---

AI Legal Knowledge Steward

Responsible for:

- Monitoring document consistency.
- Detecting conflicts.
- Tracking revision history.
- Maintaining cross-references.
- Supporting legal research.
- Identifying improvement opportunities.

Human stewards retain final authority over all canonical legal revisions and interpretations.

---

Prerequisites

Before modifying any canonical legal document:

- Proposed revisions are documented.
- Supporting rationale is prepared.
- Related documents are identified.
- Legal implications are evaluated.
- Required reviewers are assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Change Proposal

Document:

- Proposed revision.
- Purpose.
- Legal justification.
- Expected benefits.
- Related documents.
- Anticipated institutional impacts.

---

Step 2 — Canonical Review

Evaluate:

- Constitutional alignment.
- Legal consistency.
- Governance implications.
- Operational impacts.
- Long-term institutional value.

---

Step 3 — Approval

The appropriate governance authority may:

- Approve.
- Approve with revisions.
- Return for additional review.
- Reject.

All approval decisions shall be documented.

---

Step 4 — Version Management

Every approved revision shall include:

- Version number.
- Revision date.
- Change summary.
- Approval record.
- Updated cross-references where applicable.

Superseded versions shall remain permanently archived.

---

Step 5 — Publication

Publish approved revisions within the official Aureus repository.

Update indexes, document references, and governance records where appropriate.

---

Step 6 — Archival

Archive prior versions while preserving:

- Original content.
- Approval history.
- Revision history.
- Historical context.

Institutional legal memory shall remain permanently available.

---

Step 7 — Periodic Review

Conduct scheduled reviews to identify:

- Obsolete guidance.
- Governance inconsistencies.
- Emerging legal needs.
- Opportunities for simplification.
- Legal governance improvement initiatives.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Review completion.
- Version accuracy.
- Cross-reference integrity.
- Canon consistency.
- Amendment quality.
- Publication timeliness.
- Historical preservation.
- Steward satisfaction.

---

Exceptions

Emergency legal governance revisions may follow expedited governance procedures when necessary to comply with urgent legal obligations, preserve institutional rights, or protect members.

Emergency revisions shall receive formal retrospective review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Amendment proposals.
- Review records.
- Approval records.
- Version histories.
- Archive records.
- Canon indexes.
- Interpretation guidance.
- Governance reports.

---

Continuous Improvement

Legal Canon governance shall improve through governance reviews, legal assessments, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, documentation quality reviews, regulatory evaluations, and institutional learning.
