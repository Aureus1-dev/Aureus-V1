OAS-LEG-103 — Intellectual Property Registration and Licensing Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-LEG-103
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-LEG-004 — Intellectual Property and Licensing Framework
Owner: Chief Legal Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for identifying, documenting, registering, protecting, licensing, monitoring, enforcing, renewing, and archiving Aureus' intellectual property assets.

Its purpose is to preserve institutional knowledge, protect legal rights, encourage responsible innovation, maintain brand integrity, and ensure intellectual property is managed consistently throughout its lifecycle.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Copyrights
- Trademarks
- Patents
- Trade secrets
- Software
- Educational materials
- Research publications
- AI-generated works
- Branding assets
- Licensing agreements
- Intellectual property archives

---

Objectives

Intellectual property management shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Safeguard institutional assets.
- Encourage innovation.
- Promote ethical collaboration.
- Preserve institutional knowledge.
- Strengthen long-term sustainability.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Intellectual Property Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining the IP register.
- Coordinating registrations.
- Managing licensing activities.
- Monitoring renewals.
- Preserving IP documentation.

---

Legal Review Steward

Responsible for:

- Reviewing ownership.
- Confirming legal protection strategies.
- Supporting licensing negotiations.
- Advising on infringement response.

---

AI Intellectual Property Steward

Responsible for:

- Tracking intellectual property assets.
- Monitoring renewal deadlines.
- Organizing documentation.
- Detecting potential conflicts.
- Preparing licensing summaries.
- Supporting asset inventories.

Human stewards remain responsible for ownership determinations, registrations, licensing approvals, and enforcement actions.

---

Prerequisites

Before protecting or licensing intellectual property:

- Ownership is documented.
- The asset is identified and classified.
- Applicable legal protections are evaluated.
- Required approvals are obtained.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Asset Identification

Identify and document:

- Asset description.
- Creator or contributors.
- Date created.
- Ownership status.
- Business purpose.
- Applicable intellectual property category.

---

Step 2 — Registration Evaluation

Determine whether registration or other legal protection is appropriate based on:

- Strategic value.
- Legal requirements.
- Cost.
- Risk exposure.
- Mission impact.

---

Step 3 — Protection

Implement appropriate protections including:

- Copyright notices.
- Trademark filings.
- Patent applications where appropriate.
- Confidentiality measures.
- Access controls.
- Secure repositories.

---

Step 4 — Licensing

Before granting or receiving licenses:

- Confirm ownership or authority.
- Review proposed terms.
- Evaluate mission alignment.
- Obtain required approvals.
- Document the agreement.

---

Step 5 — Monitoring

Monitor:

- License compliance.
- Renewal deadlines.
- Unauthorized use.
- Brand consistency.
- Intellectual property portfolio status.

---

Step 6 — Enforcement

When potential infringement is identified:

- Document the issue.
- Preserve evidence.
- Assess legal options.
- Escalate to appropriate legal authority.
- Record the outcome.

---

Step 7 — Archival

Maintain permanent records of:

- Registrations.
- Licensing agreements.
- Ownership documentation.
- Renewals.
- Enforcement actions.
- Historical versions where appropriate.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Asset registration completion.
- Renewal compliance.
- Licensing effectiveness.
- Portfolio accuracy.
- Documentation completeness.
- Brand protection.
- Governance compliance.
- Intellectual property preservation.

---

Exceptions

Urgent intellectual property actions may require expedited review while maintaining complete documentation and subsequent governance approval.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Intellectual property register.
- Registration records.
- Ownership documentation.
- Licensing agreements.
- Renewal records.
- Enforcement files.
- Asset inventories.
- Governance approvals.

---

Continuous Improvement

Intellectual property management shall improve through governance reviews, legal developments, steward feedback, AI-assisted analysis, portfolio assessments, and lessons learned.
