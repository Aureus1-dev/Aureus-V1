OAS-LEG-106 — Legal Documentation and Records Management Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-LEG-106
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-LEG-007 — Legal Documentation and Records Framework
Owner: Chief Legal Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for creating, classifying, securing, maintaining, retaining, archiving, accessing, preserving, and disposing of Aureus' legal records and documentation.

Its purpose is to ensure legal records remain accurate, authentic, confidential, accessible to authorized stewards, legally defensible, and preserved as part of Aureus' institutional legal memory.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Legal correspondence
- Contracts
- Governance records
- Legal opinions
- Regulatory filings
- Litigation records
- Evidence files
- Legal holds
- Archive management
- Record disposal

---

Objectives

Legal records management shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain legal integrity.
- Protect confidential information.
- Support governance.
- Preserve institutional history.
- Ensure audit and litigation readiness.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Legal Records Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining legal records.
- Coordinating record classification.
- Managing archives.
- Supporting legal retrieval.
- Monitoring retention schedules.

---

Chief Legal Steward

Responsible for:

- Approving legal hold actions.
- Authorizing record disposal.
- Reviewing documentation standards.
- Protecting privileged information.

---

AI Legal Records Steward

Responsible for:

- Indexing legal records.
- Monitoring retention schedules.
- Supporting document retrieval.
- Detecting incomplete files.
- Managing cross-references.
- Preparing archive summaries.

Human stewards remain responsible for legal privilege determinations, legal holds, access approvals, and record destruction.

---

Prerequisites

Before creating or archiving legal records:

- Record classification standards are applied.
- Required metadata is completed.
- Confidentiality requirements are identified.
- Appropriate storage locations are assigned.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Record Creation

Create complete records for:

- Contracts.
- Legal advice.
- Regulatory filings.
- Governance actions.
- Litigation matters.
- Legal correspondence.

Every record shall be complete, dated, and attributable.

---

Step 2 — Classification

Assign classifications according to:

- Record type.
- Sensitivity.
- Confidentiality.
- Privilege status where applicable.
- Retention schedule.

---

Step 3 — Secure Storage

Store records using approved systems that provide:

- Security.
- Backup.
- Integrity protection.
- Authorized access controls.
- Disaster recovery capability.

---

Step 4 — Access Management

Provide access only to authorized individuals according to legal, operational, and governance requirements.

Maintain access logs where appropriate.

---

Step 5 — Legal Holds

When required:

- Suspend scheduled destruction.
- Preserve relevant records.
- Notify responsible stewards.
- Track hold status.
- Release holds only through authorized approval.

---

Step 6 — Archival

Archive inactive records while preserving:

- Authenticity.
- Integrity.
- Accessibility.
- Historical context.

Archived records shall remain searchable and retrievable.

---

Step 7 — Disposal

Dispose of legal records only after:

- Retention requirements are satisfied.
- Legal holds are released.
- Required approvals are obtained.
- Secure destruction procedures are completed.

Document every disposal action.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Record completeness.
- Retrieval efficiency.
- Retention compliance.
- Archive integrity.
- Access control effectiveness.
- Legal hold compliance.
- Documentation quality.
- Governance readiness.

---

Exceptions

Legal proceedings, investigations, audits, or governance directives may require records to remain preserved beyond standard retention schedules.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Legal record inventories.
- Classification registers.
- Retention schedules.
- Legal hold logs.
- Archive indexes.
- Access logs.
- Disposal records.
- Governance approvals.

---

Continuous Improvement

Legal records management shall improve through governance reviews, legal developments, steward feedback, AI-assisted analysis, documentation assessments, and lessons learned.
