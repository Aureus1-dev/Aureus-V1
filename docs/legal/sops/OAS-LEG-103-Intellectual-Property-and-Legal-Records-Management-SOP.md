OAS-LEG-103 — Intellectual Property and Legal Records Management Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Canonical Designation: OAS-LEG-103
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-LEG-004 — Intellectual Property Framework
Owner: Chief Legal Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026
Review Cycle: Annual

---

Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the canonical process for identifying, documenting, protecting, registering, licensing, maintaining, enforcing, archiving, and retiring Aureus' intellectual property and legal records.

Its purpose is to preserve Aureus' intellectual assets, maintain accurate legal documentation, reduce legal risk, protect institutional innovation, and strengthen long-term institutional stewardship.

---

Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Copyrights
- Trademarks
- Patents
- Trade secrets
- Licensing agreements
- Brand assets
- Legal records
- Intellectual property registrations
- Legal archives
- Intellectual property enforcement

---

Objectives

Intellectual property management shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Safeguard institutional innovation.
- Reduce legal risk.
- Maintain complete legal records.
- Strengthen institutional resilience.
- Support continuous improvement.

---

Roles and Responsibilities

Intellectual Property Steward

Responsible for:

- Maintaining intellectual property inventories.
- Coordinating registrations.
- Managing legal documentation.
- Monitoring renewal deadlines.
- Preparing governance reports.

---

Intellectual Property Owners

Responsible for:

- Identifying new intellectual property.
- Reporting potential infringements.
- Supporting registration activities.
- Maintaining supporting documentation.

---

AI Intellectual Property Steward

Responsible for:

- Tracking renewal deadlines.
- Organizing documentation.
- Monitoring registration status.
- Detecting duplicate records.
- Preparing portfolio summaries.
- Supporting legal research.

Human stewards remain responsible for legal interpretation, registration decisions, licensing approvals, enforcement actions, and governance decisions.

---

Prerequisites

Before managing intellectual property:

- Ownership is documented.
- Applicable rights are identified.
- Supporting evidence is available.
- Required approvals are obtained.

---

Procedure

Step 1 — Identification

Document:

- Intellectual property type.
- Description.
- Creator or owner.
- Date created.
- Applicable legal protections.
- Supporting documentation.

---

Step 2 — Evaluation

Determine:

- Protectability.
- Ownership.
- Registration requirements.
- Licensing considerations.
- Business value.
- Associated legal risks.

---

Step 3 — Protection

Implement appropriate protections including:

- Registration where applicable.
- Confidentiality controls.
- Documentation.
- Licensing terms.
- Access restrictions.
- Legal notices.

---

Step 4 — Record Management

Maintain complete records including:

- Registration documents.
- Licenses.
- Ownership records.
- Renewal dates.
- Legal correspondence.
- Supporting evidence.

---

Step 5 — Monitoring

Monitor:

- Renewal deadlines.
- Licensing obligations.
- Potential infringement.
- Legal developments.
- Portfolio status.

Investigate significant issues promptly.

---

Step 6 — Enforcement

When potential infringement is identified:

- Document the issue.
- Preserve evidence.
- Conduct legal review.
- Determine appropriate response.
- Record outcomes.

---

Step 7 — Archival and Retirement

When intellectual property is retired or expires:

- Archive required documentation.
- Preserve historical records.
- Update inventories.
- Record final disposition.
- Maintain governance history.

---

Success Metrics

Monitor:

- Registration completion.
- Renewal compliance.
- Documentation quality.
- Portfolio accuracy.
- Infringement response time.
- Governance compliance.
- Legal risk reduction.
- Mission support.

---

Exceptions

Urgent legal actions may require expedited procedures while maintaining documentation and retrospective governance review.

---

Documentation

Maintain:

- Intellectual property inventories.
- Registration records.
- Licensing agreements.
- Renewal logs.
- Legal correspondence.
- Archive records.
- Governance reports.
- Enforcement documentation.

---

Continuous Improvement

Intellectual property management shall improve through legal reviews, stewardship feedback, AI-assisted analysis, governance assessments, portfolio audits, and lessons learned.
