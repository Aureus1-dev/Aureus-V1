OAS-LEG-007 — Legal Documentation and Records Framework

Canonical Designation: OAS-LEG-007
Status: Canonical Draft v1.0
Authority: OAS-LEG-002 — Legal Governance and Compliance Framework
Owner: Chief Legal Steward
Effective Date: July 13, 2026

---

Purpose

This Framework establishes the canonical model for creating, maintaining, protecting, retaining, archiving, accessing, and disposing of legal records and documentation throughout Aureus.

Its purpose is to preserve legal integrity, support governance, maintain audit readiness, protect confidential information, and ensure institutional legal history remains accurate and accessible.

---

Mission

Legal documentation exists to preserve the official legal record of Aureus, support lawful operations, protect institutional rights, and provide reliable evidence for governance and legal proceedings.

---

Scope

This Framework governs:

- Legal records
- Contracts
- Governance documents
- Legal correspondence
- Regulatory filings
- Legal opinions
- Litigation records
- Evidence management
- Legal holds
- Archival procedures

---

Guiding Principles

Legal documentation shall:

- Advance the mission.
- Protect members.
- Preserve constitutional alignment.
- Maintain legal integrity.
- Protect confidentiality.
- Support accountability.
- Preserve institutional history.
- Enable responsible governance.

Every legal record shall be trustworthy, complete, and retrievable.

---

Record Creation

Legal records shall be:

- Accurate.
- Complete.
- Properly identified.
- Timely created.
- Appropriately classified.
- Securely maintained.

Supporting documentation shall accompany significant legal actions where appropriate.

---

Classification

Legal records shall be classified according to:

- Sensitivity.
- Confidentiality.
- Legal privilege where applicable.
- Retention requirements.
- Operational importance.

Classification shall guide handling and access.

---

Access Control

Access shall be limited to authorized individuals according to operational need, legal requirements, and approved governance procedures.

Access activity may be logged where appropriate.

---

Legal Holds

When required by litigation, investigation, audit, or other lawful authority:

- Record destruction shall cease.
- Relevant records shall be preserved.
- Responsible stewards shall be notified.
- Legal hold status shall be documented.

---

Retention and Archival

Legal records shall be retained according to approved schedules that consider:

- Applicable law.
- Regulatory obligations.
- Governance requirements.
- Historical value.
- Litigation considerations.

Archives shall preserve authenticity and accessibility.

---

AI-Assisted Records Management

AI may assist through:

- Document organization.
- Record indexing.
- Retrieval support.
- Retention monitoring.
- Cross-reference management.
- Legal knowledge summaries.

Human stewards remain responsible for legal privilege determinations, document approvals, legal holds, and record disposition.

---

Performance Measurement

Legal documentation governance shall evaluate:

- Record completeness.
- Retrieval efficiency.
- Retention compliance.
- Access control effectiveness.
- Archive integrity.
- Documentation quality.
- Governance effectiveness.

Measurements shall strengthen institutional stewardship.

---

Continuous Improvement

Legal records management shall improve through:

- Governance reviews.
- Steward feedback.
- AI-assisted analysis.
- Documentation assessments.
- Legal developments.
- Lessons learned.

Continuous improvement strengthens institutional resilience.

---

Canonical Relationship

This Framework governs all legal documentation and records management throughout Aureus.

All Legal SOPs, document standards, legal hold procedures, retention schedules, archival procedures, evidence management standards, and legal knowledge systems shall conform to this Framework unless superseded by the Constitution or the Legal Charter.
