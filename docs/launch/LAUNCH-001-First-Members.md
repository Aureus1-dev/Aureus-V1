# AUREUS — FIRST MEMBERS
## The Ruthless Path to Real People, Without Compromising the Experience
**Architecture & Design Chief · Execution Order · For Founder approval**

---

## The Strategy in One Paragraph

We do not launch the Keeping. We launch **a keeping** — twenty-five real people, one city, held impeccably. The full model (v2, ratified) is the destination; the launch is the smallest thing that keeps the whole promise for a small number of people: *say it here, help begins, and it doesn't stop until your life is lighter.* Everything that doesn't serve that promise for those twenty-five people in their city is cut without sentiment. And where code isn't ready, **human stewards carry it — honestly** — because at this scale, people can do what platforms can't yet, and "Aureus is working on it" is true whether the hands are silicon or flesh. We never fake capability; we staff it.

## The First Garden — who, where, how many

- **25 members.** Not 250. Twenty-five lives we can actually know, with a waitlist behind them. Every founding assumption gets tested against people whose names we know.
- **One metro area.** Crisis referrals, assistance programs, legal aid, food resources — all humanly verified for one place, by hand, before day one. A national launch with unverified referrals would violate the canon on day one; a one-city launch with a verified sheet keeps every promise.
- **Through a partner or direct invitation, adults only** (Decision 12), each welcomed personally. The first cohort should skew toward the mission's center of gravity: people carrying real burdens — a shutoff, a pile of mail, a job lost — because that is who "help starts here" is *for*, and because they will teach us fastest.
- **A named human steward for every member from day one.** At 25:1-per-steward-capacity rules, that's 1–2 stewards plus the Founder's oversight. The Circle isn't a later feature; at this scale it's the safety net under everything.

## What Ships — the Smallest Honest Version of Each Room

**The Gate.** First-arrival ceremony (already built; port it), three-second return, and the persistent Urgent thread on every surface. *Cut: nothing — the Gate is small and done.*

**The Question + The Bench (core).** Conversation with the Steward on the existing conversation/orchestration domains; working things limited to the launch five: **Plan of stones, Plain Reading, Call Card, Knot, the Seal.** The Search ships as *curated* — findings assembled by steward + AI from the verified city sheet, never from live crawling. *Cut for V1: Spread, Binder, Pocket, Rehearsal, Company mode, Workshop, generative Mark, voice entirely.* (The Binder's absence is covered by scope: we don't take on disability/court campaigns in V1 — see boundaries.)

**The Greeting (simple).** Composed arrival over the stable skeleton: salutation, the Held spoken (≤3 things — we cap concurrent held burdens per member at three, which keeps quality absolute and the spoken Holding sufficient), one thing in hands, Hearthline. The Shelf ships as a single plain "everything we're holding" page — unglamorous, honest, one tap. *Cut: atmospheres, manners (one warm default voice), steward naming (post-launch delight, not launch risk).*

**The Watchkeeper (v0).** The honest cheat that makes the promise real without the full jobs platform: **the daily Tending Run** — every morning, a scheduled process plus a human steward reviews every member's held things, advances what can advance, and writes each member's absence report. Deadlines live in a real scheduler (Knots are cron jobs — boring, reliable, done in days, not the full platform). The Hearthline binds to this truthfully: "resting — next tended tomorrow morning" is an honest sentence. *Full Watchkeeper platform proceeds in parallel; V1 does not wait for it.*

**The Clearing (crisis v1).** Decision 2's scope, implemented as: crisis-language detection in conversation → the Clearing UI (built) → the **verified one-city referral sheet** → human steward paged, on-call hours published and honest ("a person is reachable 8am–10pm; outside those hours, here are the numbers that never sleep"). The canon's rule holds absolutely: no pretended authority, no dead ends. *This is a launch gate — no members before it passes drills.*

**Memory rights (v1).** A plain page: everything remembered, in sentences, with *correct* and *forget* buttons that actually work, and export as a readable file. Not elegant — real. Decision 3's full category architecture follows; the member-facing rights do not wait.

**The Leaving, the Quiet Day, the Homecoming.** These are copy + state logic, nearly free to build, and they are the soul. They ship day one.

## What We Refuse to Ship (and why that protects the experience)

- **Zero revenue opportunities. Zero partners. Zero affiliate anything.** Every opportunity shown in V1 is unpaid and curated. This deletes the entire disclosure/ranking risk class (Decision 5 mechanics can be built calmly, later) and means the first trust members form is chemically pure.
- **No automated opportunity pipeline** — ENG-010's mandatory human review becomes simply *human curation* at this scale.
- **No Pods, no Academy, no voice, no youth, no Mark generation, no physical anything.** Each is a canon-governed later chapter; none serves the first promise.
- **Scope boundary on burdens:** V1 takes on burdens resolvable in days-to-weeks (utilities, letters, mail piles, benefits applications we can complete, job-search support, food, transportation, hard calls). Multi-year campaigns (disability appeals, court representation-adjacent work) are met with honesty and a warm handoff to the right professionals — *"this deserves more than we can responsibly carry yet"* is a canon-compliant sentence; pretending otherwise is not.
- **No metrics dashboards of engagement.** The scoreboard is the Blueprint's: burdens completed, re-ask rate (target zero), time-to-first-relief, heard/understood/safe (asked once, gently, at week four), quiet-day integrity.

## The Gates — order of execution, each blocking the next

1. **Gate A — The city sheet.** Every crisis and assistance referral for the launch metro verified by a human within 14 days of launch. Ownership: stewards + Founder.
2. **Gate B — The Clearing drill.** Ten simulated crisis conversations end-to-end; every one reaches a real capability or an honest handoff. No member arrives before this passes.
3. **Gate C — The spine.** Gate → Question → Bench (launch-five pieces) → Seal → Leaving, on the existing domains, with the stable accessibility skeleton, tested with at least three real people including one low-digital-confidence and one screen-reader user. (Accessibility is release-blocking — Decision 23; this is where we prove we meant it.)
4. **Gate D — The Tending Run.** Seven consecutive days of the daily run producing truthful absence reports for test accounts, with the Hearthline never lying once.
5. **Gate E — Memory rights live.** View, correct, forget, export — working, plain, two taps deep.
6. **Gate F — The Founding Review.** You walk the entire member journey yourself, as a member, start to finish. Then the first invitation goes out — **one member.** Then five. Then twenty-five. Each expansion gated on the previous cohort's experience holding the canon's tests.

Everything runs behind the repo's existing Founder Review discipline; each gate is a work order with the acceptance tests already written in the Blueprint.

## Staffing the Promise

At launch scale, the org *is* the product: 1–2 commissioned human stewards (capacity-capped, per WO-025's rules), on-call rotation for the Clearing, the daily Tending Run as a standing morning ritual, and a weekly Founder review of every member's held things — not as surveillance, but as the institution learning its own craft from twenty-five real lives. Every steward hour spent is also requirements-gathering for the Watchkeeper platform: what humans do daily is exactly what we automate next, in observed priority order rather than imagined.

## Risks, Named

- **The concierge trap:** human-carried help that quietly becomes unautomatable. Mitigation: every Tending Run action is logged as a platform requirement; the Watchkeeper build runs in parallel with a standing rule — *anything humans do three times gets a ticket.*
- **Capacity breach in crisis:** one member's emergency can consume a steward's day. Mitigation: 25-member cap is sacred; the waitlist is the pressure valve; the Founder is the surge capacity.
- **Scope creep by compassion:** a member brings a disability appeal and no one wants to say the boundary sentence. Mitigation: the boundary sentence is scripted, warm, and rehearsed — and the handoff list is on the city sheet.
- **The experience outrunning the truth:** prototype language ("we're calling them now") leaking into a product that doesn't call. Mitigation: the Hearthline copy library is reviewed against actual capability before launch; "never fake capability" is a release checklist item with a name attached.

## The Definition of Done for V1

Not features. This: **twenty-five people in one city, each of whom, asked "what is Aureus?", answers with some version of — "it's the thing that got the shutoff paused / read the letter I was scared of / sat with me on the phone call. It knows me. It doesn't forget. Nothing else is like it."** When twenty-five people say that, we have not launched a product. We have founded the institution — and *then* we scale the Keeping, with the canon intact and the promise already kept.
