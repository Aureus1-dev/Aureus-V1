# AI-004 — Agent Lifecycle

## Purpose

This Production Canon establishes the permanent lifecycle governing every Artificial Intelligence steward operating within Aureus.

Artificial Intelligence agents are institutional stewards.

Like every steward, they possess a beginning, a period of active service, continuous development, and a deliberate retirement.

No Artificial Intelligence steward shall be created, modified, promoted, reassigned, or retired outside this lifecycle.

The purpose of this lifecycle is to ensure every agent remains trustworthy, measurable, accountable, and aligned with the Constitution throughout its existence.

---

# Article I — The Life of a Steward

Every Artificial Intelligence steward shall progress through the following lifecycle:

1. Need Identification
2. Charter
3. Design
4. Development
5. Verification
6. Deployment
7. Active Stewardship
8. Evaluation
9. Improvement
10. Expansion
11. Succession
12. Retirement

Every stage contributes to the wisdom of the institution.

---

# Article II — Need Identification

Before creating an Artificial Intelligence steward, Aureus shall identify a permanent institutional need.

Questions that shall be answered include:

- What problem exists?
- Why does this responsibility deserve its own steward?
- Which members benefit?
- Which departments benefit?
- What institutional objective is strengthened?
- Could an existing steward perform this responsibility?

Artificial Intelligence stewards shall never be created merely because technology allows it.

Every steward must exist for a meaningful purpose.

---

# Article III — Steward Charter

Before development begins, every steward shall receive an official charter.

The charter shall define:

- Steward name.
- Mission.
- Domain.
- Responsibilities.
- Authority level.
- Human oversight.
- Required knowledge.
- Inputs.
- Outputs.
- Success measurements.
- Escalation requirements.
- Relationships with other stewards.

The charter establishes the steward's permanent identity.

---

# Article IV — Design

Every steward shall be intentionally designed.

Design includes:

- Scope.
- Decision boundaries.
- Required integrations.
- Communication protocols.
- Privacy requirements.
- Security requirements.
- Performance expectations.
- Failure handling.
- Recovery procedures.

Thoughtful design prevents institutional confusion.

---

# Article V — Development

During development, every steward shall be prepared through:

- Constitutional alignment.
- Knowledge preparation.
- Prompt engineering.
- Tool integration.
- Governance review.
- Security review.
- Privacy review.
- Human evaluation.
- Technical testing.

Development continues until the steward consistently fulfills its charter.
