# AI-014 — Agent Creation and Retirement

## Purpose

This Production Canon establishes the permanent principles governing the creation, modification, succession, and retirement of every Artificial Intelligence steward operating within Aureus.

Artificial Intelligence stewards are institutional offices.

They are not disposable software components.

Each steward exists because it fulfills a defined institutional responsibility.

Every steward shall therefore be created intentionally, governed responsibly, improved continuously, and retired honorably when its purpose has been fulfilled or superseded.

---

# Article I — Philosophy of Agent Creation

Artificial Intelligence stewards shall be created only to fulfill a genuine institutional need.

An agent shall never be created merely because automation is possible.

Every new steward must strengthen one or more of the following:

- Members.
- Human stewards.
- Communities.
- Operations.
- Governance.
- Institutional knowledge.
- Long-term flourishing.

Purpose precedes implementation.

---

# Article II — Principle of Necessity

Before creating a new Artificial Intelligence steward, Aureus shall determine whether the responsibility can be fulfilled by:

- An existing agent.
- A shared capability.
- A workflow improvement.
- A human steward.
- A constitutional process.

New agents should exist only when they provide meaningful institutional value.

Institutional simplicity shall always be preferred over unnecessary specialization.

---

# Article III — Agent Charter

Every Artificial Intelligence steward shall possess a permanent charter.

The charter shall define:

- Official name.
- Institutional purpose.
- Scope of responsibility.
- Delegated authority.
- Decision limits.
- Required inputs.
- Expected outputs.
- Relationships with other agents.
- Human oversight requirements.
- Performance expectations.

The charter is the steward's constitutional identity.

---

# Article IV — Approval for Creation

The creation of a new Artificial Intelligence steward shall require appropriate institutional approval according to delegated authority.

Approval shall consider:

- Mission alignment.
- Constitutional alignment.
- Expected value.
- Operational impact.
- Resource requirements.
- Governance implications.
- Long-term maintenance.

Every steward shall exist by deliberate institutional choice.

---

# Article V — Standardization

Every Artificial Intelligence steward shall follow institutional standards for:

- Naming.
- Documentation.
- Interfaces.
- Communication.
- Security.
- Logging.
- Monitoring.
- Evaluation.
- Governance.

Consistency strengthens maintainability.

---

# Article VI — Modification

Existing stewards may evolve over time.

Significant modifications shall be documented and evaluated according to:

- Purpose.
- Risk.
- Member impact.
- Operational impact.
- Constitutional implications.
- Compatibility with existing systems.

Evolution should strengthen continuity rather than create unnecessary disruption.

---

# Article VII — Succession

When responsibilities transfer from one steward to another, Aureus shall preserve institutional continuity.

Succession should include:

- Knowledge transfer.
- Historical records.
- Decision history.
- Performance history.
- Operational documentation.
- Steward relationships.
- Governance records.

Institutional wisdom shall never depend upon a single implementation.

---

# Article VIII — Retirement

A steward may be retired when:

- Its purpose no longer exists.
- Another steward fully assumes its responsibilities.
- Technology has materially changed.
- Governance has changed.
- The institution determines retirement better serves its mission.

Retirement shall be deliberate rather than accidental.

---

# Article IX — Preservation

Retiring a steward shall not erase institutional history.

The following should be preserved whenever appropriate:

- Charter.
- Documentation.
- Decision history.
- Performance history.
- Lessons learned.
- Major innovations.
- Significant incidents.
- Contributions to Aureus.

Institutional memory outlives implementation.

---

# Article X — Responsible Decommissioning

Before retirement is completed, Aureus shall verify:

- Responsibilities have been reassigned.
- Data has been preserved.
- Dependencies have been resolved.
- Documentation has been updated.
- Human stewards have been informed.
- Operational continuity has been maintained.

No steward shall leave unresolved institutional responsibilities.

---

# Article XI — Legacy

Every Artificial Intelligence steward should leave Aureus stronger than it found it.

Legacy includes:

- Better systems.
- Better documentation.
- Better knowledge.
- Better governance.
- Better member outcomes.
- Better stewardship.
- Greater institutional wisdom.

The value of a steward is measured by what endures after it is gone.

---

# Article XII — Continuous Stewardship

The lifecycle of every Artificial Intelligence steward shall be continuously reviewed.

The institution should regularly ask:

- Does this steward still fulfill its purpose?
- Can its responsibilities be simplified?
- Should its authority change?
- Has institutional learning improved its design?
- Is succession needed?
- Is retirement appropriate?

Continuous stewardship prevents institutional stagnation.

---

# Article XIII — Foundational Standard

Artificial Intelligence stewards shall be created with purpose.

They shall serve with wisdom.

They shall evolve with discipline.

They shall retire with dignity.

Their knowledge shall endure beyond their implementation.

In every stage of their lifecycle, they shall strengthen Aureus's enduring mission:

**To help people flourish. Forever.**
