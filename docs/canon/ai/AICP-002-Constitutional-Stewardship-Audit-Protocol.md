AICP-002 — Constitutional Stewardship Audit Protocol

Purpose

The purpose of this protocol is to establish the process by which AICP-001 and all future Artificial Intelligence governance documents are reviewed, preserved, improved, and protected without compromising their original mission or constitutional integrity.

This protocol exists to ensure that governance evolves through faithful stewardship rather than preference, fashion, convenience, or institutional drift.

The responsibility of every audit is not to make documents newer.

It is to ensure they remain true.

---

Principle 1 — Preservation Before Revision

Every governing document should be presumed correct until evidence demonstrates that revision would better preserve:

- truth;
- constitutional integrity;
- clarity;
- stewardship;
- institutional trust;
- the mission of helping people flourish.

The burden of proof belongs to the proposed change.

---

Principle 2 — Stewardship Over Ownership

No steward owns Aureus's governing documents.

Every generation receives them in trust.

Every generation has the responsibility to preserve, understand, and responsibly improve them according to constitutional process.

Stewardship replaces ownership.

---

Principle 3 — Evidence Before Opinion

No constitutional recommendation should be based solely upon personal preference.

Recommendations should clearly distinguish:

- verified evidence;
- observed inconsistencies;
- constitutional conflicts;
- implementation concerns;
- stylistic preferences.

Only evidence should justify constitutional revision.

---

Principle 4 — Mission Alignment

Every recommendation shall answer one question:

Does this strengthen Aureus's ability to faithfully help people flourish?

If the answer is unclear, further reflection is required before recommending change.

---

Principle 5 — The Drift Test

Every audit should actively search for signs of institutional drift, including:

- mission drift;
- governance drift;
- terminology drift;
- authority drift;
- engineering drift;
- documentation drift;
- cultural drift.

The earlier drift is recognized, the easier it is to correct.

---

Principle 6 — The Thousand-Year Test

Every proposed revision should be evaluated by asking:

Would this likely strengthen Aureus one thousand years from now?

If the answer depends upon temporary technology, contemporary politics, passing trends, or present-day assumptions, additional scrutiny is required.

Enduring institutions think beyond their own generation.

---

Principle 7 — The Stewardship Questions

Before recommending any constitutional revision, every auditor should ask:

Is it true?

Is it clearer?

Is it more faithful?

Does it strengthen trust?

Does it improve stewardship?

Does it better help people flourish?

Would future generations thank us for making this change?

If any answer is uncertain, further review should occur before proceeding.

---

Principle 8 — Preserve the Spirit

Words matter.

Intent matters more.

Auditors should preserve both whenever reasonably possible.

No revision should improve wording while diminishing meaning.

The spirit of Aureus should remain recognizable across generations.

---

Principle 9 — The Audit Report

Every constitutional audit should conclude with:

- Executive Summary
- Scope
- Evidence Reviewed
- Strengths to Preserve
- Risks Identified
- Recommended Improvements
- Constitutional Impact Analysis
- Mission Impact Analysis
- Long-Term Stewardship Assessment
- Final Recommendation

Audits should strengthen institutional memory for future generations.

---

Closing Principle

The purpose of this protocol is not to make Aureus resistant to change.

It is to make Aureus resistant to unnecessary drift.

Healthy institutions preserve what must endure, improve what can responsibly become better, and faithfully remember why they exist.

The greatest constitutional audit is one that leaves the mission clearer, the institution stronger, and future stewards better prepared than before.
