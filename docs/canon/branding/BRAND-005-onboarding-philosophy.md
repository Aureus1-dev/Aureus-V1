# BRAND-005 — Onboarding Philosophy

Canonical Designation: BRAND-005

Status: Living Draft

Purpose:
This document establishes the constitutional philosophy governing onboarding within Aureus. It defines how the institution welcomes new members, establishes trust, begins personalization, and prepares each person for a lifelong relationship of flourishing.

Relationship to Canon:
This document is governed by BRAND-001 (Brand Constitution), BRAND-002 (Brand Identity), BRAND-003 (Member Experience Philosophy), and BRAND-004 (Personalization Philosophy). Every onboarding experience shall conform to the principles established herein.

Living Draft Notice:
This document is designated as a Living Draft. It shall be continuously refined through operational experience, ethical experimentation, member outcomes, and evidence gathered through real-world implementation.

---

# Article I — Purpose

Onboarding exists to begin a relationship.

It does not exist to complete one.

The purpose of onboarding is to welcome members, establish trust, understand only what is necessary to begin helping, and deliver meaningful value as quickly as possible.

---

# Article II — First Impressions

The first experience shall communicate:

• Hope

• Peace

• Hospitality

• Human dignity

• Simplicity

• Competence

• Trust

Members should leave onboarding feeling welcomed rather than evaluated.

---

# Article III — Earn Trust Before Asking

Trust shall precede curiosity.

Members should never feel interrogated.

Every question asked during onboarding shall justify its existence by materially improving Aureus's ability to help the member immediately.

Questions that can reasonably be asked later should generally be deferred until trust has grown.

---

# Article IV — Every Question Must Earn Its Place

Every onboarding question shall satisfy at least one of the following purposes:

• It enables immediate assistance.

• It materially improves the first member experience.

• It protects member safety.

• It fulfills a legal or operational requirement.

• It meaningfully improves long-term flourishing.

Questions failing these standards should generally not appear during onboarding.

---

# Article V — Progressive Discovery

The institution acknowledges that meaningful understanding develops over time.

Accordingly, onboarding should collect only the information necessary to begin serving the member effectively.

Additional understanding should develop naturally through ongoing service, conversation, observation, and member choice.

---

# Article VI — Deliver Value Early

Members should experience meaningful value before Aureus requests unnecessary information.

Whenever possible, onboarding should demonstrate usefulness quickly, reinforcing trust through action rather than promises.

---

# Article VII — Ethical Experimentation

Aureus shall continually improve onboarding through ethical experimentation and evidence.

Experiments shall never compromise:

• Human dignity

• Fairness

• Transparency

• Accessibility

• Trust

Learning exists to improve service—not manipulate behavior.

---

# Article VIII — Simplicity

Every additional screen, question, interaction, animation, or decision increases cognitive burden.

Accordingly, onboarding should strive for clarity, simplicity, and confidence.

The simplest onboarding capable of achieving the institutional mission should generally be preferred.

---

# Article IX — Success

Successful onboarding is not measured by completion alone.

Success is measured by whether members begin their Aureus journey with:

• Trust

• Hope

• Confidence

• Clarity

• A meaningful next step

• A desire to continue the relationship

---

# Article X — Long-Term Objective

Onboarding is the first chapter of a lifelong relationship.

It should create the foundation upon which trust, personalization, stewardship, learning, and flourishing continue to grow.

The institution shall therefore optimize not for immediate completion rates alone, but for long-term member success.

---

# Living Draft Appendix

## Current Hypotheses

- Shorter onboarding builds stronger trust than lengthy questionnaires.
- Delivering value before requesting additional information increases confidence.
- Progressive discovery produces richer understanding than exhaustive intake.
- Members are more likely to continue when they experience meaningful help early.

---

## Open Questions

- Which onboarding questions produce the greatest long-term benefit?
- How quickly should personalization begin?
- Which first experiences most consistently increase trust?
- What is the minimum onboarding necessary to begin helping effectively?

---

## Success Measures

Evidence should demonstrate that members increasingly report:

• Feeling welcomed.

• Feeling respected.

• Feeling understood.

• Feeling hopeful.

• Feeling confident about their next step.

• Feeling that onboarding was worthwhile rather than burdensome.
