# BRAND-018 — Photography & Imagery

Status: Living Draft

---

# Purpose

This document establishes the constitutional philosophy governing photography, imagery, illustration, and visual storytelling throughout Aureus.

Imagery exists to reinforce the institution's mission, emotional foundations, and commitment to human flourishing.

Every image should communicate truth, dignity, hope, and authentic human experience.

---

# Philosophy

People should recognize the Aureus visual language before they recognize the logo.

Imagery should feel genuine, welcoming, and deeply human.

Aureus does not portray perfection.

It portrays possibility.

---

# Emotional Objectives

Photography and imagery should reinforce:

• Trust

• Hope

• Peace

• Belonging

• Hospitality

• Human Dignity

• Wisdom

• Love

• Stewardship

• Human Flourishing

---

# Preferred Subjects

Preference should be given to imagery portraying:

• Families

• Friends

• Neighbors

• Teachers

• Mentors

• Craftspeople

• Volunteers

• Gardeners

• Students

• Everyday people serving one another

---

# Preferred Moments

Images should emphasize:

• Shared meals

• Reading together

• Learning

• Mentoring

• Gardening

• Building

• Repairing

• Walking

• Conversation

• Reflection

• Celebration

• Everyday kindness

The ordinary often communicates trust more effectively than the extraordinary.

---

# Environment

Preferred environments include:

• Homes

• Front porches

• Libraries

• Parks

• Gardens

• Community spaces

• Workshops

• Nature trails

• Neighborhoods

• Schools

Spaces should feel lived in, welcoming, and cared for.

---

# Light

Natural light should be preferred whenever practical.

Morning light, golden hour, and soft interior light often reinforce hope and hospitality.

Lighting should reveal rather than dramatize.

---

# Authenticity

Preference should be given to authentic moments over heavily staged scenes.

Images should reflect real relationships, real communities, and genuine acts of care.

---

# Diversity

Imagery should reflect the diversity of humanity.

People of different ages, cultures, backgrounds, abilities, and life experiences should be represented with equal dignity and respect.

Representation should feel natural rather than performative.

---

# What to Avoid

Avoid imagery that communicates:

• Fear

• Isolation

• Excessive luxury

• Material status

• Aggression

• Artificial perfection

• Manipulation

• Empty corporate symbolism

Images should inspire trust rather than envy.

---

# Illustration

Illustrations should complement photography while maintaining the same emotional principles.

Illustrations should simplify, clarify, and inspire—not replace authentic human stories.

---

# Living Draft

This document shall remain under active review while Aureus continues emotional design research, member testing, and visual exploration before canonization.
