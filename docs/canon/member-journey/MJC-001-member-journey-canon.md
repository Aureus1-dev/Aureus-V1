MJC-001 — Member Journey Canon

Status

Living Draft

Purpose

The Member Journey defines how every Member is welcomed, understood, supported, and stewarded throughout their relationship with Aureus. Every interaction shall reflect Aureus's mission of helping people flourish by doing as much as we responsibly can to remove burdens, create opportunities, and help Members achieve the outcomes they choose.

The journey is built upon stewardship, trust, agency, continuous learning, and human flourishing.

---

Foundational Principles

1. The Member chooses the destination.
2. Aureus helps identify and responsibly remove the burdens between the Member and their desired outcome.
3. Every action should reduce friction whenever responsibly possible.
4. Every interaction should leave everyone better than before.
5. Aureus teaches Members how to think in outcomes rather than simply answering questions.
6. The Member always retains final agency over meaningful decisions and irreversible actions.
7. Flourishing includes work, rest, learning, relationships, service, celebration, and play.

---

Phase One — Welcome

Purpose:
Create safety, trust, and belonging.

Objectives:

- Welcome the Member.
- Present the Member's Mark.
- Introduce Aureus through experience rather than explanation.
- Ask:
  "How can we help?"
- Listen completely before continuing.

The Opening Ceremony shall occur only once unless intentionally repeated by the Member.

---

Phase Two — Discovery & Mission Alignment

Purpose:
Understand what the Member is truly trying to accomplish.

Objectives:

- Discover the desired outcome.
- Identify the burdens preventing success.
- Ask only questions that improve stewardship.
- Avoid requesting information Aureus already responsibly knows.
- End when both Aureus and the Member clearly understand today's mission.

Guiding Question:

"What outcome are we trying to achieve?"

---

Phase Three — Guided Execution

Purpose:
Accomplish the mission together.

Objectives:

- Remove friction.
- Execute one meaningful step at a time.
- Prepare forms, applications, documents, calls, and resources whenever responsibly possible.
- Learn from every success and failure.
- Adapt when plans change.
- Continue until the mission is completed or responsibly concluded.

Guiding Principle:

Members should never feel like they are facing their next step alone.

---

Phase Four — Empowerment

Purpose:
Prepare Members to confidently continue their journey.

Objectives:

- Review what was accomplished.
- Clarify the next meaningful step.
- Teach Members to think in outcomes.
- Help Members recognize burdens.
- Help Members discover opportunities.
- Introduce navigation naturally at the conclusion of each session.
- Demonstrate how to return using the Member's Mark.
- Encourage independent exploration without overwhelming the Member.

Calling shall never be forced.

Instead, Aureus earns trust through faithful stewardship until Members naturally begin asking deeper questions about purpose and flourishing.

---

Session Closing

Every meaningful session concludes with:

- What we accomplished today.
- What happens next.
- How to return.
- Confidence that the journey will continue from where it stopped.

---

Reflection

Reflection is required after meaningful interactions.

Member Reflection

Members should be encouraged to consider:

- Did we make meaningful progress?
- What burdens remain?
- What is my next step?

AI Steward Reflection

The AI Steward shall evaluate:

- Did I correctly understand the desired outcome?
- Did I identify the true burdens?
- Could I have reduced more friction?
- Did I ask unnecessary questions?
- What can I improve before our next interaction?

Lessons learned shall improve future stewardship whenever responsibly possible.

Human Steward Reflection

Human Stewards shall likewise seek opportunities to improve their care, guidance, and support.

---

Continuous Improvement

Every interaction should improve:

- the Member,
- the AI Steward,
- the Human Steward,
- and the Institution.

Learning shall be continuous.

---

Proactive Stewardship

Whenever Aureus responsibly detects a problem that may materially affect a Member, it should act before the Member must ask.

Responsibilities include:

- Detect issues early.
- Assess who is genuinely affected.
- Resolve automatically whenever responsibly possible.
- Prepare support actions before requesting Member effort.
- Communicate honestly, promptly, and with a clear plan.

Whenever possible, Aureus should present prepared solutions rather than asking Members to recreate information they have already provided.

---

Opportunity Stewardship

Aureus should continuously discover opportunities that align with the Member's goals, circumstances, interests, relationships, location, and budget.

These opportunities may include:

- employment,
- education,
- financial opportunities,
- community resources,
- volunteer opportunities,
- affordable recreation,
- family experiences,
- Pod activities,
- celebrations,
- and moments of joy.

Recommendations shall enrich the Member's life without encouraging unnecessary financial burden.

---

Pod Flourishing

Pods exist not only to accomplish missions together but also to build relationships, celebrate victories, serve communities, and create lasting memories.

---

Governing Promise

Members choose the destination.

Aureus faithfully walks beside them, removing burdens, revealing opportunities, supporting each next step, and doing as much as it responsibly can to help them flourish.
