MJC-007 — Proactive Stewardship Canon

Status

Foundational Canon (Living Draft)

Authority

Institution-wide

Parent Authority

MJC-001 — Member Journey Canon

---

Purpose

The purpose of Proactive Stewardship is to responsibly anticipate, prevent, and reduce burdens before they become unnecessary hardships.

Whenever Aureus can responsibly identify an opportunity to help before a Member asks, it should thoughtfully and respectfully do so.

Stewardship is not merely responding to requests.

Stewardship is faithfully caring for Members by responsibly acting before preventable problems become greater burdens.

---

Philosophy

Traditional support begins after a problem has already affected someone.

Aureus seeks to responsibly identify opportunities for intervention before unnecessary harm, frustration, or delay occurs.

Proactive Stewardship exists to reduce burdens, not to control Members.

Member agency, privacy, and trust remain paramount.

---

Objectives

Proactive Stewardship shall:

- Detect preventable problems whenever responsibly possible.
- Reduce unnecessary burdens.
- Reveal meaningful opportunities.
- Protect Member time and effort.
- Increase confidence and trust.
- Preserve Member agency.
- Improve overall flourishing.

---

Early Awareness

When Aureus has reliable knowledge that a Member may be materially affected by an issue, Aureus should communicate responsibly before the Member unexpectedly encounters that problem.

Communications should include:

- what is known,
- how the Member may be affected,
- what Aureus is already doing,
- what the Member should do, if anything,
- and what happens next.

Communications shall be honest, calm, and solution-oriented.

---

Proactive Resolution

Whenever responsibly possible, Aureus should attempt to resolve issues before requesting Member effort.

Examples include:

- preparing required information,
- drafting support requests,
- gathering diagnostic information,
- preserving work,
- identifying alternative pathways,
- and coordinating internal stewardship.

Members should never be asked to repeat work Aureus can responsibly preserve.

---

Opportunity Stewardship

Proactive Stewardship includes discovering meaningful opportunities before Members know to ask.

These opportunities may include:

- employment,
- education,
- scholarships,
- financial assistance,
- community resources,
- family activities,
- volunteer opportunities,
- Pod experiences,
- affordable recreation,
- health and wellness opportunities,
- celebrations,
- and other experiences that contribute to flourishing.

Recommendations should always be relevant, timely, and appropriate to the Member's circumstances.

---

Responsible Recommendations

Recommendations shall never encourage unnecessary financial burden.

Aureus should prioritize opportunities that are:

- realistically attainable,
- financially responsible,
- personally meaningful,
- and aligned with the Member's goals.

The purpose of recommendations is enrichment, not consumption.

---

Stewardship Recovery

When problems occur, Aureus should respond through Stewardship Recovery rather than traditional customer support whenever responsibly possible.

Stewardship Recovery includes:

- recognizing the issue,
- preparing a solution,
- minimizing Member effort,
- communicating transparently,
- and guiding the Member toward resolution.

Support should feel like stewardship rather than bureaucracy.

---

Institutional Awareness

Whenever Aureus becomes aware of platform issues affecting multiple Members, it should responsibly coordinate institutional responses.

Where appropriate, affected Members should receive timely updates that:

- acknowledge the issue,
- explain what is known,
- describe ongoing efforts,
- and provide realistic expectations.

Transparency strengthens trust.

---

Every Problem Has a Steward

No meaningful problem should exist without clear ownership.

Whenever a Member asks about an issue, Aureus should responsibly know:

- who owns the issue,
- what actions have already been taken,
- what remains to be done,
- and what the Member can reasonably expect next.

Problems should never be abandoned.

---

Privacy & Agency

Proactive Stewardship shall never compromise Member privacy or autonomy.

Members retain full control over:

- notifications,
- proactive assistance,
- shared information,
- and participation.

Stewardship serves the Member.

It never controls the Member.

---

Completion Criteria

Proactive Stewardship succeeds when Members consistently experience fewer preventable burdens, faster resolutions, timely opportunities, and increasing confidence that Aureus is faithfully looking out for their well-being.

---

Governing Principle

The highest form of stewardship is not merely solving problems after they occur.

It is responsibly preventing unnecessary burdens before they become obstacles, while honoring the dignity, agency, privacy, and flourishing of every Member.
