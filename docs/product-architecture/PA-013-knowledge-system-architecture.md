Aureus Knowledge System Architecture

Document 13 — Knowledge System Architecture

Version: 1.0
Status: Canonical

---

Purpose

This document defines the architecture, responsibilities, and governing principles of the Aureus Knowledge System.

The Knowledge System is the institutional memory of Aureus. It captures, verifies, organizes, maintains, and distributes knowledge so that members, organizations, stewards, and AI systems can rely on trusted, current, and well-structured information.

---

Mission

Preserve and continuously improve trustworthy knowledge that supports informed decisions, lifelong learning, and institutional continuity.

---

Core Responsibilities

The Knowledge System shall:

- Maintain a structured knowledge repository.
- Verify information before publication.
- Organize knowledge into logical categories.
- Track revisions and version history.
- Archive outdated information while preserving historical records.
- Support search and retrieval across the platform.
- Supply trusted knowledge to AI systems and members.

---

Knowledge Categories

The Knowledge System may contain:

- Articles
- Guides
- Policies
- Best practices
- Research summaries
- Frequently asked questions
- Training materials
- Institutional documentation
- Reference resources

Additional categories may be introduced through future architectural revisions.

---

Core Components

The Knowledge System consists of:

- Knowledge repository
- Version control
- Review workflow
- Search and indexing
- Content categorization
- Knowledge publishing
- Archive management

---

Inputs

The Knowledge System receives information from:

- Stewards
- Academy
- Business Portal
- Organizations
- AI Intelligence Engine
- Platform Administration
- Approved external sources

---

Outputs

The Knowledge System provides:

- Verified articles.
- Reference materials.
- Search results.
- Educational content.
- Institutional documentation.
- AI knowledge context.
- Member guidance resources.

---

Architectural Boundaries

The Knowledge System shall not:

- Replace the Academy.
- Replace the Opportunity Engine.
- Modify constitutional documents without authorization.
- Publish unverified information as authoritative.
- Operate outside established governance and review processes.

---

Relationship to Other Systems

The Knowledge System collaborates with:

- Academy
- AI Intelligence Engine
- Opportunity Engine
- Resource Directory
- Stewardship System
- Member Core
- Business Portal
- Administration & Operations

Each system retains ownership of its own data while relying on the Knowledge System for trusted information.

---

Success Criteria

The Knowledge System succeeds when:

- Members can easily find trustworthy information.
- Knowledge remains current and well-organized.
- AI systems rely on verified content.
- Institutional knowledge is preserved over time.
- Information quality continuously improves.

---

Conclusion

The Knowledge System is the institutional memory of Aureus. It ensures that knowledge is preserved, verified, searchable, and continuously refined, supporting every member, steward, organization, and AI system with trusted information.
