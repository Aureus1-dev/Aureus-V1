Aureus Information Architecture

Document 16 — Information Architecture

Version: 1.0
Status: Canonical

---

Purpose

This document defines how information is organized, owned, shared, and governed throughout the Aureus platform.

The Information Architecture ensures that every piece of information has a clear owner, a defined lifecycle, and a consistent structure, enabling every Aureus system to work together without duplication or ambiguity.

---

Mission

Create a unified information model that allows members, AI systems, stewards, organizations, and platform services to exchange information accurately, securely, and efficiently.

---

Architectural Principles

The Information Architecture shall:

- Establish a single source of truth for every type of information.
- Eliminate unnecessary duplication.
- Define clear ownership for all information.
- Support secure information sharing between systems.
- Preserve data integrity and traceability.
- Scale as Aureus evolves.

---

Core Information Domains

The platform manages the following primary information domains:

- Member information
- Opportunities
- Journeys
- Learning content
- Knowledge
- Resources
- Organizations
- Businesses
- Pods
- Communications
- Stewardship
- Platform administration

Each domain has one authoritative owner within the Product Architecture.

---

Information Ownership

Every information domain shall have:

- One authoritative system of record.
- Defined consumers.
- Defined update rules.
- Defined retention policies.
- Defined governance requirements.

No domain may have multiple competing systems of record.

---

Information Flow

Information flows through Aureus according to these principles:

- Systems publish authoritative information they own.
- Other systems consume, but do not redefine, that information.
- AI systems interpret information but do not become the system of record.
- Members remain owners of their personal goals and choices.

---

Information Lifecycle

Every information asset progresses through a lifecycle that includes:

- Creation
- Verification
- Storage
- Use
- Revision
- Archiving
- Retirement (where appropriate)

---

Security & Privacy

Information shall be:

- Protected according to its sensitivity.
- Accessible only to authorized users and systems.
- Handled transparently.
- Governed by the Aureus Constitution and platform policies.

---

Success Criteria

The Information Architecture succeeds when:

- Every piece of information has a clear owner.
- Systems exchange information without duplication.
- Members trust how their information is handled.
- AI recommendations rely on authoritative data.
- The platform remains scalable and maintainable.

---

Conclusion

The Information Architecture provides the structural foundation for information management across Aureus. It enables every product system to work together while preserving clarity, trust, ownership, and long-term scalability.
