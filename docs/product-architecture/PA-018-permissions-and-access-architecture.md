Aureus Permissions & Access Architecture

Document 18 — Permissions & Access Architecture

Version: 1.0
Status: Canonical

---

Purpose

This document defines the permission model and access architecture for the Aureus platform.

Its purpose is to ensure that every member, steward, organization, business, administrator, and AI system has appropriate access to platform capabilities while preserving security, privacy, accountability, and constitutional governance.

---

Mission

Provide secure, transparent, and role-based access to Aureus that protects members, respects privacy, and enables collaboration without granting unnecessary authority.

---

Architectural Principles

The Permissions & Access Architecture shall:

- Follow the Principle of Least Privilege.
- Grant permissions based on role rather than individual preference.
- Separate identity, authentication, authorization, and governance.
- Maintain complete auditability of permission changes.
- Support future expansion without redesign.

---

Access Levels

The platform recognizes the following access levels:

- Visitor
- Member
- Steward
- Organization Representative
- Business Representative
- Platform Administrator
- System Administrator
- AI Service Account

Additional access levels may be introduced through future constitutional or architectural revisions.

---

Permission Categories

Permissions include, but are not limited to:

- View
- Create
- Edit
- Delete
- Publish
- Approve
- Moderate
- Manage Members
- Manage Organizations
- Manage Business Accounts
- Configure Platform
- Access Reports
- Administrative Operations

Each permission shall be explicitly assigned to one or more roles.

---

Authorization Rules

Authorization decisions shall be based on:

- User identity
- Assigned role(s)
- Resource ownership
- Organizational membership
- Stewardship authority
- Constitutional and platform policies

---

AI Access

The AI Intelligence Engine shall:

- Operate only within authorized permissions.
- Access information required to fulfill approved tasks.
- Never grant itself additional permissions.
- Never bypass human governance or constitutional controls.

---

Security Principles

The platform shall:

- Log significant permission changes.
- Support secure authentication.
- Protect sensitive information.
- Enforce authorization consistently.
- Minimize unnecessary access to personal information.

---

Success Criteria

The Permissions & Access Architecture succeeds when:

- Members have appropriate access to platform features.
- Sensitive information is protected.
- Permission decisions are consistent and auditable.
- AI operates within defined boundaries.
- Governance and security reinforce member trust.

---

Conclusion

The Permissions & Access Architecture establishes the security and authorization foundation of Aureus. It enables every participant to contribute appropriately while protecting members, preserving trust, and ensuring alignment with the Aureus Constitution.
