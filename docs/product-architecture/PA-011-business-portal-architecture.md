Aureus Business Portal Architecture

Document 11 — Business Portal Architecture

Version: 1.0
Status: Canonical

---

Purpose

This document defines the architecture, responsibilities, and governing principles of the Aureus Business Portal.

The Business Portal enables businesses, nonprofit organizations, educational institutions, government agencies, and other approved organizations to participate in the Aureus ecosystem by creating opportunities, sharing resources, engaging with members, and forming mission-aligned partnerships.

---

Mission

Create a trusted environment where organizations and members can work together to generate meaningful opportunities and measurable mutual value while advancing the Aureus mission.

---

Core Responsibilities

The Business Portal shall:

- Enable organizations to create verified accounts.
- Publish opportunities for members.
- Recruit qualified candidates.
- Share educational and community resources.
- Build partnerships with Aureus.
- Measure the impact of participation.
- Maintain transparent organizational profiles.

---

Organization Types

The Business Portal supports:

- Businesses
- Nonprofit organizations
- Educational institutions
- Government agencies
- Community organizations
- Strategic partners

Additional organization types may be introduced through future architectural revisions.

---

Core Components

The Business Portal consists of:

- Organization profiles
- Opportunity publishing
- Recruitment tools
- Partnership management
- Organization dashboards
- Analytics and reporting
- Member engagement tools

---

Inputs

The Business Portal receives information from:

- Organization representatives
- Opportunity Engine
- Member activity
- AI Intelligence Engine
- Stewardship System
- Platform Administration

---

Outputs

The Business Portal provides:

- Published opportunities.
- Organization information.
- Recruitment activity.
- Partnership data.
- Member engagement metrics.
- Organizational analytics.

---

Architectural Boundaries

The Business Portal shall not:

- Replace the Opportunity Engine.
- Replace Member Core.
- Override platform governance.
- Access member information beyond authorized permissions.
- Circumvent stewardship or verification processes.

---

Relationship to Other Systems

The Business Portal works closely with:

- Opportunity Engine
- Member Core
- AI Intelligence Engine
- Resource Directory
- Knowledge System
- Stewardship System
- Administration & Operations

Each system retains ownership of its responsibilities while supporting productive relationships between organizations and members.

---

Success Criteria

The Business Portal succeeds when organizations:

- Publish high-quality opportunities.
- Build trusted relationships with members.
- Recruit effectively.
- Contribute valuable resources.
- Demonstrate measurable positive impact.
- Strengthen the Aureus ecosystem.

---

Conclusion

The Business Portal serves as the organizational participation layer of Aureus. It enables trusted institutions to collaborate with members through opportunities, resources, partnerships, and shared mission while preserving transparency, accountability, and member trust.
