FPB-008 — Voice & Multimodal Interaction Specification

Status: Production Blueprint
Version: 1.0
Authority: Multimodal Interaction Standard

---

1. Purpose

This document defines how members interact with Aureus through conversation, voice, visual interfaces, touch, and future interaction modalities.

Conversation remains the primary interface.

Visual experiences support conversation.

Touch enables member control.

Together they create one continuous experience.

---

2. Experience Philosophy

Members should feel as though they are interacting with one steward—not switching between separate systems.

Conversation, voice, screens, and actions shall behave as one unified experience.

The technology disappears behind the relationship.

---

3. Conversation-Led Navigation

Members should express intent naturally.

Examples include:

- "Show me my opportunities."
- "Compare those choices."
- "Take me back to yesterday."
- "Explain this document."
- "Help me finish this application."

Whenever practical, Aureus opens the appropriate interface automatically while maintaining the conversation.

---

4. Voice Interaction

Voice interactions shall support natural conversation.

The Conversation Engine should, whenever technically feasible:

- recognize conversational pauses,
- distinguish incomplete thoughts from completed thoughts,
- recognize invitations to respond,
- avoid unnecessary interruptions,
- adapt pacing to the member,
- preserve conversational context.

Listening is the default behavior.

---

5. Multispeaker Conversations

When multiple people participate, Aureus should, whenever technically feasible:

- distinguish speakers,
- preserve speaker context,
- summarize differing viewpoints accurately,
- ask for clarification rather than guessing.

Conversation should resemble respectful participation rather than interruption.

---

6. Dynamic Visual Presentation

The interface shall appear only when it improves understanding or enables action.

Aureus may display:

- opportunities,
- documents,
- comparisons,
- charts,
- timelines,
- educational content,
- approval panels,
- forms,
- media,
- maps,
- progress summaries.

Conversation remains active throughout.

---

7. Participatory Automation

Aureus prepares work while explaining what it is doing.

The steward should:

- explain important decisions,
- identify member choices,
- highlight implications,
- invite participation,
- request approval before meaningful actions.

Automation increases capability rather than replacing understanding.

---

8. Connected Experiences

When authorized by the member, Aureus may coordinate approved connected services.

Examples include:

- email,
- calendar,
- cloud storage,
- financial services,
- educational services,
- government resources,
- future approved integrations.

Every connected experience shall remain transparent, permission-based, and revocable.

---

9. Context Continuity

Members should never lose context while switching between conversation and visual interfaces.

The steward remembers where the member was, what they were discussing, what has already been completed, and what remains.

Returning members resume naturally.

---

10. Future Modalities

This specification applies not only to today's platforms but also to future interaction technologies.

Whether Aureus is experienced through:

- phones,
- tablets,
- desktops,
- smart displays,
- wearables,
- mixed reality,
- or future interfaces,

the institutional principles remain unchanged.

---

11. Constitutional Test

Every multimodal interaction shall answer:

- Does conversation remain primary?
- Does the interface support understanding?
- Does automation preserve member sovereignty?
- Does the member remain in control?
- Does this strengthen trust?

If not, redesign the interaction.
